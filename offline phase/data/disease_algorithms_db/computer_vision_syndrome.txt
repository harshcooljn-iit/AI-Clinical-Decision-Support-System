DISEASE: Computer Vision Syndrome

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CVS: Computer Vision Syndrome
- CRT: Cathode Ray Tube
- LCD: Liquid Crystal Display
- AIOS: All India Ophthalmological Society

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Symptoms include tiredness of eyes, headache, blurred near and distant vision, dry or irritated eyes, neck pain and/or backaches, diplopia (double vision), difficulty in re-focusing the eyes.
- Diagnostic tests include tear film break-up time and Schirmer test.
- Differential diagnosis includes Cervical spondylitis, anxiety and migraine.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# Non-pharmacological treatment
IF (Patient has Computer Vision Syndrome) THEN
    # Counselling and Work Environment Modification
    Counsel patient regarding the syndrome and work environment modification.

    # Eye Exam
    IF (Patient is starting computer use) THEN
        Get a computerized eye exam.
    ENDIF
    IF (Patient has been using computer for > 1 year) THEN
        Repeat computerized eye exam once a year.
    ENDIF

    # Lighting
    Use proper lighting.
    Eliminate exterior light by closing drapes, shades, or blinds.
    Reduce interior lighting by using lower intensity bulbs and tubes.

    # Glare Minimization
    Minimize glare:
    IF (Glare is present) THEN
        Install an anti-glare screen on the monitor.
        Paint bright white walls a darker color with a matt finish.
    ENDIF

    # Display Upgrade
    Upgrade display:
    IF (Using CRT monitor) THEN
        Use LCD monitor instead.
    ENDIF

    # Screen Adjustment
    Adjust the brightness and contrast of the computer screen.

    # Blinking
    Encourage patient to blink more often.

    # Eye Exercises
    Exercise eyes: Follow 20-20-20 rule (after every 20 minutes, look at 20 feet distance for 20 seconds).

    # Breaks
    Take frequent breaks:
    - Two 15-minute breaks.
    - Four additional five-minute "minibreaks" throughout the workday (6-8 hrs).

    # Workstation Modification
    Modify workstation for proper posture:
    - Position computer screen 20 to 24 inches from eyes.
    - Center of screen should be about 10 to 15 degrees below eye level.
    - Top of screen tilted back slightly (10-20 degrees) away from the operator.

    # Eyeglasses
    Consider customized eyeglasses specific for computer use.
    IF (Using eyeglasses for computer work) THEN
        Use anti-reflective coating in the lenses.
        Avoid contact lens use during computer work.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# Pharmacological treatment
IF (Patient has Computer Vision Syndrome AND symptoms are aggravated during computer work) THEN
    # Tear Substitute
    Consider tear substitute.
    Examples of commercially available tear substitutes include sodium carboxymethylcellulose and hydroxypropyl methylcellulose.
ENDIF
