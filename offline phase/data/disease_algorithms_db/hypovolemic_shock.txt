```text
DISEASE: Hypovolemic Shock

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IV: Intravenous
- PCV: Packed Red Blood Cells
- Inj.: Injection
- H2: Histamine-2

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical signs and symptoms suggestive of shock (e.g., tachycardia, hypotension, poor perfusion, altered mental status) in the context of volume loss (diarrhea, vomiting, hemorrhage, burns, nephrotic syndrome).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient is in Hypovolemic Shock THEN
    - Establish IV or intraosseous access.
    - Administer initial fluid bolus: 20 ml/kg of isotonic saline over 5-10 minutes.

    IF Patient's shock persists after initial bolus THEN
        - Repeat fluid bolus: Up to 60-80 ml/kg of isotonic saline if required.
        - Monitor patient response.

    IF Shock is refractory to fluid resuscitation THEN
        - Consider inotropic therapy.
        - IF Dopamine is chosen THEN
            - Administer Inj. dopamine at 3-20 microgram/kg/min IV.
        - ELSE IF Dobutamine is chosen THEN
            - Administer Inj. dobutamine at 1-10 microgram/kg/min IV.
        - ELSE IF Nor-epinephrine is chosen THEN
            - Administer Inj. nor-epinephrine at 0.05-1.5 microgram/kg/min IV.

    IF Etiology is hemorrhagic shock THEN
        - Stop ongoing blood loss.
        - IF Significant blood loss persists THEN
            - Consider transfusion with PCV.

    IF Etiology is nephrotic syndrome THEN
        - Administer IV albumin.

    IF Etiology involves diarrhea or vomiting THEN
        - Treat diarrhea with appropriate antibiotics.
        - Treat vomiting with anti-emetics.
        - Consider H2 receptor blockers or proton pump inhibitors.
ENDIF
```
