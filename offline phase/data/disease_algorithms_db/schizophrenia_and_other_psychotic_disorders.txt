DISEASE: Schizophrenia And Other Psychotic Disorders

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Schizophrenia: Schizophrenia
- Schizophreniform disorder: Schizophreniform disorder
- Brief psychotic disorder: Brief psychotic disorder
- Delusional disorder: Delusional disorder
- Schizoaffective disorder: Schizoaffective disorder
- Psychotic Disorders: Psychotic Disorders

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Schizophrenia: Clinical features including delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, grossly disorganized or catatonic behavior, negative symptoms. Duration of at least 6 months with at least 1 month of active-phase symptoms.
- Schizophreniform disorder: Clinical features similar to schizophrenia, but duration of symptoms is less than 6 months.
- Brief psychotic disorder: Clinical features similar to schizophrenia, but duration of symptoms is less than 1 month.
- Delusional disorder: Characterized by single or multiple non-bizarre delusions of 1 month duration. Functioning and behavior in other areas are intact.
- Schizoaffective disorder: Symptoms of schizophrenia are present continuously, with overlapping features of bipolar affective disorder.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF DISEASE is Schizophrenia THEN
    Pharmacological treatment is similar to Schizophrenia.
ELSE IF DISEASE is Schizophreniform disorder THEN
    Pharmacological treatment is similar to Schizophrenia.
ELSE IF DISEASE is Brief psychotic disorder THEN
    Pharmacological treatment is similar to Schizophrenia.
ELSE IF DISEASE is Delusional disorder THEN
    Insight oriented psychotherapy is the mainstay of treatment.
    Pharmacological treatment is similar to Schizophrenia.
ELSE IF DISEASE is Schizoaffective disorder THEN
    Pharmacological treatment includes antipsychotics and mood stabilizers (as in Schizophrenia and Bipolar disorder respectively).
ENDIF
