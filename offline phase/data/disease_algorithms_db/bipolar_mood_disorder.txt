DISEASE: Bipolar Mood Disorder

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ADHD: Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder
- EEG: Electroencephalogram
- ECG: Electrocardiogram
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
- PET: Positron Emission Tomography
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
- SSRI: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor
- SNRI: Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitor
- MAOI: Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitor
- TCA: Tricyclic Antidepressant
- CBT: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
- DBT: Dialectical Behavior Therapy
- IPT: Interpersonal Therapy
- ECT: Electroconvulsive Therapy
- EPS: Extrapyramidal Symptoms
- NMS: Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome
- SIADH: Syndrome of Inappropriate Antidiuretic Hormone Secretion
- LFTs: Liver Function Tests
- RFTs: Renal Function Tests
- CBC: Complete Blood Count
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- CRP: C-Reactive Protein
- TSH: Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone
- FT4: Free Thyroxine
- HbA1c: Glycated Hemoglobin

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Characterized by episodes of mania and depression or mania alone with intervening periods of normalcy.
- Diagnosis involves clinical assessment of mood episodes, history, and ruling out other conditions.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF patient presents with symptoms suggestive of a manic episode AND is known to have a history of depression:
        THEN stop antidepressants.
        IF patient is taking anti-manic medication:
            THEN check compliance and dose.
            IF antipsychotics are being taken:
                THEN increase dose if necessary AND consider adding lithium or valproate.
            ELSE IF lithium is being taken:
                THEN check plasma levels.
                IF levels are low:
                    THEN consider increasing the dose to achieve levels of 1.0 to 1.2 mEq/L AND/OR consider adding antipsychotics.
            ELSE IF valproate is being taken:
                THEN check plasma levels.
                IF levels are low:
                    THEN consider increasing the dose if tolerated AND consider adding antipsychotics.
        ELSE:
            IF symptoms are severe:
                THEN consider an antipsychotic.
                THEN consider adding lithium (if future adherence likely) or valproate (avoid in women of childbearing potential).
            IF response is inadequate (to antipsychotic alone or lithium/valproate):
                THEN combine antipsychotic with lithium/valproate.
        IN ALL PATIENTS (manic episode):
            ADD short-term benzodiazepine (lorazepam or clonazepam), which can be tapered off.

2.  **Treatment of Depressive Episode:**
    IF patient presents with a depressive episode:
        THEN treat as in major depressive disorder.
        CONSIDER lamotrigine 25-100 mg as the treatment of choice.

3.  **Prophylactic Treatment (Prevention of Recurrent Episodes):**
    IF prophylactic treatment is indicated:
        THEN administer one of the following:
        - Lithium carbonate 900-1500 mg /day in divided doses.
        - Sodium valproate 600-2000 mg /day in divided doses.
        - Carbamazepine 600-1200 mg / day in divided doses.

4.  **Dosage Schedule of Anti-Manic Drugs:**
    IF Lithium is chosen:
        THEN start with 600 to 900 mg/day after getting baseline investigations done.
        ADJUST dose after 3-4 days according to plasma levels.
        Initial dose can also be 400 mg/day adjusted after 3-4 days.
    IF Sodium valproate is chosen:
        THEN start with 200mg thrice a day.
        GRADUALLY increase up to the per kg maximum dose (20-30 mg/kg/day for slow release) if no or less response.
    IF Oxcarbazepine is chosen:
        THEN start with 300mg.
        BUILD UP to 1500 mg.
        NOTE: Oxcarbazepine has lesser side effects than carbamazepine.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Sodium valproate: AVOID in women of childbearing potential (due to teratogenicity).
- Lithium:
    IF patient develops fever, vomiting, or diarrhea while on lithium:
        THEN reduce the dose of lithium to half and contact a physician or psychiatrist.
    ADVISE patients on lithium to take plenty of fluids, especially during summer, and not to restrict salt intake.
