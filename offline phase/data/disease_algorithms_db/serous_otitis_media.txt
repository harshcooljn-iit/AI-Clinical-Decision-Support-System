```text
DISEASE: Serous Otitis Media

ABBREVIATIONS:
- TDS: Three times a day
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Audiogram reveals conductive deafness.
- Impedance audiometry shows a flat type curve (B curve).
- Salient features: Deafness, sensation of fluid, tinnitus. Earache may be absent. Ear drum may appear normal, lusterless, retracted, or show fluid level or air bubbles.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Serous Otitis Media THEN

    // Pharmacological Treatment
    IF Patient is an adult THEN
        // Amoxicillin/Clavulanic Acid
        Administer Cap. amoxicillin 250 mg with clavulanic acid 375 mg, TDS before meals for 21 days.

        // Pseudoephedrine
        Administer Tab. pseudoephedrine 120 mg 8 hourly.
    ELSE IF Patient is a child THEN
        // Amoxicillin/Clavulanic Acid
        Administer Cap. amoxicillin 250 mg with clavulanic acid 375 mg, TDS before meals for 21 days.

        // Pseudoephedrine
        Administer pseudoephedrine 1-2 mg/kg twice daily.
    ENDIF

    // Surgical Treatment (if pharmacological treatment fails)
    IF Patient does not respond to long-term pharmacological measures THEN
        Consider surgical options:
        - Myringotomy with or without grommet insertion.
        - Adenoidectomy.
        - Treatment for sinusitis.
        - Mastoidotomy (in refractory cases).
    ENDIF

    // Patient Education
    Advise patient NOT to instill oil in the ear.

ENDIF
```
