DISEASE: Tetanus

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- ICU: Intensive Care Unit
- CVS: Cardiovascular System
- TIG: Tetanus Immunoglobulin
- mg: Milligram
- gm: Gram
- Inj.: Injection
- tab: tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on symptoms (trismus, rigidity, spasms).
- Culture of _C. tetani_ from wound provides supportive evidence.
- Differential diagnosis includes strychnine poisoning, dystonic reactions, hypocalcemia, meningoencephalitis (neonatal tetanus), oropharyngeal infection (cephalic tetanus).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Non-pharmacological treatment (applies to all grades)
IF (Patient has Tetanus) THEN
    Admit patient in a quiet room or ICU with minimum stimulation.
    Protect airways (intubation/tracheostomy) with or without mechanical ventilation.
    Monitor cardiovascular parameters.
    Clean, explore, and debride wound.
    Maintain hydration and enteral/parentral nutrition (high calorie, high protein).
ENDIF

// Pharmacological treatment based on Grade

// Grade 1
IF (Grade is 1) THEN
    Administer Tab. diazepam 5-20 mg three times a day.
    // Alternative for moderate to severe tetanus: slow IV infusion of diazepam.
    // Maximum dose: not exceed 80-100 mg in 24 hours.
    IF (Spasms are not controlled) THEN
        Administer Inj. phenobarbitone 200 mg IM every 8-12 hours OR
        Administer Inj. chlorpromazine 50 mg IM four times a day.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Grade 2
IF (Grade is 2) THEN
    Perform tracheostomy.
    Administer Inj. diazepam (dosage not specified, likely similar to Grade 1 but potentially higher or IV).
    IF (Spasms are not controlled) THEN
        Administer Inj. phenobarbitone 200 mg IM every 8-12 hours OR
        Administer Inj. chlorpromazine 50 mg IM four times a day.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Grade 3 and 4
IF (Grade is 3 OR Grade is 4) THEN
    Provide ventilatory support.
    Administer Inj. pancuronium 2-4 mg IV OR
    Administer Inj. gallamine 20-40 mg IV.
    IF (Hypotension is present) THEN
        Administer vasopressor support.
    ENDIF
    IF (Hypertension is present) THEN
        Administer antihypertensive.
    ENDIF
    IF (Bradyarrhythmias are present) THEN
        Administer Inj. atropine 0.6-1.2 mg IV.
    ENDIF
    Administer Inj. diazepam (dosage not specified, likely similar to Grade 1 but potentially higher or IV).
    IF (Spasms are not controlled) THEN
        Administer Inj. phenobarbitone 200 mg IM every 8-12 hours OR
        Administer Inj. chlorpromazine 50 mg IM four times a day.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Additional treatments for ALL patients
IF (Patient has Tetanus) THEN
    Administer Inj. crystalline penicillin G 2 mega units IV every 6 hours for 10 days OR
    Administer Inj. metronidazole 500 mg IV every 8 hours OR
    Administer Inj. metronidazole 1 gm IV every 12 hours.
    // Dosage may be adjusted according to the infected wound.
    Administer Inj. human tetanus immunoglobulin (TIG) 3000-5000 units IV or IM OR
    IF (Human TIG is not available) THEN
        Administer Inj. equine antiserum 10000 units by slow IV injection after sensitivity test.
    ENDIF
    Administer antiserum before local manipulation of the wound.
ENDIF
