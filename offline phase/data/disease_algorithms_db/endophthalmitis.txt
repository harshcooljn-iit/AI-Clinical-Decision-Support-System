DISEASE: Endophthalmitis

DIAGNOSTICS:
- History of eye surgery, penetrating injury, fever, infection, or predisposing systemic diseases.
- Symptoms: visual loss, pain, discharge, photophobia, lid swelling.
- Signs: conjunctival congestion, hypopyon, vitreous exudates, loss of red fundus reflex.
- Smear and culture testing of aqueous humour or vitreous humour.
- Antibiotic susceptibility testing.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# Post-operative Endophthalmitis Treatment
IF Disease is Post-operative Endophthalmitis THEN
    # Intravitreal Injection
    Intravitreal Inj. vancomycin hydrochloride 1 mg in 0.1 ml PLUS Inj. ceftazidime 2 mg in 0.1 ml OR Inj. amikacin sulfate 0.4 mg in 0.1 ml.

    # Subconjunctival Injection
    Subconjunctival Inj. vancomycin 25 mg/0.5 ml PLUS Inj. ceftazidime 100 mg/0.5 ml PLUS dexamethasone 0.25 mg/0.5 ml.

    # Topical Eye Drops
    Vancomycin eye drops 50 mg/ml plus amikacin eye drops 15 mg/ml, 1 drop every 6 hours.

    # Cycloplegic Drops
    Homatropine 2% eye drops 3 times a day OR atropine 1% eye ointment 2 times a day.

    # Corticosteroid Eye Drops
    Prednisolone acetate 1% eye drops OR dexamethasone 0.1% eye drops OR betamethasone 0.1% eye drops, every 6 hours.

    # Oral Corticosteroids
    IF Antibiotics have been used for 24 hours THEN
        Tab. prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day in a single morning dose for 5-10 days, 2 times a day.
    ENDIF

    # Parenteral Antibiotics
    Parenteral antibiotics are of questionable value and given only as supportive therapy.
ENDIF

# Traumatic Endophthalmitis Treatment
IF Disease is Traumatic Endophthalmitis THEN
    # Hospitalization and Immunization
    Hospitalize the patient and give immunization for tetanus.

    # Parenteral Antibiotics
    Inj. vancomycin 1 g IV infused over 1 hour, every 12 hours.
    OR
    Inj. gentamicin 2 mg/kg every 12 hours.
    OR
    Inj. ceftazidime 2 g IV every 12 hours.
    OR
    Inj. ceftriaxone 2 g IV/day.

    # Topical, Subconjunctival, Intravitreal, and Cycloplegic Treatment
    Topical fortified eye drops, subconjunctival injection, intravitreal injection, and cycloplegic drops as in cases of postoperative bacterial endophthalmitis.

    # Surgical Treatment
    Repair the ruptured eyeball at the earliest.
ENDIF

# Fungal Endophthalmitis Treatment
IF Disease is Fungal Endophthalmitis THEN
    # Vitrectomy
    Vitrectomy to debulk the vitreous of fungi.

    # Intravitreal Injection
    Intravitreal Inj. amphotericin B 5-10 mcg/0.1 ml OR Inj. fluconazole 25 mcg/0.1 ml.

    # Systemic Antifungals
    # Option 1: Amphotericin B
    Inj. amphotericin B 0.5-1.5 mg/kg/day slow infusion over 2-6 hours for 10-14 days.
    OR
    # Option 2: Fluconazole (Adults)
    Tab. fluconazole 400 mg loading dose followed by 200 mg daily, total dose should not exceed 600 mg/day.
    OR
    # Option 3: Fluconazole (Children)
    Tab. fluconazole 12 mg/kg loading dose followed by 6 mg/kg/day.
    OR
    # Option 4: Ketoconazole (Children above 2 years)
    Tab. ketoconazole 3.3-6.6 mg/kg/day, 2 times a day or daily.
    OR
    # Option 5: Ketoconazole (Adults - not explicitly stated, but implied)
    Tab. ketoconazole 200 mg orally 2 times a day or daily.

    # Cycloplegic Drops
    Homatropine 2% eye drops 4 times a day OR atropine 1% eye ointment 2 times a day.
ENDIF

# Surgical Indication
IF Visual acuity is limited to light perception OR poor response to treatment in 30-36 hours THEN
    Pars plana vitrectomy is indicated.
ENDIF
