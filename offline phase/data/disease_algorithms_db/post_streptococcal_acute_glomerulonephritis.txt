DISEASE: Post Streptococcal Acute Glomerulonephritis (AGN)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- AGN: Acute Glomerulonephritis
- ASOT: Anti Streptolysin O Titre
- Inj.: Injection
- IV: Intravenous
- K: Potassium
- U: Units
- mmol: millimoles
- mg: milligrams
- kg: kilograms
- U: Units
- tab: tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Sudden onset of hematuria, oliguria, edema, and hypertension.
- Raised urea and creatinine.
- Positive tests for streptococcal infection.
- Low C3 level.
- Renal biopsy may be done in severe/prolonged cases.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Post Streptococcal Acute Glomerulonephritis (AGN) THEN

  // Monitoring
  MONITOR fluid intake, output, blood pressure, urea, creatinine, and electrolytes regularly.
  PERFORM Complete blood count, ASOT, C3, renal ultrasound at presentation.

  // General Measures
  Fluid restriction to urine output + insensible losses.
  Potassium restriction.

  // Antibiotics (if indicated)
  IF Evidence of active streptococcal throat or skin infection THEN
    ADMINISTER Inj. Procaine penicillin 4 lac units once daily.
  ENDIF

  // Treatment of Complications
  IF Complications present (hyperkalemia, hypertension, fluid overload) THEN
    // Hyperkalemia Management
    IF Hyperkalemia present THEN
      // Step 1: Restrict K intake
      RESTRICT K intake.

      // Step 2: Transient reversal of cardiotoxicity
      ADMINISTER Calcium gluconate (10%) 1 ml/kg in distilled water, slow IV infusion.

      // Step 3: Temporary reduction of potassium in extracellular fluid
      ADMINISTER Nebulised salbutamol.
      ADMINISTER Sodium Bicarbonate: 1-2 mmol/kg.
      ADMINISTER Glucose (0.5 g/kg) + insulin (0.1 U/kg) infusion.

      // Step 4: Removal of potassium from the body
      ADMINISTER Frusemide (1-2 mg/kg).
      ADMINISTER Potassium exchange resins (1 g/kg/day).
      CONSIDER Dialysis.
    ENDIF

    // Hypertension Management (Not detailed in provided text, assume standard pediatric protocols)

    IF Conservative management of complications is insufficient THEN
      INITIATE early dialysis.
    ENDIF
  ENDIF

  // Patient Education
  EXPLAIN natural course to parents (most recover within 2-4 weeks, few may develop chronic renal insufficiency).

ENDIF
