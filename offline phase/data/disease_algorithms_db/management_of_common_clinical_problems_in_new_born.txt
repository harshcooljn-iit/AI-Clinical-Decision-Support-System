DISEASE: Management Of Common Clinical Problems In New Born

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Gentian violet: Topical antiseptic
- Povidone iodine: Topical antiseptic
- Erythromycin: Antibiotic
- Silver nitrate: Cauterizing agent

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Regurgitation/Vomiting: Observation of non-projectile expulsion of stomach contents.
- Bowel disorders: Observation of stool frequency and consistency.
- Delayed passage of urine: Observation of urine output within 48 hours of birth.
- Dehydration fever: Temperature measurement, assessment of activity, feeding, and hydration status.
- Excessive crying: Observation of crying pattern (consolable vs. inconsolable, pitch).
- Umbilical sepsis: Visual inspection of the umbilical cord stump for pus, erythema, or induration.
- Umbilical granuloma: Visual inspection of the umbilical cord base for a red nodule.
- Engorgement/Vaginal bleeding: Observation of breast tissue and vaginal discharge.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# Regurgitation/Vomiting
IF Condition is non-projectile expulsion of stomach contents (regurgitation)
THEN Advise regarding feeding technique.
ELSE IF Condition is projectile vomiting
THEN Further investigation and management needed (not specified in text).

# Bowel Disorders
IF Condition is passage of stools after each feed
THEN Advise that this is normal (exaggerated gastrocolic reflex) and no medication is needed.
ELSE IF Condition is exclusively breast-fed baby passing loose stools (10-15 times/day) between day 3 and day 14
THEN Advise that these are transitional stools and no medication is needed.
ELSE IF Condition is other bowel disorder (not specified)
THEN Further investigation and management needed (not specified in text).

# Delayed Passage of Urine
IF Condition is non-passage of urine by 48 hours after birth
THEN Investigate for urinary tract anomalies.
ELSE IF Condition is crying before passing urine
THEN Advise that this is normal.

# Dehydration Fever
IF Condition is transitory moderate fever (up to 38.5°C) during the second or third day of life
AND Baby is active
AND Baby sucks well
THEN Advise lowering the environmental temperature.
ELSE IF Condition is fever with other symptoms or high temperature
THEN Further investigation and management needed (not specified in text).

# Excessive Crying
IF Condition is excessive inconsolable crying or high-pitched crying
THEN Suspect meningitis or other painful inflammatory conditions. Further investigation needed.
ELSE IF Condition is crying due to hunger, full bladder, wet napkin, or nose block
THEN Address the underlying cause (feeding, diaper change, nasal suction).

# Umbilical Sepsis
IF Condition is pus discharge from umbilicus NOT extending to periumbilical skin
THEN Apply 10% gentian violet or povidone iodine locally twice a day.
ELSE IF Condition is periumbilical erythema or induration
THEN Administer syrup erythromycin 40 mg/kg/day in 3-4 divided doses.
ELSE IF Condition is umbilical sepsis with high risk factors
THEN Refer to a higher centre.

# Umbilical Granuloma
IF Condition is a red flesh-like nodule at the base of the umbilical cord
THEN Manage by cautery with silver nitrate or application of common salt for 3 to 4 days.

# Engorgement/Vaginal Bleeding
IF Condition is engorgement of breasts in both sexes or vaginal bleeding after 4 days of birth
THEN Advise that this is normal.
