DISEASE: Leptospirosis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
- MAT: Micro agglutination test
- ELISA: Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay
- MSAT: Microscopic Slide Agglutination Test
- ANTD: Allergy Negative Test Dose
- IV: Intravenous
- IU: International Units
- mg: Milligrams
- kg: Kilograms
- Tab: Tablet
- Cap: Capsule
- Inj: Injection
- stat: Immediately
- prerenal: Before renal

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Culture of Blood, CSF, and urine.
- Micro agglutination test (MAT) showing seroconversion or four-fold rise in titer.
- Positive ELISA or MSAT.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient is clinically suspected of leptospirosis in an endemic area during the rainy season:
  THEN start presumptive treatment for leptospirosis and malaria.
  IF patient is an adult:
    THEN administer Tab. doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for 7 days.
    THEN administer Tab. chloroquine 600mg (base) stat, followed by 600 mg after 24 hours, and 300 mg after another 24 hours.
  ELSE IF patient is a child less than 6 years old:
    THEN administer Cap. amoxicillin or Cap. ampicillin 30-50 mg/kg/day in divided doses every 6 hours for 7 days.

ELSE IF definitive diagnosis is confirmed:
  IF patient is not penicillin sensitive:
    THEN administer Inj. crystalline penicillin 20 lacs IU IV every 6 hours after a negative test dose (ANTD) for 7 days.
    IF patient is a child:
      THEN administer crystalline penicillin 2-4 lacs IU/kg/day IV every 6 hours for 7 days.
  ELSE IF patient is penicillin sensitive:
    THEN administer Inj. ceftriaxone 1 gm IV once daily for 7 days.
    OR administer Inj. cefotaxime 1 gm IV every 6 hours daily for 7 days.

SYMPTOMATIC AND SUPPORTIVE TREATMENT:
- Manage fluid and electrolyte balance.
- Treat hypovolemia and hypotension with IV fluids.
- If oligouria and prerenal azotemia suspected, attempt diuresis with fluid therapy.
- Treat headache and myalgia with analgesics.
- Treat fever with antipyretics.
- Treat restlessness and anxiety with sedatives.
- Treat anemia with blood transfusion.
- For renal failure, consider peritoneal dialysis (if no contraindications) or hemodialysis.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Doxycycline: Children less than 6 years old (unless alternative amoxicillin/ampicillin regimen is used).
- Crystalline penicillin: Penicillin sensitive patients.
- Peritoneal dialysis: Patients with contraindications to peritoneal dialysis.
