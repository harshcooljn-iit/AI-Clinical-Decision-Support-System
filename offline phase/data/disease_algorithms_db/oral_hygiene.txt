DISEASE: Oral Hygiene

ABBREVIATIONS: None

DIAGNOSTICS: Based on clinical examination, patient history, and assessment of oral health practices.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient needs improved oral hygiene THEN
    IF toothbrush quality is inadequate THEN
        SELECT a toothbrush that is short, soft, and has uniformly trimmed bristles.
    ENDIF
    IF toothbrush is older than 3 months THEN
        CHANGE toothbrush.
    ENDIF
    IF brushing frequency is less than twice daily THEN
        ADVISE brushing teeth at least twice daily for 2-3 minutes.
    ENDIF
    IF brushing technique is incorrect OR excessive force is used THEN
        ADVISE using the right teeth brushing technique and avoiding excessive force.
    ENDIF
    IF flossing frequency is less than once daily THEN
        ADVISE using flossing at least once daily.
    ENDIF
    IF excessive sugar intake OR excessive aerated drink intake occurs THEN
        ADVISE avoiding too much sugar and aerated drinks.
    ENDIF
    IF eating occurs between meals THEN
        IF rinsing mouth or brushing teeth is not possible after eating between meals THEN
            ADVISE rinsing mouth or brushing teeth after eating between meals.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
    IF fluorinated toothpaste is ingested or swallowed during brushing THEN
        ADVISE not to ingest or swallow fluorinated toothpaste while brushing.
    ENDIF
    IF dental checkup interval is not 6 months THEN
        ADVISE ensuring regular dental checkups at 6-monthly intervals.
    ENDIF
ENDIF
