```text
DISEASE: Nutritional Rickets

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IU: International Units
- IM: Intramuscular
- S.: Serum

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical features: Skeletal deformities (genu valgum/genu varum, broadening of wrists), susceptibility to fractures, weakness, hypotonia, growth disturbances.
- X-ray: Increased thickness of epiphyseal growth plate, cupped with hazy metaphyseal borders.
- Lab tests: S. calcium normal/low, phosphate low/normal, alkaline phosphatase raised.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient is diagnosed with Nutritional Rickets THEN
    - Encourage outdoor play/sunlight exposure.
    - Enhance dietary intake of vitamin D (dairy, egg, fish liver oil).
    - Administer Vitamin D 600,000 IU orally or IM stat (especially if sick due to infection).
    - Repeat X-ray wrist after 4 weeks.

    IF X-ray after 4 weeks shows a positive response (healing line of rickets) THEN
        - Continue oral Vitamin D 400 IU/day.
    ELSE IF X-ray after 4 weeks shows no response THEN
        - Refer patient to a higher center for evaluation of non-nutritional rickets.
    ENDIF
ENDIF
```
