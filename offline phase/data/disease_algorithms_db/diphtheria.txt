```text
DISEASE: Diphtheria

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IU: International Units
- Kg: Kilogram
- IV: Intravenous
- DTP: Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis vaccine

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Fever, chills, fatigue, sore throat, hoarseness, cough, difficulty swallowing, painful swallowing, difficulty breathing, foul-smelling bloodstained nasal discharge.
- Etiological agent: Corynebacterium diphtheriae.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Diphtheria THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    Hydrate patient.
    Maintain airway.
    IF hypoxia is present THEN
        Perform tracheotomy.
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment
    Administer Inj. diphtheria Antitoxin 20000 to 100000 IU after test dose.

    // Antibiotic treatment
    IF patient is NOT allergic to penicillin THEN
        Administer Inj. crystalline penicillin 1,00,000-1,50,000 IU/Kg/Day IV in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
    ELSE
        Administer erythromycin 40 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Penicillin allergy is a contraindication for Inj. crystalline penicillin.
```
