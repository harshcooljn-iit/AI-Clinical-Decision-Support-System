DISEASE: Inflammatory Gingival Enlargement

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Tab.: Tablet
- b.d.: Twice daily
- mg: Milligram
- q.i.d.: Four times daily

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination revealing gingival enlargement (acute or chronic).
- Acute type: Localized or generalized, very painful, deep red, soft, friable, shiny surface.
- Chronic type: Localized or generalized, often painless, slowly progressive.
- Etiological factor evaluation is required before treatment.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF Etiological factor is evaluated THEN
    IF Acute inflammatory gingival enlargement THEN
        IF Pain is severe THEN
            THEN Administer Tab. diclofenac potassium 50 mg b.d. for 3-5 days OR Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg 3 times a day for 3-5 days.
        ENDIF
        THEN Administer Tab. ciprofloxacin 500 mg 2 times a day for 3-5 days.
        THEN Instruct patient to rinse with 0.2% chlorhexidine mouthwash twice daily.
        THEN Consider referral to a periodontist for surgical management and drainage of pus.
    ELSE IF Chronic inflammatory gingival enlargement THEN
        THEN Administer Tab. diclofenac potassium 50 mg b.d. for 3-5 days OR Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg 3 times a day for 3-5 days.
        THEN Administer Tab. ciprofloxacin 500 mg 2 times a day for 3-5 days.
        THEN Instruct patient to rinse with 0.2% chlorhexidine mouthwash twice daily.
        THEN Consider referral to a periodontist for surgical management and drainage of pus.
    ENDIF
ELSE
    THEN Evaluate etiological factor before initiating treatment.
ENDIF

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Instruct patient on proper brushing technique twice daily using a super soft toothbrush.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text, but standard contraindications for listed drugs apply, e.g., allergy to fluoroquinolones for ciprofloxacin, NSAID allergy/sensitivity for diclofenac/ibuprofen).
