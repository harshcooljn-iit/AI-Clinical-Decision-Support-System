```text
DISEASE: Viral Keratitis (specifically Herpes Simplex Keratitis)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- HSV: Herpes simplex virus
- IOP: Intraocular pressure

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Irritation, photophobia, reduction in vision (if central cornea affected), corneal anesthesia.
- Fluorescein staining: Visualization of dendritic ulcers (branching, linear pattern with feathery edges and terminal bulbs).
- History: Primary or recurrent infection.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Disease is Viral Keratitis (specifically Herpes Simplex Keratitis) THEN

  IF Treatment is Pharmacological THEN

    IF Topical Antiviral Treatment is indicated THEN

      IF Drug is Trifluridine THEN
        Frequency: Every 2 hours while awake
        Strength: 1% solution
      ENDIF

      IF Drug is Acyclovir THEN
        Frequency: 5 times daily
        Strength: 3% ointment
      ENDIF

      IF Drug is Idoxuridine THEN
        Frequency: Hourly while awake
        Strength: 0.1% solution
      ENDIF

      IF Drug is Vidarabine THEN
        Frequency: 5 times daily
        Strength: 3% ointment
      ENDIF

    ENDIF

    IF Oral Acyclovir is indicated (severe disease, atopic individuals) THEN

      IF Patient is non-immunocompromised THEN
        Dosage: 400 mg
        Frequency: Five times daily
      ENDIF

      IF Patient is immunocompromised OR atopic THEN
        Dosage: 800 mg
        Frequency: Five times daily
      ENDIF

    ENDIF

    IF Oral Famciclovir or Valacyclovir is used THEN
      Dosage and Frequency: Not specified in text.
    ENDIF

  ENDIF

  IF Topical Corticosteroids are used THEN
    CAUTION: Accelerate corneal thinning, increasing risk of corneal perforation.
  ENDIF

  IF Epithelial Debridement is performed (for dendritic keratitis) THEN
    Method: Remove infected epithelium with a tightly wound cotton tip applicator.
    Adjunctive Therapy: Topical antiviral to accelerate epithelial healing.
  ENDIF

  IF Surgical Management (Penetrating Keratoplasty) is indicated (severe corneal scarring) THEN
    Condition: Herpetic disease must be inactive for many months before surgery.
  ENDIF

ELSE

  IF Disease is not Viral Keratitis THEN
    Treatment: Not specified in this guideline.
  ENDIF

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Topical corticosteroids: Contraindicated in active epithelial keratitis due to risk of corneal perforation.
- Surgical management (Penetrating Keratoplasty): Contraindicated if herpetic disease is active.
```
