```text
DISEASE: Ear Wax

ABBREVIATIONS:
- SOS: As needed
- g/kg/day: grams per kilogram per day
- Otolaryngologist: Ear, Nose, and Throat specialist

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Otoscopy
- Brownish-black mass in external auditory canal.
- Symptoms: Blocked ear, pain, tinnitus, reflex cough on manipulation, vertigo.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF severe pain is present THEN
    IF patient is an adult THEN
        Administer ibuprofen 400 mg SOS.
    ELSE IF patient is a child THEN
        Administer ibuprofen 20 mg/kg/day divided into 3 doses.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

IF ear wax is hard THEN
    Administer wax softener for 3-4 days before cleaning.
    Available wax softeners include:
        - Turpentine oil - 15%
        - Benzocaine - 2.7% (often combined with turpentine oil)
        - Chlorbutol - 5%
        - Paradichlorobenzene - 2%
ENDIF

Follow wax softening with surgical removal by an Otolaryngologist.

IF surgical removal is performed THEN
    Perform syringing with sterile saline solution at body temperature.
    Push saline along the posterior wall of the meatus.
    Mop the meatus dry after syringing.
ENDIF

IF there is a previous history of ear discharge OR perforated tympanic membrane OR instrumental manipulation with ring probe, hook, or forceps OR suction cleaning THEN
    Caution is advised regarding syringing.
ENDIF

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Explain that ear wax is a normal secretion providing protection.
- Advise removal only if it disturbs hearing.
- Advise against cleaning the ear with buds as it can push wax deeper.
```
