DISEASE: Acute Flaccid Paralysis (AFP)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- AFP: Acute Flaccid Paralysis
- NPSP: National Polio Surveillance Project

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Child aged <15 years
- Acute onset of flaccid paralysis
- No obvious cause (e.g., severe trauma, electrolyte imbalance)
- Paralysis onset <4 weeks
- Affected limb(s) are flaccid
- Sensation is typically unaffected (if due to polio)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient is a child aged <15 years
THEN
    IF Patient presents with acute onset of flaccid paralysis without obvious cause
    THEN
        IF Paralysis onset is <4 weeks
        THEN
            IF Patient has isolated single lower limb involvement
            THEN
                IF Reporting is after 4 days of onset of paralysis
                THEN
                    IF Paralysis is not progressing for more than 48 hours
                    THEN
                        Output: "Exclude from treatment protocol (specific cases)."
                    ELSE
                        Proceed to general treatment.
                    ENDIF
                ELSE
                    Proceed to general treatment.
                ENDIF
            ELSE
                Proceed to general treatment.
            ENDIF
        ELSE
            Output: "Paralysis onset is not acute (<4 weeks)."
        ENDIF
    ELSE
        Output: "Patient does not have acute flaccid paralysis without obvious cause."
    ENDIF
ELSE
    Output: "Patient is not a child aged <15 years."
ENDIF

Non-pharmacological treatment:
- Complete bed rest.
- Correct positioning: Hip - slight flexion, knee - 5 degrees flexion, foot - 90 degrees with support against the soles.
- Support legs laterally with pillows/towels/salt/sand packs to prevent rotation.
- Passive joint movements (10 min, 2-3 times/day) when pain subsides.
- Warm water fomentation (10 min, 2-3 times/day) for pain relief.
- Alternate hot/cold compresses over suprapubic region for transient urinary retention.

Caution:
- No massage.
- No intramuscular injections.
- Watch for progression, especially respiratory muscle involvement.

Pharmacological treatment:
- No specific drug therapy for polio.
- For fever and pain: Use paracetamol or ibuprofen.

Referral criteria:
IF Any of the following are present
THEN
    Refer to a tertiary care level center with ventilatory support facilities:
    - Progression of paralysis
    - Respiratory distress
    - Bulbar involvement
    - Paralysis of upper limbs <3 days old
    - Marked drowsiness
    - Any other complication
ENDIF

Patient education:
- No dietary restrictions.
- Continue breastfeeding or regular feeding.
- Paralysis usually progresses for 4-7 days.
- Recovery may start thereafter over days to weeks.
- Regular physiotherapy facilitates recovery.
- Post-polio residual paralysis should be referred for rehabilitative services.
