DISEASE: Amoebic Liver Abscess

ABBREVIATIONS:
- TC: Total Count
- PT: Prothrombin Time
- APTT: Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time
- SGPT: Serum Glutamic Pyruvic Transaminase (Alanine Aminotransferase)
- TDS: Three times a day
- BD: Twice a day
- IM: Intramuscular
- IV: Intravenous
- cc: cubic centimeters

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Right upper quadrant abdominal pain, fever, history of diarrhea.
- Physical examination: Tenderness in the right hypochondriac region, palpable liver, signs of jaundice.
- Laboratory tests: Elevated Total Count (TC), elevated Prothrombin Time (PT), elevated Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (APTT), abnormal Liver Function Tests (S. Bilirubin, SGPT, Albumin).
- Imaging: Chest X-ray (elevated right diaphragm, rule out perforation), Ultrasound (USG) Abdomen, Contrast-Enhanced CT (CECT) Abdomen (if rupture suspected).
- Definitive diagnosis: Diagnostic aspiration of abscess followed by microscopic examination and culture-sensitivity of pus.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    *   IF Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Amoebic Liver Abscess (RUQ pain, fever, diarrhea history) AND physical exam findings (RUQ tenderness, palpable liver, jaundice) AND laboratory/imaging findings consistent with liver abscess:
        *   THEN Proceed to treatment.
        *   ELSE Consider alternative diagnoses.

2.  **Pharmacological Treatment:**
    *   IF Patient requires pharmacological treatment:
        *   THEN Administer Metronidazole.
        *   IF Metronidazole is indicated:
            *   THEN Administer Tab. metronidazole 800 mg TDS for 2 weeks.
        *   THEN Administer Diloxanide Furoate.
        *   IF Diloxanide Furoate is indicated:
            *   THEN Administer Tab. diloxanide furoate 500 mg BD for 10 days.
        *   THEN Administer Diclofenac Sodium.
        *   IF Diclofenac Sodium is indicated:
            *   THEN Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 75 mg IM 8 hourly.
        *   THEN Administer Pantoprazole.
        *   IF Pantoprazole is indicated:
            *   THEN Administer Inj. pantoprazole 40 mg IV 12 hourly.
        *   THEN Administer Vitamin K.
        *   IF PT/APTT is altered:
            *   THEN Administer Vit. K injections.

3.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    *   THEN Recommend High glucose diet.
    *   THEN Recommend Low protein diet.

4.  **Surgical Treatment:**
    *   IF Amoebic liver abscess requires drainage:
        *   THEN Consider Percutaneous aspiration.
        *   IF Abscess size is large (>100 cc):
            *   THEN Perform Percutaneous aspiration.
        *   ELSE Consider Exploratory laparotomy.
        *   IF Suspected ruptured liver abscess:
            *   THEN Perform Exploratory laparotomy with drainage of abscess.
