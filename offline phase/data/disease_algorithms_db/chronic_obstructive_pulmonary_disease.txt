DISEASE: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- COPD: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease
- FEV1: Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 second
- FVC: Forced Vital Capacity
- GOLD: Global Initiative for Chronic Obstructive Lung Disease
- MDI: Metered Dose Inhaler
- DPI: Dry Powder Inhaler
- µg: micrograms
- mg: milligrams
- TDS: three times a day
- BD: twice a day
- OD: once a day
- O2: Oxygen
- PaCO2: Partial pressure of carbon dioxide in arterial blood
- pH: potential of hydrogen
- RR: Respiratory Rate
- H2O: Water
- cm: centimeters
- mmHg: millimeters of mercury
- NIV: Non Invasive Ventilation
- CMV: Controlled Mechanical Ventilation
- AC: Assist Control
- V-ACMV: Volume Assist Control Mechanical Ventilation
- PEEP: Positive End-Expiratory Pressure
- I:E: Inspiration to Expiration ratio
- PaO2: Partial pressure of oxygen in arterial blood
- SpO2: Peripheral oxygen saturation
- TDS: three times a day
- LVRS: Lung Volume Reduction Surgery

DIAGNOSICS:
- Spirometry is used for diagnosis.
- Severity is classified according to GOLD criteria based on symptoms and spirometry (FEV1/FVC ratio and FEV1 % predicted).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Assess Patient:** Evaluate symptoms (chronic cough, sputum production, breathlessness), exposure history (smoking, dusts, etc.), and perform spirometry.

2.  **Determine GOLD Stage/Severity:**
    IF FEV1/FVC < 0.7 AND FEV1 is >= 80% predicted:
        THEN GOLD Stage I (Mild).
    ELSE IF FEV1/FVC < 0.7 AND FEV1 is 50% to < 80% predicted:
        THEN GOLD Stage IIA (Moderate).
    ELSE IF FEV1/FVC < 0.7 AND FEV1 is 30% to < 50% predicted:
        THEN GOLD Stage III (Severe).
    ELSE IF FEV1/FVC < 0.7 AND (FEV1 < 30% predicted OR FEV1 < 50% predicted with respiratory failure or signs of right heart failure):
        THEN GOLD Stage IV (Very Severe).
    ELSE IF FEV1/FVC >= 0.7:
        THEN At Risk (GOLD Stage 0).

3.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Recommend annual influenza vaccine.
    - Recommend polyvalent pneumococcal vaccine.
    - Consider pulmonary rehabilitation for symptomatic patients.
    - Consider Lung Volume Reduction Surgery (LVRS) for selected patients with emphysema (upper lobe predominant, low post-rehab exercise capacity).
    - Consider Lung Transplantation for candidates <65 years, severe disability despite maximal therapy, free of major co-morbidities.

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment (Stable COPD):**
    - **Bronchodilators (Preferred route: Inhaled):**
        - **Short-acting Beta-agonists (SABA):**
            - Salbutamol: 100-200 µg as needed.
            - Levosalbutamol: 45-90 µg as needed.
            - Terbutaline: 250-500 µg as needed.
        - **Short-acting Anti-cholinergics (SAMA):**
            - Ipratropium bromide: 20-40 µg as needed.
        - **Long-acting Beta-agonists (LABA):**
            - Salmeterol: 25-50 µg twice daily.
            - Formoterol: 6-12 µg twice daily.
            - Indaceterol: 150-300 µg once daily.
        - **Long-acting Anti-cholinergics (LAMA):**
            - Tiotropium: 18 µg once daily.
    - **Theophylline:**
        - Sustained release theophylline: 100-400 mg once daily.
        - Doxophylline: 400 mg twice/thrice daily.
        - Monitor blood levels to minimize toxicity.
    - **Oral Beta-agonists:**
        - Salbutamol: 2-4 mg twice/thrice daily.
        - Bambuterol: 10-20 mg once daily.
        - Terbutaline: 2.5-5 mg TDS.
    - **Supplemental Oxygen:**
        - Prescribe for resting hypoxemia (SpO2 < 88% or <90% with signs of pulmonary hypertension or right heart failure).
        - Prescribe for exertional or nocturnal hypoxemia.
    - **Corticosteroids (Inhaled):**
        - Fluticasone: 25-500 µg twice daily.
        - Budesonide: 100-400 µg twice daily.
        - Beclomethasone: 100-400 µg twice daily.
        - Ciclesonide: 80-160 µg twice daily.
        - Mometasone: 25-500 µg twice daily.
    - **Other agents:**
        - N-acetyl cysteine: 600 mg TDS for 10 to 14 days.

5.  **Acute Exacerbation of COPD:**
    - **Assess Severity:** Check for symptoms (worsening dyspnea, cough, sputum), signs (accessory muscle use, paradoxical breathing, cyanosis, RR > 30, HR > 110, altered sensorium, BP < 90 mmHg), and SpO2 (<90%).
    - **Determine Need for Hospitalization:**
        IF Marked reduction in activity OR Altered sensorium OR New onset cyanosis OR Use of accessory respiratory muscles OR Paradoxical chest wall movements OR Central cyanosis OR Systolic BP < 90 mmHg OR RR > 30/minute OR Heart rate > 110/minute OR Asterixis OR Altered mental status OR SpO2 < 90% OR Presence of severe comorbid conditions OR Lack of social support:
            THEN Hospitalize.
    - **Pharmacological Treatment (Hospitalized):**
        - **Bronchodilators:** Increase dose/frequency of short-acting bronchodilators (SABA/SAMA).
        - **Corticosteroids:** Administer systemic corticosteroids (e.g., prednisone 40 mg daily for 5 days).
        - **Antibiotics:**
            - First line: Amoxicillin 500-1000 mg TDS OR Doxycycline 100 mg BD OR Azithromycin 500 mg OD.
            - Second line: Amoxicillin/clavulanic acid 625 mg TDS OR Cefixime 200 mg BD.
            - Administer for 5-7 days (except Azithromycin for 3 days).
    - **Non-Pharmacological Treatment (Hospitalized):**
        - **Oxygen Therapy:** Titrate to maintain SpO2 88-92%.
        - **Ventilatory Support:**
            - **Noninvasive Ventilation (NIV):**
                IF Respiratory acidosis (pH <= 7.35 and/or PaCO2 >= 45 mmHg) OR Severe dyspnea OR RR > 30 breaths/min OR Use of accessory muscles OR Paradoxical breathing:
                    THEN Initiate NIV (Full face mask, start IPAP 6-8 cm H2O, CPAP 3-4 cm H2O, titrate to TV > 5 ml/kg, RR < 35/min, adjust blood gases).
                    IF Failure in improvement of clinical parameters/gas exchange at 1 hour OR Development of altered sensorium OR Hemodynamic instability OR Inability to tolerate mask:
                        THEN Consider Invasive Mechanical Ventilation.
            - **Invasive Mechanical Ventilation:**
                IF pH <= 7.25 OR Unable to tolerate NIV/NIV failure OR Respiratory/cardiac arrest OR Massive aspiration OR Diminished consciousness OR Failure to handle secretions OR HR < 50/min with loss of alertness OR Hemodynamic instability without fluid responsiveness OR Severe ventricular arrhythmias OR Life-threatening hypoxia:
                    THEN Initiate Invasive Mechanical Ventilation (Mode: V-ACMV, Initial TV: 4-6 ml/kg, RR: 8-12/min, PEEP: 5-8 cm H2O, I:E: 1:3-1:6, Square waveform).
                    Goals: Plateau pressure < 30 cm H2O, PaO2 55-60 mmHg, pH 7.2-7.4.

6.  **Criteria for Hospital Discharge:**
    - Able to use long-acting bronchodilators.
    - Inhaled short-acting drugs no more than every 4 hours.
    - Patient is previously ambulatory and able to move across room, eat, and sleep adequately.
    - Patient is clinically stable for at least 24 hours.

7.  **Follow-up:**
    - Smoking cessation counseling.
    - Assess inhaler technique.
    - Measure FEV1.
    - Ensure compliance with treatment.
    - Reassess need for long-term oxygen therapy/home nebulizer.
    - Manage co-morbidities.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- **Theophylline:** Use with caution in patients with cardiac disease, arrhythmias, seizures, or hepatic/renal impairment. Requires monitoring of blood levels.
- **Supplemental Oxygen:** Not routinely indicated unless hypoxemia is present. High flow rates in some patients can suppress respiratory drive (rare).
- **Lung Volume Reduction Surgery (LVRS):** Contraindicated in patients with severe pulmonary hypertension, significant comorbidities, or poor nutritional status.
- **Lung Transplantation:** Contraindicated in patients with severe co-morbidities (liver, renal, cardiac disease), active malignancy, active infection, or severe psychosocial issues.
- **Corticosteroids (Systemic - for exacerbations):** Use with caution in patients with diabetes, osteoporosis, peptic ulcer disease, or infections.
- **Antibiotics:** Use with caution in patients with known allergies.
