DISEASE: Trachoma

ABBREVIATIONS:
- SPK: Superior Punctate Keratitis
- SEIs: Superior Epithelial Infiltrates
- WHO: World Health Organization
- TF: Trachomatous inflammation: follicular
- TI: Trachomatous inflammation: intense
- TS: Trachomatous scarring
- TT: Trachomatous trichiasis
- CO: Corneal opacity
- SAFE: Surgery, Antibiotics, Facial hygiene, Environmental improvement

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Presence of at least two of the following signs: superior tarsal follicles, limbal follicles (Herbert's pit), typical conjunctival scarring and vascular pannus.
- WHO Classification: TF, TI, TS, TT, CO.
- MacCallan Classification: Stage 1, Stage 2, Stage 3, Stage 4.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF Trachoma is diagnosed THEN
    BEGIN SAFE STRATEGY
        S: Surgery for trichiasis (TT)
        A: Antibiotics for active disease (TF, TI)
        F: Facial hygiene
        E: Environmental improvement
    END SAFE STRATEGY

    IF Antibiotics are indicated (TF or TI) THEN
        BEGIN ANTIBIOTIC TREATMENT
            IF Patient is eligible for Azithromycin THEN
                ADMINISTER Azithromycin single dose (20 mg/kg up to 1g).
            ELSE IF Patient is eligible for Topical Tetracycline Ointment THEN
                ADMINISTER Topical 1% tetracycline ointment for 6 weeks.
            ENDIF
        END ANTIBIOTIC TREATMENT
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- No specific contraindications listed for Azithromycin or Topical Tetracycline Ointment in the provided text, but standard contraindications apply (e.g., allergy).
