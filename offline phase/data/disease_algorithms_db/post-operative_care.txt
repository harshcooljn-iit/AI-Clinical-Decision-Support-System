DISEASE: Post-Operative Care

ABBREVIATIONS:
- NSAIDs: Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs
- IM: Intramuscular
- IV: Intravenous
- TDS: Three times a day
- SOS: As and when required
- OD: Once a day
- SSI: Surgical Site Infection

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Postoperative Pain: Assessed based on patient report, vital signs, and clinical observation.
- Postoperative Nausea and Vomiting: Assessed based on patient report (nausea, vomiting).
- Postoperative Pneumonia: Assessed based on clinical features (fever, productive cough, dyspnoea, chest pain), physical examination (bronchial breathing, rales), and chest X-ray (area of consolidation).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

I. POSTOPERATIVE PAIN RELIEF

IF patient requires immediate postoperative analgesia (IM or IV route) THEN
    - Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 75 mg 6-8 hourly.
    OR
    - Administer Inj. tramadol (50 mg/ml) IM/IV 4-6 hourly.
    OR
    - Administer Inj. pentazocine (30mg/ml) 30-60mg IM/IV repeated 3-4 hourly.
    OR
    - Administer Inj. morphine (15 mg/ml) 10-15mg, can be repeated 4-6 times.
ENDIF

IF patient is in a tertiary care center THEN
    - Consider epidural analgesia, intravenous patient controlled analgesia (PCA), or intrapleural analgesia under expert care.
ENDIF

IF patient is able to accept oral medication THEN
    - Administer Tab. paracetamol 500mg 3-4 times a day.
    OR
    - Administer Tab. ibuprofen 400-600mg 8 hourly.
ENDIF

II. POSTOPERATIVE NAUSEA AND VOMITING

IF nausea and vomiting occur THEN
    - Rule out bowel obstruction (mechanical or paralytic ileus) via examination and investigations.
    - Manage with bed rest, intravenous fluids, analgesics for pain relief, and nasogastric decompression if needed.
    - Administer Inj. metoclopramide (5mg/ml) 10 mg IM/IV TDS or SOS.
    OR
    - Administer Inj. ondansetron (2mg/ml) 4 mg slow IV or IM OD.
ENDIF

III. POSTOPERATIVE PNEUMONIA

IF patient develops symptoms suggestive of postoperative pneumonia (fever, productive cough, dyspnoea, chest pain) AND/OR signs (bronchial breathing, rales) AND/OR chest X-ray shows consolidation THEN
    - Administer antibiotics based on sputum culture and sensitivity. Initial treatment may include an aminoglycoside and cephalosporin.
    - Administer Inj. diclofenac 75mg IM every 6-8 hours.
    - Perform chest physiotherapy.
    - Use nebulized bronchodilators if bronchospasm is present.
ENDIF

IV. ANTIBIOTIC PROPHYLAXIS IN SURGERY

IF procedure is clean-contaminated, contaminated, or dirty THEN
    - Administer antibiotic prophylaxis.
ENDIF

IF procedure is clean AND specific risk criteria are met OR certain patients require it THEN
    - Consider antibiotic prophylaxis (optional).
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for the listed drugs, but general contraindications for each drug class would apply, e.g., allergy, specific medical conditions).
