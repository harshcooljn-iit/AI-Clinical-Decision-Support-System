DISEASE: Mumps

ABBREVIATIONS:
- OPD: Outpatient Department
- SOS: Stat Omnis Sero (Immediately as needed)
- CNS: Central Nervous System
- MMR: Mumps-Measles-Rubella

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Swollen and painful parotid glands over 1-3 days.
- Fever (up to 103°F / 39.4°C).
- Headache.
- Loss of appetite.
- Potential involvement of brain, pancreas, or other organs.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Mumps (parotid swelling, fever, headache, loss of appetite).
    THEN Proceed to treatment steps.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Encourage fluid intake (water, decaffeinated soft drinks, tea).
    - Avoid acidic fruit juices (orange, grapefruit, lemonade).
    - Apply warm or cold packs to swollen parotid glands as preferred by the child.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment (Symptomatic):**
    IF Patient has fever or pain.
    THEN Administer paracetamol.
    ELSE No pharmacological treatment needed for fever/pain.

4.  **Paracetamol Dosage:**
    IF Patient has fever or pain.
    THEN Administer paracetamol at 10-15 mg/kg/day.
    Frequency: SOS (Stat Omnis Sero - immediately as needed) or every 4-6 hours.
    NOTE: Aspirin is contraindicated in children with viral illnesses due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.

5.  **Antibiotics:**
    IF Patient has Mumps.
    THEN Antibiotics are NOT indicated as Mumps is a viral illness.

6.  **Hospital Admission Criteria:**
    IF Patient presents with ANY of the following:
        - Abdominal pain.
        - Testicular swelling (in boys).
        - Signs of raised intracranial tension.
    THEN Admit the patient to the hospital.
    ELSE Treat as outpatient (OPD basis).

7.  **Patient Education:**
    - Explain warning signs to parents:
        - High fever with testicular pain/swelling (boys).
        - Abdominal pain (pancreatitis or oophoritis).
        - Severe headache, stiff neck, convulsions, extreme drowsiness (CNS involvement).
        - Recurrence of high fever (>101°F / 38.3°C) suggests complications.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Aspirin: Contraindicated in children with viral illnesses (like Mumps) due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
