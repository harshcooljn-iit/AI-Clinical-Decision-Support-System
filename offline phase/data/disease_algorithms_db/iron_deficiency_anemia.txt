```text
DISEASE: Iron Deficiency Anemia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- RBC: Red Blood Cell
- TIBC: Total Iron Binding Capacity
- Hb: Hemoglobin
- CCF: Congestive Cardiac Failure
- ADR: Adverse Drug Reaction
- IM: Intramuscular
- PCV: Packed Red Blood Cells
- wt: weight
- g/dl: grams per deciliter
- ml/kg: milliliters per kilogram
- hr: hour

DIAGNOSTICS:
- RBC indices (reticulocyte count, serum iron concentration, TIBC).
- Clinical features (easy tiredness, lack of concentration, decreased school performance, growth retardation, severe cases may present with CCF).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - Determine patient's age and hemoglobin (Hb) levels.
    - Assess dietary intake.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF age is > 6 months:
        - THEN recommend adding cereal-based diet.
        - THEN encourage green leafy vegetables and fruits.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Oral Iron Therapy:**
    - IF oral iron therapy is indicated (preferred route):
        - THEN determine therapeutic iron dose: 4 mg/kg/day of elemental iron.
        - THEN divide the dose into 2-3 administrations per day.
        - THEN administer between meals.
        - THEN continue treatment for 2 months after normal Hb concentration for age is achieved.
        - IF prematurity:
            - THEN start iron syrup from the 2nd week of life to 1 year.
        - IF response to therapy is inadequate:
            - THEN check for prescribed dose, compliance, presence of diarrhea or malnutrition, infections, or misdiagnosis (e.g., thalassemia trait).

4.  **Parenteral Iron Therapy:**
    - IF oral iron is not tolerated OR severe ADRs to oral iron OR presence of malabsorption syndromes:
        - THEN consider parenteral iron therapy.
        - THEN calculate total dose of iron: Iron(mg) = wt(kg) * Hb deficit (g/dl) * 4.
        - THEN administer injection iron dextran or iron sorbitol citrate (50 mg/ml) via deep gluteal IM injection.

5.  **Blood Transfusion:**
    - IF Hb is less than 3 g/dl:
        - THEN administer PCV 15 ml/kg at a rate of 4-5 ml/kg/hour.
    - ELSE IF Hb is 4-6 g/dl AND patient has cardiac compromise:
        - THEN administer PCV 15 ml/kg at a rate of 4-5 ml/kg/hour.

6.  **Patient/Parent Education:**
    - Educate on dietary modification for long-term management.
    - Advise taking iron between meals, not with tea or milk.
    - Inform about potential harmless black discoloration of teeth and stools.
    - Advise rinsing mouth with water after doses to prevent teeth discoloration.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Oral Iron Therapy:
    - IF patient cannot tolerate oral iron.
    - IF patient experiences severe ADRs to oral iron.
    - IF patient has malabsorption syndromes.
- Parenteral Iron Therapy:
    - IF oral iron therapy is tolerated and effective.
    - IF no malabsorption syndromes.
- Blood Transfusion:
    - IF Hb is > 6 g/dl (unless cardiac compromise) and patient is stable.
```
