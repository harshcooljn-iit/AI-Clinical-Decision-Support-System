DISEASE: Rheumatoid Arthritis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- RA: Rheumatoid Arthritis
- NSAIDs: Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs
- DMARD: Disease Modifying Antirheumatic Drug
- LFT: Liver Function Test
- MCP: Metacarpophalangeal
- MTP: Metatarsophalangeal

DIAGNOSTICS:
- > 3 swollen joints
- > 30 minutes of morning stiffness
- Involvement of MCP or MTP joints
- Symmetric arthritis
- Rheumatoid nodule
- Based on the revised criterion of American College of Rheumatology (1987)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Initial Assessment and Non-Pharmacological Treatment
IF patient has RA symptoms:
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    THEN advise rest and splinting for acute pain.
    THEN advise exercises to maintain muscle strength and joint mobility (avoid exacerbating inflammation).
    THEN consider orthotic devices (splints) and assistive devices (cane, walker) for support and alignment.

// Pharmacological Treatment - Initial Steps
IF patient has acute inflammation:
    THEN consider NSAIDs (except paracetamol) as used for osteoarthritis.
    THEN note that NSAID effects may take 2-4 weeks.

// NSAID Considerations
IF patient is elderly OR has impaired renal function:
    THEN use reduced NSAID dosages.
IF patient is using more than one NSAID:
    THEN advise against it due to increased toxicity risk without added benefit.
IF patient does not respond to one NSAID:
    THEN consider trying another NSAID.

// Topical Treatments
THEN consider topical applications (salicylates, capsaicin, nicotinates, menthol, camphor, NSAIDs) for symptomatic relief.

// Referral and DMARD Initiation
THEN refer to a specialist (physician) at a higher center.
THEN begin DMARD early in the disease.
THEN note that prediction of response to a specific DMARD is not possible, and effects take 4-6 weeks.

// First-line DMARD - Methotrexate
IF initiating DMARD therapy:
    THEN consider Tab. methotrexate 7.5 mg to 15 mg every week.
    THEN consider concomitant Tab. folic acid 1 mg/day to reduce side effects.
    // Methotrexate Cautions
    IF patient experiences nausea, mouth sores, liver damage, increased chest infections, or macrocytic anaemia:
        THEN monitor LFT regularly.
        THEN advise avoiding alcohol during therapy.

// Treatment for Severe/Refractory Disease
IF patient has severe disease affecting activities of daily living AND not responding to adequate trial of NSAIDs and DMARD for sufficient duration:
    THEN consider Tab. prednisolone 40-60 mg/day for 2-4 weeks.
    THEN review periodically and possibly taper down slowly.
    THEN if required for longer duration, administer at doses of 5-10 mg/day.

// Intra-articular Corticosteroids
IF patient has predominantly monoarticular arthritis of a large joint:
    THEN consider intra-articular corticosteroids (e.g., methylprednisolone acetate, 20-80 mg).

// Referral for Non-Response/Severe Cases
IF patient has no response to medical therapy after 4-8 weeks OR has severe extra-articular symptoms, deformities, contractures OR is crippled/unable to carry out activities of daily living despite adequate medical treatment:
    THEN refer the patient to a higher center.

// Surgical Treatment
IF patient has predominantly monoarticular involvement not responding to conservative therapy:
    THEN consider synovectomy.
IF patient has disorganized joints:
    THEN consider reconstructive surgery.

// Contraindications
IF patient is receiving methotrexate:
    THEN avoid alcohol consumption.
    THEN monitor LFT regularly.
