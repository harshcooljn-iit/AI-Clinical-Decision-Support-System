DISEASE: Fever

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- FUO: Fever of Unknown Origin
- mg: Milligram
- tab: tablet
- hr: hourly
- X-ray: Radiograph

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Defined as body temperature > 37.7°C.
- History of symptoms, associated focal symptoms, exposure, occupational history.
- Physical examination.
- If no clinical clues: Complete haemogram with ESR, malaria smear, blood culture, Widal test, urine analysis including urine culture.
- If febrile illness > 2 weeks: X-ray chest.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Fever is present:
    IF Routine use of antipyretics is considered for low-grade fever:
        THEN Do not use antipyretics as it may mask clinical indications.
    ELSE IF Acute febrile illness suggestive of viral or bacterial cause:
        THEN Treat fever with antipyretics.

    IF Non-pharmacological treatment is indicated:
        THEN Use hydrotherapy and rest.

    IF Pharmacological treatment is indicated:
        THEN Use non-specific antipyretics.

        IF Paracetamol is chosen:
            THEN Administer Tab. paracetamol 500-1000 mg 6-8 hourly.
        ELSE IF Ibuprofen is chosen:
            THEN Administer Tab. ibuprofen 400-600 mg 8 hourly.

    IF Specific treatment is needed:
        THEN Administer antibiotics/antimalarials based on clinical and laboratory evaluation.

    IF Diagnosis is not reached within 3 weeks:
        THEN Patient has Fever of Unknown Origin (FUO).
        THEN Manage patient accordingly for FUO.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Routine use of antipyretics in low-grade fever is not justified.
