DISEASE: Atopic Conjunctivitis (Vernal Keratoconjunctivitis - VKC)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- VKC: Vernal Keratoconjunctivitis
- QID: Four times a day
- OD: Once daily
- BD: Twice daily
- TDS: Three times daily
- IOP: Intraocular Pressure
- QID: Four times a day

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Recurrent bilateral disorder.
- IgE- and cell-mediated immune mechanisms.
- Primarily affects boys, onset from age 5 (mean age 7).
- Symptoms: Marked itching, thick ropy discharge, seasonal occurrence (spring/summer).
- History of atopy may be present.
- Large conjunctival papillae (upper eyelid or limbus - limbal VKC), cobblestone appearance.
- Superior corneal "shield" ulcer (well-delineated, sterile, gray-white infiltrate) may be present.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Atopic Conjunctivitis:
    THEN treat as for allergic conjunctivitis.

    IF patient requires prophylactic treatment before the season starts:
        THEN use:
            - Mast cell stabilizer (e.g., sodium chromoglycate QID)
            OR
            - Dual action mast cell stabilizer and antihistamine (e.g., olopatadine 0.1% OD/BD)
            OR
            - Ketotifen 0.025%
            OR
            - Azelastine 0.05%
        FOR 2 to 3 weeks.

    IF patient has a shield ulcer:
        THEN add topical steroid (e.g., loteprednol 0.5%, flurometholone 0.1%, prednisolone 1%).
        THEN administer steroid four to six times per day.
        THEN taper steroids over 4 weeks as condition improves.
        THEN consider scraping the shield ulcer to remove superficial plaque-like material before re-epithelialization.

    IF associated infection is present OR steroids are prescribed:
        THEN give topical antibiotic (e.g., moxifloxacin 0.3% eye drop, tobramycin 1% QID).

    IF cornea is involved AND there is much pain due to ciliary spasm:
        THEN give cycloplegic agent (e.g., cyclopentolate 1% BD drops or atropine 1% TDS in form of drops or ointment).

    IF patient is not responding to preceding treatment:
        THEN consider cyclosporine 0.05% BD.
        NOTE: Maximal effect may not be seen for several weeks.

    FOLLOW-UP:
        IF shield ulcer is present:
            THEN follow-up every 1 to 3 days.
        ELSE:
            THEN follow-up every few weeks.

    STERIOD TAPERING:
        Taper steroids slowly as improvement is noted.

    ANTI-ALLERGY DROPS MAINTENANCE:
        Maintain anti-allergy drops for the duration of the season.
        Often reinitiate a few weeks before the next spring.

    MONITORING:
        IF patient is on topical steroids:
            THEN monitor regularly, including IOP monitoring, even if used only on the skin.

    PATIENT EDUCATION:
        - Avoid exposure to substances causing atopy.
        - Maintain hygiene to prevent secondary infection.
        - Instruct patients and relatives of children strictly not to use or continue steroids without medical advice.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Steroids: Use with caution and monitor IOP. Avoid prolonged use without medical supervision, especially in children.
