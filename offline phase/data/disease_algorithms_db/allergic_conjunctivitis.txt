DISEASE: Allergic Conjunctivitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- OD: Ocular Drops (or Ounce of Day, but context suggests Ocular Drops)
- BD: Bis in Die (Twice a day)
- TDS: Ter in Die (Three times a day)
- QID: Quarter in Die (Four times a day)
- mg: Milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Patient presents with marked itching and watery discharge.
- History of allergy is usually present.
- Examination shows conjunctival papillae and edema over eyelids.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Non-pharmacological treatment is sufficient THEN
    - Apply cool compresses several times per day.
    - Avoid offending agents.
ELSE
    IF Pharmacological treatment is needed THEN
        IF Severity is mild to moderate THEN
            - Administer topical anti-inflammatory and anti-histaminic drops.
            - Options include:
                - Olopatadine 0.1% OD/BD
                - Nedocromil 2% TDS
                - Ketotifen 0.025% BD
                - Ketorolac 0.5% QID
        ELSE IF Severity is severe THEN
            - Administer topical anti-inflammatory and anti-histaminic drops (as above).
            - CONSIDER adding mild topical steroid for 1 to 2 weeks.
            - Options for topical steroid include:
                - Loteprednol 0.2% QID
                - Fluorometholone 0.1% QID
            - Taper steroids gradually as patient responds.
            - Administer preservative-free artificial tears four to eight times per day.
            - CONSIDER oral antihistamine.
            - Options for oral antihistamine include:
                - Diphenhydramine 25 mg TDS/QID
                - Cetirizine 10 mg OD
                - Levocetirizine 10 mg OD
                - Montelukast 10 mg OD
                - Loratadine 10 mg OD
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for the listed drugs in this context, but standard contraindications for steroids and antihistamines apply based on patient history and other medications).
