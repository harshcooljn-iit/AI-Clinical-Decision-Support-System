DISEASE: Bacterial Parotitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Cap.: Capsule
- Tab.: Tablet
- TDS: Three times a day
- CT: Computed Tomography
- USG: Ultrasonography
- mg: Milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical features: Pain, swelling, tenderness, induration of the gland, erythema of skin and oral mucosa, fever.
- Investigations: Leucocytosis, culture and sensitivity of purulent discharge from gland milking, palpation and X-ray for stone. CT scan or USG to confirm abscess if non-pharmacological treatment fails.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF patient has Bacterial Parotitis THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    IF patient requires non-pharmacological treatment THEN
        Adequate hydration
        Good oral hygiene
        Warm compression and repeated massage of the gland
        IF stone is present in the distal duct THEN
            Express stone manually OR
            Remove stone after enlarging the duct orifice with a small incision
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment
    IF patient requires pharmacological treatment THEN
        IF patient is an adult THEN
            Administer Cap. amoxicillin 500mg thrice a day OR
            Administer Tab. amoxicillin with clavulanic acid 625 mg three times a day (TDS)
            FOR 7 days
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    // Abscess Management
    IF non-pharmacological treatment fails THEN
        Consider abscess formation
        Confirm abscess with CT scan or USG
        // (Further management for abscess not specified in the text, e.g., drainage, antibiotics)
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
// No specific contraindications mentioned for amoxicillin or amoxicillin/clavulanic acid in this context. Standard contraindications apply (e.g., penicillin allergy).
