```text
DISEASE: Sprains

ABBREVIATIONS:
- X-rays: Radiographs
- oedema: Edema
- ecchymosis: Bruising
- tab: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical evaluation based on symptoms (pain, swelling, discoloration/bruising, impaired joint function).
- Physical examination to assess severity (joint instability, range of motion).
- X-rays to rule out fracture.
- Stress X-rays may be used for grade III sprains to assess joint instability.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - IF Sprain suspected:
        - THEN Check sensation and circulation distal to the injury.
        - THEN Obtain X-rays of the involved region to rule out a fracture.

2.  **Severity Grading:**
    - IF Grade I sprain (partial tearing, minimal swelling, no instability):
        - THEN Apply a compression bandage for 5-7 days.
        - THEN Allow weight-bearing after a week.
        - THEN Pharmacological treatment (see step 4).
        - THEN Patient education (see step 5).
    - ELSE IF Grade II sprain (pain, edema, ecchymosis, some loss of motion, no instability):
        - THEN Consider splintage (slab later converted to cast) to restrict joint motion.
        - THEN Patient must remain non-weight bearing for 4-6 weeks.
        - THEN Pharmacological treatment (see step 4).
        - THEN Patient education (see step 5).
    - ELSE IF Grade III sprain (gross instability, complete tearing, marked swelling, severe pain):
        - THEN Consider splintage (slab later converted to cast) to restrict joint motion.
        - THEN Patient must remain non-weight bearing for 4-6 weeks.
        - THEN Pharmacological treatment (see step 4).
        - THEN Patient education (see step 5).
    - ELSE:
        - THEN Diagnosis unclear, further investigation needed.

3.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment (General):**
    - THEN Protection, support, and rest.
    - THEN Restrict movement of the affected area.
    - THEN Apply cold compresses immediately (avoid direct skin contact).
    - THEN Elevate the limb above the heart level, especially at night.

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - THEN Administer Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg three times a day for 5-7 days.
    - OR
    - THEN Administer Tab. diclofenac sodium 50 mg three times a day for 5-7 days.

5.  **Patient Education:**
    - THEN Advise against massage or hot fomentation in the acute stage (increases swelling).
    - THEN Advise rest until pain subsides (7-10 days for mild, 3-5 weeks for severe).
    - THEN Advise avoiding activities causing pain or swelling.
    - THEN Advise moderation in physical activities.
    - THEN IF Pain and swelling decrease within 48 hours:
        - THEN Advise gentle movement of the affected joint in all directions.
    - THEN IF Ankle sprain:
        - THEN Advise avoiding high-heeled shoes.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for Ibuprofen or Diclofenac related to sprains, standard contraindications apply).
```
