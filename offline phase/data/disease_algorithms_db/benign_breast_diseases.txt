DISEASE: Benign Breast Diseases

ABBREVIATIONS:
- FNAC: Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology
- TDS: Three times a day
- OD: Once a day
- Tab.: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination (history, physical exam)
- Imaging: Ultrasound of breast and axilla, Mammography (for patients > 35 years)
- Cytology: FNAC (may show fibroadenoma or fibroadenosis changes)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has cyclical mastalgia:
    THEN consider Evening primrose oil 1000 mg TDS for 3 months.
ELSE IF patient has noncyclical mastalgia:
    THEN Reassurance.
    THEN Adequate support.
    THEN Consider exclusion of caffeine.
    THEN Consider Evening primrose oil 1000 mg TDS for 3 months.
    IF symptoms persist after Evening primrose oil:
        THEN Consider Tab. danazol 100 mg OD.
        IF symptoms persist after 100 mg OD:
            THEN Consider increasing dose up to 100 mg TDS after discussing side effects (hirsutism, contraception failure) with the patient.

IF patient has a benign breast lump (fibroadenoma):
    THEN No treatment required unless patient desires.
    IF patient desires treatment OR cytology is suspicious OR lump size is large (>5 cm):
        THEN Consider excision.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Danazol: Patients who desire contraception (risk of contraception failure), patients not counseled on side effects (hirsutism).
