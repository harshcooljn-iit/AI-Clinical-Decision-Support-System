DISEASE: Acute Gastroenteritis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ORS: Oral Rehydration Salt
- IV: Intravenous
- Tab: Tablet
- mg: Milligram
- ml: Milliliter
- kg: Kilogram
- °C: Celsius
- AIDS: Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome
- C. difficile: Clostridioides difficile
- HIV: Human Immunodeficiency Virus

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Diarrhea, abdominal cramps, nausea, vomiting, dehydration, hypotension.
- Stool tests (if indicated): Fecal leukocytes, routine stool culture, C. difficile assay, ova and parasite testing.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient presents with Acute Diarrhea lasting less than 7 days:
    THEN Assess for severe illness criteria:
        IF Severe illness present (Temperature > 38.5 °C, abdominal pain, bloody diarrhea, >6 stools/24 hrs, dehydration) OR Patient is immunocompromised (e.g., AIDS, post-transplantation) OR Patient is elderly:
            THEN Send stool for:
                - Fecal leukocytes
                - Routine stool culture
                - C. difficile assay (if recent hospitalization or antibiotic use)
                - Ova and parasite testing (if diarrhea > 10 days, travel to endemic region, community waterborne outbreak, or HIV infection in men who have sex with men)
                Consider empiric antibiotic therapy while awaiting culture results IF Fecal leukocytes are positive OR Bloody diarrhea, fever, and abdominal pain are present.
        ELSE (No severe illness, not immunocompromised, not elderly):
            THEN Symptomatic therapy:
                - Antidiarrheal agents (e.g., Loperamide, Bismuth Subsalicylate)
            IF Illness resolves:
                THEN No further action.
            ELSE IF Illness persists for 7-10 days or worsens:
                THEN Send stool for tests as described in the "Yes" branch (Fecal leukocytes, culture, C. difficile, Ova and parasite).

    IF Patient requires rehydration:
        IF Mild to moderate dehydration:
            THEN Give Oral Rehydration Salt (ORS): 75 ml/kg in 4 hours.
        IF Severe dehydration:
            THEN Give IV fluid: 100 ml/kg.
            - Give 30 ml/kg in 30 minutes.
            - Followed by 70 ml/kg in 2½ hours.

    IF Patient has very ill symptoms with systemic symptoms associated with bloody diarrhea:
        THEN Consider Tab ciprofloxacin 500 mg two times a day for 3 to 5 days.

    IF Patient has amoebic dysentery:
        THEN Give Tab metronidazole 400 mg three times a day for 5 to 7 days OR Give Oral Tab tinidazole 600 mg twice a day for 3 to 5 days.

    IF Patient has acute giardiasis:
        THEN Give Tab tinidazole 1000 mg single dose OR Give Tab metronidazole 400 mg three times a day for 3 days.

    IF Patient is hospitalized (due to dehydration, suspected cholera, immunocompromised, or severe systemic symptoms):
        THEN Hospitalize.

    IF Patient is not vomiting:
        THEN Advise sips of fluid (juices, soups, ORS).

    AVOID Milk and related products for at least 2 weeks due to potential secondary lactase deficiency.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Loperamide, Bismuth Subsalicylate: Not specified in the text, but generally contraindicated in severe infections (e.g., invasive bacterial diarrhea, bloody diarrhea) or fever due to risk of worsening infection or toxic megacolon.
- Milk and related products: Avoided for at least 2 weeks in acute gastroenteritis due to potential secondary lactase deficiency.
