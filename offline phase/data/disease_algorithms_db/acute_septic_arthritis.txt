DISEASE: Acute Septic Arthritis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- POP: Plaster of Paris
- GM-: Gram-negative
- GM+: Gram-positive
- IV: Intravenous
- PO: Per os (by mouth)
- MRSA: Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus
- VRE: Vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus
- MSSA: Methicillin-sensitive Staphylococcus aureus
- GBS: Group B Streptococcus
- C&S: Culture and Sensitivity
- IM: Intramuscular

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical signs: Inability to move joint, refusal to walk/limp, hip pain referred to knee, local inflammation (warm, painful joint with effusion), signs of sepsis (fever, malaise). Neonates may present with pseudo paralysis.
- Ultrasound examination of suspected septic hip joint aids diagnosis.
- Joint aspiration for gram staining, culture, and sensitivity (C&S).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  REFERRAL:
    IF patient is suspected of having Acute Septic Arthritis THEN
    REFER the patient immediately to an orthopaedic surgeon.

2.  NON-PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT:
    IF patient has Acute Septic Arthritis THEN
    - Keep the joint in a position of comfort.
    - Perform joint aspiration for gram staining, culture, and sensitivity (C&S).
    - IF aspirate is purulent THEN
    - Perform emergency drainage of the joint.
    - ELSE IF joint aspiration is doubtful BUT strong clinical suspicion exists THEN
    - Perform drainage of the joint.
    - ENDIF
    - After drainage, splint the joint with a POP slab or skin traction until the patient is afebrile, pain-free, and the joint is clinically quiescent.
    - Permit intermittent mobilization to preserve range of movement.
