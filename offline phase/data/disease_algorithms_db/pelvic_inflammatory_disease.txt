DISEASE: Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PID: Pelvic Inflammatory Disease
- IM: Intramuscular
- IV: Intravenous
- LFT: Liver Function Test
- RFT: Renal Function Test
- STD: Sexually Transmitted Disease
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- SOS: Stat Oral Suspension (as needed)
- INH: Isoniazid
- mg/kg: Milligrams per kilogram
- tab: tablet
- cap: capsule
- Inj: Injection

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Lower abdominal pain
- Cervical motion tenderness
- Adenexal tenderness
- Fever
- Cervical discharge
- Leucocytosis
- Investigations: Haemogram with ESR, LFT, RFT, serum electrolytes, blood culture, endocervical swab culture, ultrasonography if adenexal mass.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# ACUTE PID TREATMENT

IF Severity is mild AND patient can tolerate oral medication AND no contraindications to outpatient treatment:
    THEN Treat as outpatient.
    IF Regimen A is chosen:
        THEN Administer Inj. cefoxitin 2 g IM plus probenecid 1 g orally, as a single dose OR Inj. ceftriaxone 250 mg IM as a single dose OR Inj. ceftizoxime or cefotaxime 500 mg IM as a single dose.
        AND Administer Cap. doxycycline 100 mg orally 2 times a day for 14 days.
    ELSE IF Regimen B is chosen:
        THEN Administer Tab. ofloxacin 400 mg orally 2 times a day for 4 days.
        AND Administer Tab. Clindamycin 450 mg orally 4 times a day for 14 days OR Tab. metronidazole 400 mg orally 2 times a day for 14 days.
    ENDIF
    Follow up after 2-3 days.
    IF Poor response to outpatient therapy:
        THEN Admit patient for intravenous antibiotics.
ELSE
    THEN Treat as inpatient (severe illness, nausea/vomiting, HIV positive, unable to tolerate outpatient regimen, or outpatient therapy failed).
    Administer Bed Rest and Hydrotherapy if febrile.
    Administer IV fluids if vomiting and dehydration, and correct electrolyte imbalance.
    Monitor clinical condition, vital signs, signs/symptoms of pelvic abscess, and peritonitis.
    IF Regimen A (IV) is chosen:
        THEN Administer Inj. cefoxitin 2 g IV every 6 hours OR Inj. cefotetan 2 g IV every 12 hours.
        AND Administer Inj. doxycycline 100 mg IV or orally every 12 hours.
        AND Administer Inj. metronidazole 500 mg IV every 8 hours.
    ELSE IF Regimen B (IV) is chosen:
        THEN Administer Inj. clindamycin 900 mg IV every 8 hours.
        AND Administer Inj. gentamicin 2 mg/kg IV followed by 1.5 mg/kg every 8 hours.
        AND Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 75 mg deep IM every 8 hours OR Inj. paracetamol 500 mg IM SOS.
        AND Administer Inj. metronidazole 500 mg IV every 8 hours.
    ENDIF
    Continue injectable regimen for at least 48 hours after clinical improvement (afebrile, decreased tenderness, improved constitutional symptoms).
    After improvement, continue oral therapy for a total of 14 days:
        IF Using Regimen A (IV):
            THEN Administer doxycycline 100 mg orally 2 times a day.
        ELSE IF Using Regimen B (IV):
            THEN Administer clindamycin 450 mg orally 4 times a day.
        ENDIF
    IF Clinical improvement does not occur within 3 days OR symptoms worsen:
        THEN Consider further diagnostic tests / laparoscopy.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# SURGICAL CONSIDERATIONS (Inpatient/Severe Cases)
IF Colpotomy is needed:
    THEN Perform colpotomy for drainage of midline pelvic abscess.
IF Dilatation and evacuation is needed:
    THEN Perform dilatation and evacuation of septic products of conception in post abortal sepsis.
IF Laparotomy is needed:
    THEN Perform laparotomy in cases of pyoperitoneum, resistant peritonitis, intestinal obstruction, ruptured tubo-ovarian abscess, or enlarging pelvic mass despite medical therapy.
IF Laparoscopy is needed:
    THEN Perform laparoscopy if diagnosis is uncertain, no response to treatment, to reconfirm diagnosis, obtain cultures from cul de sac and fallopian tubes, and drain pus if necessary.

# MALE PARTNER TREATMENT
IF Asymptomatic male partner:
    THEN Administer Inj. ceftriaxone 125 mg IM followed by oral doxycycline 200 mg orally 2 times a day for 14 days.

# CHRONIC PID TREATMENT
IF Chronic PID is due to pathological lesion, considering patient's age and desire for childbearing:
    THEN Consider surgical treatment.
    IF Definitive surgery is chosen (e.g., total abdominal hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy):
        THEN Perform total abdominal hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy.
    ELSE
        THEN Perform conservative surgery (preferred in young females).
    ENDIF
ELSE IF Chronic PID is due to pelvic tuberculosis:
    THEN Administer medical therapy with anti-tubercular drugs for 6 months.
    Administer Tab. isoniazid 5 mg/kg (maximum 300 mg).
    Administer Cap. rifampicin 10 mg/kg (maximum 600 mg).
    Administer Tab. pyrazinamide 15-30 mg/kg (maximum 2 g).
    Administer Cap. ethambutol 15-25 mg/kg (maximum 2.5 g).
    All 4 drugs for initial phase (2 months), then INH and rifampicin for 4 months.
    IF Surgery is indicated (primary unresponsiveness, persistent/enlarging adenexal mass after 4-6 months, persistent/recurrent pelvic pain):
        THEN Perform total abdominal hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# PATIENT EDUCATION
- Emphasize behavioural and contraceptive methods to prevent STDs.
- Encourage completion of the full 14-day antibiotic course.
- Advise sexual abstinence until complete treatment.
- Advise treatment of sexual partner to prevent reinfection.
