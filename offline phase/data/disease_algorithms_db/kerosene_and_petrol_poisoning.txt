DISEASE: Kerosene And Petrol Poisoning

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ml: milliliter
- IV: Intravenous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- History of exposure to kerosene or petrol.
- Clinical signs and symptoms (mild or severe, as listed in Salient features).
- Chest x-ray findings (perihilar densities, basal pneumonitis, atelectasis, consolidation).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has kerosene/petrol poisoning THEN
    - Remove contaminated clothes.
    - Flush skin and oral cavity for at least 15 minutes.
    - IF chemical was swallowed THEN
        - Immediately give water or milk.
    - IF poison was inhaled THEN
        - Immediately move patient to fresh air.
        - Maintain patent airway.
        - Position patient with head low.
    - Do not induce emesis.
    - Monitor vital signs (temperature, pulse, blood pressure, respiratory rate).
    - Provide oxygen via nasal cannula or face mask.
    - Establish IV line.
    - Start intravenous crystalloids for supportive therapy.
    - Monitor for respiratory complications.
ENDIF
