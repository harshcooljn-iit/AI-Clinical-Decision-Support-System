DIAGNOSTICS:
- Painful uterine contractions after 22 weeks gestation.
- Pain associated with blood-stained mucus vaginal discharge (show).
- Watery vaginal discharge or a sudden gush of water.
- Cervical dilatation and effacement.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF patient reports to hospital with labour pains THEN
    - Conduct examination by a doctor.
    - Assign risk category (based on Table 4).

2.  **General Examination:**
    - Record height and weight.
    - Record Pulse rate (PR), blood pressure (BP), respiratory rate (RR), temperature.
    - Look for pallor, pedal oedema, jaundice, cyanosis.
    - Auscultate cardiovascular and respiratory system.

3.  **Per Abdomen Examination:**
    - Assess height of the uterus/POG.
    - Determine presentation, attitude, palpable foetal head (rule of fifths).
    - Assess foetal heart rate.
    - Assess uterine contractions (frequency, intensity, duration).
    - Assess amount of liquor.
    - Estimate baby size/weight.
    - Look for overriding of foetal head.
    - Assess for features of obstructed labour and uterine contour.

4.  **Per Vaginum Examination:**
    IF performing per vaginum examination THEN
    - Use aseptic precautions (hand washing, sterile gloves).
    - Assess cervical dilatation and effacement.
    - Assess presentation and station of presenting part.
    - Assess position and degree of flexion.
    - Assess status of membranes and liquor colour (I, R, C, M, B).
    - Rule out cord prolapse or presentation.
    - Rule out cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD).
    - Frequency: At least once every 4 hours during the first stage, and after rupture of membranes.

5.  **Investigations:**
    - Minimum required: Hb, urine albumin and sugar, blood grouping.
    - In prolonged/obstructed labour: Urine acetone.

6.  **Assessment of Progress of Labour:**
    - Measure changes in cervical effacement and dilatation during the latent phase.
    - Measure the rate of cervical dilatation and fetal descent during the active phase.
    - Assess fetal descent during the second stage.
    - Plot progress on a partograph once the patient enters the active phase (cervix >= 4 cm).

7.  **Partograph Monitoring:**
    - Record patient information (Name, Gravida, Para, Hospital number, Date/Time of admission, Time of ruptured membranes).
    - Record Fetal heart rate every half hour.
    - Record Amniotic fluid colour at every vaginal examination (I, R, C, M, B).
    - Record Moulding (1, 2, 3).
    - Record Cervical dilatation (cross **X**) at every vaginal examination, starting at 4 cm.
    - Plot Alert line (starts at 4 cm, rate of 1 cm/hour).
    - Plot Action line (parallel to Alert line, 4 hours to the right).
    - Record Descent (O) at every abdominal examination.
    - Record Hours elapsed since onset of active phase.
    - Record actual Time.
    - Record Contractions (number in 10 min, duration in seconds):
        IF duration < 20 seconds THEN record light stippling.
        IF duration between 20 and 40 seconds THEN record diagonal hatching.
        IF duration > 40 seconds THEN record solid black square.
    - Record Oxytocin (amount per volume IV fluids, drops/min) every 30 minutes if used.
    - Record Drugs given.
    - Record Pulse (dot ●) every 30 minutes.
    - Record Blood pressure (arrows) every four hours.
    - Record Temperature every two hours.
    - Record Protein, acetone, and volume when urine is passed.
    - IF Partograph passes to the right of Alert line THEN reassess and consider referral.
    - IF Partograph passes to the right of Action line THEN refer urgently unless birth is imminent.
    - Record drugs and IV fluids administered.
    - Maintain intake-output chart.

8.  **Second Stage of Labour Management:**
    - Transfer patient to delivery table.
    - Inform paediatrician.
    - Ensure delivery equipment and newborn resuscitation equipment are available.
    - Ensure place of delivery is clean and warm (25°C).
    - Position patient (dorsal/squatting).
    - Observe universal precautions.
    - Clean and drape parts.
    - IF episiotomy is planned THEN infiltrate perineum with local anaesthetic (e.g., 10 ml of 0.5 - 1% lignocaine).
    - Encourage patient to bear down.
    - IF required THEN perform episiotomy.
    - Control birth of the head (flexion, support perineum).
    - Suction nose and mouth.
    - Deliver shoulders and rest of the foetus.
    - Clamp and cut umbilical cord.
    - Ensure baby's respiration or institute resuscitation.
    - Administer Inj. oxytocin 5 Units IM at delivery of the anterior shoulder to prevent PPH.

9.  **Third Stage of Labour Management:**
    - Recognize signs of placental separation (globular/firm uterus, suprapubic bulge, gush of blood, lengthening cord).
    - Deliver placenta by controlled cord traction.
    - Examine placenta for completeness and anomalies.
    - Administer Inj. oxytocin 5-10 U IM.
    - Active management of third stage (unless contraindicated).
    - Examine woman for tears and repair if any.
    - Stitch episiotomy.
    - Observe woman closely for one hour after delivery (record PR, BP).
    - Evaluate uterus frequently and inspect perineum for excessive bleeding/haematoma.

10. **Transfer from Labour Room:**
    - Observe for 2 hours.
    - Check pulse rate, BP, uterus, bleeding, pre vaginum, external genitalia (stitches, haematoma), urine output before transfer.

11. **Episiotomy Procedure:**
    - Not performed routinely.
    - Applied selectively for: breech, forceps/vacuum delivery, occiput posterior positions, rigid perineum, scarred perineum, shoulder dystocia.
    - Clean area with antiseptic solution.
    - Infiltrate with local anaesthetic (e.g., 10 ml of 0.5 - 1% lignocaine).
    - Check anaesthetic effect before incision.
    - Perform at crowning.
    - Place two fingers between head and perineum.
    - Cut 3-4 cm of perineum in mediolateral direction.

12. **Episiotomy Repair:**
    - Examine for extensions of tears after delivery.
    - Repair with vicryl rapide 2-0 suture (or chromic catgut if vicryl unavailable).
    - Close vaginal mucosa with continuous suture starting 1 cm above apex.
    - Approximate perineal muscles with interrupted sutures.
    - Close skin with subcuticular (or interrupted) sutures.

13. **Episiotomy Care:**
    - Perineal hygiene (clean with antiseptic after urination/defaecation).
    - Prescribe analgesics for pain.

14. **Referral Criteria:**
    IF foetal heart rate < 120 or > 160 beats per minute THEN assess facilities/expertise and refer if necessary.
    IF prolapsed cord THEN assess facilities/expertise and refer if necessary.
    IF breech presentation THEN assess facilities/expertise and refer if necessary.
    IF stuck shoulders THEN assess facilities/expertise and refer if necessary.
    IF multiple births THEN assess facilities/expertise and refer if necessary.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Active management of third stage: IF contraindicated THEN do not administer Inj. oxytocin 5-10 U IM.
