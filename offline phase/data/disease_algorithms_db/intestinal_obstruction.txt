DISEASE: Intestinal Obstruction

ABBREVIATIONS:
- NG: Nasogastric
- CT: Contrast enhanced computed tomography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Sudden onset severe colicky central abdominal pain, distension, early vomiting, constipation (acute); short history of distension/vomiting against background of pain/constipation (acute on chronic); lower abdominal colic, absolute constipation followed by distension (chronic large bowel); dehydration, hypokalemia, pyrexia, abdominal tenderness, signs of strangulation (constant pain, tenderness with rigidity, shock).
- Imaging: X-ray abdomen (standing, multiple air-fluid levels), Ultrasound abdomen, Contrast enhanced CT scan abdomen with pelvis.
- Labs: Total and differential counts.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Intestinal Obstruction THEN
    // Initial Management
    Perform Investigations (X-ray, Ultrasound, CT, Labs)
    Assess for Strangulation (constant pain, tenderness with rigidity, shock)

    IF Strangulation suspected OR confirmed THEN
        // Surgical Treatment
        Proceed to Exploratory laparotomy
        Perform adhesiolysis, resection of non-viable intestine, or extirpation of obstructing lesion
    ELSE
        // Non-surgical treatment considered first, especially for simple obstruction
        Initiate Nasogastric (NG) decompression
        Correct dehydration (IV fluids)
        Correct electrolyte imbalance (e.g., hypokalemia)
        Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics

        // Re-evaluate patient response to non-surgical treatment
        IF Symptoms persist or worsen despite non-surgical treatment THEN
            // Surgical Treatment
            Proceed to Exploratory laparotomy
            Perform adhesiolysis, resection of non-viable intestine, or extirpation of obstructing lesion
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF
