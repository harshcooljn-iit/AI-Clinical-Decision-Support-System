```text
DISEASE: Acute Angle Closure Glaucoma

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IOP: Intraocular Pressure
- mmHg: Millimeters of Mercury
- Inj.: Injection
- QID: Four times a day
- BD: Twice a day
- Tab.: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Patient presents acutely with colored halos around lights, ocular pain, headache.
- IOP usually very high (> 40 - 50 mmHg).
- Conjunctival hyperaemia.
- Corneal edema.
- Unreactive mid-dilated vertically oval pupil.
- Fellow eye generally shows an occludable angle.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Acute Angle Closure Glaucoma is diagnosed THEN

    # Initial Management (Hospital Admission usually required)

    # Positioning
    Patient should assume a supine position.

    # Systemic Agents
    IF Severity is high THEN
        # Osmotic Agents
        Administer Inj. mannitol 20% 1–2 g/kg intravenously over half hour
        OR
        Administer glycerol 50% 1 g/kg orally (after checking for contraindications).
    ENDIF

    # Topical Agents (Affected Eye)
    Administer Pilocarpine 2–4% one drop, repeat after half an hour.
    # Consider omitting Pilocarpine if IOP is very high due to potential ischemia.

    # Topical Agents (Fellow Eye - Prophylaxis)
    Administer Pilocarpine 1% one drop.

    # Oral Agents
    Administer Tab. acetazolamide 500 mg orally.

    # Topical Agents (Affected Eye)
    Administer topical timolol 0.5%
    AND
    Administer topical prednisolone 1%
    OR
    Administer topical dexamethasone 0.1%
    (Leave 5 minutes between each instillation).

    # Symptomatic Relief
    Administer analgesia and/or an antiemetic if required.

    # Subsequent Medical Treatment (Outpatient or Continued Hospital)

    # Topical Agents (Affected Eye)
    Administer Pilocarpine 2% QID.

    # Topical Agents (Fellow Eye)
    Administer Pilocarpine 1% QID.

    # Topical Steroids (Affected Eye - if acutely inflamed)
    Administer topical prednisolone 1% QID
    OR
    Administer topical dexamethasone 0.1% QID.

    # Hypotensive Agents (Continue as necessary based on response)
    Administer topical timolol 0.5% BD
    AND/OR
    Administer oral acetazolamide 250 mg QID.

    # Surgical Treatment
    # Definitive treatment after corneal edema clears:
    Perform laser iridotomy or iridoplasty.
    # Continue topical steroids and necessary hypotensives for at least a week post-procedure.
    # Surgical options for resistant cases: surgical peripheral iridectomy, lens extraction, goniosynechialysis, trabeculectomy.

    # Patient Education
    Educate patient to report headache, chronic eye ache, or colored halos around lights.

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Glycerol 50% 1 g/kg orally: Contraindications should be checked before administration (specific contraindications not listed in text).
```
