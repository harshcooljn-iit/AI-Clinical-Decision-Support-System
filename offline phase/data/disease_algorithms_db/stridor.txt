DISEASE: Stridor

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IV: Intravenous
- QID: Four times a day
- Inj.: Injection
- mg: Milligram
- stat: Immediately
- subcutaneously: Subcutaneous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Noisy, harsh, difficult breathing.
- Classification based on timing: Inspiratory, expiratory, or biphasic.
- Specific signs: Restlessness, anxiety, distress, labored/noisy breathing (predominantly inspiratory), difficulty swallowing/speaking, cyanosis, chest retraction, tachypnea, altered level of consciousness, feeding difficulties (in children).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Stridor is present THEN
    - Administer humidified oxygen.

    IF Patient requires pharmacological intervention THEN
        - Administer Inj. hydrocortisone 100 mg IV stat.
        - IF Hydrocortisone needs to be repeated THEN
            - Administer Inj. hydrocortisone 100 mg IV as required.

        - Administer Inj. ampicillin 500mg QID IV for 5-7 days.

        IF Patient requires further intervention (e.g., severe distress) THEN
            - Administer Inj. adrenaline 0.2-0.5mg subcutaneously.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF
