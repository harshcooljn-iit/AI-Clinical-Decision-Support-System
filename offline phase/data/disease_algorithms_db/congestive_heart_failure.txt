DISEASE: Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CHF: Congestive Heart Failure
- LV: Left Ventricle
- SVR: Systemic Vascular Resistance
- CO: Cardiac Output
- NSAIDs: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs
- IV: Intravenous
- IU: International Units
- SC: Subcutaneous
- LVEF: Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Symptoms: Cough expectoration, breathlessness, pedal edema, hypo or hyper tension, easy fatigability, raised jugular venous pressure, positive abdominojugular reflex, basal rales, pulmonary edema, orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (PND).
- Signs: S3, hepatomegaly, ascites, sinus tachycardia.
- Investigations: Chest X-ray shows cardiomegaly, pulmonary congestion.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Assess Cardiac Output (CO) and Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR):**
    IF CO is low (↓ CO) AND SVR is high (↑ SVR)?
        THEN proceed to step 2.
    ELSE
        IF CO is normal OR high AND SVR is normal OR low?
            THEN proceed to step 3.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

2.  **Assess Left Ventricular (LV) Filling Pressures:**
    IF LV filling pressures are elevated (Yes)?
        THEN Patient is in Profile C ("Cold and wet"). Proceed to treatment for Profile C.
    ELSE
        IF LV filling pressures are not elevated (No)?
            THEN Patient is in Profile L ("Cold and dry"). Proceed to treatment for Profile L.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

3.  **Assess Left Ventricular (LV) Filling Pressures:**
    IF LV filling pressures are elevated (Yes)?
        THEN Patient is in Profile B ("Warm and wet"). Proceed to treatment for Profile B.
    ELSE
        IF LV filling pressures are not elevated (No)?
            THEN Patient is in Profile A ("Warm and dry"). No further treatment needed.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

**Treatment based on Profile:**

*   **Profile A ("Warm and dry"):** No further treatment needed.
*   **Profile B ("Warm and wet"):**
    *   Pharmacological treatment: Diuretics and vasodilators.
        *   Inj. furosemide 40 – 80 mg IV stat every 2 to 3 hours with maximum up to 200 mg/day.
        *   Maintenance dose: Inj. furosemide 40 mg IV 12 hourly.
        *   Tab. spironolactone 25 – 200 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. chlorthiazide 250 – 500 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. indapamide 2.5 – 5 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. benzthiazide 25 mg plus Tab. triamterene 50 mg/day.
        *   Tab. enalapril 2.5 -20 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. lisinopril 2.5 – 10 mg/day
        *   Tab. isosorbide mononitrate 60 mg/day as slow release preparation at night
*   **Profile C ("Cold and wet"):**
    *   Pharmacological treatment: Ionotropic agents, Diuretics, Vasodilators.
        *   Inj. furosemide 40 – 80 mg IV stat every 2 to 3 hours with maximum up to 200 mg/day.
        *   Maintenance dose: Inj. furosemide 40 mg IV 12 hourly.
        *   Tab. spironolactone 25 – 200 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. chlorthiazide 250 – 500 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. indapamide 2.5 – 5 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. benzthiazide 25 mg plus Tab. triamterene 50 mg/day.
        *   Tab. enalapril 2.5 -20 mg/day OR
        *   Tab. lisinopril 2.5 – 10 mg/day
        *   Tab. isosorbide mononitrate 60 mg/day as slow release preparation at night
        *   Tab. digoxin 0.5 mg first day followed by 0.25 mg/day 5 days/week (IF fast ventricular rate OR LVEF < 30%).
        *   Tab. carvedilol 3.15 – 25 mg per day in one or two divided doses (IF symptomatic or asymptomatic heart failure OR reduced ejection fraction (40%)).
*   **Profile L ("Cold and dry"):** Fluid challenge can be given.

**Non-pharmacological treatment (General):**
- Restrict physical activity, bed rest in propped up position with a back rest.
- Dietary salt (sodium) restriction (2 – 3 g/day).
- Fluid restriction depending on output and other conditions.

**Contraindications:**
- Avoid and discontinue NSAIDs.
- Avoid and discontinue high dose Beta blockers.
- Avoid and discontinue calcium channel blockers.
- Inj. heparin 5000 IU 12 hourly SC (IF patient is bedridden).
