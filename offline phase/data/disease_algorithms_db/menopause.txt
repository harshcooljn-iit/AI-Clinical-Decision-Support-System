DISEASE: Menopause

ABBREVIATIONS:
- FSH: Follicle-Stimulating Hormone
- HRT: Hormone Replacement Therapy
- GIT: Gastrointestinal
- mg: Milligram
- mcg: Microgram
- ml: Milliliter
- USG: Ultrasonography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on permanent cessation of menses for one year.
- Serum FSH and oestradiol levels may be helpful in doubtful cases.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Non-pharmacological treatment:**
    - IF patient has menopause symptoms THEN recommend balanced diet (fruits, vegetables, semi-skimmed milk), reduction/avoidance of smoking and alcohol, and exercise (walking/swimming 20-30 min daily).

2.  **Pharmacological treatment:**
    - IF patient has menopause symptoms THEN consider pharmacological options.
    - IF patient requires calcium supplementation THEN prescribe Tab. calcium 1500 mg daily.
    - IF patient requires Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) THEN check for contraindications.

3.  **HRT Contraindications:**
    - IF patient has endometrial cancer, breast cancer, acute phase myocardial infarction, undiagnosed breast lump, abnormal vaginal bleeding, or acute liver disease THEN DO NOT prescribe HRT.
    - IF patient has hypertension or diabetes THEN control these conditions before prescribing HRT.

4.  **Oestrogen Therapy (for hysterectomized patients):**
    - IF patient has had a hysterectomy THEN consider oestrogen therapy.
    - IF symptoms recur during drug-free period THEN give continuous therapy.
    - Option 1: Conjugated equine oestrogen 0.625 - 1.25 mg daily (1st-25th day every month or continuously).
    - Option 2: Oestriol 1 - 2 mg daily (1st-25th day every month or continuously).
    - Option 3: Transdermal oestradiol patch 50 or 100 mcg/day applied twice a week (away from breast, preferably on buttock, thigh, or legs).
    - IF patient has gall bladder disease, hypertriglyceridemia, history of thromboembolism, poorly-controlled hypertension, recent myocardial infarction, vascular diseases, migraine, chronic hepatic dysfunction, or malabsorption syndrome THEN prefer Transdermal oestradiol patch.

5.  **Combined Therapy (for women with intact uterus):**
    - IF patient has an intact uterus THEN use combined therapy (oestrogen + progestogen).
    - Option 1 (Cyclic Sequential Regimen): Add progestogen from 13th to 25th days OR 1st to 12th of every month.
        - Progestogen options: Medroxyprogesterone acetate 5 - 10 mg, Dihydrogesterone 10 - 20 mg, Norethisterone 2.5 mg, or Micronized progesterone 200 ml.
    - Option 2 (Continuous Sequential Regimen): Add progestogen from 1st to 12th of every month.
        - Progestogen options: Medroxyprogesterone acetate 5 - 10 mg, Dihydrogesterone 10 - 20 mg, Norethisterone 2.5 mg, or Micronized progesterone 200 ml.
    - Option 3 (Continuous Combined Treatment):
        - IF withdrawal bleeding is not acceptable THEN use continuous combined treatment.
        - Option A: 0.625 mg conjugated equine oestrogen + 2.5 mg medroxyprogesterone acetate.
        - Option B: 1 mg micronized oestrogen + 100 mg micronized progesterone.

6.  **Alternative HRT (If conventional HRT is contraindicated):**
    - IF conventional HRT is contraindicated THEN consider alternative options.
    - Option 1: Tab. Tibolone 2.5 mg per day. (Note: Major side effects include weight gain, oedema, breast tenderness, GIT symptoms, and vaginal bleeding).
    - Option 2 (For symptomatic elderly women with atrophic vaginitis): Oestriol cream daily application of 0.5 g (delivering 0.5 mg oestriol) for 3 weeks, followed by twice weekly application for 3 - 4 weeks.

7.  **Follow-up:**
    - Follow up at 2-3 months, then at 6 monthly intervals.
    - Advise yearly mammography, Pap's smear, pelvic USG, and serum oestradiol.

8.  **Patient Education:**
    - Explain about the side effects of estrogen and progestogens.
    - Schedule follow-up visits at 2 - 3 months, then at 6 monthly intervals.
