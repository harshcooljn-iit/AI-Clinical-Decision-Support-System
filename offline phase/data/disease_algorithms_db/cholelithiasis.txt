```text
DISEASE: Cholelithiasis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IV: Intravenous
- Inj.: Injection
- SOS: Stat Order Set (immediate use)
- IM: Intramuscular
- CBD: Common Bile Duct
- T-tube: T-tube cholangiography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Ultrasound abdomen
- Total and differential count
- Liver function test

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Initial Assessment
IF Patient presents with symptomatic cholelithiasis:
    // Surgical Treatment
    IF Patient is symptomatic:
        THEN Definitive treatment is cholecystectomy.
    ELSE
        // Conservative Management (if patient presents after 48 hours)
        IF Patient presents after 48 hours:
            THEN Manage conservatively.
            THEN Cholecystectomy after 6-8 weeks.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ELSE
    // Asymptomatic Cholelithiasis
    IF Patient is asymptomatic:
        THEN Cholecystectomy is indicated IF patient has diabetes OR a solitary large stone OR multiple small stones with wide cystic duct OR porcelain gall bladder OR is an anxious patient.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Management of Acute Cholecystitis, Empyema Gall Bladder, or Stones in CBD
IF Patient has acute cholecystitis OR empyema gall bladder OR stones in the CBD:
    THEN Institute following treatment (pharmacological).
ENDIF

// Pharmacological Treatment (General)
THEN Maintenance IV fluids.

// Antibiotics
IF Patient requires antibiotics:
    THEN Inj. ciprofloxacin (infusion 100 mg/50 ml) 100 ml IV twice a day.
    THEN Inj. gentamicin (40 mg/ml) 2 ml IV 8 hourly.
    IF Anaerobic bacterial infection is suspected or anticipated:
        THEN Inj. metronidazole (500 mg/100 ml) 100 ml IV 8 hourly.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Pain Management
IF Patient requires pain management:
    THEN Inj. diclofenac sodium (25 mg/ml) 2-3 ml IM SOS or 6 hourly.
    OR
    THEN Inj. pentazocine (30 mg/ml) 1 ml IM SOS.
    THEN Inj. hyoscine butylbromide (20 mg/ml) 1 ml IV SOS.
ENDIF

// Obstructive Jaundice Management
IF Patient has obstructive jaundice:
    THEN Inj. vitamin K (10 mg/ml) 1 ml IM once or twice a day till prothrombin time reaches a satisfactory level.
ENDIF

// Antibiotic Duration
THEN Antibiotics are usually stopped after 5-7 days.
IF Patient has evidence of persistent infection OR has indwelling tube (e.g., T-tube):
    THEN Continue antibiotics beyond 5-7 days.
ENDIF

// Patient Education
THEN Advise to avoid fatty and fried meals for 3 months.
THEN Encourage ambulation as early as possible.
IF Cholecystectomy was laparoscopic:
    THEN Avoid heavy physical exertion for 2 weeks.
ELSE IF Cholecystectomy was conventional:
    THEN Avoid heavy physical exertion for 3 months.
ENDIF

// T-tube Management
IF T-tube has been placed:
    THEN Remove after 2-3 weeks.
    THEN Ensure CBD is patent, non-dilated, and free flow of contrast into the duodenum during T-tube cholangiography before removal.
ENDIF
```
