DISEASE: Obstructive Jaundice

ABBREVIATIONS:
- USG: Ultrasound
- MRCP: Magnetic Resonance Cholangiopancreatography
- CECT: Contrast-Enhanced Computed Tomography
- PT: Prothrombin Time
- INR: International Normalized Ratio
- SGPT: Serum Glutamic Pyruvic Transaminase (ALT)
- SGOT: Serum Glutamic Oxaloacetic Transaminase (AST)
- SAP: Serum Alkaline Phosphatase
- CA 19-9: Carbohydrate Antigen 19-9
- ERCP: Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography
- CBD: Common Bile Duct
- FFP: Fresh Frozen Plasma
- Inj.: Injection
- IV: Intravenous
- GM: Gram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical features: Jaundice, itching, anorexia, weight loss, clay-colored stools, dark urine, abdominal lump, palpable mass, cachexia.
- Laboratory tests: Elevated total and direct bilirubin (>2.5 mg/dL), elevated PT/INR, elevated or normal SGPT/SGOT, elevated SAP (>3 folds), elevated CA 19-9 (in periampullary cancers).
- Imaging: USG-Abdomen, MRCP, CECT-Abdomen, ERCP.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Management:**
    *   IF patient has Obstructive Jaundice THEN
        *   Administer IV fluids to maintain hydration.
        *   Correct electrolyte imbalance.
        *   Administer Inj. vitamin K.
        *   Administer FFP when indicated.
        *   Administer prophylactic antibiotics, Inj. cefotaxime 1 gm IV 8 hourly.

2.  **Dietary Management:**
    *   IF patient has Obstructive Jaundice THEN
        *   Recommend low fat, high carbohydrate diet.

3.  **Surgical/Procedural Treatment (based on cause):**
    *   IF cause is choledocholithiasis AND stones are amenable to ERCP retrieval THEN
        *   Perform ERCP with sphincterotomy and stenting.
        *   Followed by cholecystectomy.
    *   ELSE IF cause is choledocholithiasis AND stones are NOT amenable to ERCP retrieval THEN
        *   Perform cholecystectomy with CBD exploration.
    *   ELSE IF cause is resectable periampullary mass THEN
        *   Perform Whipple procedure (pancreaticoduodenectomy).
    *   ELSE IF cause is unresectable periampullary mass THEN
        *   Perform bypass procedures (hepaticojejunostomy with/without gastrojejunostomy).

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for the listed pharmacological treatments, but standard contraindications for each drug/procedure would apply in a clinical setting).
- FFP: Contraindicated in patients with fluid overload or hypervolemia.
- Cefotaxime: Contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to cephalosporins.
- Vitamin K: Generally well-tolerated, but hypersensitivity reactions are possible.
