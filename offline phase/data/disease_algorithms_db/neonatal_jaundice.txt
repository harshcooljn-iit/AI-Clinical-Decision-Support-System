DISEASE: Neonatal Jaundice

ABBREVIATIONS:
- RBCs: Red Blood Cells
- BERA: Brainstem Evoked Response Audiometry
- IV: Intravenous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical assessment: Yellow eyes, mucus membrane, or skin.
- Progression: Cephalopedal (head to toe).
- Serum bilirubin levels:
    - 10-12 mg/dl: Yellow coloration of trunk.
    - >15 mg/dl: Staining of palms and soles.
- Timeline:
    - Physiological jaundice: Appears within 3rd day, resolves within 14 days.
    - Pathological jaundice: May persist > 2 weeks, requires investigation for cholestatic jaundice.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  ASSESSMENT: All neonates need assessment for jaundice.

2.  PHOTOTHERAPY DECISION:
    - IF Total Serum Bilirubin (TSB) level reaches phototherapy threshold:
        - THEN Initiate phototherapy.
        - Phototherapy threshold: 10-12 mg/dl for preterm babies OR 15 mg/dl for term babies.
    - ELSE: Observe the neonate.

3.  PHOTOTHERAPY ADMINISTRATION:
    - Position the naked infant (eyes covered) 45 cm below the phototherapy unit.
    - Administer phototherapy continuously.
    - Interrupt only for nursing and feeding.
    - Change position every 2-4 hours.

4.  PHOTOTHERAPY FAILURE:
    - IF Inability to observe a decline in bilirubin of 1-2 mg/dl after 4-6 hours AND/OR inability to keep bilirubin level below exchange transfusion level:
        - THEN Phototherapy is considered a failure.
        - Consider alternative or adjunct treatments (e.g., Exchange Transfusion).

5.  EXCHANGE TRANSFUSION DECISION:
    - IF Bilirubin level exceeds threshold levels for age and gestational age OR features of bilirubin encephalopathy are present:
        - THEN Indicate Exchange Transfusion.

6.  EXCHANGE TRANSFUSION PROCEDURE:
    - Use Group and type specific, compatible packed RBCs with anticoagulant, <7 days old.
    - Monitor heart rate, saturation, respiration, temperature, glucose, and bilirubin levels.

7.  ADJUNCT TREATMENTS:
    - Consider Intravenous Immunoglobulin (IVIG).
    - Consider Phenobarbitone.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for Phototherapy, Exchange Transfusion, IVIG, or Phenobarbitone).
