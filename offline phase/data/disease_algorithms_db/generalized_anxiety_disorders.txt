DISEASE: Generalized Anxiety Disorders

ABBREVIATIONS:
- GAD: Generalized Anxiety Disorder
- HS: Bedtime (at night)
- TDS: Three times a day
- SSRIs: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors
- BD: Twice a day

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Diagnosis is based on history and mental status examination.
- Investigations for co-morbidities (e.g., hyperthyroidism, hypothyroidism, hypo parathyroidism, pheochromocytoma, nutritional deficiencies) should be done to rule out organic causes.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# Non-pharmacological Treatment
IF patient prefers non-pharmacological treatment THEN
    - Cognitive behavior therapy (CBT): Focuses on identifying cognitive distortions and altering faulty coping strategies. Techniques include relaxation and biofeedback.
    - Supportive therapy: Offers reassurance and comfort.
    - Insight-oriented psychotherapy: Focuses on uncovering unconscious conflicts and identifying ego strengths.
ENDIF

# Pharmacological Treatment

# Benzodiazepines
IF benzodiazepines are chosen THEN
    - Diazepam:
        - Start with 5 mg HS or 10 mg HS.
    - Clonazepam:
        - Start with 0.5 mg HS or TDS depending on symptom severity.
    - Lorazepam:
        - Start with 2 mg HS, increase up to a maximum of 8 mg.
ENDIF

# SSRIs
IF SSRIs are chosen THEN
    - Sertraline, escitalopram, or paroxetine are options.
    - Paroxetine:
        - Start with Tab. paroxetine 10 mg or 20 mg per day.
        - Increase up to a maximum of 50 mg/day.
ENDIF

# Beta Blockers
IF beta blockers are chosen for symptomatic relief THEN
    - Propranolol:
        - Start with Tab. propranolol 20 mg BD.
        - Or use a sustained-release preparation up to 80 mg/day in divided doses.
ENDIF
