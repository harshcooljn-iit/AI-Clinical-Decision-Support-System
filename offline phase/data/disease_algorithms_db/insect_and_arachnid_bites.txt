DISEASE: Insect And Arachnid Bites

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IM: Intramuscular
- Inj.: Injection
- mg: Milligram
- ml: Milliliter
- tab: Tablet
- cetrizine: Cetirizine
- paracetamol: Paracetamol
- ibuprofen: Ibuprofen
- aspirin: Aspirin
- hydrocortisone: Hydrocortisone
- calamine lotion: Calamine Lotion
- adrenaline: Adrenaline
- hydrogen tartrate: Hydrogen Tartrate

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Skin around the bite becomes red, swollen and itchy.
- Venom from a sting often causes a swollen, itchy, red mark (weal).
- Severity varies depending on the type of insect and person's sensitivity.
- Serious allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) is rare but possible.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF the bite or sting is minor (redness, swelling, itching)
    THEN
        - Wash the affected area with soap and water.
        - Place a cold compress over the affected area to reduce swelling.
        - Do not scratch the area.
        - Wrap an ice pack in a towel and place it on the swelling.
    ENDIF

IF the bite or sting is painful or swollen
    THEN
        - Administer oral analgesic:
            IF age is under 16 years old
                THEN
                    - Administer tab. paracetamol OR tab. ibuprofen.
                    - DO NOT administer aspirin.
                ELSE
                    - Administer tab. paracetamol OR tab. ibuprofen.
                ENDIF
        - Apply topical treatment to the affected area:
            - Spray or cream containing local anesthetic.
            - Spray or cream containing antihistamine.
            - Spray or cream containing mild hydrocortisone (1%).
        - Administer oral antihistamine:
            - Tab. cetrizine 10 mg once a day.
        - Apply topical calamine lotion to relieve pruritus.
    ENDIF

IF there are severe side-effects (e.g., severe swelling, systemic symptoms)
    THEN
        - Consider systemic corticosteroids.
    ENDIF

IF the person collapses, complains of wheezing, feeling of anxiety or faintness, generalized itching, or tightness in the chest within 1 hour of being stung
    THEN
        - Treat as anaphylactic shock.
        - Administer Inj. adrenaline 1 mg (as hydrogen tartrate) 0.5-1.0 ml IM injection (1:1000 solution).
        - Repeat Inj. adrenaline every 15-20 min if required.
        - Observe the patient for at least 24 hours for recurrent anaphylaxis.
    ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Aspirin: DO NOT give to children under 16 years old.
