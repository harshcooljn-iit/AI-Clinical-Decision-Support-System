DISEASE: Typhoid Perforation

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- gm: gram
- IV: Intravenous
- tab: tablet
- mg: milligram
- TC: Total Count (likely referring to White Blood Cell count)
- DC: Differential Count (likely referring to White Blood Cell differential)
- Widal test: Widal test (for typhoid fever diagnosis)
- X ray: X-ray

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Patient history: Residence or recent visit to an endemic area, presentation in the 2nd or 3rd week of illness.
- Clinical presentation: High temperature, toxic appearance, Ross spots, abdominal pain, malena, diarrhea, abdominal tenderness, and guarding.
- Laboratory findings: TC and DC showing relative neutropenia, positive Widal test.
- Culture: Positive Blood and Stool culture for _Salmonella typhi_.
- Imaging: X-ray abdomen standing showing free gas under the diaphragm.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient presents with signs and symptoms suggestive of Typhoid Perforation:
    THEN Admit patient to Intensive Care Unit (ICU).
    THEN Initiate vigorous fluid resuscitation.

    // Surgical Treatment
    THEN Perform emergency exploratory laparotomy.
    THEN Identify the site of perforation (commonest site is terminal ileum).
    THEN Look for additional perforations.
    THEN Consider exteriorization for very ill patients.
    THEN Close the peritoneum.
    THEN Leave the wound open for secondary healing.

    // Pharmacological Treatment (Post-Surgery)
    THEN Administer Inj. ceftriaxone:
        IF Loading dose required:
            THEN Administer 2 gm IV once.
        THEN Administer 1 gm IV every 8 hours.

    THEN Administer Tab. metronidazole:
        THEN Administer 400 mg IV every 8 hours.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(None specified in the provided text for the listed medications in this specific context, but general contraindications for ceftriaxone and metronidazole would apply if known).
