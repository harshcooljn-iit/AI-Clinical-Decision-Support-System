DISEASE: Pertussis
ABBREVIATIONS:
    URTI: Upper Respiratory Tract Infection
    DPT: Diphtheria Pertussis Tetanus

DIAGNOSTICS:
    Clinical presentation: Starts as mild URTI (catarrhal stage), progresses to severe coughing paroxysms, often with a characteristic whoop, followed by vomiting. Child may exhibit breathlessness, bulging eyes, flushed face, lacrimation, salivation, tongue protrusion, neck vein distension. Episodes are exhausting and triggered by yawning, sneezing, eating.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Pertussis:
    THEN:
        IF Disease stage is coryzal phase (first 14 days):
            THEN:
                Administer erythromycin:
                    IF Age is not specified:
                        THEN:
                            Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                    ELSE IF Age is < 6 months:
                        THEN:
                            Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                    ELSE IF Age is 6 months to 1 year:
                        THEN:
                            Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                    ELSE IF Age is 1 year to 5 years:
                        THEN:
                            Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                    ELSE IF Age is > 5 years:
                        THEN:
                            Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                ENDIF
            ELSE:
                IF Paroxysms have started:
                    THEN:
                        Administer erythromycin for eradication of secondary pulmonary infection only.
                        Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                    ENDIF
                ELSE:
                    Administer erythromycin.
                    Dosage: 40-50 mg/kg/day orally in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
                ENDIF
            ENDIF
        ELSE:
            IF Paroxysms have started:
                THEN:
                    Administer salbutamol for severe coughing paroxysms:
                        Dosage: 1-2 mg/kg/day orally in 3-4 divided doses for about a week.
                ENDIF
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

    IF Patient is < 6 months old OR has respiratory distress:
        THEN:
            Admit for intravenous fluids and oxygen therapy.
        ENDIF

    IF Patient has severe respiratory distress:
        THEN:
            Administer oxygen therapy.
        ENDIF

    Maintain hydration with intravenous or oral fluids.
    Avoid cough suppressants.

    Continue antibiotic therapy for at least 14 days to prevent relapse.

    Treat all contacts below 7 years of age with erythromycin for 14 days.

    Monitor for listlessness, apnoea, or seizures (especially in infants < 6 months). Contact doctor immediately if these occur.

    Immunization:
    - Primary doses: 3 doses of DPT vaccine for all infants.
    - Booster doses: After 1.5 years and 4.5 years.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    (None specified in the text for erythromycin or salbutamol, but standard contraindications apply based on drug monographs).
