DISEASE: Bacterial Conjunctivitis (Non-Gonococcal)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- QID: Four times a day
- mg/kg/day: Milligrams per kilogram per day
- H. influenzae: Haemophilus influenzae
- S. pneumoniae: Streptococcus pneumoniae
- S. aureus: Staphylococcus aureus
- Moraxella catarrhalis: Moraxella catarrhalis
- OTC: Over the counter

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Patient complains of redness, foreign body sensation, mucoid or mucopurulent discharge. Itching is less prominent.
- Examination shows purulent discharge, conjunctival papillae, and chemosis.
- Gram stain may be performed, especially if severe or recurrent, to evaluate for gonococcus.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has acute bacterial conjunctivitis (non-gonococcal) THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    THEN Advise patient to avoid touching eyes, shaking hands, sharing handkerchiefs/towels.
    THEN Advise restricting work/school if significant exposure to others.
    THEN Advise frequent hand washing.

    // Pharmacological treatment
    THEN
        IF Severity is mild/moderate THEN
            THEN Use topical antibiotic therapy (e.g., moxifloxacin 0.5% drops QID) for 5 to 7 days.
        ELSE IF Severity is severe THEN
            THEN Use topical antibiotic therapy (e.g., moxifloxacin 0.5% drops QID or more frequent) for 5 to 7 days.
        ENDIF

        IF Causative agent is suspected or confirmed to be _H. influenzae_ THEN
            THEN Use oral amoxicillin/clavulanic acid (20 to 40 mg/kg/day in three divided doses) because of occasional extra ocular involvement.
            THEN Adjust antibiotic therapy according to culture and sensitivity results.
        ENDIF

        IF Associated with dacryocystitis THEN
            THEN Use systemic antibiotics.
        ENDIF

    // Patient Education
    THEN Advise follow up every 2 to 3 days initially, then every 5 to 7 days until resolved.
    THEN Advise maintaining ocular hygiene.

ENDIF
