```text
1.  DISEASE: Hypokalemia
2.  ABBREVIATIONS:
    *   KCL: Potassium Chloride
    *   TDS: Three times a day
    *   mEq/l: milliequivalents per liter
    *   IV: Intravenous
3.  DIAGNOSTICS: Serum potassium level < 3.5 mEq/l.
4.  TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
    *   IF Serum Potassium < 3.5 mEq/l THEN:
        *   Consider non-pharmacological treatment: Dietary intake of potassium-rich foods (fruit juices, banana, coconut water, dry fruits).
        *   Consider pharmacological treatment based on severity and symptoms.
        *   IF Urgent treatment required (e.g., periodic paralysis, ventricular ectopics, myocardial infarction) THEN:
            *   Administer IV KCL if patient unable to take orally AND Potassium < 2.5 mEq/l.
        *   ELSE IF Patient able to take orally AND Potassium >= 2.5 mEq/l THEN:
            *   Administer Oral potassium chloride 15ml TDS.
5.  TABULAR DOSAGES: Not applicable in this guideline.
6.  CONTRAINDICATIONS: Not specified in this guideline.
```
