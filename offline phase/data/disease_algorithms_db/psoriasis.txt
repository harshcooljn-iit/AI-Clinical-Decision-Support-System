```text
DISEASE: Psoriasis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- BSA: Body Surface Area
- SOS: As needed (implied from context, not explicitly defined in text)

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination
- Skin biopsy

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Psoriasis is diagnosed THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    IF Patient has identifiable triggering factors THEN
        Advise patient to identify and avoid triggering factors.
    ENDIF

    Advise patient to avoid stress.
    Advise patient to avoid alcohol.
    Advise patient to avoid smoking.

    // Pharmacological treatment - Topical agents (if < 10% BSA involved)
    IF Body Surface Area (BSA) involvement is less than 10% THEN
        // Emollients
        Recommend emollients such as liquid paraffin, vaseline, light liquid paraffin, or white soft paraffin.

        // Topical Corticosteroids
        Recommend topical corticosteroids such as clobetasol or betamethasone, either alone or in combination.

        // Salicylic Acid
        Recommend salicylic acid 3-6%.

        // Vitamin D3 analogues
        Recommend Vitamin D3 analogues such as calcitriol or calcipotriol.
    ELSE

        // Pharmacological treatment - Systemic therapy (if > 20% BSA involved)
        IF BSA involvement is greater than 20% THEN
            Recommend referral to a tertiary care centre for systemic therapy.

            // Systemic options at tertiary care centre
            IF Methotrexate is considered THEN
                Recommend Tab. methotrexate 5-7.5 mg/week.
            ENDIF

            IF Cyclosporine is considered THEN
                Recommend Tab. cyclosporine 100-200 mg/day.
            ENDIF

            IF Acitretin is considered THEN
                Recommend Tab. acitretin 25 mg/day.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        // Phototherapy
        Recommend Phototherapy.
    ENDIF

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Avoid beta blockers.
- Avoid chloroquine.
```
