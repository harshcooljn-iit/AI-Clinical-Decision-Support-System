- Hemo dialysis: Hemodialysis
- Peritoneal dialysis: Peritoneal dialysis

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Defined as S. Ca > 10.5 mEq/L.
- Symptoms appear when S. Ca > 11.5-12.0 mg/dl.
- Severe hypercalcemia > 15-18 mg/dl can be life-threatening.
- ECG may show Short QTc and arrhythmias.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Serum Calcium (S. Ca) is > 10.5 mEq/L THEN:

    IF Symptoms are present (S. Ca > 11.5-12.0 mg/dl) THEN:

        IF Hypercalcemia is Mild THEN:
            - Rehydrate with isotonic saline 2-4 lt/day.
            - After correcting dehydration, administer Inj. frusemide 20-40 mg BD.
            - Monitor electrolytes (potassium, magnesium) and replace if needed.

        ELSE IF Hypercalcemia is Moderate to Severe THEN:
            - Aggressive rehydration with isotonic saline 6 lt.
            - Administer Inj. frusemide up to 100 mg every 1 to 2 hours.
            - Administer Inj. calcitonin 2-8 U/kg IV/SC/IM every 6-12 hr.
            - Administer ONE of the following bisphosphonates:
                - Inj. pamindronate 30-90 mg IV infusion in 0.9% saline over 4-24 hrs.
                - Tab. alendronate sodium 10 mg orally in the morning with a full glass of water at least 30 mins before any food or drink; remain in upright position for at least 30 mins.
                - Inj. zolendronate 1-4 mg IV in few mins.
            - Administer Tab. prednisolone 40-100 mg/day in 4 divided doses (if indicated for osteolytic malignancies, Vit. D intoxication, or sarcoidosis).
            - Administer Tab. phosphorus (sodium or potassium phosphate) 1-1.5 g/day in 4 divided doses for several days if hypophosphatemia is present.
            - Consider Peritoneal/Hemo dialysis with calcium-free dialysate, especially if complicated by renal failure.

    ELSE IF S. Ca is > 15-18 mg/dl (Severe Hypercalcemia) THEN:
        - Aggressive rehydration with isotonic saline 6 lt.
        - Administer Inj. frusemide up to 100 mg every 1 to 2 hours.
        - Administer Inj. calcitonin 2-8 U/kg IV/SC/IM every 6-12 hr.
        - Administer ONE of the following bisphosphonates:
            - Inj. pamindronate 30-90 mg IV infusion in 0.9% saline over 4-24 hrs.
            - Tab. alendronate sodium 10 mg orally in the morning with a full glass of water at least 30 mins before any food or drink; remain in upright position for at least 30 mins.
            - Inj. zolendronate 1-4 mg IV in few mins.
        - Administer Tab. prednisolone 40-100 mg/day in 4 divided doses (if indicated for osteolytic malignancies, Vit. D intoxication, or sarcoidosis).
        - Administer Tab. phosphorus (sodium or potassium phosphate) 1-1.5 g/day in 4 divided doses for several days if hypophosphatemia is present.
        - Consider Peritoneal/Hemo dialysis with calcium-free dialysate.

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for the listed drugs in the context of hypercalcemia treatment, but standard contraindications apply to each drug).
    - Furosemide: Known hypersensitivity, hepatic encephalopathy, severe electrolyte depletion.
    - Calcitonin: Known hypersensitivity.
    - Pamidronate/Alendronate/Zolendronate: Hypersensitivity, esophageal disorders (for oral bisphosphonates), renal impairment (use with caution).
    - Prednisolone: Known hypersensitivity.
    - Phosphate supplements: Severe renal impairment, hyperkalemia, hypocalcemia (unless treating specific conditions).
