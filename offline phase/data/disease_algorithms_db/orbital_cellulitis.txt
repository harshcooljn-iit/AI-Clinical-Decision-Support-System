DISEASE: Orbital Cellulitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- TDS: Three times a day
- Inj.: Injection
- gm: gram
- mg: milligram
- kg: kilogram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical symptoms: Swelling of eyelids, severe pain increased by eye movement/pressure, fever, nausea, vomiting, possible vision loss.
- Physical exam: Marked lid swelling, conjunctival chemosis, restriction of ocular movements, possible fundus examination findings (retinal vein congestion, papillitis, papilloedema).
- Laboratory/Imaging: Bacterial cultures (nasal, conjunctival swabs, blood), X-ray (sinusitis), orbital ultrasonography (abscess).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Orbital Cellulitis THEN

  // Non-pharmacological treatment
  Warm compresses should be applied.

  // Pharmacological treatment
  Obtain nasal, conjunctival, and blood culture samples.

  // Antibiotic Therapy - Option 1: Amoxicillin and Clavulanic Acid
  IF Choosing Amoxicillin and Clavulanic Acid THEN
    Administer Tab. amoxicillin and clavulanic acid 625mg TDS.
  ENDIF

  // Antibiotic Therapy - Option 2: Cefotaxime
  IF Choosing Cefotaxime THEN
    IF Patient is an adult THEN
      Administer Inj. cefotaxime 1-2 gm every 12 hours.
    ELSEIF Patient is a child THEN
      Administer Inj. cefotaxime 100-150 mg/kg/dose, given in 2 to 3 divided doses per day.
    ENDIF
  ENDIF

  // Antibiotic Adjustment
  Change antibacterials based on culture sensitivity reports.
  Continue antibiotics until resolution of symptoms.

  // Alternative Antibiotics
  IF Cefotaxime or Amoxicillin/Clavulanate are not suitable OR as alternatives THEN
    Consider Ciprofloxacin or Vancomycin.
  ENDIF

  // Analgesics/Anti-inflammatories
  Administer analgesic and anti-inflammatory drugs for pain and fever control.

  // Surgical Intervention
  IF Patient is unresponsive to antibacterials AND/OR
     Patient has decrease in vision AND/OR
     Patient has an orbital or subperiosteal abscess THEN
    Surgical intervention is needed.
  ENDIF

ENDIF
