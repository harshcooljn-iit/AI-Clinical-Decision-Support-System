DISEASE: Pityrosporum Infections

ABBREVIATIONS:
- KOH: Potassium Hydroxide
- Cap.: Capsule
- Tab.: Tablet
- mg: Milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination (asymptomatic hypo or hyperpigmented irregular macules with branny scales).
- Wood’s lamp (yellowish fluorescence).
- 10% KOH mount (non-branching mycelia and yeasts, "banana and grapes" appearance).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Disease is Pityrosporum Infections:
    IF Severity is Mild superficial fungal infection of stratum corneum:
        THEN Treatment options include:
            - Topical antifungal cream (ketoconazole, miconazole, clotrimazole, or terbinafine).
            - Topical selenium sulfide 2.5% shampoo or lotion.
            - Systemic antifungals (Cap. fluconazole 400 mg stat).
            - For recurrence, Tab. ketoconazole 400 mg once per month.
    ELSE IF Disease is Pityriasis Capitis (Dandruff):
        THEN Treatment options include:
            - Non-pharmacological: Frequent hair washes with light massage, avoid oil and soap application.
            - Pharmacological: Antifungal lotions/shampoos (ketoconazole or econazole), keratolytics (salicylic acid or coal tar) applied 15 min before bath.
            - Pharmacological: Topical steroids (betamethasone lotion) in combination with antifungal lotion after hair wash.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for the listed drugs in the context of Pityrosporum Infections or Pityriasis Capitis).
