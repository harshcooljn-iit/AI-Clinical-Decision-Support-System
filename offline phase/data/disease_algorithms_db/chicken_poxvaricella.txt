DISEASE: Chicken Pox/Varicella

ABBREVIATIONS:
- VZIG: Varicella Zoster Immunoglobulin
- IV: Intravenous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Crops of small red papules evolving into clear vesicles on an erythematous base, appearing over 3-4 days, often accompanied by fever and systemic symptoms.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient is a healthy child:
    THEN:
        - Manage fever with appropriate antipyretics (avoiding aspirin/salicylates).
        - Use local anti-pruritic agents like calamine lotion for itching.
        - If itching persists, use Tab. pheniramine 25 mg twice daily OR Tab. cetirizine (2-6 years: 5 mg once daily; >6 years: 10 mg once daily).
        - Disease is self-limiting.
        - Exclude from school/daycare until the 6th day of rash or until scabs have formed.
        - Advise against "over the counter" fever medicines containing aspirin/salicylates.

ELSE IF Patient is immuno-compromised (on long-term steroids, anti-cancer drugs, immunosuppressives, HIV positive) OR older than 12 years OR has chronic cutaneous/pulmonary disorders OR is at increased risk of severe disease:
    THEN:
        IF Rash onset is within 24 hours:
            THEN:
                IF Patient is severely immuno-compromised OR has severe disease in adults:
                    THEN:
                        - Administer Inj. acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV every 8 hours for 7 days.
                ELSE:
                    - Administer Tab. acyclovir 20 mg/kg/day divided every 6 hours for 5 days.
        ELSE:
            - Consider oral acyclovir if within 24 hours of rash onset, but IV is preferred for severe cases/immuno-compromised.

ELSE IF Patient is a contact of a case who is severely immunocompromised or pregnant (particularly in the first trimester):
    THEN:
        - Recommend post-exposure prophylaxis with Varicella Zoster Immunoglobulin (VZIG).

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Aspirin and other salicylates are contraindicated for fever management due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
