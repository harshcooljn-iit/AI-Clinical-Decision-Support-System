DISEASE: Acute Pyogenic Meningitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
- IV: Intravenous
- PMN's: Polymorphonuclear leukocytes
- IU: International Units
- mg/kg: Milligrams per kilogram
- stat: Immediately
- SOS: As needed
- mL: Milliliter
- kg: Kilogram
- tab: Tablet
- NPO: Nothing by mouth
- q: Every
- day: Per day
- divided doses: Given in multiple administrations throughout the day

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Fever, headache, vomiting, irritability, altered state of consciousness, signs of meningeal irritation, seizures.
- CSF examination: Analysis of CSF pressure, leucocyte count (with differential), protein level, and glucose level.
    - Normal CSF: Pressure 50-80 mmH2O, Leucocytosis <5 cells/mm3 (lymphocytes predominant), Protein 20-45 mg/dl, Glucose >50 mg/dl or 75% serum glucose.
    - Acute bacterial meningitis CSF: Pressure usually elevated (100-300 mmH2O), Leucocytosis 100-10,000 cells/mm3 (PMN's predominant), Protein 100-500 mg/dl, Glucose decreased (<40 mg/dl).
    - Acute viral meningo-encephalitis CSF: Pressure normal or elevated, Leucocytosis rarely >1000 cells/mm3 (lymphocytes predominant), Protein 50-200 mg/dl, Glucose normal or decreased.
    - Tubercular meningo-encephalitis CSF: Pressure usually elevated, Leucocytosis 100-500 cells/mm3 (PMN's early, lymphocytes later), Protein 100-3000 mg/dl, Glucose <50 mg/dl.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Supportive Care:**
    - Maintain airway, breathing, and circulation.
    - Control seizures:
        - IF seizure occurs THEN administer IV diazepam 0.2 to 0.4 mg/kg stat.
        - THEN administer IV phenytoin 10 – 20 mg/kg stat.
        - THEN administer IV phenytoin 5 mg/kg/day in divided doses.
    - Manage increased intracranial tension:
        - Position patient with head elevated at 15 – 30°.
        - Restrict intravenous fluids to 2/3 of maintenance requirement.
        - Administer IV mannitol 20% 5 ml/kg over 10 – 15 mins.
        - THEN administer IV mannitol 20% 3 ml/kg every 6 hours for 48 hours.
        - THEN administer IV mannitol 20% SOS if increased intracranial tension persists.
        - OR administer glycerine 1 ml/kg/day through feeding tube if increased intracranial tension persists.
    - Control fever (do not give aspirin).
    - Initial IV fluid: Two thirds of the maintenance requirement.
    - Feeding: Keep patient NPO for the first 24 – 48 hours. Later, guide feeding by level of sensorium. Use tube feeding if necessary.

2.  **Antibiotic Therapy (Empirical, pending culture results):**
    - Administer parenteral antibiotics immediately if bacterial cause is suspected.
    - Choice of antibiotics depends on patient age and local prevalence.

3.  **Age-Specific Antibiotic Regimens:**

    - **IF Age is 0-3 months THEN:**
        - Administer IV cefotaxime 200 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 14 days.
        - AND Administer IV ampicillin 300 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 14 days.

    - **IF Age is 3 months - 12 years THEN:**
        - Administer IV ceftriaxone 100 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses for 10 days.
        - OR Administer IV cefotaxime 200 mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses for 10 days.
        - OR Administer IV ampicillin 300 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 10 days.
        - AND Administer IV chloramphenicol 100 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 10 days.
        - IF *Meningococci* is suspected/isolated THEN Administer IV penicillin G 300,000 - 400,000 IU/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 7-10 days.

4.  **Discharge Advice:**
    - Advise regular follow-up for neurological assessment, including deafness.
    - Continue anticonvulsant therapy if seizures are recurrent during meningitis course.
    - Children with sequelae require assessment of handicap and multidisciplinary management.
    - Teach physiotherapy during hospital stay if appropriate.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Aspirin should NOT be given for fever control.
