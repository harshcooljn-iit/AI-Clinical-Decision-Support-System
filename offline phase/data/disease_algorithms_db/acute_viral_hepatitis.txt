DISEASE: Acute Viral Hepatitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- mg: milligram
- kg: kilogram
- tab: tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation (prodrome: fever, malaise, nausea, emesis, anorexia, abdominal discomfort; jaundice).
- History of exposure (faeco-oral route for A/E, blood products for B/C).
- Liver function tests (implied, but not explicitly stated in the provided text).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Acute Viral Hepatitis:

    # Non-pharmacological treatment
    THEN:
        IF Patient feels exhausted or fatigued:
            THEN: Recommend rest.
        ENDIF
        Recommend regular small frequent meals with high caloric content.
        Recommend hygienic high carbohydrate diets.
        Maintain adequate hydration, especially in case of vomiting.
        Avoid fatty meals.

    # Pharmacological treatment
    IF Patient has frequent vomiting:
        THEN: Administer oral metoclopramide 0.1 mg/kg/dose as needed, but not before 6 hours.
    ENDIF

    IF Patient has fever:
        THEN:
            IF Fever abates after jaundice appears:
                THEN: No specific treatment needed.
            ELSEIF Situation requires fever management:
                THEN: Use paracetamol sparingly.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

    # Hospitalization criteria
    IF Patient exhibits any of the following:
        - Alteration in sleep pattern
        - Altered behaviour
        - Abnormal movements
        - Persistent vomiting
        - Dehydration
        - Decreased urinary output
        - Bleeding from any site
        - Any other complication
    THEN:
        Recommend hospitalization.
    ENDIF

    # Patient Education
    THEN:
        Recommend continuing breastfeeding or regular feeding.
        Advise careful observation for danger signs (listed above).
        Inform patient that the disease is usually self-limiting and fever subsides after jaundice appears.
        Inform patient that recovery typically starts in 7-14 days, with a total duration of illness around 3 weeks.
        Inform patient about the differences between Hepatitis A and B.
        Inform patient about Hepatitis A transmission routes (contaminated food/water, close contact).
        Inform patient about Hepatitis A sources (raw/undercooked food handled by infected individuals).

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- None specified for the mentioned drugs (metoclopramide, paracetamol) in the context of acute viral hepatitis, but standard contraindications apply (e.g., hypersensitivity, severe liver disease for paracetamol in high doses).
