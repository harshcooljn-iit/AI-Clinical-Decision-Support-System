ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- SC: Subcutaneous
- OD: Once daily
- HS: Half-strength (often implies twice daily or bedtime, context dependent)

- mg: Milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on transient erythematous wheals, possibly with burning/stinging sensation, and/or angioedema.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Urticaria is acute (duration < 6 weeks) AND with/without angioedema:
    THEN Administer Inj. chlorpheniramine maleate.
    AND Administer Inj. dexamethasone IV or IM.
ELSE IF Urticaria is chronic (duration > 6 weeks) AND with/without angioedema:
    THEN Administer Tab. levocetrizine 5mg OD.
    AND/OR Administer Tab. chlorpheniramine maleate 4mg HS.
ELSE IF Laryngopharyngeal edema is present:
    THEN Administer Inj. epinephrine 0.5 to 1 ml SC immediately.
    AND Administer Inj. hydrocortisone 100mg IV immediately.
