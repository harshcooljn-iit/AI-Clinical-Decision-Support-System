```text
DISEASE: Acute Sinusitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- WBC: White Blood Cell count
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- ENT: Ear, Nose, and Throat
- URI: Upper Respiratory Infection
- OTC: Over-the-counter
- NSAID: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug
- HCL: Hydrochloric Acid

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical symptoms (headache, nasal obstruction, hawking, postnasal drip, fever, malaise, congested nasal mucosa, pus in middle meatus, tender sinuses).
- Investigations: X-ray (Water's view - haziness/opacity/fluid level), CT scan (selected cases), Transillumination test (opaque sinuses).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Acute Sinusitis THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    Steam inhalation via nose 2-3 times/day for 2-3 days.
    Rest.

    // Pharmacological treatment - Pain/Fever
    IF Patient is an adult THEN
        Tab. paracetamol 500 mg 3-4 times a day for 5 days.
    ELSE IF Patient is a child (weight >= 10 kg) THEN
        Tab. paracetamol 500 mg 3-4 times a day for 5 days (10 kg/dose).
    ENDIF

    IF Patient is an adult THEN
        Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg - 600 mg 3 times a day for 5 days.
    ELSE IF Patient is a child (weight >= 10 kg) THEN
        Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg - 600 mg 3 times a day for 5 days (10 mg/kg/dose).
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment - Antibiotics (General)
    Cap. amoxicillin 500 mg 8 hourly for 5-7 days.
    IF Patient is a child THEN
        Cap. amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day for 5-7 days.
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment - Sinusitis of dental origin
    IF Sinusitis is of dental origin THEN
        Cap. amoxicillin 500 mg 3 times a day for 5-7 days.
        Tab. metronidazole 400 mg 3 times a day for 5-7 days.
        Tab. bromhexine 8 mg 3 times a day for 7 days.
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment - Nasal Obstruction/Rhinorrhoea
    IF Patient has nasal obstruction or rhinorrhoea THEN
        // Saline
        Normal saline nasal drops 1-2 drops in each nostril 2-3 times a day.

        // OR Ephedrine
        IF Patient is an adult THEN
            Ephedrine 0.75% nasal drops in isotonic saline 1-2 drops in each nostril 2 times a day.
        ELSE IF Patient is a child THEN
            Ephedrine 0.5% nasal drops 1-2 drops in each nostril 2 times daily.
        ENDIF

        // OR Oxymetazoline HCl
        IF Patient is an adult THEN
            Oxymetazoline HCl 0.05% nasal drops 1-2 drops in each nostril 2 times a day.
        ELSE IF Patient is a child THEN
            Oxymetazoline HCl 0.025% nasal drops 1-2 drops in each nostril 2 times daily.
        ENDIF

        // OR Xylometazoline
        IF Patient is an adult THEN
            Xylometazoline 0.1% nasal drops 2-3 drops in each nostril 2-3 times a day.
        ELSE IF Patient is a child THEN
            Xylometazoline 0.05% nasal drops 1-2 drops in each nostril 2 times daily.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    // Improvement Check
    IF Symptoms (pain, tenderness, nasal obstruction, discharge) do not improve within 48-72 hours THEN
        Refer patient to an Otolaryngologist.
    ENDIF

    // Patient Education
    Advise patient to take the full course of systemic antibiotics.
    Advise patient to avoid prolonged use (more than 7 days) of topical decongestants.

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Medicated nasal drops: Avoid use for more than 7 days due to risk of atrophic rhinitis, anosmia, and rhinitis medicamentosa.
```
