DISEASE: Hydrocele

ABBREVIATIONS:
- None found in the provided text.

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Physical examination: Translucent swelling, possible to get above swelling. Testis may be palpable.
- Ultrasound scan: Necessary to visualize testis if hydrocele sac is tense; used to exclude testicular malignancy.
- History: Age of onset (congenital vs. acquired), associated symptoms (painlessness).
- Physical Exam Findings: Swelling moves downwards and becomes less mobile if the testis is pulled gently downwards.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF the hydrocele is congenital THEN
    Perform herniotomy.
ELSE IF the hydrocele is acquired THEN
    Perform Jaboulay's procedure OR Lord's procedure.
ENDIF
