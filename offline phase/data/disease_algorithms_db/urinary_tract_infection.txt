```text
DISEASE: Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- UTI: Urinary Tract Infection
- BP: Blood Pressure
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- G-6PD: Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase
- MCU: Micturating Cystourethrogram
- DMSA: Dimercaptosuccinic acid
- USG: Ultrasonography
- G-6PD deficiency: Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase deficiency

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical symptoms (fever, loin pain, vomiting, dysuria).
- Urine culture and sensitivity testing.
- Physical examination (checking BP, examining genitals, spine).
- Imaging studies (USG, MCU, DMSA scan) for recurrent or high-risk cases.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Patient Age and Severity Assessment:**
    *   IF Patient age is less than 3 months AND patient is systemically toxic (high fever, loin pain, vomiting) AND has underlying structural abnormality of urinary tract:
        *   THEN Treat as inpatient with parenteral antibiotics.
    *   ELSE:
        *   THEN Treat as outpatient with oral antibiotics.

2.  **Initial Antibiotic Choice:**
    *   IF Local culture and sensitivity report is available:
        *   THEN Guide initial antibiotic choice based on report.
    *   ELSE:
        *   THEN Choose empirical antibiotic based on local resistance patterns and patient factors.

3.  **Duration of Therapy:**
    *   IF Patient has complicated UTI (infants, children):
        *   THEN Duration of therapy is 10-14 days.
    *   ELSE IF Patient has uncomplicated UTI:
        *   THEN Duration of therapy is 7-10 days.
    *   ELSE IF Patient is an adolescent with cystitis:
        *   THEN Duration of therapy may be shorter.

4.  **Supportive Care:**
    *   Maintain adequate hydration with oral or IV fluids as required.
    *   Use analgesics and antipyretics as needed.
    *   IF Patient has obstructive uropathy:
        *   THEN Urgent drainage may be needed.

5.  **Specific Oral Antibiotic Dosages (Pediatric Conditions):**
    *   IF Medication is Cefixime:
        *   THEN Dose is 8-10 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses.
    *   IF Medication is Amoxicillin:
        *   THEN Dose is 30-50 mg/kg/day of amoxicillin in 3 divided doses.
    *   IF Medication is Ciprofloxacin:
        *   THEN Dose is 10-20 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses. (Note: Avoid as first line, use based on culture/sensitivity).
    *   IF Medication is Ofloxacin:
        *   THEN Dose is 15-20 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses. (Note: Avoid as first line, use based on culture/sensitivity).
    *   IF Medication is Cephalexin:
        *   THEN Dose is 50-70 mg/kg/day in 2-3 divided doses.

6.  **Specific Parenteral Antibiotic Dosages:**
    *   IF Medication is Cefriaxone:
        *   THEN Dose is 75-100 mg/kg/day in 1-2 divided doses IV.
    *   IF Medication is Cefotaxime:
        *   THEN Dose is 100-150 mg/kg/day in 2-3 divided doses IV.
    *   IF Medication is Amikacin:
        *   THEN Dose is 10-15 mg/kg/day single dose IV or IM.
    *   IF Medication is Gentamicin:
        *   THEN Dose is 5-6 mg/kg/day single dose IV or IM.
    *   IF Medication is Comaxiclav:
        *   THEN Dose is 30-35 mg/kg/day of amoxicillin in 2 divided doses IV.

7.  **Monitoring and Follow-up (High Risk Patients):**
    *   IF Patient fails to show clinical response (reduction of fever and toxicity) within 48 hours:
        *   THEN Perform abdominal USG and repeat urine culture.
    *   IF Patient has more than one episode of UTI:
        *   THEN Work up for cause of recurrent UTI (USG, MCU, DMSA scan).

8.  **Antibiotic Prophylaxis Indications:**
    *   IF Patient is less than 1 year old with UTI while awaiting imaging studies:
        *   THEN Consider antibiotic prophylaxis.
    *   IF Patient has Vesico urethral reflux:
        *   THEN Consider antibiotic prophylaxis.
    *   IF Patient has frequent febrile UTI even with normal urinary tract:
        *   THEN Consider antibiotic prophylaxis.

9.  **Antibiotic Prophylaxis Contraindications:**
    *   IF Patient has urinary tract obstruction:
        *   THEN Do NOT advise antibiotic prophylaxis.
    *   IF Patient has urolithiasis:
        *   THEN Do NOT advise antibiotic prophylaxis.
    *   IF Patient has neurogenic bladder:
        *   THEN Do NOT advise antibiotic prophylaxis.
    *   IF Patient is an inpatient on clean intermittent catheterization:
        *   THEN Do NOT advise antibiotic prophylaxis.

10. **Specific Prophylactic Antibiotic Dosages:**
    *   IF Drug is Cotrimoxazole:
        *   THEN Dose is 1-2 mg/kg/day (trimethoprim).
        *   IF Patient age is less than 3 months OR Patient has G-6PD deficiency:
            *   THEN Do NOT use Cotrimoxazole.
    *   IF Drug is Nitrofurantoin:
        *   THEN Dose is 1-2 mg/kg/day.
        *   IF Patient age is less than 3 months OR Patient has G-6PD deficiency OR Patient has renal insufficiency:
            *   THEN Do NOT use Nitrofurantoin.
            *   THEN Note: May cause gastrointestinal upset.
    *   IF Drug is Cephalexin:
        *   THEN Dose is 10 mg/kg/day.
        *   THEN Note: Drug of choice in first 3-6 months of life.

11. **Long-term Follow-up:**
    *   IF Patient has recurrent UTI, renal scars, or risk factors:
        *   THEN Advise long-term monitoring.
        *   THEN Advise plenty of oral fluids, regular voiding, avoid constipation, maintain perianal hygiene.
        *   THEN Advise checking a clean midstream urine if recurrence of fever/symptoms occurs.
```
