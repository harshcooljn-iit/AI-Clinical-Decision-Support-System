```text
DISEASE: Hypothyroidism
ABBREVIATIONS:
- TSH: Thyroid stimulating hormone
- T4: Thyroxine
- T3: Triiodothyronine
- mcg/kg/day: Micrograms per kilogram per day
- L-thyroxine: Levothyroxine

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Diagnosis is based on demonstration of low serum T4.
- In primary hypothyroidism, TSH is elevated.
- Radionuclide scans are not essential but help delineate etiology.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF Patient is diagnosed with Hypothyroidism THEN
    - Start Thyroxin (L-thyroxine) treatment as soon as diagnosis is confirmed.

    - Determine initial dose based on age:
        IF Age is 0-6 months THEN
            - Initial dose: 8-15 mcg/kg/day
        ELSE IF Age is 6-12 months THEN
            - Initial dose: 7-10 mcg/kg/day
        ELSE IF Age is 1-5 years THEN
            - Initial dose: 5-7 mcg/kg/day
        ELSE IF Age is 5-10 years THEN
            - Initial dose: 3-5 mcg/kg/day
        ELSE IF Age is 10-12 years THEN
            - Initial dose: 2-4 mcg/kg/day
        ENDIF

    - Follow up:
        - Clinically every month for 6 months.
        - Clinically every 3 months until 2 years.
        - Clinically once or twice every year thereafter.
        - Periodic thyroid function tests (e.g., every 6 months).

    - Monitor for symptoms:
        - Under treatment: lethargy, constipation, weight gain.
        - Overdosing: diarrhoea, palpitations, increased appetite, weight loss.
        - Raised intracranial tension (headache, vomiting): Requires immediate admission and treatment.

    - Maintain serum T4 level in the upper normal range.
    - Maintain TSH levels suppressed to normal.

    - Patient Education:
        - Need for lifelong administration.
        - Importance of regular follow-up for monitoring and dose titration.
        - Clinical symptoms of under/overdosage and pseudotumour cerebri.
        - Administration instructions for infants: Crush tablet with milk/water, use teaspoon/syringe. DO NOT add to bottle. Repeat dose if vomiting/regurgitation occurs immediately.

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for L-thyroxine in hypothyroidism treatment, but general contraindications for thyroid hormone therapy might include untreated adrenal insufficiency or severe cardiac disease, which would need separate assessment).
```
