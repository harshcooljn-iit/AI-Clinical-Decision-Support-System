DISEASE: Hyperthyroidism

ABBREVIATIONS:
- TSH: Thyroid Stimulating Hormone
- T4: Thyroxine

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Salient features: Tiredness, emotional lability, weight loss, excessive appetite, palpitation, tachycardia, hot moist palms, exopthalmos, lid lag/retraction, agitation, thyroid goitre and bruit.
- Investigations: Thyroid function test (normal T4, suppressed TSH), thyroid scan.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Non pharmacological treatment is chosen:
    THEN Rest and sedation.

IF Pharmacological treatment is chosen:
    THEN Administer Tab. carbimazole.
    IF Patient is not euthyroid:
        THEN Administer Tab. carbimazole 10 mg four times a day.
    ELSE IF Patient is euthyroid:
        THEN Administer Tab. carbimazole 5 mg two or three times a day for 6-24 months (maintenance dose).

IF Surgical treatment is chosen:
    THEN Determine the type of goiter/thyroid condition.
    IF Large toxic multinoduler goiter:
        THEN Perform total thyroidectomy.
    ELSE IF Large diffuse toxic goiter:
        THEN Promote elective surgery after a course of treatment with antithyroid drugs and beta blocker.
        THEN Perform subtotal thyroidectomy.
    ELSE IF Toxic solitary nodular goitre OR toxic adenoma OR autonomous hot nodule:
        THEN Perform hemithyroidectomy.
    ELSE IF Thyrotoxicosis in pregnancy:
        THEN Perform subtotal thyroidectomy in mid trimester.
    ELSE IF Hashimoto’s disease OR lymphedenoid goiter:
        THEN Perform total thyroidectomy or total lobectomy.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(None specified in the provided text for specific drugs, but general surgical contraindications apply based on patient condition).
