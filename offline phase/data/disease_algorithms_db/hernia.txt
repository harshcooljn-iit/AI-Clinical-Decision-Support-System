DISEASE: Hernia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- USG: Ultrasound
- KUB: Kidney, Ureter, Bladder
- PSA: Prostate-Specific Antigen
- BPH: Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia
- TEP: Totally Extra Peritoneal meshplasty
- TAPP: Trans Abdominal Preperitoneal meshplasty

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on history and physical examination (groin swelling, reducible, impulse on coughing).
- Investigations (USG, PSA, etc.) may be used if diagnosis is inconclusive or to evaluate associated conditions.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

I. INGUINAL HERNIA

A.  PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT:
    IF predisposing factors are present THEN
        TREAT predisposing factors.
    ENDIF
    IF chronic cough is present THEN
        TREAT chronic cough according to cause.
    ENDIF
    IF chronic constipation is present THEN
        TREAT chronic constipation.
    ENDIF
    IF BPH is present THEN
        TREAT BPH (pharmacological or surgical).
    ENDIF

B.  SURGICAL TREATMENT:
    IF hernia is congenital THEN
        Herniotomy.
    ELSE
        Hernioplasty (Lichtenstein tension-free hernioplasty is the open gold standard).
        IF hernia is small and/or bilateral THEN
            Laparoscopic hernioplasty is preferred.
            IF TEP (Totally Extra Peritoneal meshplasty) is suitable THEN
                Perform TEP.
            ELSE IF TAPP (Trans Abdominal Preperitoneal meshplasty) is suitable THEN
                Perform TAPP.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

II. INCISIONAL HERNIA

A.  INVESTIGATIONS:
    - USG local part for defect.
    - Preoperative investigations as needed.

B.  SURGICAL TREATMENT:
    - Mesh placement techniques:
        IF Underlay (preperitoneal mesh placement under the defect) is chosen THEN
            Perform Underlay. This is the preferred route.
        ELSE IF Inlay (mesh placement through the defect) is chosen THEN
            Perform Inlay.
        ELSE IF Onlay/Overlay (mesh placement above the defect in muscular plane) is chosen THEN
            Perform Onlay/Overlay.
        ENDIF
    - Laparoscopic mesh placement under the defect is preferred whenever possible.
