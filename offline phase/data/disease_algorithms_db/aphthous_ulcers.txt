ABBREVIATIONS:
- mg: milligram
- kg: kilogram
- Tab.: Tablet
- hydrocortisone: hydrocortisone
- prednisolone: prednisolone

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on recurrent, painful, rounded ulcers with a yellowish-gray fibrinoid center.
- Rule out secondary causes (malabsorption syndrome, inflammatory bowel disease, Behcet's disease, recurrent trauma).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has aphthous ulcers THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    BEGIN
        Maintain good oral hygiene.
        Rinse mouth repeatedly with plain water, especially after eating.
    END

    // Pharmacological treatment - Symptomatic
    BEGIN
        Apply a gel containing a local anesthetic before meals for symptomatic relief.
    END

    // Pharmacological treatment - Severe cases (large, multiple ulcers)
    IF ulcers are severe (large, multiple) THEN
        // Option 1: Hydrocortisone pellets
        BEGIN
            Administer hydrocortisone pellets (5 mg) to be kept on the ulcer and sucked every 4 hours for 3-5 days.
        END
        // Option 2: Prednisolone tablets
        ELSE IF ulcers are severe (large, multiple) THEN
        BEGIN
            Administer Tab. prednisolone at a dose of 0.5 mg/kg/day in a single dose for 3-5 days.
        END
    END
END

- (None specified in the text for hydrocortisone or prednisolone in this context, but standard contraindications apply based on patient factors not mentioned here, e.g., allergy, infection, etc.)
