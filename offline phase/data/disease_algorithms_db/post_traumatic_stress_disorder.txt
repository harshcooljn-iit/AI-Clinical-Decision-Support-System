DISEASE: Post Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- SSRIs: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Exposure to a traumatic event (direct experience or witnessing).
- Presence of symptoms (re-experiencing, avoidance, hyperarousal, detachment/numbing) for more than 1 month.
- If symptoms persist for less than 4 weeks, it is Acute Stress Disorder.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - IF patient has symptoms consistent with PTSD for > 1 month: Proceed to treatment options.
    - ELSE IF patient has symptoms consistent with PTSD for < 4 weeks: Diagnose Acute Stress Disorder. Consider non-pharmacological treatment.
    - ELSE: Rule out PTSD.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF patient is suitable for psychotherapy:
        - THEN recommend Focused Cognitive Behavior Therapy (psychoeducation, relaxation techniques, cognitive coping).
        - THEN consider Gradual Exposure therapy.
        - THEN consider Crisis Intervention/Psychological Debriefing.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF patient requires pharmacological treatment (e.g., symptoms severe, psychotherapy ineffective/unavailable):
        - THEN consider SSRIs as agents of choice.
            - IF SSRI is chosen:
                - THEN prescribe SSRI at doses typically used for depression.
        - THEN consider Propranolol for symptomatic treatment.
