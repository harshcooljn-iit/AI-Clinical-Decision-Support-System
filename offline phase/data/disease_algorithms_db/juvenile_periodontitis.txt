```text
1.  DISEASE: Juvenile Periodontitis

2.  ABBREVIATIONS:
    *   Cap: Capsule
    *   mg: milligram
    *   tab: tablet

3.  DIAGNOSTICS:
    *   Rapid destruction of periodontal tissues.
    *   Common in age group 13-25 years.
    *   Mobility in incisors and molars.
    *   Spacing in upper incisors.
    *   Distolabial migration of upper incisors.
    *   Arc-shaped bone loss (distal surface of second premolar to mesial surface of second molar).

4.  TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    IF Patient has Juvenile Periodontitis THEN
        // Pharmacological Treatment
        IF Patient is eligible for tetracycline treatment THEN
            Administer Cap. tetracycline 250 mg four times a day for 14 days.
        ENDIF

        // Surgical Treatment
        IF Patient has badly involved teeth THEN
            Extract badly involved teeth.
            Refer patient to a periodontist for further periodontical management.
        ENDIF

        // Patient Education
        Instruct patient on proper brushing technique twice daily using a super soft toothbrush.
    ENDIF

5.  TABULAR DOSAGES: Not applicable in this guideline.

6.  CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    *   Tetracycline is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to tetracyclines, pregnant women, and nursing mothers. (This is a general contraindication for tetracycline, not explicitly stated in the provided text but standard knowledge).
```
