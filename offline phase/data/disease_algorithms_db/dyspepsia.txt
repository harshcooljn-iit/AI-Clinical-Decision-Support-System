DISEASE: Dyspepsia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Cap.: Capsule
- Tab.: Tablet
- H.pylori: Helicobacter pylori
- NSAIDs: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs
- PPI: Proton Pump Inhibitor

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Non-specific group of upper gastrointestinal symptoms (e.g., epigastric burning pain, early satiety, postprandial fullness, hiccups, halitosis).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has dyspepsia symptoms:
    // Initial Pharmacological Treatment
    IF patient requires initial treatment:
        THEN administer EITHER:
            IF patient prefers omeprazole:
                THEN administer Cap. omeprazole 20 mg once a day 45 minutes before breakfast for 4 to 6 weeks.
            ELSE IF patient prefers ranitidine:
                THEN administer Tab. ranitidine 150 mg twice a day 45 minutes before breakfast and dinner for 4 to 6 weeks.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

    // Symptomatic Relief (Antacids)
    IF patient has symptoms despite initial medication:
        THEN administer Antacids 2 to 3 teaspoon or 2 tabs (chewable) when symptomatic.

    // Treatment for Dysmotility Symptoms
    IF patient has dysmotility symptoms:
        THEN administer Tab. domperidone 10 mg three times a day 30 minutes before breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

    // Duration of Treatment
    IF treatment duration is needed:
        THEN treatment duration can be:
            - Short courses (4 to 6 weeks), repeated if necessary.
            - Long-term treatment (up to 1 year).
            - Intermittent therapy or biweekly PPI for long-term treatment.
        ENDIF

    // Anti-H.pylori Treatment Indication
    IF patient is on long-term NSAIDs OR has Duodenal/gastric ulcers (complicated, e.g., bleeding):
        THEN administer Anti-_H.pylori_ treatment:
            - Combination therapy:
                - Cap. omeprazole 20 mg twice a day
                - Cap. amoxicillin 500 mg thrice a day
                - Tab. metronidazole 400 mg thrice a day
            - Followed by: Cap. omeprazole 20 mg once a day for three weeks.
            - Duration: Anti-_H.pylori_ regimen should be taken for at least 14 days.
        ENDIF

// Patient Education
    // Lifestyle Modifications
    IF patient requires education:
        THEN advise:
            - Avoid excess tea, coffee, fried food, alcohol, and smoking.
            - Avoid unnecessary NSAIDs; prefer paracetamol, especially with ulcer-like symptoms or documented ulcers.
            - Follow meals at regular intervals (e.g., 4 hourly, including snacks).
            - Daily exercise to maintain optimum weight.
        ENDIF
