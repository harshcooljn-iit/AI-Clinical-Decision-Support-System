DISEASE: Pemphigus Vulgaris
ABBREVIATIONS:
    - OD: Once daily
    - BD: Twice daily
    - QID: Four times daily
    - mg: Milligram
    - kg: Kilogram
    - bw: Body weight
    - Tab.: Tablet
    - Inj.: Injection
    - SOS: As needed (stat order set)

DIAGNOSTICS:
    - Clinical examination
    - Tzanck smear (demonstrates acantholysis)
    - Biopsy
    - Anti-desmoglein autoantibodies test

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
    IF Pemphigus Vulgaris is diagnosed THEN
        # Non-pharmacological treatment
        Hospitalize the patient.
        Provide fluid replacement.

        # Pharmacological treatment (only at tertiary care centre)
        # Oral antibiotics for infection prevention
        IF infection is present THEN
            IF culture sensitivity indicates suitability THEN
                Administer Tab. azithromycin 500 mg OD for 5 days OR
                Administer Tab. cotrimoxazole 2 BD for 7 days
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        # IV corticosteroids
        Administer IV dexamethasone at a dose of 1 mg/kg to 2 mg/kg body weight.
    ENDIF

DISEASE: Cutaneous Adverse Drug Reactions
ABBREVIATIONS:
    - OD: Once daily
    - BD: Twice daily
    - mg: Milligram
    - kg: Kilogram
    - Tab.: Tablet
    - Inj.: Injection
    - SOS: As needed (stat order set)

DIAGNOSTICS:
    - Clinical examination
    - Complete blood count (for Erythema Multiforme)
    - Liver function tests (for Erythema Multiforme)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
    IF Cutaneous Adverse Drug Reaction is diagnosed THEN
        # I. Maculopapular Rash
        IF Maculopapular Rash is diagnosed THEN
            # Non-pharmacological treatment
            Stop the causal drug.

            # Pharmacological treatment
            Administer Topical calamine lotion.
            Administer Tab. cetirizine 10 mg/day.
        ENDIF

        # II. Erythema Multiforme
        IF Erythema Multiforme is diagnosed THEN
            # Non-pharmacological treatment
            Discontinue the causal drug.

            # Pharmacological treatment
            Administer Topical antiseptics for eroded skin lesions.
            Administer Antiseptic and local anaesthetic solutions for oral lesions (if herpes simplex).
            Administer Topical ophthalmic preparations (normal saline or lubricating eye drops) for eye lesions.
            Administer Tab. cetirizine 10 mg or Tab. chlorpheniramine 4 mg for 3 or 4 days.
            Administer Tab. prednisone 0.5–1 mg/kg/day.
        ENDIF

        # III. Fixed Drug Eruption
        IF Fixed Drug Eruption is diagnosed THEN
            # Non-pharmacological treatment
            Stop the offending drug.
            Enlist the drugs to be avoided.

            # Pharmacological treatment
            Administer Topical calamine lotion.
            Administer Tab. cetirizine 10 mg/day or Tab. chlorpheniramine 4 mg/day.
            IF Generalized bullous FDE is present THEN
                Administer Tab. prednisolone 0.5-1 mg/kg/day.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        # IV. Stevens Johnson Syndrome/Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis
        IF Stevens Johnson Syndrome OR Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis is diagnosed THEN
            # Non-pharmacological treatment
            Withdraw offending drug.
            Provide oral liquid diet with protein supplements.
            Maintain oral hygiene with antiseptic mouth washes.
            Maintain optimum temperature of 30-32° C.

            # Pharmacological treatment (tertiary care centre)
            Hospitalize the patient.
            Manage fluid and electrolytes.
            Implement measures to prevent secondary infections.
            Administer Ophthalmic care (saline or antibiotic eye drops).
            Administer Systemic antibiotics to prevent infection according to culture sensitivity:
                - Macrolides: Tab. azithromycin 500 mg OD for 3/5 days OR
                - Tab. erythromycin 500 mg QID for 5/7 days.
            Administer Tab. prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day OR
            Administer parentral Inj. dexamethasone 8-16 mg/day.
            # Corticosteroid administration details
            IF Corticosteroids are administered THEN
                Start within 72 hours of onset.
                Continue for 3-5 days.
                Perform rapid tapering.
            ENDIF
            Administer Tab. cyclosporine 3-5 mg/kg/day for two weeks followed by weaning over another two weeks SOS.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    - Specific contraindications are not listed in the provided text. General contraindications for the listed drugs (e.g., allergies, specific conditions) would apply based on standard medical practice.
