DISEASE: Diabetic Ketoacidosis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- DKA: Diabetic Ketoacidosis
- IV: Intravenous
- mg/dl: milligrams per deciliter
- mEq/L: milliequivalents per liter
- OHA: Oral Hypoglycemic Agents
- SC: Subcutaneous
- CXR: Chest X-ray
- ECG: Electrocardiogram
- CHD: Coronary Heart Disease
- LDL: Low-Density Lipoprotein
- HDL: High-Density Lipoprotein
- ARBs: Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers
- ACE: Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme
- SSRI: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor
- OHA: Oral Hypoglycemic Agent
- SC: Subcutaneous
- CVD: Cardiovascular Disease

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Hyperglycemia
- Ketosis (positive serum ketones or ketones in urine)
- Metabolic acidosis (low arterial pH, low bicarbonate >15 mmol/l, high anion gap >15 mmol/l)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  ADMISSION:
    - Admit to hospital.
    - Consider intensive care if pH < 7.00 or patient is unconscious.
    - If vomiting or altered mental status, insert nasogastric tube.

2.  ASSESSMENT:
    - Assess serum electrolytes (K+, Na+, Mg2+, Cl–, bicarbonate, phosphate).
    - Assess acid-base status (pH, HCO3–, PCO2).
    - Assess beta-hydroxybutyrate or serum acetone levels.
    - Assess renal function (creatinine, urine output).
    - Initiate workup for precipitating event (cultures, CXR, ECG).

3.  FLUID REPLACEMENT:
    - Initial: 2–3 L of 0.9% saline over first 1–3 hr (15–20 ml/kg per hour).
    - Subsequent: 0.45% saline at 250–500 ml/h.
    - Change to 5% glucose and 0.45% saline at 150–250 ml/h when plasma glucose reaches 200 mg/dl.
    - Monitor closely in elderly or those with cardiac/renal compromise.

4.  INSULIN ADMINISTRATION:
    - Administer short-acting insulin IV.
    - Initial bolus: 0.1 units/kg.
    - Continuous IV infusion: 0.1 units/kg per hour.
    - Increase infusion rate two- to threefold if no response by 2–4 hours.

5.  POTASSIUM (K+) REPLACEMENT:
    - IF initial serum K+ < 3.3 mEq/L THEN do not administer insulin until K+ is corrected.
    - IF initial serum K+ > 5.2 mEq/L THEN do not supplement K+ until K+ is corrected.
    - IF plasma K+ < 5.0–5.2 mEq/L AND ECG is normal AND urine flow is normal AND creatinine is normal THEN administer 10 meq/h (or 20–30 mEq/L of infusion fluid).
    - IF plasma K+ < 3.5 mEq/L OR bicarbonate is given THEN administer 40–80 mEq/h.
    - Goal: Maintain serum K+ > 3.5 mEq/L.

6.  BICARBONATE REPLACEMENT:
    - IF arterial pH < 6.9 THEN administer sodium bicarbonate [50 mEq/L in 200 ml sterile water with 10 mEq/L KCl] per hour for 2 hours until pH > 7.0.
    - ELSE do not administer bicarbonate.

7.  PHOSPHATE AND MAGNESIUM REPLACEMENT:
    - IF serum phosphate < 1 mg/dl THEN consider phosphate supplement and monitor serum calcium.
    - IF hypomagnesemia develops THEN consider magnesium supplementation.

8.  MONITORING:
    - Measure capillary glucose every 1–2 hours.
    - Measure electrolytes (especially K+, bicarbonate, phosphate) and anion gap every 4 hours for the first 24 hours.
    - Monitor blood pressure, pulse, respirations, mental status, fluid intake, and output every 1–4 hours.

9.  TREATMENT CONTINUATION:
    - Continue treatment until patient is stable, glucose goal is 150–250 mg/dl, and acidosis is resolved.
    - May decrease insulin infusion to 0.05–0.1 units/kg per hour.
    - Administer long-acting insulin as soon as patient is eating, allowing for overlap with IV insulin infusion and SC insulin injection.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Insulin administration IF initial serum K+ < 3.3 mEq/L.
- K+ supplementation IF initial serum K+ > 5.2 mEq/L.
