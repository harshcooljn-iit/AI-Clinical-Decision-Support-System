DISEASE: Bradyarrhythmias

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IV: Intravenous
- mcg/min: micrograms per minute
- Inj.: Injection

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Sinus bradycardia: Regular heart rate < 60 beats per minute.
- Types include sinoatrial block and atrioventricular block.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Symptomatic Bradyarrhythmia THEN
    IF Atropine is available and indicated THEN
        Administer Inj. atropine 0.6 mg IV.
        IF Heart rate does not increase sufficiently THEN
            Repeat Inj. atropine 0.6 mg IV every 4-6 hours.
    ELSE IF Isoproterenol is available and indicated THEN
        Administer Inj. isoproterenol infusion at 2-10 mcg/min.
    ENDIF
ELSE
    Consider Temporary pacemaker.
    IF Temporary pacemaker is insufficient or contraindicated THEN
        Consider permanent pacemaker insertion.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for specific drugs, but general contraindications for atropine/isoproterenol/pacemakers would apply in a real clinical setting, e.g., atropine in certain types of heart block, isoproterenol in certain ischemic conditions, pacemakers in certain infection scenarios).
