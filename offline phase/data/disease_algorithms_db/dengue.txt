```text
DISEASE: Dengue

ABBREVIATIONS:
- DF: Dengue Fever
- DHF: Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever
- DSS: Dengue Shock Syndrome
- ORS: Oral Rehydration Solution
- BP: Blood Pressure
- PCV: Packed Red Blood Cells
- ml/kg/hr: milliliters per kilogram per hour
- IV: Intravenous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Dengue fever: Acute febrile illness (2-7 days) with headache, retro-orbital pain, myalgia, arthralgia, rash, hemorrhagic manifestations (petechiae, positive tourniquet test), and leucopenia.
- Dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF): Presence of one or more of the following: positive tourniquet test, petechiae/purpura/ecchymosis, bleeding from mucosa, haematemesis/melena, thrombocytopenia (platelets ≤ 100,000 cells/mm³), and evidence of plasma leakage.
- Dengue shock syndrome (DSS): All criteria for DHF plus signs of circulatory failure.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - IF patient has Dengue Fever (DF) symptoms (fever, headache, retro-orbital pain, myalgia, arthralgia, rash, leucopenia, hemorrhagic manifestations):
        - THEN proceed to symptomatic and supportive care.
        - ELSE proceed to DHF/DSS assessment.

2.  **Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) / Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS) Assessment:**
    - IF patient has signs of DHF (positive tourniquet test, petechiae/purpura/ecchymosis, mucosal bleeding, haematemesis/melena, thrombocytopenia ≤ 100,000 cells/mm³, plasma leakage):
        - THEN proceed to DHF management.
        - ELSE proceed to DF management.
    - IF patient has signs of DHF PLUS signs of circulatory failure (shock):
        - THEN diagnose as DSS and proceed to DSS management.

3.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment (for DF, DHF, DSS):**
    - Bed rest is advisable during the acute phase.
    - Use cold sponging to keep temperature below 39ºC.

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment (for DF, DHF, DSS):**
    - Management is symptomatic and supportive.
    - Antipyretics may be used to lower body temperature.
    - AVOID Aspirin/NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen) due to risk of gastritis, vomiting, acidosis, and platelet dysfunction.
    - Paracetamol is preferable.

5.  **Paracetamol Dosage:**
    - IF patient age is 1-2 years:
        - THEN dose is 60 – 120 mg per dose.
    - ELSE IF patient age is 3-6 years:
        - THEN dose is 120 mg per dose.
    - ELSE IF patient age is 7-12 years:
        - THEN dose is 240 mg per dose.
    - ELSE IF patient is an Adult:
        - THEN dose is 500 mg per dose.
    - Note: In children, dose can also be calculated as 10 mg/kg body weight per dose, repeated every 6 hours.

6.  **Fluid Management (for DF, DHF, DSS):**
    - Oral fluid and electrolyte therapy recommended for excessive sweating or vomiting.
    - Oral hydration solution (ORS) or fruit juices preferable to plain water.
    - Monitor patients in DHF endemic areas until afebrile for one day without antipyretics, and after platelet and haematocrit are stable (platelet count > 50,000/mm³).

7.  **Management of Febrile Phase (DHF/DSS):**
    - Similar to DF management.
    - Paracetamol recommended to keep temperature below 39ºC.
    - Give copious amounts of oral fluids (ORS, fruit juices) as tolerated.
    - IF patient is vomiting persistently or refusing to feed:
        - THEN administer IV fluids.
    - Monitor closely for initial signs of shock (critical period: transition from febrile to afebrile phase, usually after day 3).
    - Perform serial haematocrit determinations (daily from day 3 until afebrile for 1-2 days) to guide IV fluid administration.
    - IF haematocrit determination is not possible:
        - THEN perform haemoglobin determination as an alternative.

8.  **IV Fluid Therapy Algorithm (Figure 4):**
    - IF patient has haemorrhagic tendencies (bleeding) AND thrombocytopenia:
        - THEN initiate IV therapy at 6 ml/kg/hr crystalloid solution for 1-2 hours.
        - IF Improvement occurs:
            - THEN continue IV therapy by successively reducing the rate from 6 to 3 ml/kg/hr.
            - IF Further improvement occurs:
                - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.
        - ELSE IF No Improvement occurs:
            - THEN increase IV rate to 10 ml/kg/hr crystalloid solution for 2 hours.
            - IF Improvement occurs after increased rate:
                - THEN continue IV therapy by successively reducing the rate from 6 to 3 ml/kg/hr.
                - IF Further improvement occurs:
                    - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.
            - ELSE IF No Improvement occurs AND unstable vital signs:
                - IF Haematocrit rises:
                    - THEN administer IV colloid (dextran 40) at 10 ml/kg/hr for 1 hour.
                    - IF Improvement occurs after colloid:
                        - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.
                - ELSE IF Haematocrit falls:
                    - THEN administer Blood transfusion at 10 ml/kg/hr.
                    - IF Improvement occurs after transfusion:
                        - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.

9.  **Management of DHF Grade I and II:**
    - Hospitalize patients with DHF Grade I/II (thrombocytopenia, haemoconcentration, abdominal pain, black tarry stools, epistaxis, gum bleeding, infection).
    - Observe for signs of shock (critical period: transition from febrile to afebrile phase, usually after day 3).
    - IF rise in haemoconcentration occurs:
        - THEN initiate IV fluid therapy.
    - IF despite treatment, patient develops fall in BP, decreased urine output, or other features of shock:
        - THEN institute management for Grade III/IV DHF/DSS.
    - Give oral rehydration, antipyretics (paracetamol), and sponging.

10. **Volume Replacement Flow Chart (Figure 5):**
    - IF Unstable Vital Signs AND Urine output falls (signs of shock):
        - THEN initiate immediate rapid volume replacement: IV therapy at 10-20 ml/kg/hr crystalloid solution for 1 hour.
        - IF Improvement occurs:
            - THEN continue IV therapy by successively reducing the rate from 20 to 10, 10 to 6, and 6 to 3 ml/kg/hr.
            - IF Further improvement occurs:
                - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.
        - ELSE IF No Improvement occurs:
            - THEN administer Oxygen.
            - IF Haematocrit rises:
                - THEN administer IV Colloid (Dextran 40) or plasma at 10 ml/kg/hr as an intravenous bolus (repeat if necessary).
                - IF Improvement occurs after colloid/plasma:
                    - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.
            - ELSE IF Haematocrit falls rapidly (due to haemorrhage):
                - THEN administer Blood transfusion at 10 ml/kg/hr.
                - IF Improvement occurs after transfusion:
                    - THEN discontinue IV after 24 hours.

11. **Fluid Requirement Calculation:**
    - The volume of fluid should be sufficient to maintain effective circulation during plasma leakage.
    - Adjust IV fluid rate throughout the 24-48 hour period of plasma leakage based on periodic haematocrit and vital signs.
    - Calculate required regimen based on body weight and chart on a 1-3 hourly basis (or more frequently in shock).

12. **Fluid Requirement based on Weight (Table 5):**
    - IF Weight on admission is < 7 kg:
        - THEN Fluid requirement is 220 ml/kg body weight/day.
    - ELSE IF Weight on admission is 7-11 kg:
        - THEN Fluid requirement is 165 ml/kg body weight/day.
    - ELSE IF Weight on admission is 12-18 kg:
        - THEN Fluid requirement is 130 ml/kg body weight/day.
    - ELSE IF Weight on admission is 18 kg:
        - THEN Fluid requirement is 90 ml/kg body weight/day.

13. **Maintenance Fluid Calculation (for children > 40 kg, Table 6):**
    - IF Body weight is < 10 kg:
        - THEN Maintenance volume for 24 hours is 100 ml/kg.
    - ELSE IF Body weight is 10-20 kg:
        - THEN Maintenance volume for 24 hours is 1000 + 50 ml/kg.
    - ELSE IF Body weight is 20 kg:
        - THEN Maintenance volume for 24 hours is 1500 + 20 ml/kg.
    - For a child weighing 40 kg:
        - Maintenance volume = 1500 + (20 * 20) = 1900 ml.
        - Total IV fluid for 24 hours = 2 * Maintenance volume = 2 * 1900 = 3800 ml.

14. **Indications for Red Cell Transfusion:**
    - Overt blood loss (>10% of total blood volume).
    - Use whole blood/component.
    - Refractory shock despite adequate fluid administration and declining haematocrit (replacement volume: 10 ml/kg body wt at a time, perform coagulogram).
    - IF fluid overload is present:
        - THEN give Packed Red Blood Cells (PCV).

15. **Indications for Platelet Transfusion:**
    - Generally, no need for prophylactic platelets even at < 20,000 cells/mm³.
    - May give prophylactic platelets at < 10,000 cells/mm³ in absence of bleeding.
    - In prolonged shock with coagulopathy and abnormal coagulogram.
    - In case of systemic massive bleeding, may need platelets in addition to red cells.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Aspirin/NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen) should be avoided due to risk of gastritis, vomiting, acidosis, and platelet dysfunction.
```
