DISEASE: Suturing Of Primary Wound

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- IM: Intramuscular
- stat: Immediately
- ml: Milliliter
- mg: Milligram
- tab: Tablet
- MO: Medical Officer
- hrs: Hours
- MO: Medical Officer

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical assessment of wound (time, etiology, comorbidities, number, site, size, depth, active bleeding, discharge, foreign material, associated neurovascular injury, tendon injury, bone fracture).
- X-rays of local part if fracture or foreign body is suspected.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF wound is recent (< 6 hours) AND contamination is minimal:
    THEN Perform cleaning followed by primary suturing.
ELSE IF duration of injury is > 6 hours OR contamination is present:
    THEN Debride the wound.
    THEN Close the wound by secondary suturing at a later date when clean.

PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT:
    Administer Inj. tetanus toxoid 0.5 ml IM stat.
    Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 3 ml (75mg) IM stat.
    Administer Tab. ampicillin 500 mg three times a day for 3-5 days.
    Administer Tab. diclofenac 50 mg twice a day for 3-5 days.
    IF patient is deficient in vitamins/iron:
        THEN Administer Tab. multivitamins/Iron.
    IF patient has Diabetes mellitus:
        THEN Control Diabetes mellitus.

WOUND MANAGEMENT:
    For primary suturing: Thorough cleaning of wound by saline/Hydrogen Peroxide/Betadine.
    Remove any foreign material.
    Control active bleeding.

CHOICE OF SUTURE MATERIAL:
    IF high stress area requiring strong retention (e.g., deep fascia repair):
        THEN Use 1-0 or 2-0 suture material.
    IF area requiring good retention (e.g., scalp, torso, hands):
        THEN Use 3-0 suture material.
    IF area requiring minimal retention (e.g., extremities) OR superficial wound closure:
        THEN Use 4-0 suture material.
    IF area involving the face, nose, ears, eyebrows, eyelids:
        THEN Use 5-0 suture material.
    IF area requiring little or no retention OR cosmetic effect desired:
        THEN Use 6-0 suture material.

SUTURE TECHNIQUE:
    Place wound layers in close approximation.
    Keep minimal tension across the suture line.
    Evert wound edges slightly.
    Use equal bites horizontally and vertically.
    Debride devitalized tissue.
    Perform alternate day dressing with antiseptic solution until suture removal.

SUTURE REMOVAL:
    IF location is Face:
        THEN Remove sutures in 4-5 days.
    IF location is Body & scalp:
        THEN Remove sutures in 7 days.
    IF location is Soles, palms, back or over joints:
        THEN Remove sutures in 10 days.
    IF suture has pus or signs of infection:
        THEN Remove suture immediately.
    Once MO determines removal is appropriate:
        Use suture removal kit (scissors, tooth forceps).
        Grasp knot with tooth forceps.
        Snip suture below knot with scissors as close as possible to the skin.
        Pull suture line through tissue.
        Count removed sutures to match patient record.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- None specified in the text for the listed pharmacological treatments.
- Suture removal IF pus or signs of infection are present.
