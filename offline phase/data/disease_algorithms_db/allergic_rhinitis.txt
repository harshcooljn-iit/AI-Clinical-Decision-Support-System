```text
DISEASE: Allergic Rhinitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IgE: Immunoglobulin E
- SAR: Seasonal Allergic Rhinitis
- PAR: Perennial Allergic Rhinitis
- od: Once daily
- mcg: Micrograms
- mL: Milliliters
- kg: Kilograms
- hr: Hour

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical symptoms (sneezing, itching, watery nasal discharge, nasal obstruction, allergic conjunctivitis, bronchial asthma).
- Peripheral blood eosinophilia.
- Nasal smear showing eosinophilia.
- X-ray of nose and paranasal sinuses.
- Intranasal provocation test.
- Specific IgE antibody test.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Allergic Rhinitis THEN

    # Non-pharmacological treatment
    AVOID allergens.

    # Pharmacological treatment

    # Step 1: Antihistamines
    IF patient is an adult THEN
        THEN administer Cetirizine 10-20 mg once daily for 7 days.
    ELSE IF patient is a child THEN
        THEN administer Cetirizine 5 mg once daily for 7 days.
    ENDIF

    OR

    IF patient is an adult THEN
        THEN administer Chlorpheniramine maleate 4 mg every 6-8 hours for 7 days.
    ELSE IF patient is older than 1 year THEN
        THEN administer Chlorpheniramine maleate 4 mg every 6-8 hours for 7 days.
    ELSE IF patient is less than 1 year old THEN
        THEN DO NOT administer Chlorpheniramine maleate.
    ENDIF

    OR

    IF patient is an adult THEN
        THEN administer Pheniramine maleate 25-50 mg every 8 hours for 7 days.
    ELSE IF patient is a child THEN
        THEN administer Pheniramine maleate 0.5 mg/kg/day divided in 3 doses for 7 days.
    ENDIF

    # Step 2: Nasal Decongestants (for nasal obstruction and rhinorrhoea)
    IF patient has nasal obstruction and rhinorrhoea THEN
        IF patient is an adult THEN
            THEN administer Normal saline nasal drops 1-2 drops in each nostril 2 times daily.
        ENDIF

        OR

        IF patient is an adult THEN
            THEN administer Xylometazoline 0.1% nasal drops 1-2 drops 2-3 times daily for 5-7 days.
        ELSE IF patient is a child THEN
            THEN administer Xylometazoline 0.05% nasal drops 1-2 drops 2 times daily.
        ENDIF

        OR

        IF patient is an adult THEN
            THEN administer Oxymetazoline 0.5% nasal drops 2-3 drops 2-3 times daily for 5-7 days.
        ELSE IF patient is a child THEN
            THEN administer Oxymetazoline 0.025% nasal drops 1-2 drops 2 times in each nostril.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    # Step 3: Nasal Corticosteroids/Antihistamines (for persistent symptoms)
    IF signs and symptoms are persistent THEN
        THEN administer Betamethasone/hydrocortisone nasal drops 2-3 times/day for up to 7 days.

        OR

        THEN administer Beclomethasone inhaler (50 mcg/puff) 2 puffs every 12 hours.

        OR

        THEN administer Budesonide (50-100 mcg/puff) 1-2 puffs a day.

        OR

        THEN administer Fluticasone 150 mcg/puff 1-2 puffs a day.

        OR

        THEN administer Topical azelastine intranasal spray 2-3 times a day.
    ENDIF

    # Step 4: Systemic Corticosteroids (for no response to above treatment)
    IF no response to previous treatments THEN
        THEN administer Tab. prednisolone 5-60 mg/day in 3-4 divided doses for 5-7 days.

        OR

        THEN administer Tab. dexamethasone 0.5-5.0 mg/day in 3-4 divided doses for 5-7 days.

        OR

        THEN administer Tab. betamethasone 0.5-5.0 mg/day in 3-4 divided doses for 5-7 days.
    ENDIF

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Chlorpheniramine maleate: DO NOT administer to children under 1 year old.
- Topical decongestant nasal drops: Avoid prolonged use (can cause atrophic rhinitis, anosmia, rhinitis medicamentosa).
- Systemic steroids: Taper dose before cessation of therapy.
- Antihistamines (Chlorpheniramine, pheniramine etc.): May cause sedation, cognitive impairment; patient should avoid tasks requiring alertness and skill. May cause dryness of mouth and urinary hesitancy.
- Medicated nasal drops: DO NOT use for more than seven days.
```
