DISEASE: Alcohol Use Disorder

ABBREVIATIONS:
- TDS: Three times a day
- BD: Twice a day
- IV: Intravenous
- Inj.: Injection
- Stat: Immediately

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Dependence characterized by compulsion to drink, tolerance, intoxication, withdrawal, and persistence of drinking despite harmful consequences.
- Alcohol intoxication characterized by recent intake and behavioral changes (slurred speech, unsteady gait, incoordination, nystagmus, impaired attention/memory, stupor, coma).
- Simple alcohol withdrawal characterized by abrupt cessation and symptoms (sweating, palpitations, tremors, nausea, vomiting, insomnia, hallucinations, agitation).
- Complicated withdrawal may include seizures, delirium tremens.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient requires hospitalization:
    - Hospitalization is advised for smooth detoxification, managing withdrawal complications (seizures, delirium tremens), and medical co-morbidities.

IF patient requires pharmacological treatment for withdrawal:
    IF liver function tests are normal:
        - Start Tab. chlordiazepoxide 25mg TDS.
        - May increase dose up to 100 mg/day.
    ELSE IF liver functions are compromised or unknown:
        - Start Tab. lorazepam 2 mg TDS.
        - May increase dose up to 8 mg/day.

    - Start Tab. thiamine supplementation to prevent/treat vitamin deficiency.
    - Start dose: 75 mg BD.
    - May increase dose up to 300 mg/day.
    - Injectable vitamins can be used if severe deficiency signs are present.

    - Correct fluid and electrolyte disturbances, especially if vomiting or fever is present.

    - IF seizures occur (Rum fits within 24 hours of abstinence):
        - Treat with Inj. diazepam 10 mg IV stat OR lorazepam 2 mg IV stat.
        - Especially if seizures are repeated.
        - Prophylactic treatment for true alcohol withdrawal fits is NOT recommended.

    - IF delirium tremens occurs:
        - Preferably treat in an intensive care unit.
        - Start IV line immediately.
        - Administer thiamine 100 mg IV.
        - Continue thiamine along with multivitamin parenterally until normal diet resumes.
        - Give IV dextrose and saline to replace fluid losses and maintain blood pressure.
        - Manage hyperthermia with cold sponge.
        - May use Tab. paracetamol 500 mg orally 4 times a day if no hepatic dysfunction.
        - Give Inj. diazepam 10 mg slowly IV.
        - Repeat every 15-20 minutes until sedation is achieved.
        - Physical restraint may be necessary if combative.

    - Treat associated medical and surgical problems.

    - Once patient is stable:
        - Gradually taper off the benzodiazepine dose (20% per day) over 10 to 14 days.

IF patient requires relapse prevention:
    - Tab. disulfiram (250mg to 500mg daily) can be used.
    - Obtain informed consent before administering disulfiram.
    - Explain disulfiram reaction and potential lethality.

- Provide patient education regarding short-term and long-term complications of substance use (medical, psychological, social, legal).

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Tab. chlordiazepoxide: Contraindicated if liver function tests are abnormal.
- Tab. paracetamol: Contraindicated if hepatic dysfunction is present.
