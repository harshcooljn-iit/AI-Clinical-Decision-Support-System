DISEASE: Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IBS: Irritable Bowel Syndrome
- HS: Bedtime (from amitriptyline 10 mg HS)
- PEG: Polyethylene glycol
- mg: Milligram
- ml: Milliliter
- Tab: Tablet
- Tab. mebeverine hydrochloride: Mebeverine hydrochloride tablet
- Tab. drotaverine: Drotaverine tablet
- Tab. amitriptyline: Amitriptyline tablet
- Tab. loperamide: Loperamide tablet
- Isaphghul husk: Psyllium husk

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Constellation of gastrointestinal symptoms associated with lower bowel symptoms.
- Absence of organic disease.
- Symptoms include abdominal pain, alternating diarrhea and constipation, abdominal distension, relief of abdominal symptoms with bowel motions, increased stool frequency, sense of incomplete evacuation, pencil-thin stool, heartburn, bloating, back pain, weakness, faintness, palpitation.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has predominant abdominal pain THEN
    IF using mebeverine hydrochloride THEN
        Administer Tab. mebeverine hydrochloride 270 mg three times a day for long term.
    ELSE IF using drotaverine THEN
        Administer Tab. drotaverine 40 to 80 mg three times a day.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

IF patient has depressive symptoms THEN
    Administer Tab. amitriptyline 10 mg HS for 4 to 6 weeks.
ENDIF

IF patient has diarrheal symptoms THEN
    Administer Tab. loperamide 2 to 4 mg daily for several days / weeks depending on the critical response.
ENDIF

IF patient has constipation symptoms THEN
    IF using isotonic polyethylene glycol (PEG electrolyte) solution THEN
        Administer Isotonic polyethylene glycol (PEG electrolyte) solution 125-250 ml.
    ELSE IF using lactulose solution THEN
        Administer Lactulose solution 15 to 20 ml at night.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Diet should contain high fiber.
- Supplement with bulk forming agents such as isaphghul husk.
- Avoid caffeine and alcohol.
- Avoid milk and other dietary constituents that worsen symptoms.
- Psychotherapy may be helpful in selected cases.

- (None specified in the provided text for the listed medications in the context of IBS, but standard contraindications for each drug would apply in a real clinical setting).
