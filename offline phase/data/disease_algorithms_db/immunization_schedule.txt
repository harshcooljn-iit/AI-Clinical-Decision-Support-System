```text
DISEASE: Immunization Schedule

ABBREVIATIONS:
- BCG: Bacillus Calmette-Guérin
- OPV: Oral Polio Vaccine
- DPT: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus vaccine
- DT: Diphtheria, Tetanus vaccine
- TT: Tetanus Toxoid vaccine
- HBsAg: Hepatitis B surface Antigen
- HBIG: Hepatitis B immune globulin
- Hib: Haemophilus influenzae type b
- MMR: Measles, Mumps, Rubella vaccine
- HIV: Human Immunodeficiency Virus
- DTP: Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis vaccine

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Based on age and clinical guidelines for vaccine administration.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **General Principles:**
    - IF vaccines are administered simultaneously, THEN use different sites and separate needles for each component.
    - IF 2 live vaccines are not given together, THEN a gap of 1 month is recommended between them.
    - IF skin is infected or compromised by a lesion, THEN avoid giving injections.
    - Always use a sterile syringe and needle for each injection and to reconstitute each unit of medication.
    - Dispose of syringes and needles carefully after use.

2.  **National Immunization Schedule (Universal Immunization Programme):**
    - IF Age is Birth or 6 weeks, THEN administer BCG.
    - IF Age is Birth, THEN administer OPV.
    - IF Age is 6 weeks, THEN administer OPV, Hepatitis B, DPT.
    - IF Age is 10 weeks, THEN administer OPV, Hepatitis B, DPT.
    - IF Age is 14 weeks, THEN administer OPV, Hepatitis B, DPT.
    - IF Age is 15-18 months, THEN administer DPT, MMR.
    - IF Age is 5 years, THEN administer DT.
    - IF Age is 10 years, THEN administer TT.
    - IF Age is 16 years, THEN administer TT.
    - IF TT is given for the first time at age 10 or 16 years, THEN give 2 doses at a 4-week interval.

3.  **Indian Academy of Pediatrics Recommended Immunization:**
    - IF Age is From birth to 2 weeks, THEN administer BCG.
    - IF Age is At birth, THEN administer OPV.
    - IF Age is 6 weeks, THEN administer OPV, DTP, Hepatitis B.
    - IF Age is 10 weeks, THEN administer OPV, DTP, Hepatitis B.
    - IF Age is 14 weeks, THEN administer OPV, DTP, Hepatitis B.
    - IF Age is 15-18 months, THEN administer DTP, MMR, Hib.
    - IF Age is 5 years, THEN administer DT.
    - IF Age is 9 months, THEN administer Measles.
    - IF Age is 2 years or older, THEN administer Typhoid.
    - IF Age is 10 years, THEN administer TT.
    - IF Age is 16 years, THEN administer TT.
    - IF Pregnant, THEN administer 2 doses of TT at a 1-month interval.
    - IF Age is Above 1 year, THEN administer Varicella.
    - IF Age is Above 1 year, THEN administer Hepatitis A.

4.  **Hepatitis B Vaccination Guidelines:**
    - IF Mother is HBsAg negative, THEN administer HB vaccine along with DTP at 6, 10, and 14 weeks.
    - IF Mother's HBsAg status is unknown, THEN start HB vaccination soon after birth.
    - IF Mother is HBsAg positive (especially HBeAg positive), THEN administer HBIG within 24 hours of birth along with HB vaccine.

5.  **Combination Vaccines:**
    - Combination vaccines offer convenience but are not more effective than separate vaccines.
    - Follow manufacturer's instructions strictly when mixing vaccines in the same syringe.

6.  **Immunization in Special Circumstances:**
    - **Preterm Infants:** Administer vaccines according to chronological age, irrespective of birth weight or gestation period, if the infant is stable.
    - **Corticosteroids:**
        - IF receiving high-dose oral corticosteroids (prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day) for >14 days, THEN do not receive live virus vaccines until 1 month after steroid discontinuation.
        - Killed vaccines are safe but may be less effective.
        - Topical or inhaled steroids are not a contraindication.
    - **Splenectomy:** Initiate pneumococcal, Hib, and meningococcal vaccines a few weeks prior to splenectomy.
    - **HIV Infection:** Immune response may be suboptimal; consider readministration after immune status improves with antiretroviral therapy.
    - **Lapsed Immunization:** Do not restart the series; continue from where it left off or start the schedule as for an unimmunized child if status is unknown.
    - **Minor Illnesses:** Fever, diarrhea, respiratory infections, and malnutrition are not contraindications.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- IF skin is infected or compromised by a lesion, THEN avoid giving injections.
- IF receiving high-dose oral corticosteroids (>14 days), THEN avoid live virus vaccines until 1 month after discontinuation.
```
