DISEASE: Screening Guidelines For Early Detection Of Cancer

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CBE: Clinical Breast Examination
- ACS: American Cancer Society
- CIN: Cervical Intraepithelial Neoplasia
- HNPCC: Hereditary Non Polyposis Colon Cancer
- VIA: Visual Inspection After Application of 5% Acetic Acid
- Pap Test: Papanicolaou test
- HPV: Human Papillomavirus
- ICMR: Indian Council of Medical Research

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Breast Cancer: Clinical Breast Examination (CBE), Mammography, Self Breast Examination.
- Cervical Cancer: Pap smear (conventional cytology), Liquid Based Cytology (LBC), HPV DNA testing, VIA.
- Endometrial Cancer: Endometrial biopsy.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

SCREENING FOR BREAST CANCER (ACS Guidelines)

IF patient is a woman
  IF age is between 20 and 39 years
    THEN advise self breast examination starting at age 20.
    THEN perform clinical breast examination (CBE) every 3 years.
  ELSE IF age is 40 years or older
    THEN advise self breast examination starting at age 20.
    THEN begin annual mammography at age 40.
    THEN continue annual mammography screening.
  ENDIF
ENDIF

SCREENING FOR CERVICAL CANCER

IF patient is a woman
  IF age is less than 21 years OR 3 years before onset of vaginal intercourse
    THEN do not screen.
  ELSE IF age is between 21 and 29 years
    THEN screen annually with conventional cytology smears OR biennially using liquid based cytology.
  ELSE IF age is 30 years or older
    IF patient has had 3 consecutive, technically satisfying normal/negative cytology results
      THEN screen every 2-3 years using conventional or liquid based cytology.
      OR
      THEN screen every 3 years using HPV DNA testing with conventional or liquid based cytology.
    ENDIF
  ENDIF

  IF age is 70 years or older AND has an intact cervix AND no abnormal/positive cytology tests within the 10 year period before age 70
    THEN may choose to cease cervical cancer screening.
  ENDIF

  IF patient is immune-compromised (organ transplantation, chemotherapy, corticosteroid treatment, HIV+)
    THEN test twice during the first year after diagnosis.
    THEN test annually thereafter.
  ENDIF

  IF patient has a history of CIN 2/3 before hysterectomy
    THEN continue screening until there is a 10 year history of no abnormal positive cytology tests.
  ENDIF

  IF age is 30 years
    THEN undergo Pap smear at least once (ICMR guidelines).
  ENDIF

  IF in countries with limited resources
    IF age is around 30 years
      THEN have one Pap test.
    ENDIF
  ENDIF

  IF in countries with limited resources
    THEN use Pap smear OR VIA (visual inspection after application of 5% acetic acid).
  ENDIF

  IF performing Pap smear
    THEN follow these precautions:
      - Do not douche for 48 hours.
      - Do not use vaginal cream for 1 week.
      - Abstain from coitus for 24 hours.
      - Smear to be done on the 7th day after menstrual period.
    ENDIF

  IF abnormal smear results
    THEN refer for colposcopy and biopsy.
  ENDIF

SCREENING FOR ENDOMETRIAL CANCER

IF patient is a woman
  IF patient is at high risk (due to HNPCC associated genetic mutation carrier status)
    THEN consider beginning annual testing for early endometrial cancer detection at age 35.
  ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- None specified in the provided text for the screening methods themselves, but general medical contraindications for procedures like biopsy or specific tests might apply based on patient condition.
- For Pap smear precautions:
  - IF patient has recently douched (within 48 hours)
    THEN Pap smear may be less accurate.
  - IF patient has used vaginal cream recently (within 1 week)
    THEN Pap smear may be less accurate.
  - IF patient has had recent coitus (within 24 hours)
    THEN Pap smear may be less accurate.
  - IF patient is currently menstruating
    THEN Pap smear should be delayed until after the menstrual period (ideally done on day 7).
