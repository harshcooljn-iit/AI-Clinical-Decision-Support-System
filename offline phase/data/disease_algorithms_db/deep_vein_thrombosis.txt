ABBREVIATIONS:
- DVT: Deep Vein Thrombosis
- LMWH: Low Molecular Weight Heparin
- PT: Prothrombin Time
- APTT: Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time
- PE: Pulmonary Embolus

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical signs/symptoms (pain, swelling, edema, dilated veins, Homan's sign, pyrexia).
- Risk factors (age, obesity, immobility, etc.).
- Investigations: D-dimer, PT, APTT, duplex ultrasound.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) THEN
    // Initial Treatment
    Start Low Molecular Weight Heparin (LMWH) AND Warfarin.

    // Warfarin Dosing (Initial)
    IF Day is Day 1 THEN
        Administer Warfarin 10 mg.
    ELSE IF Day is Day 2 THEN
        Administer Warfarin 10 mg.
    ELSE IF Day is Day 3 THEN
        Administer Warfarin 5 mg.

    ENDIF

    IF DVT is severe THEN
        Consider Venous thrombectomy.
    ENDIF

    // Recurrent/High Risk Cases
    IF DVT is recurrent OR Patient is high risk THEN
        Consider Venacaval filter.
    ENDIF
ENDIF
