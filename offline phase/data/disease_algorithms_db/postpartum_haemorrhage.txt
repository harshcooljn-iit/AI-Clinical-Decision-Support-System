```text
1.  DISEASE: Postpartum Haemorrhage (PPH)

2.  ABBREVIATIONS:
    *   PPH: Postpartum Haemorrhage
    *   GA: General Anaesthesia
    *   IM: Intramuscularly
    *   IV: Intravenous
    *   Ringer's lactate: Ringer's Lactate Solution
    *   PGF2A: 15-Methyl Prostaglandin F2 alpha
    *   APH: Antepartum Haemorrhage

3.  DIAGNOSTICS:
    *   Excessive blood loss from the genital tract after delivery of the foetus exceeding 500 ml.
    *   Blood loss affecting the general condition of the mother.
    *   Primary PPH: Bleeding within 24 hours of delivery.
    *   Secondary PPH: Bleeding between 24 hours and 6 weeks of delivery.
    *   Clinical examination to ascertain the cause (e.g., uterine atony, tears, retained placenta, inversion, rupture).
    *   Monitoring vital signs (pulse rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate) and urine output.

4.  TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    *   IF PPH occurs:
        *   Monitor vital signs and urine output.
        *   Initiate resuscitative measures (IV fluids, blood replacement therapy).
        *   Perform a thorough clinical examination to determine the cause.
        *   Plan definitive treatment based on the cause.

    *   IF Primary PPH (within 24 hours):
        *   Consider common causes: uterine atony (90%), cervical/vaginal tears.
        *   Consider less common causes: occult uterine inversion, uterine rupture, coagulation defect.

    *   IF Secondary PPH (24 hours to 6 weeks):
        *   Consider common causes: sepsis, retained placental bits, placental polyp, choriocarcinoma.

    *   IF Atonic PPH:
        *   Non-pharmacological treatment: Placental removal with cord traction (if separated), uterine massage, bimanual compression.
        *   Pharmacological treatment:
            *   Prevention: Oxytocin 5 units IM at birth of anterior shoulder or after placenta delivery (unless contraindicated).
            *   Treatment:
                *   Oxytocin infusion: 10-40 units in 500 ml Ringer's lactate/normal saline at 125 ml/min.
                *   Methyl ergometrine maleate: 0.2 mg IV (may repeat IM after 5-10 min).
                *   IF bleeding not controlled:
                    *   15-Methyl Prostaglandin F2 alpha (PGF2A): 0.25 mg IM, may repeat every 15-90 min up to 2 mg (Total 8 doses).
                    *   IF patient has bronchial asthma or epilepsy: Administer Tab. misopristol 600 mcg per rectum with caution.
                *   IF bleeding still not controlled:
                    *   Consider surgical treatment (uterine artery ligation, hysterectomy).

    *   IF Oxytocin is used:
        *   IV: Infuse 20 units in 1 L IV fluids at 60 drops per minute.
        *   Continuing IV Dose: Infuse 20 units in 1 L IV fluids at 40 drops per minute.
        *   Maximum IV Dose: Not more than 3 L of IV fluids containing oxytocin.
        *   Do not give as an IV bolus.
        *   IM: 5-10 units.

    *   IF Methyl-Ergometrine is used:
        *   Route: IM or IV (slowly).
        *   Dose: 0.2 mg.
        *   Continuing Dose: Repeat 0.2 mg IM after 15 minutes. If required, give 0.2 mg IM or IV (slowly) every four hours.
        *   Maximum Dose: 1.0 mg (Total five doses).
        *   Contraindications: High blood pressure, pre-eclampsia, heart disease.

    *   IF 15-Methyl Prostaglandin F2 alpha (PGF2A) is used:
        *   Route: IM.
        *   Dose: 0.25 mg.
        *   Continuing Dose: 0.25 mg every 15 minutes.
        *   Maximum Dose: 2 mg (Total eight doses).
        *   Contraindications: Asthma.
        *   Caution: Contraindicated in bronchial asthma, epilepsy.

    *   IF Misopristol is used (for patients with asthma/epilepsy):
        *   Route: Rectal.
        *   Dose: 600 mcg per tablet.

    *   IF bleeding is not controlled despite medical therapy and facilities are limited:
        *   Arrange transfer to a higher center.
        *   Consider intrauterine packing in the meantime.

    *   IF surgical treatment is needed:
        *   Retained placenta: Manual removal under GA.
        *   Cervical/vaginal tears: Exploration and repair.
        *   Uterine rupture: Laparotomy with repair/hysterectomy.
        *   Uterine inversion: Reposition under GA.
        *   Atonic PPH not controlled: Laparotomy with uterine artery ligation with medical measures.
        *   Internal artery ligation/Hysterectomy.

5.  TABULAR DOSAGES:
    *   Oxytocin:
        *   IF IV route: Infuse 20 units in 1 L IV fluids at 60 drops per minute.
        *   IF continuing IV route: Infuse 20 units in 1 L IV fluids at 40 drops per minute.
        *   Maximum IV dose: Not more than 3 L of IV fluids containing oxytocin.
        *   Do not give as an IV bolus.
        *   IF IM route: 5-10 units.
    *   Methyl-Ergometrine:
        *   IF initial dose: 0.2 mg (IM or IV slowly).
        *   IF repeat dose: 0.2 mg IM after 15 minutes.
        *   IF further repeat dose: 0.2 mg IM or IV (slowly) every four hours.
        *   Maximum dose: 1.0 mg (Total five doses).
    *   15-Methyl Prostaglandin F2 alpha:
        *   IF initial dose: 0.25 mg IM.
        *   IF repeat dose: 0.25 mg every 15 minutes.
        *   Maximum dose: 2 mg (Total eight doses).

6.  CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    *   IF Methyl-Ergometrine:
        *   THEN cannot be given if patient has heart disease.
        *   THEN cannot be given if patient has hypertension.
        *   THEN cannot be given if patient has pre-eclampsia.
    *   IF 15-Methyl Prostaglandin F2 alpha:
        *   THEN cannot be given if patient has bronchial asthma.
        *   THEN cannot be given if patient has epilepsy (use with caution).
    *   IF Oxytocin:
        *   THEN do not give as an IV bolus.
```
