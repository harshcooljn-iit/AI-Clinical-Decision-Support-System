DISEASE: Fistula In Ano

ABBREVIATIONS:
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
- eg: for example

DIAGNOSTICS:
- History of previous anorectal abscess.
- Symptoms: perianal discharge, pain, swelling, bleeding, diarrhea, skin excoriation, external opening.
- Investigations (selected cases): Fistulography, Endoanal/endorectal ultrasonography, MRI, Barium enema/small bowel series, Anal manometry, Examination under anesthesia, Proctosigmoidoscopy/colonoscopy.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF Underlying cause exists THEN treat underlying cause.
IF Surgical procedure needed for eradication of tract THEN proceed with surgical treatment.

SURGICAL TREATMENT OPTIONS:
- Fistulotomy
- Fistulectomy
- Mucosal advancement flap
- Seton placement

INDICATIONS FOR SETON PLACEMENT:
IF Fistula is complex (high transsphincteric, suprasphincteric, extrasphincteric) THEN consider Seton placement.
IF Fistula is multiple THEN consider Seton placement.
IF Fistula is recurrent after previous fistulotomy THEN consider Seton placement.
IF Fistula is anterior in female patients THEN consider Seton placement.
IF Preoperative sphincter pressures are poor THEN consider Seton placement.
IF Patient has Crohn’s disease THEN consider Seton placement.
IF Patient is immunosuppressed THEN consider Seton placement.

PATIENT EDUCATION AND FOLLOW-UP CARE:
- Treatment often ambulatory.
- Provide discharge instructions and close follow-up.
- Recommend Sitz baths.
- Recommend analgesics.
- Recommend stool-bulking agents (e.g., bran, psyllium products).
- Schedule frequent office visits within the first few weeks for healing and wound care monitoring.
