DISEASE: Acute Airway Obstruction

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ECG: Electrocardiogram
- X-ray: Radiography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Assessment of severity.
- Portable radiographic ultrasonography (implied).
- ECG monitoring.
- Pulse oximetry.
- Blood pressure measurement.
- Fibre optic endoscopy / micro-laryngoscopy / bronchoscopy (in stable patients).
- Plain X-ray neck (in stable patients).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Severity of airway obstruction is assessed:
    THEN Proceed to next step.

IF Portable radiographic ultrasonography is available/indicated:
    THEN Perform portable radiographic ultrasonography.

IF ECG monitoring is available/indicated:
    THEN Perform ECG monitoring.

IF Supplemental oxygen is available/indicated:
    THEN Administer supplemental oxygen.

IF Pulse oximetry is available/indicated:
    THEN Perform pulse oximetry.

IF Blood pressure measurement is available/indicated:
    THEN Measure blood pressure.

IF Patient is unstable (based on assessment):
    THEN Perform Cricothyrotomy.
    OR Perform Tracheostomy.
    OR Perform Emergency intubation.

IF Patient is stable:
    THEN Perform Plain X-ray neck.
    THEN Perform Fibre optic endoscopy / micro-laryngoscopy / bronchoscopy to find out the cause.

EMERGENCY TREATMENT (Initial steps):
    IF Patient presents with acute airway obstruction:
        THEN Perform Head extension with lower jaw thrust forward.
        THEN Perform Suctioning for clearing secretions.

EMERGENCY CONDITIONS (Severe obstruction):
    IF Patient requires immediate respiratory support:
        THEN Use Ventilatory mask with 100% oxygen.
        OR Perform Endotracheal intubation.
        OR Perform Tracheostomy.
