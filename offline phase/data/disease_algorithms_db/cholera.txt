DIAGNOSTICS:
- Identification of _V. cholerae_ in stool examination (dark-field microscopy, specific antiserum immobilization).
- Laboratory isolation using selective media (TTG agar, TCBS agar).
- Standard biochemical testing for _Enterobacteriaceae_.
- Point-of-care antigen-detection cholera dipstick assay.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  ASSESSMENT: Assess the degree of dehydration (None/Mild, Moderate, Severe) based on clinical findings and estimated body weight loss (Table 1).

2.  TREATMENT BASED ON DEHYDRATION DEGREE:

    IF Degree of Dehydration is None or Mild, but Diarrhea:
        IF Patient's Age (Weight) is &lt;2 years:
            Administer 1/4–1/2 cup (50–100 ml) of ORS, to a maximum of 0.5 L/d.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is 2 – 9 years:
            Administer 1/2–1 cup (100–200 ml) of ORS, to a maximum of 1 L/d.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is More than 10 years:
            Administer as much ORS as desired, to a maximum of 2 L/d.

    ELSE IF Degree of Dehydration is Moderate:
        IF Patient's Age (Weight) is &lt;4 months (&lt;5 kg):
            Administer 200–400 ml of ORS.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is 4–11 months (5–&lt;8 kg):
            Administer 400–600 ml of ORS.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is 12–23 months (8–&lt;11 kg):
            Administer 600–800 ml of ORS.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is 2–4 years (11–&lt;16 kg):
            Administer 800–1200 ml of ORS.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is 5–14 years (16–&lt;30 kg):
            Administer 1200–2200 ml of ORS.
        ELSE IF Patient's Age (Weight) is 15 years ( ≥ 30 kg):
            Administer 2200–4000 ml of ORS.

    ELSE IF Degree of Dehydration is Severe:
        Administer IV fluid replacement with Ringer's lactate (or, if not available, normal saline):
            - 100 ml/kg in the first 3-h period (or first 6-h period for children &lt;12 months old).
            - Start rapidly, then slow down.
            - Total of 200 ml/kg in the first 24 h.
            - Continue until the patient is awake, can ingest ORS, and no longer has a weak pulse.

3.  ADDITIONAL THERAPY:
    - WHO recommends "low-osmolarity" ORS.
    - Rice-based ORS is considered superior to standard ORS if available.
    - ORS can be administered via a nasogastric tube if the patient cannot ingest fluid.
    - Potassium intake (bananas, green coconut water) should be encouraged.
    - For severe dehydration, Ringer's lactate is preferred; supplement with potassium, preferably orally.
    - For continued large-volume diarrhea, prolonged IV treatment may be needed.
    - Severe hypokalemia can be treated with potassium IV or orally.
    - If adequate staff are unavailable for monitoring, oral rehydration and potassium replacement are safer than IV.

4.  ANTIBIOTIC THERAPY (Optional):
    - A single dose of doxycycline (300 mg) may be effective in adults.
    - Tetracycline (12.5 mg/kg four times a day for 3 days) may be effective in adults.
    - DO NOT use doxycycline or tetracycline in children &lt;8 years of age.
    - Erythromycin or azithromycin (10 mg/kg in children) are recommended for pregnant women and children.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- DO NOT use doxycycline or tetracycline in children &lt;8 years of age due to possible deposition in bone and developing teeth.
