DISEASE: Postmenopausal Bleeding (PMB)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PMB: Postmenopausal Bleeding
- HRT: Hormone Replacement Therapy
- SIS: Saline Infusion Sonography
- D &C: Dilatation and Curettage
- PCB: Postcoital Bleeding

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Confirmed by endometrial or cervical biopsy.
- Non-invasive tests: Saline infusion sonography (SIS), vaginal probe ultrasound.
- Dilatation and curettage (D &C) may be necessary for definitive diagnosis.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Cause is Benign & malignant neoplasm of vulva THEN
    Refer to higher center or treat cervix, vagina, uterus or ovaries according to cause and facilities available.
ELSE IF Cause is Indiscriminate use of oestrogen for HRT THEN
    Stop oestrogen therapy.
ELSE IF Cause is Infections THEN
    Administer Antibiotics.
ELSE IF Cause is Injuries THEN
    Perform Repair.
ELSE IF Cause is Coagulation disorder THEN
    Treat accordingly.
ELSE IF Cause is Endometritis THEN
    Administer Antibiotics.
ELSE IF Cause is Postmenopausal atrophic vaginitis THEN
    Administer Vaginal oestrogen cream/ointment.
ELSE IF PMB is not due to cancer and cannot be controlled by other treatments THEN
    Perform hysterectomy.

REFERRAL CRITERIA:

URGENT REFERRAL:
IF Palpable pelvic mass OR lesions suspicious of cancer on vulva or vagina or cervix on examination or on ultrasound THEN
    Refer urgently.
ELSE IF More than one OR a single heavy episode of PMB in women aged >55 years (not on HRT) THEN
    Refer urgently.
ELSE IF Postcoital bleeding (PCB) in a woman aged >35 years that has persisted for more than 4 weeks THEN
    Refer urgently.
ELSE IF Prolonged or unexpected bleeding that persists for more than 4 weeks after stopping HRT THEN
    Refer urgently.

EARLY REFERRAL (within 4-6 weeks):
IF Any other woman with PMB not on HRT who does not satisfy the criteria for urgent referral THEN
    Refer early (within 4-6 weeks).
ELSE IF Unexplained repeated postcoital bleeding THEN
    Refer early (within 4-6 weeks).

ADDITIONAL NOTES:
IF Woman is over 45 years with persistent abdominal distension or pain THEN
    Consider ovarian cancer and perform a pelvic examination.
IF Excessive bleeding THEN
    Administer hemostatic drugs (oral or intravenous).
