```text
DISEASE: Common Cold (Coryza)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ENT: Ear, Nose, Throat
- Tab: Tablet
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- HCL: Hydrochloric Acid
- Saline: Saline nasal drops
- Ephedrine: Ephedrine nasal drop
- Oxymetazoline HCL: Oxymetazoline Hydrochloride nasal drops
- Xylometazoline: Xylometazoline nasal drop
- Rhinitis medicamentosa: Rebound congestion and rhinitis medicamentosa

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Nasal stuffiness, rhinorrhea, sneezing, low-grade fever, nasal obstruction, profuse mucopurulent secretions, sore throat (initial), cough, nasal discharge.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:** Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Common Cold.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF patient has nasal stuffiness or obstruction:
        - THEN recommend steam inhalation 2-3 times/day.
        - THEN recommend saline nasal drops (1-2 drops in each nostril 2-3 times daily) for nasal obstruction.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Antihistamines:**
    - IF patient requires antihistamine therapy:
        - THEN consider the following options:
            - Option A: Tab. chlorpheniramine 4mg 8 hourly for 5-7 days.
                - IF patient is a child:
                    - THEN administer 0.35mg/kg/day in 3 equal doses.
            - Option B: Tab. pheniramine 25mg 2-3 times daily for 5-7 days.
                - IF patient is a child:
                    - THEN administer 0.5mg/kg/day divided in equal doses.
            - Option C: Tab. cetirizine dihydrochloride 10mg once a day for 5-7 days.
                - IF patient is a child:
                    - THEN administer 5mg once per day.

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Analgesic/Antipyretic:**
    - IF patient has malaise and/or fever:
        - THEN consider Tab. paracetamol.
            - IF patient is an adult:
                - THEN administer 1500mg 3-4 times a day for 2-3 days, then as needed.
            - IF patient is a child:
                - THEN administer 40-60mg/kg/day divided in 4 doses.

5.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Decongestants (Severe Nasal Obstruction):**
    - IF patient has severe nasal obstruction:
        - THEN consider medicated nasal drops for a maximum of 2-3 days.
        - Option A: Ephedrine 0.75% nasal drop, 1-2 drops in each nostril 3 times a day for 2-3 days.
            - IF patient is a child:
                - THEN administer Ephedrine 0.75% nasal drop, 1-2 drops in each nostril 3 times a day for 2-3 days.
        - Option B: Oxymetazoline HCL 0.05% nasal drops, 1-2 drops in each nostril 3 times a day for 2-3 days.
            - IF patient is a child:
                - THEN administer Oxymetazoline HCL 0.025% nasal drops, 1 drop 2 times daily for 2-3 days.
        - Option C: Xylometazoline 0.1%, 1-2 drops in each nostril 3 times a day for 2-3 days.
            - IF patient is a child:
                - THEN administer Xylometazoline 0.05%, 1-2 drops 2 times daily for 2-3 days.
        - IF patient is a child below 6 years of age:
            - THEN AVOID medicated nasal drops.
        - General Caution for Medicated Nasal Drops:
            - THEN advise patient that these should not be used for more than 7 days due to the risk of rebound congestion (rhinitis medicamentosa).

6.  **Patient Education:**
    - Inform patient that the common cold is a self-limiting viral infection, usually resolving within a week.
    - Advise symptomatic relief only.
    - State that antibiotics are not effective.
    - Warn that chlorpheniramine and pheniramine can cause sedation and cognitive impairment, advising avoidance for tasks requiring alertness.
    - Warn about the risk of rebound congestion (rhinitis medicamentosa) with prolonged use (>7 days) of medicated nasal drops.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Medicated nasal drops (Ephedrine, Oxymetazoline HCL, Xylometazoline):
    - IF patient is a child below 6 years of age:
        - THEN AVOID use.
    - General Contraindication for prolonged use:
        - IF medicated nasal drops are used for more than 7 days:
            - THEN risk of rebound congestion (rhinitis medicamentosa) increases.
- Chlorpheniramine and Pheniramine:
    - IF patient needs to perform tasks requiring alertness and skill:
        - THEN AVOID use due to potential sedation and cognitive impairment.
```
