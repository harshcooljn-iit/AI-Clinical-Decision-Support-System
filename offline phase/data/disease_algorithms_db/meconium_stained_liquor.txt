DISEASE: Meconium Stained Liquor

ABBREVIATIONS:
- UTI: Urinary Tract Infection
- HIV: Human Immunodeficiency Virus

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Presence of meconium staining in amniotic fluid.
- Assessment of meconium thickness (slight vs. thick).
- Monitoring fetal heart rate for abnormalities.
- Consideration of fetal presentation (e.g., breech).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Meconium is present in amniotic fluid THEN
    IF Meconium is slight AND Fetal heart rate is normal THEN
        - Maintain vigilance.
    ELSE IF Meconium is thick THEN
        - Consider expedited delivery.
        - Prepare for management of neonatal upper airway at birth to prevent meconium aspiration.
    ENDIF
ELSE
    - No specific action related to meconium.
ENDIF

MANAGEMENT DURING LABOR:
IF Meconium is present THEN
    - Position the woman: Prop her up or place her on her left side.
    - IF Oxytocin is being administered THEN
        - Stop oxytocin administration.
    ENDIF
    - Administer oxygen: 4–6 L by mask or nasal cannulae.
ENDIF

POSTPARTUM CARE ADVICE:
- Advise continuous monitoring for the first 24 hours.
- Advise against vaginal insertion of objects.
- Advise adequate rest and sleep.
- Advise hand washing to prevent infection.
- Advise daily perineal washing and after faecal excretion.
- Advise avoiding sexual intercourse until perineal wound heals.
- Counsel on birth spacing and family planning.
- Advise on routine postpartum care visits:
    - First visit within the first week, preferably within 2-3 days.
    - Second visit after 4-6 weeks.
    - Earlier visits if problems occur (fever, UTI, perineal infection, hypertension, urinary incontinence, severe anaemia, postpartum blues, HIV positive).

BREASTFEEDING COUNSEL:
- Advise starting breastfeeding within 1 hour of birth.
- Advise against giving other food or drink before breastfeeding.
- Advise exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months of life.
- Explain benefits of breast milk (nutrients, digestion, infection protection).
