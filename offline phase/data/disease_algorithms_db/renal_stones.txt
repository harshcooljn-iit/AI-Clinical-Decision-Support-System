DISEASE: Renal Stones

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- IM: Intramuscular
- Tab.: Tablet
- KUB: Kidney, Ureter, Bladder
- IVP: Intravenous Pyelogram
- PCNL: Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy
- ESWL: Extracorporeal Shockwave Lithotripsy
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)
- ml: milliliter
- mg: milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Salient calculus
- Colicky pain in lumbar region, worsened by movements
- Hematuria
- Burning micturition
- Rigidity of lateral abdominal muscles
- Tenderness in lumbar region
- Investigations: X ray KUB, ultrasonography of KUB, intravenous urography, CT-IVP

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient presents with renal stone symptoms:
    THEN Assess stone size and location.

    IF Stone size is less than 5 mm:
        THEN Advise high fluid intake and avoidance of lithogenic food/beverages.
        THEN Consider pharmacological treatment for pain relief.
        IF Pain is severe:
            THEN Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 3 ml IM stat.
            THEN Administer Inj. dicyclomine 2 ml IM stat.
        ELSE
            THEN Consider oral analgesics.
        ENDIF
        THEN Consider Potassium citrate 5 ml in 100 ml of water 3-4 times/day.
        THEN Monitor for spontaneous passage.
    ELSE
        THEN Consider surgical intervention based on stone size, location, and patient factors.
        IF Stone is in the kidney:
            THEN Consider PCNL, ESWL, Open pyelolithotomy, or Nephrolithotomy.
        ELSE IF Stone is in the ureter:
            THEN Consider Endoscopic removal (uretero renoscopy, dormia basket, ureteric meatotomy) or open ureterolithotomy.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    IF Infection is present:
        THEN Administer Tab. ciprofloxacin 500 mg BD for 5 days.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (No specific contraindications mentioned in the text for the listed drugs, but standard contraindications for NSAIDs like diclofenac and antibiotics like ciprofloxacin apply based on patient history and allergies).
