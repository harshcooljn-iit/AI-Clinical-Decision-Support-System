DISEASE: Fetal Distress In Labour

ABBREVIATIONS:
- FHR: Fetal Heart Rate
- IV: Intravenous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Abnormal FHR (less than 100 or more than 180 beats per minute)
- Thick meconium-stained amniotic fluid

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF maternal cause identified (e.g., maternal fever, drugs) THEN
    Initiate appropriate management for the maternal cause.
ELSE IF maternal cause not identified AND FHR remains abnormal throughout at least three contractions THEN
    Perform a vaginal examination to check for explanatory signs of distress.
    IF bleeding with intermittent or constant pain is found THEN
        Suspect abruptio placentae.
    ENDIF
    IF signs of infection (fever, foul-smelling vaginal discharge) are found THEN
        Give antibiotics as for amnionitis.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

IF FHR abnormalities persist OR additional signs of distress (thick meconium-stained fluid) are present THEN
    Plan delivery.
    IF cervix is fully dilated AND fetal head is not more than 1/5 above the symphysis pubis OR leading bony edge of the fetal head is at 0 station THEN
        Deliver by vacuum extraction or forceps.
    ELSE IF cervix is not fully dilated OR fetal head is more than 1/5 above the symphysis pubis OR leading bony edge of the fetal head is above 0 station THEN
        Deliver by caesarean section.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(None specified in the provided text for specific drugs, but general contraindications for vacuum/forceps/C-section apply based on clinical context not detailed here).
