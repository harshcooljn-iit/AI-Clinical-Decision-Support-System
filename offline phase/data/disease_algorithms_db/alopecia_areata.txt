DISEASE: Alopecia Areata

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- Tab.: Tablet
- mg: milligram
- ml: milliliter
- kg: kilogram
- PUVA: Psoralen plus ultraviolet A light
- fluticasone dipropionate: Fluticasone dipropionate
- hydrocortisone acetate: Hydrocortisone acetate
- Triamcinolone acetonide: Triamcinolone acetonide
- cyclosporine: Cyclosporine
- prednisolone: Prednisolone
- azelaic acid: Azelaic acid
- tazarotene: Tazarotene
- diphencyprone: Diphencyprone
- squaric acid dibutyl ester: Squaric acid dibutyl ester
- anthralin: Anthralin
- phenol: Phenol
- minoxidil: Minoxidil
- psoralen: Psoralen

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination (patchy, non-scarring hair loss).
- Biopsy (optional): Peribulbar lymphocytic infiltrate ("swarm of bees" pattern).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF disease is Alopecia Areata THEN

    # Non-pharmacological treatment
    Counsel patient and provide reassurance regarding the self-limiting nature of the disease.

    # Pharmacological treatment

    # Topical treatments
    IF patient prefers topical therapy OR lesions are localized THEN
        # Irritant application (tertiary care)
        IF treatment setting is tertiary care center THEN
            APPLY topical irritants such as anthralin, phenol, tazarotene, azelaic acid, tincture iodine, squaric acid dibutyl ester, or diphencyprone.
        ENDIF

        # Intralesional therapy
        APPLY intralesional Triamcinolone acetonide 10 mg/ml 0.2-0.5ml per patch every 3 weeks.

        # Topical corticosteroids or other agents
        APPLY topical hydrocortisone acetate ointments or cream 1% OR topical fluticasone dipropionate 0.1% OR topical psoralen OR topical minoxidil.
    ELSE

        # Systemic therapy (for progressive lesions or those not responding to topical agents)

        # Systemic corticosteroids
        IF lesions are progressive OR unresponsive to topical agents THEN
            # Pulsed therapy options
            IF patient is suitable for pulsed therapy THEN
                # Option 1: Injectable Triamcinolone
                ADMINISTER Inj. triamcinolone (40mg/ml) monthly.
                # Option 2: Oral Prednisolone mini-pulse
                OR ADMINISTER Tab. prednisolone 40mg on two successive days each week.
                # Duration depends on response.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        # Systemic immunomodulators
        IF lesions are progressive OR unresponsive to topical agents AND systemic corticosteroids are contraindicated or ineffective THEN
            ADMINISTER Tab. cyclosporine (2mg/kg/day).
        ENDIF

        # Oral phototherapy (PUVA)
        IF lesions are extensive, progressive, OR unresponsive to other systemic therapies AND patient is suitable AND treatment setting is tertiary care center THEN
            ADMINISTER Oral phototherapy (PUVA).
            # Note: Associated with side effects and cumulative dose-dependent toxicities.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF
