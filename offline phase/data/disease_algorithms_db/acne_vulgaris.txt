```text
DISEASE: Acne Vulgaris

ABBREVIATIONS:
- p.acnes: Propionibacterium acnes
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination.
- Hormonal assay may be considered for severe acne, menstrual irregularity, or hirsutism.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Disease is Acne Vulgaris THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment (General advice)
    THEN
        IF Patient is advised on diet THEN
            THEN Advise to avoid sugar-rich diet, milk, etc.
        ENDIF
        IF Patient is advised on medication THEN
            THEN Advise to avoid drugs known to cause acne (e.g., steroids, androgens, halogens).
        ENDIF
        IF Patient is advised on skincare THEN
            THEN Advise to avoid oily products, pomades, and cosmetics.
        ENDIF
        IF Patient needs education THEN
            THEN Educate patient about premenstrual flares and stress management.
        ENDIF
        IF Patient needs hygiene advice THEN
            THEN Advise washing/cleaning face to keep skin dry, shampooing scalp.
        ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment (Based on severity)
    IF Severity is Mild THEN
        THEN
            TREATMENT: Benzoyl peroxide 2.5% cream OR Azelaic acid 10% cream.
        ENDIF
    ELSEIF Severity is Moderate THEN
        THEN
            TREATMENT: Topical retinoid (e.g., Tretinoin 0.025% gel OR Adapalene 0.1% gel) AND Topical antibacterial (e.g., Clindamycin 1% cream OR Nadifloxacin 1% cream).
        ENDIF
    ELSEIF Severity is Severe THEN
        THEN
            TREATMENT: Topical therapy (as for moderate or mild) PLUS Systemic antibiotic OR Systemic retinoid.

            // Systemic Antibiotic Options
            IF Systemic antibiotic is chosen THEN
                IF Antibiotic is Azithromycin THEN
                    THEN Dosage: Tab. azithromycin 500 mg daily for 5 days in a month.
                ELSEIF Antibiotic is Doxycycline THEN
                    THEN Dosage: Cap. doxycycline 100 mg BD.
                ELSEIF Antibiotic is Minocycline THEN
                    THEN Dosage: Tab. minocycline 50/100 mg daily.
                ENDIF
            ENDIF

            // Systemic Retinoid Option
            IF Systemic retinoid is chosen THEN
                IF Retinoid is Isotretinoin THEN
                    THEN Dosage: Tab. isotretinoin 0.5-1 mg/kg daily for 3-6 months.
                    THEN Referral: Tertiary care centre recommended.
                ENDIF
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    // Hormonal Therapy
    IF Patient is female AND Acne is unresponsive to other treatments AND Patient has menstrual irregularity THEN
        THEN
            TREATMENT: Hormonal therapy (specific agent not specified in guideline).
        ENDIF

ENDIF
```
