```text
DISEASE: Acute Pericarditis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- NSAIDs: Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- Tab: Tablet
- mg: Milligram
- orally: By mouth
- q3-4h: Every 3-4 hours
- qd: Every day
- Anti tuberculosis treatment: Antituberculosis therapy
- Uremia: Uremic
- dialysis: Hemodialysis
- pericardial effusion: Fluid accumulation in the pericardial sac
- cardiac tamponade: Life-threatening condition caused by fluid accumulation in the pericardial sac
- pericardiocentesis: Procedure to remove fluid from the pericardial sac

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Pleuritic chest pain relieved by bending forward
- Breathlessness
- Tachycardia
- Fever
- Pleural rub
- Elevated ESR
- Irregular pulse
- Symmetric enlargement of cardiac silhouette (may be present)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Cause is Viral, Bacterial, Mycobacterial, Connective Tissue Disorder, Uremia, Malignancy, Radiation, or Trauma THEN
    IF Cause is Tuberculosis THEN
        Administer Anti tuberculosis treatment.
    ELSE IF Cause is Uremia THEN
        Administer dialysis.
    ELSE
        Administer pharmacological treatment.

IF Pharmacological treatment is indicated THEN
    IF Patient is able to take oral medication THEN
        Administer Tab. aspirin 650 mg orally every 3-4 hours.
    OR
        Administer other NSAIDs like Tab. indomethacin 100-150 mg orally daily in divided doses.

IF Patient develops pericardial effusion THEN
    Observe carefully.
    IF Cardiac tamponade develops THEN
        Perform pericardiocentesis immediately.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for aspirin or indomethacin in the context of acute pericarditis, but standard contraindications apply, e.g., allergy, GI bleeding risk, renal impairment for NSAIDs).
```
