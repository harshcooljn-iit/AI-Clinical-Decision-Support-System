```text
DISEASE: Anterior Uveitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)
- TDS: Ter in die (three times a day)
- QID: Quarter in die (four times a day)
- IOP: Intraocular pressure
- FML: Flurometholone
- CME: Cystoid macular edema
- KP: Keratic precipitates

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Symptoms: Pain, redness, photophobia, consensual photophobia, excessive tearing, decreased vision (acute); decreased vision (from vitreous debris, CME, or cataract), exacerbations/remissions (chronic).
- Slit lamp examination: Cells and flare in the anterior chamber, ciliary flush, keratic precipitates (KP).
- Ocular examination: Complete ocular examination, IOP check, dilated fundus examination, vitreous evaluation for cells.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:** Evaluate symptoms, slit lamp findings (cells, flare, KP), IOP, and fundus.

2.  **Non-pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF patient has symptoms like photophobia or pain, THEN advise wearing dark glasses.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Cycloplegics:**
    - IF inflammation is mild to moderate, THEN use cyclopentolate 1% eye drops BD.
    - IF inflammation is severe, THEN use atropine ointment 1% TDS.

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Topical Steroids:**
    - Use prednisolone acetate 1% eye drops.
    - IF inflammation is mild, THEN administer one drop 1 to 6 hourly, depending on severity.
    - IF inflammation is moderate to severe, THEN administer one drop 1 to 2 hourly initially.
    - Consider FML (flurometholone) 1% ophthalmic ointment at night.

5.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Periocular Steroids:**
    - IF anterior uveitis is severe AND not responding to topical steroids, THEN consider periocular repository steroids (e.g., triamcinolone 20 to 40 mg subtenon injection).

6.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Systemic Steroids/Immunosuppressives:**
    - IF no improvement on maximal topical and repository steroids, OR if the uveitis is bilateral and severe, THEN consider systemic steroids (prednisolone 1mg/kg/day) OR immunosuppressive therapy.

7.  **Treatment of Secondary Glaucoma:**
    - IF secondary glaucoma is present, THEN treat with beta blockers.
    - DO NOT use pilocarpine or prostaglandin analogues for secondary glaucoma in this context.

8.  **Treatment of Specific Etiologies:**
    - IF an exact etiology is determined (e.g., herpes, tuberculosis, ankylosing spondylitis), THEN provide appropriate systemic management for that etiology.

9.  **Follow-up:**
    - IF acute phase, THEN follow-up every 1 to 7 days, depending on severity.
    - IF stable, THEN follow-up every 1 to 6 months.
    - At each visit, evaluate anterior chamber reaction and IOP.

10. **Steroid Tapering:**
    - IF anterior chamber reaction is improving, THEN slowly taper steroid drops.
    - Tapering schedule example: QID for 1 week, then TDS for 1 week, then BD for 1 week.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Pilocarpine and prostaglandin analogues are contraindicated for treating secondary glaucoma associated with anterior uveitis.
```
