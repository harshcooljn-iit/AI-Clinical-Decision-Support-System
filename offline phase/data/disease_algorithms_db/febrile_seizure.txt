DISEASE: Febrile Seizure

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
- EEG: Electroencephalogram
- DPT: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- h: Hour
- tab: Tablet
- syr: Syrup
- IV: Intravenous
- rect: Rectal
- q8h: Every 8 hours
- q6h: Every 6 hours
- DPT: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus
- paracetamol: Acetaminophen

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Brief (2-5 min), generalized tonic-clonic seizure in a febrile child (6 months to 5 years) without underlying neurological disease.
- CSF examination may be done to rule out meningitis, especially in the first episode.
- EEG may be considered for atypical seizures or high-risk children.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient presents with a seizure during a febrile illness:
        THEN Proceed to seizure management.
    ELSE
        THEN Manage the underlying cause of fever.

2.  **Non-pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Clear the airway.
    - Place the patient in a semi-prone lateral position.
    - Administer oxygen therapy.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment (Acute Seizure):**
    IF Seizure is ongoing:
        THEN Administer anticonvulsants.
        IF Diazepam available:
            THEN Administer Diazepam 0.3 mg/kg by slow intravenous (IV) or rectal (rect) route.
            IF Seizure does not subside after first dose:
                THEN Repeat Diazepam.
                IF Given rectally:
                    THEN Dose may be up to 0.5 mg/kg/dose.
        ELSE IF Lorazepam available:
            THEN Administer Lorazepam (dosage not specified, use standard pediatric dose).

4.  **Fever Management:**
    - Identify and treat the cause of the fever.

5.  **Intermittent Prophylaxis (During Febrile Illness):**
    IF Considering intermittent prophylaxis during febrile illness:
        THEN Administer Diazepam.
        THEN Dosage: 0.3 mg/kg/dose every 8 hours (q8h) (total 1 mg/kg/day) for 2-3 days of febrile illness.
        THEN Start on the day of onset of fever.
        IF Over sedation or ataxia noted:
            THEN Adjust dose accordingly.

6.  **Continuous Prophylaxis:**
    IF Considering continuous prophylaxis:
        THEN Phenytoin and carbamazepine are ineffective.
        THEN Phenobarbitone has serious cognitive effects (contraindicated).
        THEN Sodium valproate is effective but risks outweigh benefits (generally not recommended).

7.  **Patient Education:**
    - Reassure parents about the benign nature of simple febrile seizures.
    - Educate on fever control measures.
    - Educate on administering rectal diazepam at home if prescribed.
    - Advise routine immunization.
    - After DPT vaccination: Administer oral paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose every 6 hours (q6h) for 2 or 3 days.
    - After measles vaccination: Administer oral paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose starting on the 4th day after vaccination and continue for 3 to 4 days.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Phenobarbitone: Contraindicated due to serious effects on cognitive function for continuous prophylaxis.
- Sodium valproate: Generally not recommended for continuous prophylaxis due to potential risks outweighing benefits.
