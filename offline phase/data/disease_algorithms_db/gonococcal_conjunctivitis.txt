DISEASE: Gonococcal Conjunctivitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- QID: Four times a day
- BD: Twice a day
- Tab.: Tablet
- g: gram
- mg: milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical symptoms: Severe purulent discharge, swelling of eyelids (onset 12-24 hours).
- Examination findings: Conjunctival papillae, marked chemosis, preauricular adenopathy. Cornea examination for peripheral ulcers (risk of perforation).
- Laboratory tests: Conjunctival scrapings for Gram stain (gram-negative intracellular diplococci) and culture/sensitivity.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Gram stain shows gram-negative intracellular diplococci OR high clinical suspicion of gonococcal conjunctivitis THEN:
    - Administer Inj. ceftriaxone 1 g intramuscularly as a single dose.

    IF corneal involvement exists OR cannot be excluded due to chemosis/eyelid swelling THEN:
        - Hospitalize the patient.
        - Administer Inj. ceftriaxone 1 g intravenously every 12 to 24 hours.

    IF patient is penicillin-allergic THEN:
        - Consider oral fluoroquinolone (e.g., Tab. ciprofloxacin 500 mg for 5 days) OR
        - Consider a single oral dose of Tab. azithromycin 1 g.

    - Administer topical ciprofloxacin ointment four times a day (QID) OR
    - Administer fluoroquinolone drops every two hours (e.g., gatifloxacin, moxifloxacin, or ciprofloxacin).

    - Perform saline irrigation four times a day (QID) until discharge resolves.

    - Treat for possible chlamydial co-infection:
        - Administer Tab. azithromycin 1 g as a single dose OR
        - Administer Tab. doxycycline 100 mg twice a day (BD) for 7 days.

    - Treat sexual partners with oral antibiotics for both gonorrhea and chlamydia (as described previously).

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Penicillin allergy: Contraindicates Inj. ceftriaxone (unless alternative regimens are used).
