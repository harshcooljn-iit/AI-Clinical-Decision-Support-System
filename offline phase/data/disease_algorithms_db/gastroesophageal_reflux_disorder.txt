DISEASE: Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- GERD: Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease
- GI: Gastrointestinal
- PPIs: Proton Pump Inhibitors
- LES: Lower Esophageal Sphincter
- Nissen fundoplication: A type of anti-reflux surgery

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Symptoms: Heartburn, regurgitation, dysphagia, cough, chest pain, wheezing, hoarseness, sore throat, otitis media, enamel erosion.
- Upper GI endoscopy: To check for complications like esophagitis, strictures, Barrett esophagus, hiatal hernia, masses.
- Manometry: To measure LES pressure and check for motility disorders.
- Optional studies: 24-hour pH probe test, nuclear medicine gastric emptying study.
- Diagnosis can be assumed based on symptoms and history for uncomplicated GERD.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has symptoms consistent with uncomplicated GERD:
    THEN assume diagnosis of GERD and begin empirical therapy.
ELSE IF patient shows signs of GERD complications or other illness or does not respond to therapy:
    THEN consider further diagnostic testing (endoscopy, manometry, pH probe, gastric emptying study).

IF treatment goals are symptom control, healing esophagitis, and preventing recurrence/complications:
    THEN use a stepwise approach involving lifestyle modification and medical therapy (antacids, PPIs) or surgical treatment.

IF lifestyle modifications are considered:
    THEN advise losing weight (if overweight), avoiding alcohol, chocolate, citrus juice, tomato-based products, peppermint, coffee, and possibly the onion family.
    ALSO advise avoiding large meals, waiting 3 hours after meals before lying down, and elevating the head of the bed 8 inches.
