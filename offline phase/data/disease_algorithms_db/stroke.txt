1.  DISEASE: Stroke

2.  ABBREVIATIONS:
    *   TIA: Transient Ischemic Attacks
    *   ABC: Airway, Breathing, Circulation
    *   CT: Computed Tomography
    *   MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
    *   BP: Blood Pressure
    *   AF: Atrial Fibrillation
    *   INR: International Normalized Ratio
    *   GI: Gastrointestinal
    *   HCT: Hematocrit
    *   PTT: Partial Thromboplastin Time
    *   rtPA: recombinant tissue plasminogen activator
    *   MCA: Middle Cerebral Artery
    *   OD: Once Daily
    *   SC: Subcutaneous
    *   IV: Intravenous
    *   stat: Immediately

3.  DIAGNOSTICS:
    *   Abrupt onset of neurologic deficit attributable to a focal vascular cause.
    *   TIA: Neurologic signs/symptoms resolve within 24 hours.
    *   Brain imaging (CT or MRI) to differentiate ischemia from hemorrhage.

4.  TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    *   **Initial Assessment & Stabilization:**
        *   IF patient presents with stroke symptoms:
            *   Assess ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation).
            *   Treat hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia.
            *   Obtain brain imaging (CT preferred for early hemorrhage, MRI for early infarct).

    *   **Blood Pressure Management:**
        *   IF BP is >185/110 mm Hg AND thrombolytic therapy is anticipated:
            *   Lower BP.
        *   IF BP is >180 mmHg systolic OR >130 mmHg diastolic (Hemorrhagic Stroke):
            *   Institute antihypertensive agents.
            *   Goal: Maintain mean arterial pressure < 130 mmHg.

    *   **Fever Management:**
        *   IF patient has fever:
            *   Treat with antipyretics.
            *   Use surface cooling.

    *   **Glucose Management:**
        *   Maintain euglycemia.

    *   **Cerebral Edema Management:**
        *   IF patient develops cerebral edema causing herniation:
            *   Elevate head.
            *   Administer Tab. furosemide 40 mg OD.
            *   Consider hyperventilation.

    *   **Complication Prevention:**
        *   Prevent infections (pneumonia, urinary, skin).
        *   Prevent deep venous thrombosis (DVT) with pulmonary embolism.

    *   **Intravenous Thrombolysis (Ischemic Stroke):**
        *   IF patient meets indications for thrombolysis (see Table 1 criteria):
            *   Administer rtPA (recombinant tissue plasminogen activator) according to dosage schedule in Table 1.
            *   Monitor BP frequently.
            *   Avoid other antithrombotic treatment for 24 hours.
            *   IF neurologic decline or uncontrolled BP occurs during infusion:
                *   Stop infusion.
                *   Administer cryoprecipitate.
                *   Reimage brain emergently.
            *   Avoid urethral catheterization for 2 hours.

    *   **Aspirin Therapy:**
        *   Administer Tab. aspirin 75 mg OD (for general stroke management).
        *   Recommend Tab. aspirin 75-325 mg/day for secondary prevention of stroke and TIA.

    *   **Heparin Therapy:**
        *   Administer Inj. heparin 5000 IU SC (mentioned but not elaborated in algorithm, likely outdated or specific context).

    *   **Hemorrhagic Stroke Specifics:**
        *   Administer Inj. mannitol 20% 1g/kg stat followed by 0.5 g/kg IV infusion.
        *   Administer Inj. furosemide 40 mg IV stat.
        *   Elevate head end.
        *   Consider hyperventilation.
        *   IF platelet count < 50,000/µl:
            *   Transfuse fresh platelets.
        *   IF cerebellar hemorrhage OR depressed mental status with hydrocephalus:
            *   Urgent neurosurgical evaluation.

    *   **Secondary Prevention:**
        *   Control risk factors (hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidemia, smoking cessation).
        *   IF patient has chronic nonvalvular AF or untreated rheumatic heart disease with AF:
            *   Administer Tab. warfarin with target INR 2-3.

5.  TABULAR DOSAGES:
    *   **Table 1: Indications, contraindications and dosage schedule of thrombolytic agents in stroke**

        *   **Indications:**
            *   Clinical diagnosis of stroke.
            *   Onset of symptom to drug administration < 3 hours.
            *   CT scan showing no hemorrhage or edema of >1/3rd of middle cerebral artery (MCA) territory.
            *   Age > 18 years.
            *   Consent of patient.

        *   **Contraindications:**
            *   Sustained BP > 185/110 mm Hg despite treatment.
            *   Platelets < 100,000.
            *   HCT < 25%.
            *   Glucose < 50 or > 400 mg/dl.
            *   Use of heparin within 48 h and prolonged PTT, or elevated INR.
            *   Rapidly improving symptoms.
            *   Prior stroke or head injury within 3 months; prior intracranial hemorrhage.
            *   Major surgery in preceding 14 days.
            *   Minor stroke symptoms.
            *   GI bleeding in preceding 21 days.
            *   Recent myocardial infarction.
            *   Coma or stupor.

        *   **Administration:**
            *   Administer rtPA (recombinant tissue plasminogen activator like alteplase or tenecteplase) 0.9 mg/kg IV (maximum 90 mg) IV as 10% of total dose by bolus, followed by remainder of total dose over 1 h.
            *   Frequent cuff blood pressure monitoring.
            *   No other antithrombotic treatment for 24 h.
            *   For decline in neurologic status or uncontrolled blood pressure, stop infusion, give cryoprecipitate, and reimage brain emergently.
            *   Avoid urethral catheterization for 2 h.

6.  CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    *   **Thrombolysis (rtPA):**
        *   IF BP is sustained > 185/110 mm Hg despite treatment.
        *   IF Platelets are < 100,000.
        *   IF HCT is < 25%.
        *   IF Glucose is < 50 or > 400 mg/dl.
        *   IF Heparin was used within 48 hours AND PTT is prolonged OR INR is elevated.
        *   IF Symptoms are rapidly improving.
        *   IF Prior stroke or head injury within 3 months OR prior intracranial hemorrhage.
        *   IF Major surgery within 14 days.
        *   IF Minor stroke symptoms.
        *   IF GI bleeding within 21 days.
        *   IF Recent myocardial infarction.
        *   IF Coma or stupor.
    *   **Platelet Transfusion:**
        *   NOT indicated unless platelet count is < 50,000/µl (in the context of hemorrhagic stroke).
