DISEASE: Premature Rupture Of Membranes

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PROM: Premature Rupture Of Membranes
- IM: Intramuscular
- IV: Intravenous
- GBS: Group B streptococcus
- Inj.: Injection

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Confirmed by typical odour of amniotic fluid.
- May be difficult to confirm if rupture is not recent or leakage is gradual.
- Vaginal pad examination (visual and odour) after one hour.
- Speculum examination (fluid from cervix, pool in fornix, gush on coughing).
- Nitrazine test (alkaline amniotic fluid).
- Ferning test (fern-leaf pattern on dried fluid slide).
- Clinical presentation based on symptoms and signs (Table 2).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - Confirm gestational age.
    - Per speculum examination for vaginal discharge (amount, colour, odour) and exclude urinary incontinence.

2.  **Diagnosis Confirmation:**
    - IF typical odour of amniotic fluid is present, THEN confirm diagnosis.
    - ELSE IF membrane rupture is not recent or leakage is gradual:
        - Place vaginal pad over vulva and examine after one hour (visual and odour).
        - On speculum exam, look for fluid from cervix or pool in fornix, or gush on coughing.
        - IF available, perform nitrazine or ferning tests.

3.  **Clinical Presentation Assessment (Based on Table 2):**
    - IF presenting symptoms are watery vaginal discharge AND signs are sudden gush or intermittent leaking of fluid, fluid seen at introitus, no contractions within 1 hour, THEN probable diagnosis is PROM.
    - IF presenting symptoms are foul smelling watery discharge AND signs are fever/chills, abdominal pain, history of loss of fluid, tender uterus, rapid fetal heart rate, light vaginal bleeding, THEN probable diagnosis is Amnionitis.
    - IF presenting symptoms are foul smelling vaginal discharge AND signs are no history of loss of fluid, itching, frothy/curdish discharge, abdominal pain, dysuria, THEN probable diagnosis is Vaginitis or cervicitis.
    - IF presenting symptoms are bloody vaginal discharge AND signs are abdominal pain, loss of fetal movements, heavy prolonged vaginal bleeding, THEN probable diagnosis is Antepartum haemorrhage.

4.  **Management based on Gestational Age and Signs/Symptoms:**

    - IF there is vaginal bleeding with intermittent or constant abdominal pain, THEN suspect abruptio placentae.

    - IF there are signs of infection (fever, foul-smelling vaginal discharge), THEN treat as Amnionitis (see Amnionitis section).

    - IF there are NO signs of infection AND pregnancy is less than 37 weeks:
        - Give antibiotics to reduce maternal and neonatal infective morbidity and delay delivery.
        - Antibiotic options:
            - Tab. erythromycin 250 mg by mouth three times per day for seven days
            - OR amoxicillin 500 mg by mouth three times per day for seven days
        - Consider transfer to appropriate service for newborn care.
        - Give corticosteroids to improve fetal lung maturity:
            - betamethasone 12 mg IM, two doses 24 hours apart
            - OR Inj. dexamethasone 6 mg IM, four doses 12 hours apart
        - IF corticosteroids are used, THEN ensure there is no frank infection.

    - IF there are NO signs of infection AND pregnancy is 37 weeks or more:
        - IF membranes have been ruptured for more than 18 hours, THEN give prophylactic antibiotics to reduce GBS infection in the neonate.
            - Antibiotic options:
                - penicillin G 2 million units IV every six hours until delivery
                - OR Inj. ampicillin 2 g IV every six hours until delivery
        - Assess the cervix.
        - IF cervix is favourable (soft, thin, partly dilated), THEN induce labour using oxytocin.
        - IF cervix is unfavourable (firm, thick, closed), THEN ripen the cervix using prostaglandins and infuse oxytocin OR deliver by caesarean section.

### Amnionitis Management:

1.  **Antibiotics:** Give a combination of antibiotics until delivery.
    - Inj. ampicillin 2 g IV every six hours
    - PLUS gentamicin 5 mg/kg body weight IV every 24 hours

2.  **Postpartum/Post-Caesarean Care:**
    - IF vaginal delivery, THEN discontinue antibiotics postpartum.
    - IF caesarean section, THEN continue antibiotics and give Inj. metronidazole 500 mg IV every eight hours until the woman is fever-free for 48 hours.

3.  **Labour Management:**
    - Assess the cervix.
    - IF cervix is favourable (soft, thin, partly dilated), THEN induce labour using oxytocin.
    - IF cervix is unfavourable (firm, thick, closed), THEN ripen the cervix using prostaglandins and infuse oxytocin OR deliver by caesarean section.

4.  **Additional Considerations:**
    - IF metritis is suspected (fever, foul-smelling vaginal discharge), THEN give antibiotics.
    - IF newborn sepsis is suspected, THEN arrange for a blood culture and antibiotics.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Corticosteroids (betamethasone or dexamethasone) SHOULD NOT be used in the presence of frank infection.
