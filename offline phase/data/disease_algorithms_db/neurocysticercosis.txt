DISEASE: Neurocysticercosis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CNS: Central Nervous System
- T. solium: Taenia solium
- EITB: Enzyme-linked immunoelectrotransfer blot
- ELISA: Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay
- CT: Computed Tomography
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
- EEG: Electroencephalography
- PO: Per os (by mouth)
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)
- TDS: Ter in die sumpsit (three times a day)
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Absolute criteria: Histologic/microscopic exam of biopsy material showing cysticercus, visualization of parasite in the eye by fundoscopy, or neuro-radiologic demonstration of cystic lesion with characteristic scolex.
- Major criteria: Neuro-radiologic lesions suggestive of neuro-cysticercosis, demonstration of antibodies to cysticercus in serum, or resolution of intracranial cystic lesions spontaneously or after therapy with albendazole or praziquantel alone.
- Minor criteria: Neuro-imaging studies compatible with neuro-cysticercosis, clinical manifestations suggestive of neuro-cysticercosis, demonstration of antibodies to cysticercus or cysticercan antigen in CSF by ELISA, or evidence of cysticercosis outside the CNS.
- Epidemiologic criteria: Residence in endemic area, frequent travel to endemic area, or household contact with T. solium infection.
- Diagnosis confirmed by one absolute criterion OR combination of two major criteria, one minor criterion, and one epidemiologic criterion.
- Investigations: EITB (serum), ELISA (serum/CSF), CT scan, MRI, soft tissue radiography, EEG (children with intractable seizures), stool examination (ova).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has T. solium tapeworms in stool:
    THEN Administer Praziquantel 5-10 mg/kg single dose PO.

IF Patient requires treatment for neurocysticercosis cysts:
    THEN Administer Albendazole 400 mg PO BD for 14 days with meals.

IF Patient has seizures:
    THEN Administer Phenytoin 300 mg PO.
    IF Patient is pediatric:
        THEN Administer Phenytoin 4-8 mg/kg/day PO divided BD/TDS.

IF Patient requires treatment to avoid inflammatory response in CNS:
    THEN Start anticonvulsants.
    THEN Start high dose glucocorticoids.

IF Patient has cysts that have failed to resolve with anti-helminthic treatment AND are causing severe neurologic sequelae:
    THEN Reserve neurosurgical intervention.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for Albendazole, Praziquantel, Phenytoin, or Glucocorticoids in the context of Neurocysticercosis. Standard contraindications for these drugs would apply based on patient-specific factors not detailed here).
