DISEASE: Acute Pyogenic Osteomyelitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- CRP: C-Reactive Protein
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- Inj.: Injection
- Syr.: Syrup
- Cap.: Capsule
- Tab.: Tablet
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- g: Gram
- Staph.: Staphylococcus
- Pseud.: Pseudomonas
- Paed.: Paediatric

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Classic symptoms: Localized pain, fever, swelling in metaphyseal area, warmth, limited range of motion of adjacent joint.
- Lab findings: Pus aspirated from bone, positive bone or blood culture for pyogenic organism.
- Imaging: Radiographic changes typical of acute osteomyelitis (soft tissue swelling, periosteal reaction, lytic areas in metaphysis), usually appearing 7-10 days after onset.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - IF patient has symptoms suggestive of acute pyogenic osteomyelitis (localized pain, fever, swelling, warmth, limited motion) AND/OR positive bone/blood culture OR typical radiographic changes, THEN proceed to treatment.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Rest, splintage, limb elevation, sponging for fever.
    - IF pus aspirated from bone, THEN drain pus (orthopaedic surgeon) and send for culture/sensitivity.
    - Splintage duration: 4-6 weeks typically, may be longer (months) for extensive destruction.
    - Gradually mobilize limb and permit gradual weight bearing after splintage.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Initial IV Antibiotics:**
    - Start broad-spectrum IV antibiotics based on likely organism (_Staphylococcus aureus_ most common).
    - Antibiotic choice depends on suspected organism and local resistance patterns:
        - IF _Staphylococcus aureus_ suspected (MSSA), THEN start Inj. cloxacillin 50-100 mg/kg/day in four divided doses for 1-2 weeks.
        - OR IF _Staphylococcus aureus_ suspected (MRSA), THEN start Inj. vancomycin by IV infusion:
            - IF Neonate (upto 1 week), THEN 15 mg/kg initially, then 10 mg/kg every 12 hours.
            - IF Infant (1-4 weeks), THEN 15 mg/kg initially, then 10 mg/kg every 8 hours.
            - IF Child (over 1 month), THEN 10 mg/kg every 6 hours.
            - OR Alternatively, use linezolid in appropriate dose.
        - OR IF _Pseudomonas aeruginosa_ suspected (resistant to gentamicin/amikacin), THEN start Inj. amikacin 15 mg/kg/day in 2-3 divided doses.
        - OR IF patient is hypersensitive to penicillins and cephalosporins, THEN start Inj. clindamycin 40 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 1-2 weeks.
        - OR IF broad coverage needed (e.g., Gram-negatives), THEN start Inj. ceftriaxone 100 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses (max 2 g/day) for 1-2 weeks.
        - OR IF broad coverage needed (e.g., Gram-negatives), THEN start Inj. cefotaxime 100-200 mg/kg/day by IV infusion or IM or IV in 2-4 divided doses for 1-2 weeks.
        - OR IF gentamicin coverage needed (e.g., Gram-negatives), THEN start Inj. gentamicin 5-7.5 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses for 1-2 weeks.

4.  **Duration of IV Antibiotics:**
    - Continue IV antibiotics until favorable clinical response is achieved (decrease in swelling/fever, improved well-being/movement, falling ESR/CRP).
    - Typical duration: 1-2 weeks.

5.  **Transition to Oral Antibiotics:**
    - IF favorable clinical response after 1-2 weeks of IV therapy, THEN switch to oral antibiotics.
    - Duration of total antibiotic therapy (IV + Oral): 4-6 weeks.

6.  **Oral Antibiotic Choice:**
    - Choice depends on culture/sensitivity report.
    - IF culture report unavailable, THEN use:
        - Syr./Cap. cloxacillin 50-100 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 3-4 weeks.
        - OR Syr./Cap. cephalexin 25-50 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 3-4 weeks.

7.  **Monitoring:**
    - Monitor therapy by clinical response (decrease swelling/fever, improved well-being/movement).
    - Monitor lab markers: ESR and CRP (CRP is better indicator).
    - Repeat X-ray after 7-10 days of symptoms to assess bone destruction/damage.

8.  **Referral:**
    - Refer to orthopaedic surgeon IF:
        - Pus aspirated from bone.
        - Acute osteomyelitis suspected in adult.
        - Acute osteomyelitis suspected in diabetic patient.
        - Acute osteomyelitis suspected in haemodialysis patient.
        - Acute osteomyelitis suspected in IV drug user.
        - Acute osteomyelitis suspected in patient with orthopaedic implant.
        - Acute osteomyelitis suspected in immunocompromised host.

9.  **Symptomatic Treatment:**
    - Oral Tab. paracetamol 500 mg for fever.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Inj. cloxacillin: IF patient is hypersensitive to penicillin.
- Inj. gentamicin: IF patient has renal impairment or known hypersensitivity.
- Inj. amikacin: IF patient has renal impairment or known hypersensitivity.
- Inj. ceftriaxone: IF patient is hypersensitive to cephalosporins or has severe biliary obstruction.
- Inj. cefotaxime: IF patient is hypersensitive to cephalosporins.
- Inj. clindamycin: IF patient has a history of severe colitis or known hypersensitivity.
- Inj. vancomycin: IF patient has known hypersensitivity (red man syndrome, etc.).
- Syr./Cap. cloxacillin: IF patient is hypersensitive to penicillin.
- Syr./Cap. cephalexin: IF patient is hypersensitive to cephalosporins.
