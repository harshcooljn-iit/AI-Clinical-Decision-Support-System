DISEASE: Chikungunya

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Tab: Tablet
- GI: Gastrointestinal
- O' nyong – nyong virus: O'nyong'nyong virus
- Aedes aegypti: Aedes aegypti mosquito
- Tab. paracetamol: Paracetamol tablet
- Tab. hydroxychloroquinine: Hydroxychloroquine tablet
- Tab. chloroquinine phosphate: Chloroquine phosphate tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Acute onset fever, severe joint pain, maculopapular rash.
- Incubation period: 2-4 days.
- Symptoms: Fever, severe joint pain (arthralgia), headache, maculopapular rash on trunk and extensor surfaces.
- Course: Initial fever (1-4 days), afebrile period (3 days), return of fever with rash (3-6 days), followed by recovery. Arthropathy can persist for months to years.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:** Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Chikungunya (fever, severe joint pain, rash).

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    -   Suggest mild exercises and physiotherapy during recovery.
    -   Suggest exposure to a warm environment.
    -   Suggest non-weight-bearing exercises.
    -   Consider surgery for severely damaged joints (long-term).

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment (Acute Stage):**
    -   **Fever, Headache, Pain Management:**
        -   IF symptoms (fever, headache, pain) are present:
            -   THEN administer Tab. paracetamol 1 gm 3-4 times a day.
        -   ELSE:
            -   No specific pharmacological treatment for fever/pain indicated.

4.  **Medication Avoidance:**
    -   IF patient is being treated for fever or pain:
        -   THEN AVOID Tab. aspirin due to risk of GI side effects and Reye's syndrome.
        -   THEN AVOID steroids due to risk of GI side effects.

5.  **Refractory Arthralgia Treatment:**
    -   IF arthralgia persists despite symptomatic treatment (e.g., paracetamol):
        -   THEN consider Tab. hydroxychloroquinine 200mg orally once daily for 4 weeks.
        -   OR consider Tab. chloroquinine phosphate 300mg per day for 4 weeks.
    -   ELSE:
        -   Continue symptomatic treatment.

6.  **Hydroxychloroquine Monitoring:**
    -   IF Tab. hydroxychloroquinine is administered:
        -   THEN monitor for potential side effects: retinal damage, elevated liver enzymes.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
-   Tab. aspirin: Contraindicated due to risk of GI side effects and Reye's syndrome.
-   Steroids: Contraindicated due to risk of GI side effects.
