DISEASE: Influenza Infection

ABBREVIATIONS:
- BD: Twice daily
- IV: Intravenous
- RT-PCR: Reverse Transcription Polymerase Chain Reaction
- CDC: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention
- H1N1: Hemagglutinin 1, Neuraminidase 1
- H3N2: Hemagglutinin 3, Neuraminidase 2
- H3N1: Hemagglutinin 3, Neuraminidase 1
- ARDS: Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
- PaO2: Partial pressure of oxygen
- SpO2: Peripheral capillary oxygen saturation
- SBP: Systolic Blood Pressure
- HEPA: High-Efficiency Particulate Air
- RT-PCR: Reverse Transcription Polymerase Chain Reaction
- Hb: Hemagglutinin B (likely referring to H2 in the context of probable case definition)

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Confirmed case: Acute febrile respiratory illness with laboratory confirmation (realtime RT-PCR, viral culture, or fourfold increase in H1N1 virus specific neutralizing antibodies).
- Probable case: Acute respiratory illness positive for influenza A but negative for H2 and HB by RT-PCR.
- Suspected case: Acute febrile respiratory illness with close contact with a confirmed swine-origin influenza case or travel to/residence in a community with confirmed cases.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has confirmed, probable, or suspected H1N1 infection THEN
    // Treatment
    IF patient is < 15 kg THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 30 mg BD for 5 days.
    ELSE IF patient is 15-23 kg THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 45 mg BD for 5 days.
    ELSE IF patient is 24 ≤ 40 kg THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 60 mg BD for 5 days.
    ELSE IF patient is > 40 kg THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 75 mg BD for 5 days.
    ENDIF

    // Infants specific dosing
    IF patient age is < 3 months THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 12 mg BD for 5 days.
    ELSE IF patient age is 3-5 months THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 20 mg BD for 5 days.
    ELSE IF patient age is 6-11 months THEN
        Administer oseltamivir 25 mg BD for 5 days.
    ENDIF

    // Supportive Therapy
    Administer IV fluids, parenteral nutrition, oxygen therapy, or ventilatory support as needed.
    Administer antibiotics for secondary infection if present.
    Administer vasopressors for shock if present.
    Administer paracetamol or ibuprofen for fever, myalgia, and headache.
    Advise patient to drink plenty of fluids.
    Advise smokers to avoid smoking.
    For sore throat, consider topical decongestants, saline nasal drops, throat lozenges, and steam inhalation.

    // Oxygen Therapy
    IF patient has tachypnea, dyspnea, respiratory distress, or SpO2 < 90% THEN
        Supplement with oxygen therapy.
        Oxygen device choice depends on severity (e.g., oxygen cannula, simple mask, partial re-breathing mask, non re-breathing mask, oxygen hood/head box for children).
    ENDIF

    // Mechanical Ventilation
    IF patient has severe pneumonia and acute respiratory failure (SpO2 < 90% and PaO2 < 60 mmHg with oxygen therapy) THEN
        Support with mechanical ventilation (invasive preferred, non-invasive if unavailable).
        Use HEPA filters on ventilator circuit/high flow oxygen masks.
    ENDIF

    // Hydration and Nutrition
    Maintain hydration, electrolyte balance, and nutrition.

    // Discharge
    IF laboratory reports are negative THEN
        Discharge patient after completing the full course of oseltamivir.
    ENDIF

    // Follow-up
    IF patient has strong epidemiological criteria (even with negative tests) THEN
        Follow up the patient.
    ENDIF

    // Corticosteroids
    IF patient has persisting septic shock (SBP < 90) THEN
        Consider low dose corticosteroids (Tab. hydrocortisone 200-400 mg/day).
    ENDIF

    // Antibiotics
    IF patient has pneumonia THEN
        Administer antibiotics as per local guidelines.
    ENDIF

    IF patient is on mechanical ventilation THEN
        Administer antibiotics prophylactically.
    ENDIF

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
IF patient has influenza infection THEN
    DO NOT administer salicylate / aspirin due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
ENDIF
