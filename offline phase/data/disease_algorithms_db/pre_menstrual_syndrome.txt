DISEASE: Pre Menstrual Syndrome

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PMS: Pre Menstrual Syndrome
- NSAIDs: Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs
- mg: milligram
- tab: tablet
- day: day
- luteal phase: Luteal phase
- max: maximum
- Or: Or
- OCPs: Oral Contraceptive Pills

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Cyclic recurrence of physical, psychological, or behavioral symptoms after ovulation.
- Symptoms resolve after the onset of menstruation.
- Diagnosis confirmed by excluding other medical or psychiatric disorders.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF symptoms are severe and interfere with lifestyle, relationships, and occupational functioning THEN proceed with treatment.

# Non-pharmacological treatment (First line)
IF patient is diagnosed with PMS THEN
    - Offer daily charting of symptoms for two months.
    - Offer dietary modifications: increase complex carbohydrates, reduce/eliminate salts, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol (especially in the luteal phase); several small meals per day.
    - Offer moderate regular aerobic exercise (e.g., brisk walk 1-2 miles per day for 4-5 days/week).
    - Offer stress management courses/counseling.
ENDIF

# Pharmacological treatment

# Step 1: Initial Pharmacological Options
IF patient is diagnosed with PMS THEN
    - Option 1: Tab. pyridoxine 100 mg/day for 10-14 days (during luteal phase).
        - Maximum daily dose is 150 mg.
    - Option 2: Tab. Evening primrose oil 500 mg 3 times a day.
ENDIF

# Step 2: For Predominantly Physical Symptoms (e.g., bloatedness, irritability, swelling, weight gain, breast tenderness)
IF patient has predominantly physical symptoms AND symptoms persist THEN
    - Option 1: Tab. spironolactone 100 mg/day (during luteal phase).
    - Option 2: Tab. bromocriptine 1.25 - 5 mg/day (during luteal phase) for mastalgia.
        - IF side effects (nausea, vomiting) are very severe THEN consider vaginal administration.
ENDIF

# Step 3: For Predominantly Emotional Symptoms (if no relief from previous measures in 2-3 cycles)
IF symptoms are predominantly emotional AND no relief from previous measures in 2-3 cycles THEN
    - Consider Tab. fluoxetine 5-20 mg/day (preferably in consultation with a psychiatrist).
ENDIF

# Step 4: For Non-Responders (if no relief from previous therapies)
IF patient is a non-responder to previous therapies THEN
    - Consider ovulation suppression.
    - Option: Low dose combined oral contraceptive pills (OCPs), 1 pill daily from day 5 to day 25 of the cycle.
ENDIF

- Tab. pyridoxine: Not specified in the text.

- NSAIDs (e.g., mefenamic acid): Not specified in the text.
- Tab. spironolactone: Not specified in the text.

- Tab. fluoxetine: Not specified in the text.
- Low dose combined oral contraceptive pills: Standard contraindications for OCPs apply (e.g., history of VTE, certain migraines, uncontrolled hypertension, etc. - not detailed here).
