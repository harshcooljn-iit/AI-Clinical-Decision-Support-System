DISEASE: Trigeminal Neuralgia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- t.d.s: three times a day
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Sudden, sharp, severe or short duration, electric shock-like pain.
- Presence of trigger zones involving trigeminal nerve areas.
- Unilateral sharp, shooting, lancinating pain provoked by touch, washing face, shaving, or cold wind exposure.
- Pain occurs in recurrent bouts of attacks, lasting seconds to minutes.
- Diagnostic dose of carbamazepine is given to establish diagnosis.
- Evaluation of any primary lesion in the brain by MRI.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Trigeminal Neuralgia:
    THEN Administer a diagnostic dose of carbamazepine.
    AND Perform MRI to evaluate for any primary brain lesion.
    IF Pain intensity and frequency require treatment:
        THEN Start carbamazepine 200 mg three times a day (t.d.s).
        AND Refer patient to a Dentist for further evaluation and treatment.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for carbamazepine in Trigeminal Neuralgia, but general contraindications apply, e.g., hypersensitivity, porphyria, bone marrow suppression, severe hepatic impairment).
