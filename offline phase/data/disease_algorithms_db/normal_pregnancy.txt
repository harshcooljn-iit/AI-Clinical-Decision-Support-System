1. DISEASE: Normal Pregnancy

2. ABBREVIATIONS:
    - BP: Blood Pressure
    - cals: calories
    - ICT: Indirect Coomb's test
    - MMR: Measles, Mumps, Rubella
    - mcg: microgram
    - IM: Intramuscular
    - HIV: Human Immunodeficiency Virus
    - DFMC: Daily foetal movement charting
    - APH: Antepartum hemorrhage
    - Rh: Rhesus factor

3. DIAGNOSTICS:
    - Clinical examination (BP, weight-gain, oedema, cardio-vascular, respiratory, breast examination, symphyseal-fundal height, presentation, foetal heart rate(s), amniotic fluid volume, foetal movement charting, pelvic assessment).
    - Laboratory tests (Haemoglobin, urine routine and microscopy, blood groups and Rh typing, screen for syphilis, hepatitis B infection, HIV, cervical cytology).
    - Ultrasound (for foetal anomalies if indicated).
    - Indirect Coomb's test (ICT) for Rh negative women.

4. TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    IF Pregnancy is confirmed THEN
        # Diet
        IF Woman is physically active THEN
            Caloric requirement depends on physical work.
            Increased requirement is 300 cals/day.
        ELSE
            Caloric requirement depends on activity level.
            Increased requirement is 300 cals/day.
        ENDIF
        Diet should be light, nutritious, easily digestible, rich in proteins, vitamins, and minerals.
        Diet should include at least 0.5 liters of milk daily.
        Diet should include plenty of green leafy vegetables (one katori serving per meal).
        Diet should include fruits as available.
        Diet should include a balanced combination of cereals and pulses.
        Diet should emphasize foods rich in iron (green vegetables, jaggery) and protein (nuts).
        Fat consumption can be predominantly from animal sources for vitamins A & D.

        # Exercise
        IF Woman is accustomed to exercise THEN
            Continue exercise, but not to the point of fatigue.
            Antenatal exercises under medical supervision are useful.
        ELSE
            No new exercise should be initiated.
            Sedentary women should only walk.
        ENDIF
        IF Woman has multiple foetuses OR complications (heart disease, pregnancy induced hypertension, intrauterine growth retardation, history of preterm labour, APH, threatened abortion) THEN
            Do not exercise.
        ENDIF

        # Clothing
        Clothing should be non-constricting.

        # Travel
        Road travel is allowed with a safety belt.
        Traveling in a pressurized aircraft is allowed; walk about every 2 hours.

        # Employment
        Jobs requiring prolonged standing (>8 hours/day) are associated with a minimum risk of pre-term delivery.

        # Clinical check up
        During each antenatal visit:
            - Check BP.
            - Check weight gain.
            - Check for oedema in feet.
            - Perform cardio-vascular examination.
            - Perform respiratory examination.
            - Perform breast examination.
            - Measure symphyseal-fundal height.
            - Assess presentation.
            - Check foetal heart rate(s).
            - Assess amniotic fluid volume.
            - Inquire about daily foetal movement charting.
            - Perform pelvic assessment at 38 weeks.

        # Rh Negative Women Management
        IF Woman is Rh negative AND Husband is Rh positive THEN
            Monitor with Indirect Coomb's test (ICT) at:
                - First visit
                - 28 weeks
                - 34-36 weeks
            IF ICT is negative THEN
                Recommend Immunoglobulin 300 mcg IM at 28 weeks of pregnancy.
                Recommend Immunoglobulin 300 mcg IM postpartum if baby is Rh positive.
            ELSE IF ICT is positive THEN
                Manage only at centers with facilities for amniocentesis and/or cordocentesis, amniotic fluid optical density estimation, and intrauterine foetal transfusions.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        # Medications
        - Tab. folic acid 5 mg once daily, starting at least 3 months before conception and continuing till 12 weeks of gestation.
        - Tab. iron folic acid (containing 60-100 mg elemental iron and folic acid) once daily.
        - Tab. calcium gluconate 500 mg twice daily from 16 weeks till 3 months postpartum.

        # Immunization
        - Administer 2 doses of tetanus toxoid 4-6 weeks apart.
        - MMR vaccines are contraindicated.
        - Other vaccines can be administered if indicated.

        # Investigations during pregnancy
        - Haemoglobin
        - Urine (routine and microscopy)
        - Blood groups and Rh typing
        - Screen for syphilis
        - Screen for hepatitis B infection
        - Screen for HIV
        - Cervical cytology
        - Ultrasound for foetal anomalies if indicated
        - Diabetes screening

        # Patient Education
        - Advise on delivery preparation (when to go, what to bring, emergency procedures).
        - Explain danger signals requiring immediate reporting (vaginal bleeding, convulsions, severe headaches with blurred vision, severe abdominal pain, fast/difficult breathing, escape of fluid, change in foetal movements, persistent vomiting, oliguria).
        - Advise to go to health center immediately for signs like swelling on face/hands, abdominal pain, fever, dysuria, feeling ill.
        - Explain black staining of stools due to oral iron.
        - Advise taking iron and calcium supplements at different times (at least 2 hours apart).
        - Counsel on birth spacing and family planning.
        - Advise on correct and consistent use of condoms for dual protection (STI/HIV and pregnancy).
        - Advise avoiding sexual intercourse when abortion/preterm labour threatens, and during the last 4 weeks of pregnancy.

        # Bowel Issues
        - Constipation, fissures, and haemorrhoids are common; treat with fluids, exercise, bulk laxatives, and stool softeners.

        # Contraindications
        - Smoking, alcohol, and drug abuse are contraindicated.
        - Caffeine is best avoided.

        # Daily foetal movement charting (DFMC)
        - After 28 weeks, the woman should keep a foetal movement count.

        # Follow-up visits
        - Every 4 weeks until 28 weeks.
        - Every 2 weeks until 36 weeks.
        - Weekly thereafter.
    ENDIF
