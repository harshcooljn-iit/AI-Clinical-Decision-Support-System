DISEASE: Migraine

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PO: Per Os (by mouth)
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)
- OD: Omne die (once a day)
- NSAIDs: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs
- Tab: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Repeated attacks of headache lasting 4–72 hours.
- Normal physical examination.
- No other reasonable cause for the headache.
- At least 2 of the following features:
    - Unilateral pain.
    - Throbbing pain aggravated by movement.
    - Moderate or severe intensity.
- Along with one of the following features:
    - Nausea/vomiting.
    - Photophobia.
    - Phonophobia.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient presents with symptoms consistent with Migraine diagnosis (as per DIAGNOSTICS) THEN
        Proceed to treatment options.
    ELSE
        Consider alternative diagnoses.
    ENDIF

2.  **Non-pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Advise avoidance of migraine triggers (e.g., lack of sleep, chocolates, wine).
    - Advise suitable lifestyle modifications.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Acute Attack:**
    IF Migraine attack is mild to moderate THEN
        - Consider combination of acetaminophen, aspirin, and caffeine.
    ELSEIF Migraine attack is moderate to severe OR NSAIDs/analgesics fail THEN
        IF Patient can take oral tablets THEN
            - Option 1: Administer Tab. sumatriptan 50 mg PO.
            - Option 2: Administer Tab. sumatriptan 100 mg PO.
            - Option 3: Administer Tab. zolmitriptan 2.5 mg PO.
        ELSEIF Patient has early nausea or difficulty taking tablets THEN
            - Option 1: Administer zolmitriptan 5 mg nasal spray.
            - Option 2: Administer sumatriptan 20 mg nasal spray.
        ELSE
            - Consider parenteral administration of drugs like dihydroergotamine or sumatriptan for rapid relief.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Preventive Therapy:**
    IF Patient has increasing frequency of migraine attacks (e.g., five or more attacks a month) OR attacks are unresponsive/poorly responsive to abortive treatments THEN
        Consider preventive agents.
        - Option 1: Administer Tab. propranolol 40–120 mg BD.
        - Option 2: Administer Tab. sodium valproate 400–600 mg BD.
        - Option 3: Administer Tab. topiramate 25–200 mg/d OD.
    ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the provided text for specific drugs, but general contraindications for each drug class would apply based on standard medical practice).
