DISEASE: Cardiomyopathy

ABBREVIATIONS:
- HOCM: Hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy
- LVH: Left ventricular hypertrophy
- JVP: Jugular Venous Pressure
- NSAIDs: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs
- ACE: Angiotensin-converting enzyme
- B-agonists: Beta-agonists

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical features (fever, chest pain, basal rales, pedal edema, cardiomegaly, syncope, ventricular ectopy, LVH, raised JVP, diffuse dyskinetic apex beat, S3, hepatomegaly, peripheral edema, murmurs of mitral and tricuspid regurgitation).
- Exclusion of hypertension, congenital or acquired valvular, coronary or pericardial abnormalities as the primary cause.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# General Cardiomyopathy Treatment
IF disease is Cardiomyopathy THEN
    # Non-pharmacological treatment
    Salt restriction
    Alcohol discontinuation

    # Pharmacological treatment (Heart Failure Standard Therapy)
    # Diuretics
    IF patient needs diuretics THEN
        IF thiazide diuretic is indicated THEN
            hydrochlorothiazide 6.25–50 mg OR chlorthalidone 25–50 mg
        ENDIF
        IF loop diuretic is indicated THEN
            furosemide 40–80 mg
        ENDIF
        IF aldosterone antagonist is indicated THEN
            spironolactone 25–100 mg
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    # Beta blockers
    IF patient needs beta blocker THEN
        atenolol 25–100 mg OR metoprolol 25–100 mg OR propranolol 40–160 mg
    ENDIF

    # ACE inhibitors
    IF patient needs ACE inhibitor THEN
        captopril 25–200 mg OR lisinopril 10–40 mg OR ramipril 2.5–20 mg
    ENDIF

    # Angiotensin II antagonists (ARBs)
    IF patient needs Angiotensin II antagonist THEN
        losartan 25–100 mg OR valsartan 80–320 mg OR candesartan 2–32 mg
    ENDIF

    # Calcium antagonists
    IF patient needs calcium antagonist THEN
        nifedipine (long-acting) 30–60 mg OR verapamil (long-acting) 120–360 mg OR diltiazem (long-acting) 180-420 mg
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM) Specific Treatment
IF disease is Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy THEN
    # First-line drug
    $\beta$ blockers

    # Other effective drugs
    Calcium channel blockers, especially verapamil (for symptomatic patients)

    # Diuretics
    Diuretics for heart failure symptoms

    # Refractory cases
    Dual chamber pacing

    # Specific procedure
    Nonsurgical septal ablation with alcohol injection in septal branches
ENDIF

# Drugs to be avoided
IF disease is Dilated Cardiomyopathy THEN
    AVOID Calcium channel blockers
    AVOID NSAIDs
ENDIF

IF disease is Hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy (HOCM) THEN
    AVOID Digitalis
    AVOID Nitrates
    AVOID Vasodilators
    AVOID B-agonists
ENDIF
