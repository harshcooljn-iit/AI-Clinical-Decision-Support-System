```text
DISEASE: Epistaxis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ENT: Ear, Nose, and Throat
- CT: Computed Tomography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation of bleeding through the nose.
- Investigations may include complete hemogram, coagulation profile, blood grouping and typing, X-ray paranasal sinuses, CT paranasal sinuses (if necessary).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient shows signs of hemodynamic compromise THEN
    Perform resuscitative measures.
ENDIF

IF epistaxis is present THEN
    Consider cauterization.
ENDIF

IF epistaxis is present THEN
    Consider anterior nasal packing.
ENDIF

IF epistaxis is present THEN
    Consider posterior nasal packing.
ENDIF

IF epistaxis is present AND conservative measures fail OR specific etiology requires intervention THEN
    Consider surgical treatment (endoscopic cautery and/or ligation of vessels).
ENDIF

PATIENT EDUCATION:
IF patient is a child THEN
    Advise to avoid inserting fingers into the nose.
ENDIF

IF patient has epistaxis THEN
    Explain first aid: pinch the nose tightly and apply an ice pack over the nose while waiting for medical attention.
ENDIF
```
