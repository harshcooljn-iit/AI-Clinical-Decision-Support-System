DISEASE: Myiasis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Tab.: Tablet
- mg: milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on the presence of fly larvae (maggots) in body tissues or wounds.
- Identification of the type of myiasis (creeping, furuncle, wound).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Myiasis THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    APPLY liquid paraffin OR turpentine oil to the affected area.
    REMOVE larvae mechanically using forceps.
    EDUCATE patient on personal hygiene and proper wound care.

    // Pharmacological treatment
    IF secondary infection is present THEN
        ADMINISTER systemic antibiotics.
    ENDIF

    // Systemic antihelminthic treatment
    ADMINISTER Tab. albendazole 400mg as a single dose (stat).

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- No specific contraindications listed for the treatments mentioned (liquid paraffin, turpentine oil, mechanical removal, albendazole, antibiotics) in this guideline. Standard contraindications for these medications apply based on patient-specific factors (e.g., allergies, liver/kidney disease for albendazole, specific antibiotic contraindications).
