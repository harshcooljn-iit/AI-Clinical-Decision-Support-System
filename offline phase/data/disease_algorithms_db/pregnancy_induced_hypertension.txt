DISEASE: Pregnancy Induced Hypertension (PIH)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PIH: Pregnancy Induced Hypertension
- BP: Blood Pressure
- mmHg: Millimeters of Mercury
- POG: Period of Gestation
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- USG: Ultrasonography
- IUGR: Intrauterine Growth Restriction
- DIC: Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation
- ECG: Electrocardiogram
- BP: Blood Pressure

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Hypertension diagnosed when diastolic BP is ≥ 90 mmHg on two consecutive readings taken ≥ 4 hours apart.
- PIH is hypertension occurring after 20 weeks of gestation, during labor, or within 48 hours of delivery.
- Chronic hypertension is hypertension occurring before 20 weeks of gestation.
- Pre-eclampsia is hypertension with proteinuria.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Disease is Pregnancy Induced Hypertension (PIH) or Pre-eclampsia:
  THEN Hospitalize the patient.
  THEN Definitive therapy is termination of pregnancy.
  THEN Decision between immediate delivery and expectant management depends on:
    - Severity of disease
    - Condition of mother and fetus
    - Period of Gestation (POG)

IF Disease is Mild Pre-eclampsia:
  THEN Consider expectant management if:
    - Gestational age is < 37 weeks
    - There is no maternal or fetal compromise
  THEN Non-pharmacological treatment:
    - Complete bed rest, preferably in left lateral position
    - Regular diet adequate in proteins and calories
    - Omit extra table salt
  THEN Pharmacological treatment:
    - Start antihypertensive treatment if diastolic BP is persistently > 100 mmHg.
    - Aim for systolic BP ~ 130 mmHg and diastolic BP ~ 90 mmHg.
    - Option 1: Tab. methyldopa 250 mg every 8 hours OR every 6 hours (maximum dose 2 g/day).
    - Option 2: Tab. atenolol 50-100 mg once a day.
    - IF BP is not controlled in 72 hours with Option 1 or 2:
      - Add one of the following:
        - Cap. nifedipine 10 mg every 8 hours OR Tab. nifedipine retard 10 mg every 12 hours (maximum 30 mg every 12 hours).
        - Tab. labetalol 100-200 mg every 8 hours (maximum 600 mg every 6 hours).
  THEN Monitoring:
    - Daily: weight gain, BP, urine albumin, urine output.
    - Weekly: haemogram with platelet count, liver function tests, kidney function tests, serum uric acid, fundoscopy.
    - Fetal monitoring: clinical assessment, USG growth assessment, daily fetal movement count, non-stress test twice weekly, biophysical score weekly, Doppler studies if IUGR.
  THEN Discharge criteria (for mild PIH without proteinuria):
    - IF after hospitalization, BP is controlled with rest without antihypertensives.
    - IF no maternal/fetal compromise is detected.
    - IF the patient is reliable and will follow monitoring instructions and report ominous symptoms immediately.
  THEN Ominous symptoms: persistent severe headache, visual disturbances (dimness, double vision, blindness), epigastric pain, nausea, vomiting, oliguria.
  THEN Induction of pregnancy (definitive management) IF:
    - Gestational age ≥ 37 weeks.
    - Fetal compromise (severe growth retardation, oligohydramnios, abnormal non-stress test or biophysical score).
    - Maternal compromise (development of features of severe pre-eclampsia).
    - Onset of labor, rupture of membranes, or bleeding.

IF Disease is Severe Pre-eclampsia:
  THEN Treatment preferably in a tertiary care center.
  THEN Non-pharmacological treatment:
    - Observation in intensive care unit for 24 hours.
    - Assessment of maternal and fetal conditions.
    - BP monitoring every 2-4 hours.
    - Hourly urine output monitoring.
    - Watch for signs/symptoms of impending eclampsia and fetal distress.
  THEN Lab Investigations:
    - Haemogram with platelet count.
    - Liver function tests.
    - Kidney function tests.
    - Urinary proteins.
    - Coagulation profile.
    - Fundus examination.
    - Obstetric ultrasound with BPS.
  THEN Intravenous fluids: Ringer's lactate at 60 ml/h (maximum 125 ml/h).
  THEN Pharmacological treatment (aim: gradual lowering of BP, maintain diastolic BP 90-100 mmHg):
    - Immediate management:
      - Option 1: Tab. nifedipine 10 mg orally, repeat after 30-60 min (maximum dose 20 mg every 4 hours).
        - CAUTION: Side effects - Tachycardia, headache, flushing, aggravation of angina. Rapid BP fall can cause fetal distress.
      - IF BP not controlled with oral treatment:
        - Start IV drugs with intensive monitoring.
        - Option 2: Inj. labetalol initial dose 50 mg slow IV, followed by infusion at 60 mg/h, doubling every 15 min until BP controlled or maximum dose 220 mg in 24 hours reached.
    - Maintenance therapy:
      - After initial control, start maintenance therapy with antihypertensives (as described for mild pre-eclampsia).
      - Bed rest and more frequent maternal and fetal monitoring.
  THEN Definitive management: Induction of pregnancy.
    - IF Pregnancy > 34 weeks: Stabilize maternal condition and induce pregnancy.
    - IF Pregnancy < 24 weeks: Stabilize maternal condition and induce pregnancy.
  THEN Expectant management criteria (if considered):
    - Pregnancy between 24-34 weeks.
    - Hypertension controlled with maximum of two antihypertensive drugs.
    - Normal urine output.
    - Normal lab investigations.
    - No fetal compromise.
    - Patient hospitalized until delivery.
  THEN Indications for termination (induction or C-section):
    - Gestational age ≥ 37 weeks.
    - Fetal compromise (severe growth retardation, oligohydramnios, abnormal non-stress test or biophysical score).
    - Maternal compromise (development of features of severe pre-eclampsia).
    - Onset of labor, rupture of membranes, or bleeding.
  THEN Indications for Caesarean section:
    - Deeply unconscious patient (unless delivery is imminent).
    - Uncooperative patient due to restlessness.
    - Unlikely vaginal delivery within 6-8 hours from onset of 1st eclamptic seizure.
    - Eclamptic seizures not controlled in 6-8 hours.
    - Other obstetric indications.
