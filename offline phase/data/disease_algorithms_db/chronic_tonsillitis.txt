DISEASE: Chronic Tonsillitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Cap.: Capsule
- mg: Milligram
- TDS: Three times a day
- OD: Once a day
- Tab.: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Repeated sore throat attacks, odynophagia, fever, cough, throat irritation, halitosis, thick speech, difficulty breathing/swallowing/snoring.
- Physical examination: Uniformly enlarged/congested tonsils or small/inflamed tonsils (chronic follicular/fibrotic), congested anterior pillars, enlarged non-tender jugulodigastric nodes, Irwin Moore's sign (purulent discharge extrusion).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
IF Patient has Chronic Tonsillitis THEN
    // Pharmacological Treatment
    IF Symptoms are present THEN
        // Antibiotics
        IF Patient is suitable for amoxicillin THEN
            Administer Cap. amoxicillin 500mg TDS for 5-7 days
        ELSE IF Patient is suitable for azithromycin THEN
            Administer Tab. azithromycin 500mg OD for 5-7 days
        ENDIF

        // Analgesic/Antipyretic
        Administer Tab. paracetamol 500mg TDS for 5 days

        // Supportive Care
        Recommend Bed rest
    ENDIF

    // Surgical Treatment
    IF Recurrent episodes of tonsillitis THEN
        Consider Surgery – tonsillectomy
    ENDIF
ENDIF
