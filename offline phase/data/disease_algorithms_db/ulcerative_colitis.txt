```text
DISEASE: Ulcerative Colitis (UC)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- UC: Ulcerative Colitis
- IV: Intravenous
- NIL BY MOUTH: Nothing by mouth

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Sigmoidoscopic examination
- Mucosal biopsies

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# Mild to moderate acute ulcerative colitis (distal/left colonic involvement)
IF Disease severity is mild to moderate AND involvement is distal/left colonic THEN
    # Oral Medications
    IF Patient is taking oral medication THEN
        # Option 1: Sulphasalazine
        IF Drug choice is Sulphasalazine THEN
            Dosage: Tab. sulphasalazine 1 g 3-4 times a day.
        # Option 2: Mesalazine
        ELSE IF Drug choice is Mesalazine THEN
            Dosage: Tab. mesalazine 800 mg 3-4 times a day.
        # Option 3: Olsalazine
        ELSE IF Drug choice is Olsalazine THEN
            Dosage: Tab. olsalazine 1-3 g/day in divided doses.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    # Enema/Foam (Rectal)
    IF Patient requires rectal medication THEN
        # Option 1: Prednisolone phosphate enema
        IF Rectal medication choice is Prednisolone phosphate enema THEN
            Dosage: 20 mg in 100 ml saline 1-2 times a day.
        # Option 2: Hydrocortisone enema
        ELSE IF Rectal medication choice is Hydrocortisone enema THEN
            Dosage: 100-125 mg in 100 ml saline 1-2 times a day (prepare fresh).
        # Option 3: Hydrocortisone foam (for rectal disease only)
        ELSE IF Disease is limited to rectum AND Rectal medication choice is Hydrocortisone foam THEN
            Dosage: 125 mg 1-2 times a day.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# Moderate to severe or extensive acute disease
IF Disease severity is moderate to severe OR disease is extensive THEN
    # Oral Medications (Initial)
    IF Patient is taking oral medication THEN
        # Option 1: Sulphasalazine
        IF Drug choice is Sulphasalazine THEN
            Dosage: Tab. sulphasalazine 1 g 3-4 times a day.
        # Option 2: Mesalazine
        ELSE IF Drug choice is Mesalazine THEN
            Dosage: Tab. mesalazine 800 mg 3-4 times a day.
        # Option 3: Olsalazine
        ELSE IF Drug choice is Olsalazine THEN
            Dosage: Tab. olsalazine 1-3 g/day in divided doses.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    # Oral Steroids
    Dosage: Tab. prednisolone 20-60 mg/day in single or divided doses.
ENDIF

# Follow-up (Mild to Moderate)
IF Symptoms do not improve or worsen THEN
    Action: Hospitalization required.
ENDIF

# Acute severe disease with systemic symptoms (requires hospitalization)
IF Disease severity is acute severe AND systemic symptoms are present THEN
    # IV Steroids
    IF Patient is receiving IV medication THEN
        # Option 1: Hydrocortisone IV
        IF Drug choice is Hydrocortisone IV THEN
            Dosage: Inj. hydrocortisone 100 mg IV 4 times a day.
        # Option 2: Dexamethasone IV
        ELSE IF Drug choice is Dexamethasone IV THEN
            Dosage: Inj. dexamethasone 4 mg IV 3-4 times a day.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    # Supportive Care
    Action: Keep patient 'nil by mouth'.
    Action: Give adequate intravenous fluids and electrolytes.
    Action: Give blood transfusion as per requirement.

    # Transition to Oral Therapy
    IF Patient is improving after 5 days THEN
        Action: Switch over to oral steroids.
        Action: Start aminosalicylates as in I.
        Action: Allow patient to take orally.
    ENDIF

    # Steroid Failure
    IF Patient fails to respond to steroids THEN
        Action: Refer patient to gastroenterologist for immunosuppressive therapy or surgery.
    ENDIF

    # Steroid Tapering
    IF Remission is induced THEN
        Action: Taper steroids slowly over 4-6 weeks period.
    ENDIF

    # Steroid Duration
    Action: Do not give steroids for more than 12 weeks for an acute attack.
ENDIF

# Maintenance of remission
IF Patient requires maintenance therapy THEN
    Action: Give any of the drugs used in I lifelong.
ENDIF

# Patient education
IF Patient requires follow-up THEN
    Action: Follow up at 6 monthly intervals.
    Action: Continue maintenance treatment.
ENDIF

# Surveillance (Long-standing disease)
IF Disease duration is more than 10 years THEN
    Action: Regular sigmoidoscopy and rectal biopsy at 6 monthly intervals to look for dysplasia.
    Action: Total colonoscopic examination at 2-3 year interval.
    Action: Explain chronic nature of disease, continuation of maintenance treatment, regular follow-up, and risk of colonic cancer after 10 years.
ENDIF

# Complications Monitoring
Action: Close clinical/biochemical/radiological monitoring required for complications like toxic megacolon/perforation.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(None specified in the text for the listed drugs, but general contraindications apply based on drug class and patient factors not mentioned here).
```
