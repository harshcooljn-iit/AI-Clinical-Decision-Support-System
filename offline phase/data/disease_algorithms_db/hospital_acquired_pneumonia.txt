1.  DISEASE: Hospital Acquired Pneumonia (HAP) / Ventilator Associated Pneumonia (VAP)

2.  ABBREVIATIONS:
    *   HAP: Hospital Acquired Pneumonia
    *   VAP: Ventilator Associated Pneumonia
    *   IV: Intravenous
    *   OD: Once Daily
    *   TDS: Three Times Daily
    *   QID: Four Times Daily
    *   BD: Twice Daily
    *   MDR: Multidrug Resistant
    *   ARDS: Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
    *   PEEP: Positive End-Expiratory Pressure
    *   Fio2: Fraction of Inspired Oxygen
    *   CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
    *   ICU: Intensive Care Unit
    *   H2: Histamine H2-receptor antagonist

3.  DIAGNOSTICS:
    *   Pneumonia developing >48 hours after hospital admission (HAP).
    *   New onset of fever >48 hours after endotracheal intubation (VAP).
    *   New or progressive radiological deterioration PLUS any two of the following:
        *   New onset of fever
        *   Purulent discharge
        *   Leukocytosis
        *   Decline in oxygenation
        *   Purulent sputum
        *   Increased respiratory secretions
        *   Dyspnea or tachypnea
        *   Altered mental status
    *   For VAP in ARDS: Sustained increase in PEEP requirement >2.5 cm H2O or rise in Fio2 requirement >0.15.
    *   Blood culture before antibiotics (if clinically stable).

4.  TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    IF Patient has HAP/VAP:
        THEN Determine risk factors for MDR pathogens.

        IF Patient has NO risk factors for MDR pathogens:
            THEN Treat for 10-14 days with ONE of the following regimens:
                *   ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD
                *   moxifloxacin 400 mg IV OD
                *   ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV TDS
                *   levofloxacin 750 mg IV OD
                *   ampicillin/sulbactam 3 g IV QID

        ELSE IF Patient HAS risk factors for MDR pathogens:
            THEN Treat for 10-14 days with the following combination:
                *   Choose ONE Beta-lactam:
                    *   ceftazidime 2 g IV TDS
                    *   cefepime 2 g IV BD/TDS
                    *   piperacillin/tazobactam 4.5 g IV QID
                    *   imipenem 500 mg IV QID or 1 g IV TDS
                    *   meropenem 1 g IV TDS
                *   **PLUS** Choose ONE agent active against gram-negative bacteria:
                    *   gentamicin 7 mg/kg IV OD
                    *   tobramycin 7 mg/kg IV OD
                    *   amikacin 20 mg/kg IV OD
                    *   ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV TDS
                    *   levofloxacin 750 mg IV OD
                *   **PLUS** Choose ONE agent active against gram-positive bacteria:
                    *   linezolid 600 mg IV BD
                    *   vancomycin 15 mg/kg (up to 1 g) IV BD

    IF Patient is clinically unstable:
        THEN Do not delay treatment for diagnostic sampling.

    IF Patient is seriously ill:
        THEN Consider daily chest radiography until weaning off mechanical ventilation.

5.  CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    *   (None specified in the text for the listed drugs, but standard contraindications apply, e.g., allergy, renal/hepatic impairment for some agents).
    *   Specific contraindications for individual drugs (e.g., vancomycin - Red Man Syndrome, ototoxicity, nephrotoxicity; fluoroquinolones - tendon rupture, QTc prolongation; beta-lactams - allergy) are not detailed here but should be considered.
