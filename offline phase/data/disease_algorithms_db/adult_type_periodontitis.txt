DISEASE: Adult Type Periodontitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Cap.: Capsule
- Tab.: Tablet
- mg: Milligram
- q.i.d.: Four times a day
- b.i.d.: Twice a day
- Halitosis: Bad breath

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Swollen gums, bleeding from gums (spontaneous or while brushing).
- Pain on eating hard food, difficulty chewing, dull gum pain.
- Pus discharge from gum on pressing.
- Loosening of teeth, gum recession.
- Slowly progressive destruction of periodontium, loss of periodontal attachment, presence of periodontal pocket.
- Bad breath (Halitosis), sensitivity to hot & cold.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Non-pharmacological treatment
IF Patient has Adult Type Periodontitis THEN
    - Refer patient to a dentist for oral prophylaxis (thorough scaling and root planning).
    - Advise brushing twice daily (after breakfast and after dinner) with a super soft toothbrush for at least 3 minutes.
ENDIF

// Pharmacological treatment - Local therapy
IF Patient has Adult Type Periodontitis THEN
    - Advise antiplaque toothpaste containing antibiotics.
    - Advise Gel metronidazole massage on gums twice daily.
    - Advise rinsing with 0.2% chlorhexidine mouthwash twice daily (effective after scaling).
ENDIF

// Pharmacological treatment - Systemic therapy
IF Patient has Adult Type Periodontitis THEN
    // Standard Systemic Therapy
    - Administer Cap. tetracycline 250 mg four times a day (q.i.d.) for 5-7 days.

    // Very Deep Pockets Therapy
    IF Patient has very deep periodontal pockets THEN
        - Administer Tab. ciprofloxacin 500 mg twice a day (b.i.d.) for 5-7 days.
        - Administer Tab. tinidazole 300 mg twice a day (b.i.d.) for 5-7 days.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Follow-up
IF Patient received systemic therapy THEN
    - Recheck the depth of periodontal pockets.
    IF Pocket depth persists THEN
        - Refer patient to a dentist for further management.
    ENDIF
ENDIF
