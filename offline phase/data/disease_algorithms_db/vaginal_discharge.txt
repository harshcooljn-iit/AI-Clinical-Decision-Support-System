DISEASE: Vaginal Discharge

ABBREVIATIONS:
- RTI: Reproductive Tract Infections
- STI: Sexually Transmitted Infections
- NACO: National AIDS Control Organisation
- T.: Tablet
- Inj.: Injection
- MU: Million Units
- PID: Pelvic Inflammatory Disease

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical assessment based on symptoms (vaginal discharge).
- Speculum examination to assess discharge characteristics and presence of cervical mucopus.
- Partner history (presence of urethral discharge or genital sore).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient complains of vaginal discharge:
    IF partner has urethral discharge or genital sore:
        Perform speculum examination.
        IF cervical mucopus is present:
            Rx for gonorrhoea and chlamydia.
        ELSE IF curd-like vaginal discharge is present:
            Rx for gonorrhoea, chlamydia, and candidiasis.
        ELSE IF other abnormal vaginal discharge is present:
            Rx for gonorrhoea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, and bacterial vaginosis.
        ELSE IF no abnormal discharge is present:
            Rx for gonorrhoea and chlamydia.
    ELSE:
        Perform speculum examination.
        IF cervical mucopus is present:
            Rx for gonorrhoea and chlamydia.
        ELSE IF curd-like vaginal discharge is present:
            Rx for candidiasis.
        ELSE IF other abnormal vaginal discharge is present:
            Rx for trichomoniasis and bacterial vaginosis.
        ELSE IF no abnormal discharge is present:
            No treatment.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Kit 4 (Genital Ulcer Disease – Non Herpetic for patients allergic to Penicillin) is indicated ONLY for patients allergic to Penicillin.
