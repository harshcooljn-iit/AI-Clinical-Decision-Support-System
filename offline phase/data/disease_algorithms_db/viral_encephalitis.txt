```text
DISEASE: Viral Encephalitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- HSV: Herpes simplex virus
- VZV: Varicella zoster virus
- EBV: Epstein-Barr virus
- CMV: Cytomegalovirus
- ICP: Intracranial pressure

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Acute or subacute onset of fever, headache, altered consciousness, focal neurological symptoms.
- CSF analysis as in acute bacterial meningitis.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Etiology is HSV, VZV, or EBV THEN
    ADMINISTER Acyclovir
    IF Patient age/weight is not specified THEN
        ADMINISTER Acyclovir 10 mg/kg every 8 hours
    ENDIF
    FOR Duration of 14 to 21 days
    MONITOR for side effects: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, Neurotoxicity (lethargy or obtundation, disorientation, confusion, agitation, hallucinations, tremors, seizures)
ENDIF

IF Etiology is CMV THEN
    ADMINISTER Ganciclovir
    IF Patient age/weight is not specified THEN
        ADMINISTER Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg every 12 hours, followed by 5 mg/kg
    ENDIF
    FOR Indefinite duration
    MONITOR for side effects: Granulocytopenia, thrombocytopenia, Gastrointestinal side effects (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain)
ENDIF

IF Etiology is California encephalitis (LaCrosse) virus, severe adenovirus, or rotavirus encephalitis THEN
    ADMINISTER Ribavirin
    IF Patient age/weight is not specified THEN
        ADMINISTER Ribavirin 15 mg/kg in divided doses every 8 hours
    ENDIF
    FOR Duration not specified
    MONITOR for side effects: Hemolysis, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain
ENDIF

BASIC MANAGEMENT:
- Careful monitoring of ICP.
- Fluid restriction.
- Avoidance of hypotonic intravenous solutions.
- Suppression of fever.

SEIZURE MANAGEMENT:
- Treat seizures with standard anticonvulsant regimens.
- Consider prophylactic therapy for severe cases due to high seizure frequency.
```
