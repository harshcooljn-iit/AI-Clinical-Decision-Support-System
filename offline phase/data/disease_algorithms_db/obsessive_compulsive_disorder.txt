DISEASE: Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

ABBREVIATIONS:
- SSRIs: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors
- CBT: Cognitive Behavior Therapy
- ECTs: Electroconvulsive Therapy
- OCD: Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Characterized by recurrent irrational intrusive thoughts, images, or impulses (obsessions) that are ego-dystonic (distressing).
- Presents with anxiety-relieving mental and physical acts (compulsions).
- Common obsessions include fear of dirt/contamination, fear of terrible events, forbidden/sexual thoughts.
- Common compulsions include excessive hand washing, bathing, checking, ordering, arranging.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - IF patient has symptoms consistent with OCD (recurrent obsessions and compulsions causing distress/impairment), THEN proceed to treatment options.
    - ELSE, consider alternative diagnoses.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF patient is willing and able to engage in therapy, THEN consider behavioral therapy (Exposure and Response Prevention - ERP) as the treatment of choice.
    - Other psychotherapy options include Cognitive Behavior Therapy (CBT), psychoanalytical, or psychodynamic therapies.
    - For resistant cases, consider ECTs or surgery.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - IF patient requires pharmacological treatment, THEN consider the following options:

    - **SSRIs:**
        - IF patient is starting an SSRI (fluoxetine, fluvoxamine, paroxetine, sertraline), THEN start with a low dose (e.g., 10 mg for paroxetine, specific starting doses not detailed for others but generally low).
        - Gradually increase the dose up to a maximum of 50 mg/day, based on response and tolerability.

    - **Clomipramine:**
        - IF patient is starting clomipramine, THEN start with 75 mg sustained release preparation once daily.
        - Gradually increase the dose depending on the severity of symptoms, up to a maximum of 300 mg/day.
        - Clomipramine is the only effective tricyclic antidepressant for OCD.

    - **Augmentation:**
        - IF response to SSRI or clomipramine is inadequate, THEN consider augmenting agents like sodium valproate, lithium, or carbamazepine.

    - **Adjuvant Treatment:**
        - IF needed, benzodiazepines may be used as adjuvant treatment.

4.  **Patient Education:**
    - Reassure the patient that OCD is treatable despite being distressing.
    - Inform the patient that therapeutic response may take about 2 weeks.
    - Advise the patient that side effects may occur before therapeutic effects.
    - Counsel the patient on potential side effects (e.g., dry mouth, constipation, postural hypotension for clomipramine; GI distress, nausea, headache, nervousness, anorexia, restlessness, sexual side effects for fluoxetine).
    - Advise the patient that treatment may be long-term and not to discontinue medication without medical advice.
    - Instruct the patient to contact the clinician immediately if untoward effects occur.
    - Encourage the patient to continue regular activities.
    - Suggest complementary measures like yoga, meditation, and physical exercises.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for the listed medications, but standard contraindications for each drug class would apply, e.g., MAOI use with SSRIs/clomipramine, specific conditions for valproate, lithium, benzodiazepines).
