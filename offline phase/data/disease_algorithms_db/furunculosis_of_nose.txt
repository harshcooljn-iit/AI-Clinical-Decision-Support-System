DISEASE: Furunculosis Of Nose

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Cap: Capsule
- Tab: Tablet
- IV: Intravenous
- mg: milligram
- kg: kilogram
- hrly: hourly
- q.i.d.: four times a day
- prn: as needed
- DM: Diabetes Mellitus

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Severe pain and tenderness over the tip of the nose, possible headache, malaise, pyrexia.
- Examination findings: Congestion and swelling of the nasal vestibule.
- Etiology: Acute infection of a hair follicle, typically caused by _Staphylococcus aureus_.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Furunculosis of Nose THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    APPLY Local moist heat to enhance localization and promote drainage.

    // Pharmacological treatment - Antibiotics
    IF Patient is an adult THEN
        ADMINISTER Cap. amoxicillin 500mg 8hrly for 5-7 days
        OR
        ADMINISTER Cap. amoxicillin 250/500mg plus clavulanic acid 125mg 8 hrly for 5-7 days
    ELSE IF Patient is a child THEN
        ADMINISTER Cap. amoxicillin 25-50mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses for 5-7 days
        OR
        ADMINISTER Cap. amoxicillin 250/500mg plus clavulanic acid 125mg 8 hrly for 5-7 days
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment - Analgesics/Antipyretics
    ADMINISTER Tab ibuprofen 400-600mg 3 times a day for 5 days
    OR
    ADMINISTER Tab paracetamol 500 mg 6hrly for 2-3 days, then as needed until pain and fever subside.

    // Pediatric Analgesics/Antipyretics
    IF Patient is a child THEN
        ADMINISTER Tab paracetamol 10mg/kg/dose 6-8hrly
    ENDIF

    // Monitoring and Escalation
    MONITOR Patient regularly for improvement in pain, tenderness, and inflammation within 24-48 hours.

    IF Improvement is not seen OR Spreading facial cellulitis develops THEN
        HOSPITALIZE Patient.
        ADMINISTER Systemic IV antibiotics.
    ENDIF

    // Recurrent Cases
    IF Patient has recurrent furunculosis THEN
        INVESTIGATE Patient for Diabetes Mellitus (DM).
    ENDIF

ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- No specific contraindications listed for the mentioned treatments in this guideline. Standard contraindications for antibiotics (e.g., penicillin allergy for amoxicillin) and analgesics (e.g., NSAID allergy/GI issues for ibuprofen, liver issues for paracetamol) apply but are not detailed here.
