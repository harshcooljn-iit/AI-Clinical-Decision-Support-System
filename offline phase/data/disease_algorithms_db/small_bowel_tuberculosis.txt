DISEASE: Small Bowel Tuberculosis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- CRP: C-Reactive Protein
- X Ray: Radiography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Symptoms: Weight loss, chronic cough, malaise, evening rise of temperature with sweating, vague abdominal pain with distension, alternating constipation and diarrhea. May present with peritonitis, perforation, or obstruction. Doughy abdomen.
- Investigations: Anemia, raised ESR, raised CRP, positive sputum culture, localized collection on abdominal ultrasound, pulmonary infiltrates on Chest X Ray, multiple small bowel strictures (ileum, subhepatic cecum) on Barium meal and follow through.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has Small Bowel Tuberculosis THEN
    // General Management
    BEGIN
        Vigorous supportive care is mandatory.
        Full drug treatment is mandatory.
    END

    // Surgical Treatment Considerations
    IF Patient requires surgical treatment THEN
        BEGIN
            // Symptomatic Strictures
            IF Patient has symptomatic strictures THEN
                BEGIN
                    Appropriate resections should be performed.
                END

            // Acute Intestinal Obstruction (Distal Ileal Stricture)
            IF Patient has acute intestinal obstruction from distal ileal stricture THEN
                BEGIN
                    Thorough resuscitation is required.
                    Side-to-side ileo-transverse bypass should be performed.
                    // Post-Recovery
                    ONCE Patient has recovered with medical treatment THEN
                        Second stage definitive procedure: Right hemicolectomy should be done.
                    END
                END

            // One-Stage Resection and Anastomosis
            IF Patient's general condition permits AND Patient has a resectable lesion THEN
                BEGIN
                    One-stage resection and anastomosis can be done.
                END

            // Perforation
            IF Patient has perforation THEN
                BEGIN
                    Appropriate local resection and anastomosis should be performed.
                    // OR (if patient condition is very poor)
                    IF Patient's condition is very poor THEN
                        Exteriorization should be performed.
                        // Post-Recovery
                        ONCE Patient is fully recovered from anti-tuberculous therapy THEN
                            Restoration of bowel continuity should be done.
                    END
                END
        END
    END
END
