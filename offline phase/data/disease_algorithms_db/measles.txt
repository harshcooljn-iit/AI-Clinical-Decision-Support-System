DISEASE: Measles

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IU: International Units
- IM: Intramuscular
- MMR: Measles, Mumps, Rubella vaccine
- PEM: Protein Energy Malnutrition

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Characterized by fever, cough, coryza, conjunctivitis.
- Erythematous maculopapular rash appearing on the 4th day.
- Pathognomonic enanthem (Koplik spots).
- Rash starts behind ears/hairline, spreads to face, trunk, limbs.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient has symptoms suggestive of Measles (fever, cough, coryza, conjunctivitis, rash, Koplik spots) THEN proceed to treatment.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    IF Patient has Measles THEN
        - Isolate patient from others, especially unimmunized children, for at least 4 days after rash appearance.
        - Provide bed rest and cold sponging if febrile.
        - Ensure small frequent feeds and plenty of oral fluids.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment:**
    IF Patient has Measles THEN
        - No specific antiviral treatment.
        - Manage fever with oral paracetamol.

4.  **Vitamin A Supplementation:**
    IF Patient has Measles THEN
        - Administer Inj. vitamin A 100,000 IU IM or high dose oil-based oral preparation (50,000 IU) for 2 consecutive days.
        - IF there are signs of vitamin A deficiency, give a third dose 4 weeks later.

5.  **Treatment of Co-existing Problems:**
    IF Patient has Measles AND secondary bacterial infection (e.g., bronchopneumonia, gastrointestinal infection) THEN
        - Treat appropriately.

6.  **Patient Education:**
    IF Patient has Measles THEN
        - Advise on anorexia and risk of PEM. Encourage regular frequent feeds and extra meals during convalescence.
        - Inform that the disease lasts 10-12 days, maximum infectivity is 5 days before to 4 days after rash.
        - Advise on rash healing (desquamation, hyperpigmentation).
        - Instruct parents to report warning signs:
            - Stiff neck, convulsions, extreme drowsiness, loss of consciousness, altered behaviour.
            - Rapid/laboured breathing, difficulty feeding, cyanosis.
            - Significant dehydration (sunken eyes/fontanelles, loss of skin turgor, dry tongue, lack of tears).
            - Blood in stools.

7.  **Vaccination:**
    IF Patient has Measles THEN
        - Do NOT give measles vaccine to a child who has already had the disease.
    IF Unimmunized child < 5 years is in contact with a Measles patient THEN
        - Consider immunizing the contact child with Measles vaccine to prevent or decrease severity.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Measles vaccine: IF the child has already suffered from Measles.
