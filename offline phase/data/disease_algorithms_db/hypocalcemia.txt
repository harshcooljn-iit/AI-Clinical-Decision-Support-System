DISEASE: Hypocalcemia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Ca.gluconate: Calcium gluconate
- Inj.: Injection
- IV: Intravenous
- mg: Milligrams
- gm: Grams
- IU: International Units
- CRF: Chronic Renal Failure
- Vit D: Vitamin D
- mcg: Micrograms
- QTc: QT interval corrected

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Total Serum Calcium < 8.5 mg/dl
- ECG: Prolongation of QTc interval
- Corrected Serum Calcium: Add 1 mg/dl to estimated level for every 1 g/dl albumin falls below 4 g/dl in hypoalbuminemia.
- Clinical signs: Circumoral paresthesias, muscle cramps, confusion, tetany, convulsions, positive Chvostek's sign, Trousseau's sign.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Severe symptomatic hypocalcemia is present THEN
    Administer 20 ml of 10% Inj. Ca.gluconate solution over 10-15 minutes.
    Followed by 60-80 ml of 10% Inj. Ca.gluconate solution in 1 liter of 5% distilled water at a rate of 0.5-0.25 mg/kg/hr elemental calcium.
    IF Hypocalcemia is associated with hypomagnesemia THEN
        Administer Inj. magnesium sulphate 1-2 gm IV on Day 1.
        Followed by oral magnesium oxide 600-1200 mg 3 times a day to replenish stores.
ELSE IF Asymptomatic hypocalcemia is present OR maintenance treatment is needed THEN
    Treat underlying condition if possible.
    IF Long term treatment is required (e.g., hypoparathyroidism, pseudohypoparathyroidism, chronic vitamin D deficiency) THEN
        Administer Tab. calcium carbonate (40% elemental ca) 1-2 gm elemental calcium orally 3 times a day initially.
        Subsequently, adjust to a maintenance dose of 0.4-1.5 g elemental calcium 3 times a day.
    IF Patient has Chronic Renal Failure (CRF) THEN
        Correct concomitant hyperphosphatemia.
        Administer Vit D 50000 IU/day for 1-2 weeks, then weekly or bimonthly.
        OR
        Administer Calcitriol 0.25 mcg orally daily.
        Advise low Phosphate diet.
        Administer Phosphate binding agents.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Inj. Ca.gluconate should not be mixed with bicarbonate due to potential precipitation of calcium carbonate.
