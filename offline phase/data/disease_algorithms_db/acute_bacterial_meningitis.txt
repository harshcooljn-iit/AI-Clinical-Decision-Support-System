DISEASE: Acute Bacterial Meningitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
- CNS: Central Nervous System
- ICP: Intracranial Pressure
- PMN: Polymorphonuclear Neutrophils
- WCC: White Blood Cell Count
- IV: Intravenous
- PaCO2: Partial pressure of carbon dioxide
- RNTCP: Revised National Tuberculosis Control Programme
- U: Units
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- g: Gram
- d: Day
- h: Hour
- mL: Milliliter
- Tab: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation: Fever, headache, neck stiffness, photophobia, nausea, vomiting, altered mental status.
- CSF analysis:
    - Pressure: >30 cmH2O
    - Appearance: Turbid
    - Protein: >1 g/L
    - Glucose: <2.2 mmol/L
    - Glucose-CSF:Serum ratio: <0.4
    - WCC: >500 cells/µL
    - Gram Stain: 60-90% positive
    - Cell type: 90% PMN

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Patient Age Group Determination:**
    IF Patient Age is Preterm infants to infants <1 month THEN
        Antibiotic regimen = Ampicillin + cefotaxime
    ELSE IF Patient Age is 1–3 months THEN
        Antibiotic regimen = Ampicillin + cefotaxime OR Ampicillin + ceftriaxone
    ELSE IF Patient Age is >3 months AND Patient Age is <55 years THEN
        Antibiotic regimen = Cefotaxime + vancomycin OR Ceftriaxone + vancomycin OR Cefepime + vancomycin
    ELSE IF Patient Age is >55 years OR Patient has alcoholism OR Patient has other debilitating illnesses THEN
        Antibiotic regimen = Ampicillin + cefotaxime + vancomycin OR Ampicillin + ceftriaxone + vancomycin OR Ampicillin + cefepime + vancomycin
    ELSE IF Patient has Hospital-acquired meningitis OR Posttraumatic meningitis OR Post neurosurgery meningitis OR Neutropenic patient OR Patient has impaired cell-mediated immunity THEN
        Antibiotic regimen = Ampicillin + ceftazidime + vancomycin OR Ampicillin + meropenem + vancomycin
    ENDIF

2.  **Antibiotic Dosing (Based on Table 4):**
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Ampicillin THEN
        Dosage = 12 g/d
        Frequency = every 4 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Cefipime THEN
        Dosage = 6 g/d
        Frequency = every 8 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Cefotaxime THEN
        Dosage = 12 g/d
        Frequency = every 12 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Ceftriaxone THEN
        Dosage = 4 g/d
        Frequency = every 12 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Ceftazidime THEN
        Dosage = 6 g/d
        Frequency = every 8 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Gentamicin THEN
        Dosage = 7.5 mg/kg/d
        Frequency = every 8 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Meropenem THEN
        Dosage = 3 g/d
        Frequency = every 8 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Metronidazole THEN
        Dosage = 1500–2000 mg/d
        Frequency = every 6 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Penicillin G THEN
        Dosage = 20–24 million U/d
        Frequency = every 4 hours
    ENDIF
    IF Antibiotic regimen includes Vancomycin THEN
        Dosage = 2 g/d
        Frequency = every 12 hours
    ENDIF

3.  **Dexamethasone Administration:**
    IF Patient has Bacterial Meningitis THEN
        Administer Inj. dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg every 6 hours IV for 2 days.
        Start dexamethasone shortly before the antibiotic.
    ENDIF

4.  **Increased ICP Management:**
    IF Patient has Increased ICP THEN
        Elevate head of bed to 30–45°.
        Consider intubation and hyperventilation (target PaCO2 25–30 mm Hg).
        Administer Inj. mannitol 20% 1 g/kg stat.
        Follow with 0.5 g/kg every 4-6 hours.
    ENDIF

5.  **Duration of Therapy:**
    IF Meningitis is due to _Neisseria meningitidis_ (meningococcal meningitis) AND it is uncomplicated THEN
        Duration = 7 days
    ELSE IF Meningitis is due to _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ (pneumococcal meningitis) THEN
        Duration = 14 days (2 weeks)
    ELSE IF Meningitis is due to _Listeria monocytogenes_ THEN
        Duration = at least 21 days (3 weeks)
    ELSE IF Meningitis is due to gram-negative bacilli THEN
        Duration = 21 days (3 weeks)
    ENDIF

6.  **Gentamicin Addition (Specific Cases):**
    IF Patient is critically ill AND Meningitis is due to _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ OR Meningitis is due to gram-negative bacilli THEN
        Administer Gentamicin:
            Loading dose = 2 mg/kg
            Maintenance dose = 7.5 mg/kg/d every 8 hours
            Adjust dose for serum levels and renal function.
    ENDIF

7.  **Chemoprophylaxis for Close Contacts:**
    IF Patient has Meningococcal Meningitis THEN
        Administer chemoprophylaxis to index case and all close contacts.
        Regimen: Rifampin 600 mg every 12 hours for 2 days (adults) OR Rifampin 10 mg/kg every 12 hours for 2 days (children > 1 year).
    ENDIF

8.  **Culture-Based Therapy Adjustment:**
    Once culture report is available, change therapy accordingly.

9.  **Tuberculous Meningitis:**
    IF Meningitis is due to _Mycobacterium tuberculosis_ THEN
        Treat as per RNTCP protocol.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (No specific contraindications listed for the drugs mentioned in the context of bacterial meningitis treatment. Contraindications would depend on individual patient factors like allergies, renal function, etc., which are not specified in the guideline.)
