DISEASE: Dental Caries

ABBREVIATIONS:
- None explicitly listed in the text.

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Examination for stage of caries (white/brown/black spots, cavitated lesions).
- Assessment of symptoms (tooth sensitivity/ache to cold/sweet).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has non-cavitated lesion AND is low risk AND has good oral hygiene practices
    THEN no treatment is given.
ELSE IF patient has cavitated lesion
    THEN restoration is done.
ENDIF

IF patient is high risk (likely to progress caries)
    THEN consider pit and fissure sealant.
ENDIF

IF patient has caries
    THEN consider topical 2% sodium fluoride.
ENDIF

IF patient is caries active
    THEN consider mouth wash with 0.2% chlorhexidine twice a day.
ENDIF

IF patient has caries
    THEN advise fluorinated toothpaste or fluorinated mouthwashes.
ENDIF

Assessment of response to therapy:
IF patient is caries active
    THEN follow up visit every 3 months to check progression of lesions.
ELSE IF patient is normal
    THEN follow up every 6 months to 1 year to check for development of white spot cavitation.
ENDIF

Patient education/prevention:
IF patient is caries active/high risk
    THEN advise diet control and avoidance of sugar containing food.
    THEN advise substitution of frequent sucrose ingestion with sugar-free foods.
    THEN advise oral hygiene: brushing twice a day, flossing, thorough rinsing after every meal.
    THEN consider Fluoride application using Topical 2% sodium fluoride (by dentist) 4 applications at weekly intervals at the age of 3, 7, 11 and 13 years.
    THEN consider 0.05% sodium fluoride daily rinse (should not be swallowed).
    THEN consider 0.2% sodium fluoride supervised weekly rinse in school (age of children >7 years) IF identified as caries active patients.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- None specified in the text for the listed treatments.
