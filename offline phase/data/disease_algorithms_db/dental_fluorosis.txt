DISEASE: Dental Fluorosis

ABBREVIATIONS:
PPM: Parts Per Million

DIAGNOSTICS:
Clinical examination revealing white chalky/brown spots or brownish discoloration on the enamel, primarily affecting anterior teeth and first molars, in patients with a history of high fluoride intake (> 3 PPM) during tooth development.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Dental Fluorosis THEN
    // Step 1: Oral Hygiene
    Maintain good oral hygiene practices.
    // Step 2: Assess Severity and Patient Preference
    IF patient desires cosmetic improvement THEN
        // Step 3: Consider Bleaching
        IF patient is a suitable candidate for bleaching (e.g., no contraindications, appropriate severity) THEN
            Perform teeth bleaching (whitening).
        ELSE
            // Step 4: Consider Veneers/Laminates
            IF patient desires significant esthetic improvement or bleaching is ineffective/contraindicated THEN
                Consider placement of veneers or laminates.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    ELSE
        // Step 5: Focus on Oral Hygiene
        Maintain good oral hygiene practices.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(No specific contraindications mentioned for the treatments listed, but standard contraindications for bleaching and veneer placement apply based on individual patient factors not specified in the guideline).
