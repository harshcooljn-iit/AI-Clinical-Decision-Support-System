DISEASE: Cluster Headache (CH)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CH: Cluster Headache
- Inj.: Injection
- SC: Subcutaneous
- L: Liters
- min: minutes
- mg: milligrams
- kg: kilogram
- d: day
- mL: milliliter
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- PO: Per os (by mouth)
- NPO: Nothing by mouth
- O2: Oxygen
- NSAID: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug
- triptan: Triptan medication
- GYN: Gynecologic
- CNS: Central Nervous System

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Characterized by frequent recurring, brief but extremely severe headache associated with pain around the eye.
- Associated symptoms include tearing, redness, runny/blocked nose on the affected side, and eyelid drooping.
- Attacks peak rapidly.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# ACUTE TREATMENT

IF patient is experiencing an acute Cluster Headache attack THEN
    # Oxygen Therapy
    IF patient is willing and able to use oxygen THEN
        Administer 100% Oxygen at 10–12 L/min for 15–20 min.
    ENDIF

    # Injectable Sumatriptan
    IF patient requires rapid relief THEN
        Administer Inj. sumatriptan 6 mg SC.
    ENDIF

    # Nasal Spray Triptans
    IF patient prefers or requires nasal spray administration THEN
        Administer Sumatriptan (20 mg) nasal spray OR Zolmitriptan (5 mg) nasal spray.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# PREVENTIVE TREATMENT

# Short-Term Prevention (Episodic CH)

IF patient has Episodic Cluster Headache AND requires short-term prevention THEN
    # Prednisone
    Administer Prednisone 1 mg/kg daily, up to a maximum of 60 mg daily, tapering over 21 days.

    # Methysergide
    Administer Methysergide 3–12 mg/d.

    # Verapamil
    Administer Verapamil 160–960 mg/d.

    # Greater Occipital Nerve Injection
    Administer Greater occipital nerve injection.
ENDIF

# Long-Term Prevention (Episodic CH and Prolonged Chronic CH)

IF patient has Episodic Cluster Headache OR Prolonged Chronic Cluster Headache AND requires long-term prevention THEN
    # Verapamil
    Administer Verapamil 160–960 mg/d.

    # Methysergide
    Administer Methysergide 3-12 mg/d.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(Note: Specific contraindications for each drug are not detailed in the provided text, but general considerations apply)

- For Sumatriptan:
    IF patient has ischemic heart disease, uncontrolled hypertension, stroke, or peripheral vascular disease THEN
        DO NOT administer Sumatriptan.
    ENDIF
- For Methysergide:
    IF patient has ischemic heart disease, peripheral vascular disease, uncontrolled hypertension, or renal/hepatic impairment THEN
        DO NOT administer Methysergide.
    ENDIF
- For Prednisone:
    IF patient has untreated infections, peptic ulcer disease, or significant cardiovascular disease THEN
        Use Prednisone with caution or consider alternative treatments.
    ENDIF
