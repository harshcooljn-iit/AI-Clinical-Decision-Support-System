DISEASE: Vitamin A Deficiency

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IU: International Units
- WHO: World Health Organization
- USAID: United States Agency for International Development
- X: WHO Classification code for primary signs
- N: WHO Classification code for secondary signs
- F: WHO Classification code for secondary signs
- S: WHO Classification code for secondary signs

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical signs include Nyctalopia (night blindness), Conjunctival xerosis, Bitot spot, Persistent epithelial defect, Corneal ulcer, Keratomalacia.
- WHO Classification based on primary (X) and secondary (N, F, S) signs.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Severe xerophthalmia is present THEN
    BEGIN TREATMENT IMMEDIATELY.
ENDIF

IF Patient can take oral medication THEN
    IF Age is 0-6 months THEN
        Administer 50,000 IU Vitamin A orally on Day 0, Day 1, and after 4 weeks.
    ELSE IF Age is 6-12 months THEN
        Administer 100,000 IU Vitamin A orally on Day 0, Day 1, and after 4 weeks.
    ELSE IF Age is more than 1 year THEN
        Administer 200,000 IU Vitamin A orally on Day 0, Day 1, and after 4 weeks.
    ENDIF
ELSE IF Patient has persistent vomiting or severe malabsorption THEN
    Administer intramuscular water-soluble Vitamin A.
ENDIF

Administer topical preservative-free lubricating eye drops.
Administer antibiotic eye drops (e.g., moxifloxacin eye drops 1 to 6 hourly) to prevent secondary infection.

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Encourage regular consumption of Vitamin A-rich foods (e.g., dark green leafy vegetables).
- Pregnant and lactating women should consume Vitamin A-rich diets.
- Promote breastfeeding and colostrum feeding for newborns.

HIGH DOSE UNIVERSAL DISTRIBUTION SCHEDULE (Prevention):
IF Age is less than 6 months THEN
    Administer 50,000 IU Vitamin A orally.
ELSE IF Age is 6-12 months THEN
    Administer 100,000 IU Vitamin A orally.
ELSE IF Age is more than 12 months THEN
    Administer 200,000 IU Vitamin A orally every 4-6 months until 5 years of age.
ENDIF

IF Patient is a mother THEN
    Administer 200,000 IU Vitamin A orally within 8 weeks of delivery.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- No specific contraindications listed for oral or intramuscular Vitamin A in the context of deficiency treatment, but caution is advised in patients with hypervitaminosis A.
- Topical antibiotic eye drops should not be used if the patient has a known allergy to the specific antibiotic (e.g., moxifloxacin).
