DISEASE: Upper Gastrointestinal Bleeding

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Hb: Hemoglobin
- Abo-Rh: Blood group and Rh factor
- PT: Prothrombin Time
- APTT: Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time
- G.I.: Gastrointestinal
- IV: Intravenous
- TDS: Three times a day
- mcg: Micrograms
- TIPSS: Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunts

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Blood tests (Hb, Abo-Rh, PT, APTT)
- Upper G.I. Scopy (Endoscopy)
- Ultrasonography

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient presents with Upper Gastrointestinal Bleeding THEN

    // Initial Management
    - Obtain wide bore IV access (16G preferred).
    - Insert Ryle's tube.
    - Insert catheter.

    // Pharmacological Treatment
    - Administer Inj. pantoprazole 40 mg IV every 12 hours.
    - Administer Inj. tranexamic acid 500 mg TDS.
    - Administer Inj. octreotide 100 mcg IV every 6 hours.

    // Specific Treatments based on cause (implied)
    IF Cause is Oesophageal Varices THEN
        - Perform banding.
        - OR Insert gastric balloon of Sengstaken-Blackmore tube.
        - OR Perform injection sclerotherapy.
    ENDIF

    IF Cause is Peptic Ulcers THEN
        - Perform argon diathermy.
        - OR Perform laser therapy.
    ENDIF

    // Surgical Treatment (if needed)
    IF Bleeding is uncontrolled OR Cause requires surgical intervention THEN
        - Perform exploratory laparotomy.
        - Search for and treat the cause.
        - OR Perform TIPSS (in uncontrolled portal hypertension cases).
    ENDIF

ENDIF
