```text
DISEASE: Chickenpox

ABBREVIATIONS:
- QID: Four times a day
- kg: Kilogram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation (generalized pruritic vesicular rash, preceded by fever, chills, headache, malaise, anorexia, polymorphic vesicular lesions with central haemorrhagic crusting, centripetal arrangement).
- Microscopy (Tzanck smear).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Chickenpox THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    Rest
    Cold compresses
    Tepid baths

    // Pharmacological treatment
    Symptomatic treatment with rest, analgesics, antibiotics, and topical calamine lotion.

    // Acyclovir treatment (for severe varicella and immune-compromised patients)
    IF patient has severe varicella OR patient is immune-compromised THEN

        IF patient is a child THEN
            Acyclovir dosage: 20 mg/kg, administered four times a day (QID).
        ELSE IF patient is an adult THEN
            Acyclovir dosage: 800 mg, administered five times a day for 10 days.
        ENDIF

    ENDIF

ENDIF
```
