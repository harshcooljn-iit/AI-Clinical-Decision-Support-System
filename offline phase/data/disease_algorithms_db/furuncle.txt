```text
DISEASE: Furuncle

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IU: International Units
- TDS: Ter in die (three times a day)
- OD: Omne die (once a day)
- Cap.: Capsule
- Tab.: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- History of recent cold and influenza, sign of middle ear infection
- Otoscopy examination may reveal boils
- Maximum tenderness over tragus
- Post-auricular lymphadenitis
- Obliteration of post aural sulcus with forward displacement of auricle
- Mastoid radiograph may show well-developed clear cell on affected side

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Furuncle THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    APPLY local heat.

    // Pharmacological treatment

    // Option 1: Wick packing
    APPLY 10% ichthamol in glycerine wick pack OR antibiotic steroid cream wick pack (polymyxin B sulphate 500 IU, neomycin sulphate 3400 IU, zinc bacitracin 400 IU, hydrocortisone 10 mg, per g) on alternate days until swelling subsides.

    // Option 2: Local ear drops
    APPLY local ear drops (polymyxin B sulphate 1000 U, neomycin sulphate 3400 U, hydrocortisone 10 mg/ml).

    // Option 3: Systemic Antibiotics
    APPLY systemic antibacterial therapy:
    IF patient can take amoxicillin THEN
        APPLY Cap. amoxicillin 500mg TDS for 5-7 days.
    ELSE IF patient can take azithromycin THEN
        APPLY azithromycin 500mg OD for 3-5 days.
    ENDIF

    // Option 4: Analgesics
    APPLY analgesics as needed:
    IF pain requires treatment THEN
        APPLY Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg as and when required OR
        APPLY Tab. nimesulide 100 mg twice a day.
    ENDIF

    // Surgical intervention
    IF incision and drainage is necessary THEN
        PERFORM incision and drainage.
    ENDIF

ENDIF

// Patient Education
IF patient has recurrent boils THEN
    RULE OUT diabetes mellitus.
ENDIF

ADVISE patient to avoid ear picking.
```
