1. DISEASE: Septicemia

2. ABBREVIATIONS:
    - SIRS: Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome
    - PaCO2: partial pressure of arterial carbon dioxide
    - MODS: Multiple Organ Dysfunction Syndrome
    - IV: Intravenous
    - GI: Gastrointestinal
    - DIC: Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation
    - PT: Prothrombin Time
    - PTT: Partial Prothromboplastin Time
    - ABG: Arterial Blood Gas
    - ml: milliliter
    - mg: milligram
    - µg: microgram
    - kg: kilogram
    - mmHg: millimeters of mercury
    - Tab: Tablet
    - Inj: Injection
    - FFP: Fresh-Frozen Plasma

3. DIAGNOSTICS:
    - Presence of viable bacteria (fungi) in the blood (positive blood cultures).
    - Clinical syndrome potentially involving SIRS criteria (fever/hypothermia, tachypnea/low PaCO2, tachycardia, abnormal WBC count).
    - Signs of organ dysfunction, hypoperfusion, or hypotension (severe sepsis).
    - Hypotension unresponsive to fluid resuscitation plus organ dysfunction or perfusion abnormalities (septic shock).

4. TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    IF Patient presents with suspected Septicemia:

    THEN:
    - Initiate aggressive airway management with high-flow oxygen and endotracheal intubation if needed.
    - Start rapid infusion of crystalloid IV fluid (Ringer’s lactate or normal saline):
        - Adults: 500 ml every 5 to 10 min initially; 4 to 6 L total.
        - Children: 20 ml/kg every 5 to 10 min initially; 60 ml/kg total.
    - Remove potential source of infection (e.g., IV catheters, urinary catheters).
    - Monitor vital signs (BP, pulse, capillary refill), CVP, PCWP, and urine output.

    IF Patient has hypotension refractory to IV fluid:

    THEN:
    - Administer Inj. dopamine 5 to 20 µg/kg/min, titrate to response.

    IF Blood pressure remains <70 mmHg despite dopamine:

    THEN:
    - Administer Inj. norepinephrine loading dose of 8-12 µg/min, followed by an infusion of 2-4 µg/min to maintain mean arterial blood pressure ≥ 60 mmHg.

    IF Patient has suspected Septicemia:

    THEN:
    - Start empiric antibiotic therapy intravenously at maximum allowed dosages.
    - Consider gram-positive and gram-negative coverage.

    IF Patient is an adult:

    THEN:
    - Use a third-generation cephalosporin:
        - Ceftriaxone 1 g IV
        - OR Cefotaxime 2 g IV
        - OR Ceftazidime 2 g IV
    - OR use an antipseudomonal beta lactamase–susceptible penicillin.
    - Add an aminoglycoside:
        - Gentamicin 2 mg/kg IV
        - OR Tobramycin 2 mg/kg IV

    IF Patient is an immunocompromised adult:

    THEN:
    - Use Ceftazidime 2 g IV
    - OR Imipenem 750 mg IV
    - OR Meropenem 1 g IV (alone).

    IF Gram-positive infection is suspected (e.g., indwelling catheter, IV drug use):

    THEN:
    - Add Oxacillin 2 g IV
    - OR Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV.

    IF Anaerobic source is suspected (e.g., intraabdominal, genital tract, odontogenic, necrotizing soft tissue infection):

    THEN:
    - Add Metronidazole 7.5 mg/kg IV
    - OR Clindamycin 900 mg IV.

    IF _Legionella_ is a potential source:

    THEN:
    - Add Erythromycin 500 mg IV.

    IF Patient has acidosis:

    THEN:
    - Treat with oxygen, ventilation, and IV fluid replacement.
    - IF Acidosis is severe (as directed by ABGs):
        - Administer Sodium bicarbonate 1 meq/kg IV.

    IF Patient has DIC:

    THEN:
    - Administer Fresh-frozen plasma 15 to 20 ml/kg initially to keep PT at 1.5 to 2 times normal.
    - Administer Platelet infusion to maintain serum concentration of 50 to 100,000.

    IF Patient has suspected adrenal insufficiency:

    THEN:
    - Administer Inj. hydrocortisone 100 mg IV.
