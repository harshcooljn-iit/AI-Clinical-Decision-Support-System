DISEASE: Panic Disorder

ABBREVIATIONS:
- SSRI: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor
- HS: Bedtime
- Inj.: Injection

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Recurrent panic attacks characterized by intense fear or discomfort reaching a peak within 10 minutes.
- Associated symptoms: palpitations, sweating, shaking, breathlessness, chest pain, nausea, fainting, feeling of choking, fear of dying or going crazy.
- Persistence concern about having more attacks.
- Change in behavior related to attacks.
- Worries about consequences of the attacks.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient presents with symptoms consistent with Panic Disorder (as per DIAGNOSTICS) THEN proceed to treatment options.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    IF Patient is suitable for non-pharmacological treatment THEN
        THEN Consider cognitive and behavioral therapies (psychoeducation, cognitive distortion correction, relaxation techniques, respiratory training).
        THEN Consider family therapy (psychoeducation, support).
        THEN Consider insight-oriented psychotherapy.
    ENDIF

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment - SSRIs:**
    IF Patient is suitable for SSRI treatment THEN
        THEN Consider starting an SSRI.
        THEN IF SSRI is Paroxetine THEN
            THEN Start Paroxetine (dosage not specified, follow standard guidelines).
            ENDIF
        THEN IF SSRI is Escitalopram THEN
            THEN Start Escitalopram (dosage not specified, follow standard guidelines).
            ENDIF
        THEN IF SSRI is Citalopram THEN
            THEN Start Citalopram (dosage not specified, follow standard guidelines).
            ENDIF
        THEN IF SSRI is Fluvoxamine THEN
            THEN Start Fluvoxamine (dosage not specified, follow standard guidelines).
            ENDIF
        THEN IF SSRI is Sertraline THEN
            THEN Start Sertraline (dosage not specified, follow standard guidelines).
            ENDIF
    ENDIF

4.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Benzodiazepines (Alprazolam):**
    IF Patient requires rapid symptomatic relief AND is suitable for benzodiazepine treatment THEN
        THEN Consider Alprazolam.
        THEN Start Alprazolam 0.25 mg/day at HS.
        THEN Increase dose as needed, up to a maximum of 6 mg/day.
    ENDIF

5.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Benzodiazepines (Inj. Lorazepam/Diazepam):**
    IF Patient presents with acute anxiety AND is suitable for benzodiazepine treatment THEN
        THEN Consider Inj. Lorazepam 2mg/ml OR Diazepam 5ml/ml.
    ENDIF

6.  **Pharmacological Treatment - Tricyclic Antidepressants:**
    IF Patient is suitable for Tricyclic Antidepressant treatment THEN
        THEN Consider Amitriptyline.
        THEN Start Amitriptyline (dosage not specified, follow standard guidelines).
    ENDIF

7.  **Patient Education:**
    IF Patient is receiving treatment THEN
        THEN Provide general reassurance about the benign nature of symptoms and possibility of spontaneous recovery.
        THEN Advise continuing with breathing exercises.
    ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Alprazolam: Specific contraindications not listed in the text. Follow standard drug information.
- Inj. Lorazepam/Diazepam: Specific contraindications not listed in the text. Follow standard drug information.
- Amitriptyline: Specific contraindications not listed in the text. Follow standard drug information.
- SSRIs: Specific contraindications not listed in the text. Follow standard drug information.
