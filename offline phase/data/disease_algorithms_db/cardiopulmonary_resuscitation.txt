DISEASE: Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CPR: Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation
- CPA: Cardiopulmonary Arrest
- BLS: Basic Life Support
- ACLS: Advanced Cardiac Life Support
- IV: Intravenous
- IO: Intraosseous
- ECG: Electrocardiogram
- AED: Automated External Defibrillator
- VT: Ventricular Tachycardia
- VF: Ventricular Fibrillation
- PEA: Pulseless Electrical Activity
- ROSC: Return of Spontaneous Circulation
- Epinephrine: Adrenaline
- mg: Milligram
- units: Units

DIAGNOSTICS:
1. Absence of pulse in major arteries (carotid or femoral in older children and femoral or brachial in infants).
2. Absence of heart sounds on auscultation.
3. Asystole / Ventricular Fibrillation on ECG.
4. Absence of respiration (absent chest movements, airflow).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF patient is unresponsive AND not breathing normally (or only gasping):
        THEN Activate emergency response system.
        THEN Get AED/defibrillator OR send second rescuer.
        THEN Check for pulse (within 10 seconds).

2.  **Pulse Check Result:**
    IF definite pulse is present:
        THEN Give one breath every 5 to 6 seconds.
        THEN Recheck pulse every 2 minutes.
    ELSE (NO pulse):
        THEN Begin cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths.

3.  **AED/Defibrillator Arrival (BLS):**
    IF AED/defibrillator arrives:
        THEN Check rhythm.
        IF rhythm is shockable (VT/VF):
            THEN Give 1 shock.
            THEN Resume CPR immediately for 2 minutes.
            THEN Repeat rhythm check and shock if indicated every 2 minutes until ALS providers take over or victim starts to move.
        ELSE (rhythm is not shockable):
            THEN Resume CPR immediately for 2 minutes.
            THEN Check rhythm every 2 minutes, continue until ALS providers take over or victim starts to move.

4.  **ACLS Algorithm (Adult Cardiac Arrest):**
    IF patient in cardiac arrest (no pulse):
        THEN Shout for help/activate emergency response.
        THEN Start CPR.
        THEN Give Oxygen.
        THEN Attach monitor/defibrillator.
        THEN Check rhythm.

    IF rhythm is shockable (VT/VF):
        THEN Give 1 shock.
        THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
        THEN Obtain IV/IO access.
        THEN Check rhythm again.
        IF rhythm is shockable:
            THEN Give 1 shock.
            THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
            THEN Give Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes.
            THEN Consider advanced airway and capnography.
            THEN Check rhythm again.
            IF rhythm is shockable:
                THEN Give 1 shock.
                THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
                THEN Give Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO (1st dose).
                THEN Treat reversible causes.
                THEN Go back to checking rhythm every 2 minutes.
            ELSE (rhythm is not shockable):
                THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
                THEN Treat reversible causes.
                THEN Go back to checking rhythm every 2 minutes.
        ELSE (rhythm is not shockable after first shock):
            THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
            THEN Give Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes.
            THEN Consider advanced airway and capnography.
            THEN Check rhythm again.
            IF rhythm is shockable:
                THEN Give 1 shock.
                THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
                THEN Give Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO (1st dose).
                THEN Treat reversible causes.
                THEN Go back to checking rhythm every 2 minutes.
            ELSE (rhythm is not shockable):
                THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
                THEN Treat reversible causes.
                THEN Go back to checking rhythm every 2 minutes.

    ELSE (rhythm is not shockable - PEA/Asystole):
        THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
        THEN Obtain IV/IO access.
        THEN Give Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes.
        THEN Consider advanced airway.
        THEN Check rhythm again.
        IF rhythm is shockable:
            THEN Give 1 shock.
            THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
            THEN Treat reversible causes.
            THEN Go back to checking rhythm every 2 minutes.
        ELSE (rhythm is not shockable):
            THEN Perform CPR for 2 minutes / 5 cycles.
            THEN Treat reversible causes.
            THEN Go back to checking rhythm every 2 minutes.

5.  **Return of Spontaneous Circulation (ROSC):**
    IF signs of ROSC are present:
        THEN Provide post-cardiac arrest care.
    ELSE (no signs of ROSC):
        THEN Continue CPR.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
(None specified in the provided text for the listed drugs in the context of CPR, but standard contraindications apply based on patient condition and drug properties).
