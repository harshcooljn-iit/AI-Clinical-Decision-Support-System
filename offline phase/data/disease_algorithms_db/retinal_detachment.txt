DISEASE: Retinal Detachment

ABBREVIATIONS:
- RD: Retinal Detachment
- RPE: Retinal Pigment Epithelium

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Rhegmatogenous RD: Examination of the fundus with indirect ophthalmoscopy; retina appears grey with oscillating folds.
- Tractional RD: Caused by gliotic bands on retina.
- Exudative RD: Caused by collection of serous fluid between neurosensory retina and RPE.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Disease is Retinal Detachment (RD):
    IF Type is Rhegmatogenous RD:
        THEN Treatment is reattachment surgery.
        Surgery involves:
            - Identification of all retinal breaks.
            - Identification of areas of vitreous or retinal tractions.
            - Induction of aseptic chorioretinal inflammation around the breaks to seal them.
            - Ensuring chorioretinal apposition by approximating choroid/RPE to neurosensory retina (external tamponade, internal tamponade).
            - Drainage of subretinal fluid.
            - Adhesion between RPE and retina can be achieved by cryotherapy, diathermy, or photocoagulation.
        Pharmacological treatment: No pharmacological treatment can prevent or cure rhegmatogenous RD.
    ELSE IF Type is Exudative RD:
        IF Caused by inflammatory conditions like panuveitis:
            THEN Treatment: Systemic corticosteroids (as described in treatment of uveitis).
            IF Patient is refractory:
                THEN Treatment: Immunosuppressants.
        Pharmacological treatment: No pharmacological treatment can prevent or cure exudative RD unless due to inflammation.
    ELSE IF Type is Tractional RD:
        THEN Treatment is reattachment surgery.
        Surgery involves:
            - Identification of all retinal breaks.
            - Identification of areas of vitreous or retinal tractions.
            - Induction of aseptic chorioretinal inflammation around the breaks to seal them.
            - Ensuring chorioretinal apposition by approximating choroid/RPE to neurosensory retina (external tamponade, internal tamponade).
            - Drainage of subretinal fluid.
            - Adhesion between RPE and retina can be achieved by cryotherapy, diathermy, or photocoagulation.
        Pharmacological treatment: No pharmacological treatment can prevent or cure tractional RD.
    ELSE:
        THEN Treatment: Reattachment surgery (general approach).
        Surgery involves:
            - Identification of all retinal breaks.
            - Identification of areas of vitreous or retinal tractions.
            - Induction of aseptic chorioretinal inflammation around the breaks to seal them.
            - Ensuring chorioretinal apposition by approximating choroid/RPE to neurosensory retina (external tamponade, internal tamponade).
            - Drainage of subretinal fluid.
            - Adhesion between RPE and retina can be achieved by cryotherapy, diathermy, or photocoagulation.
        Pharmacological treatment: No pharmacological treatment can prevent or cure RD.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- No specific contraindications listed for the mentioned treatments based on the provided text.
