DISEASE: Nausea And Vomiting In Pregnancy

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Tab: Tablet
- HCl: Hydrochloric Acid
- GIT: Gastrointestinal Tract
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)
- TDS: Ter in die (three times a day)
- KCl: Potassium Chloride
- GIT: Gastrointestinal

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Diagnosis is by exclusion of medical and surgical causes of vomiting (e.g., liver disorders, pyelonephritis, diabetes mellitus).
- Molar (and multiple) pregnancy should be ruled out in hyperemesis cases by ultrasound.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Mild and Moderate Cases
IF Severity is Mild to Moderate THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    ADVICE: Reassurance, frequent small dry carbohydrate-rich meals, avoid fatty/spicy foods, avoid large volume of drinks in the morning.

    // Pharmacological treatment options
    IF Pharmacological treatment is needed THEN
        OPTION 1: Tab. doxylamine succinate 10 mg + Tab. pyridoxine HCl 10 mg, 1-2 tablets at bedtime.
        IF Symptoms persist THEN
            ADD: One more tablet of doxylamine succinate 10 mg + pyridoxine HCl 10 mg in the morning and afternoon.
        ENDIF

        OR

        OPTION 2: Tab. metoclopramide 10 mg, 2-4 times a day.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Hyperemesis Gravidarum
IF Severity is Hyperemesis Gravidarum THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    ADMIT: Admit patient to hospital.
    STOP: Stop oral intake of fluids and nutrition.
    MONITOR: Check serum electrolytes and urinary ketones at admission and every 6 hours.
    SUPPORT: Provide emotional support, psychiatric referral if required.
    RULE_OUT: Rule out multiple or molar pregnancy and liver disorders.

    // Pharmacological treatment
    REPLACE: Adequate and appropriate fluid and electrolyte replacement (Normal saline or Ringer's lactate solutions, add KCl as required).

    IF Urinary ketones are present THEN
        INFUSE: 1 liter of 10% dextrose over 3-4 hours.
    ENDIF

    // For prolonged vomiting (if not controlled with fluid/electrolyte replacement in 6-8 hours)
    IF Vomiting is prolonged THEN
        IF Orally tolerated THEN
            ADMINISTER: Tab. thiamine 25-50 mg, 3 times a day.
        ENDIF

        ADMINISTER: Inj. metoclopramide 10 mg IV or IM every 8 hours.
        ADMINISTER: Inj. ranitidine 50-100 mg every 6 hours.

        IF Vomiting is not controlled THEN
            ADMINISTER: Inj. promethazine chloride 25-50 mg IM or IV every 8 hours
            OR
            ADMINISTER: Inj. chlorpromazine 25-50 mg IM every 4-6 hours.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    // Once vomiting is controlled (for 24 hours)
    IF Vomiting controlled for 24 hours THEN
        GRADUALLY_START: Oral intake.
        IF Tolerated THEN
            OMIT: IV fluids.
            GIVE: Dry carbohydrate foods in small meals at frequent intervals.
            GRADUALLY_INTRODUCE: Full diet.
            IF Prolonged and severe disease THEN
                CONSIDER: Parenteral nutrition.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    ENDIF

    // Discharge criteria (if well tolerated for 48 hours)
    IF Well tolerated for 48 hours THEN
        DISCHARGE: Discharge patient from hospital.
        ADVICE: Provide dietary advice and reassurance.
        CONTINUE: Tab. metoclopramide 10 mg TDS and Tab. ranitidine 150 mg BD.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
// (No specific contraindications listed for these drugs in this context, but general pregnancy considerations apply)
// Doxylamine/Pyridoxine: Generally considered safe in pregnancy.
// Metoclopramide: Use with caution, potential side effects.
// Thiamine: Generally safe.
// Ranitidine: Use with caution, potential side effects.
// Promethazine/Chlorpromazine: Use with caution, potential side effects.
