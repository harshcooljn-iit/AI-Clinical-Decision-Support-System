DISEASE: Facial Palsy

ABBREVIATIONS:
- UMN: Upper Motor Neuron
- LMN: Lower Motor Neuron
- ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
- EMG: Electromyography

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical evaluation based on salient features (Bell's phenomenon, facial muscle involvement, taste/lacrimation changes, emotional movement preservation, absence of other neurological findings).
- Investigations (ESR, blood sugar, sarcoidosis workup, MRI, EMG) may be used in uncertain cases.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Facial Palsy THEN

    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    THEN
        IF patient needs eye protection THEN
            THEN use paper tape to depress upper eyelid during sleep to prevent corneal drying.
        ENDIF

        IF patient has weakened facial muscles THEN
            THEN massage the weakened muscles.
        ENDIF

        IF patient has drooping of the lower face THEN
            THEN splint the lower part of the face to prevent drooping.
        ENDIF

        THEN consider facial retraining (mime therapy).
    ENDIF

    // Pharmacological treatment
    THEN
        // Prednisone
        IF patient is a candidate for corticosteroids THEN
            THEN
                IF treatment is initiated within the first 5 days of symptom onset THEN
                    THEN administer Tab. prednisone 60-80 mg daily.
                ELSE
                    THEN do not administer prednisone for the initial 5 days.
                ENDIF

                // Tapering prednisone
                IF treatment duration extends beyond 5 days THEN
                    THEN taper prednisone modestly over the next 5 days.
                ENDIF
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        // Acyclovir
        IF patient is a candidate for antiviral therapy AND is receiving prednisone THEN
            THEN
                THEN administer Tab. acyclovir 400 mg five times daily for 10 days.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (No specific contraindications mentioned in the text for Prednisone or Acyclovir in this context, but standard contraindications apply, e.g., hypersensitivity, active infections for Prednisone).
