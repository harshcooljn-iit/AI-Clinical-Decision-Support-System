```text
DISEASE: Acute Rheumatic Fever

ABBREVIATIONS:
- BD: Twice daily
- QID: Four times daily
- Inj.: Injection
- PR: P wave R interval
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- tab: tablet
- Penicillin V: Penicillin Potassium
- Penicillin G: Penicillin Benzathine
- Erythromycin: Erythromycin base
- Aspirin: Acetylsalicylic acid
- Naproxen: Naproxen sodium
- Prednisolone: Prednisolone
- Carbamazepine: Carbamazepine
- Sodium valproate: Sodium valproate
- Haloperidol: Haloperidol

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Based on Jones criteria (Major and Minor criteria).
- Major criteria: Pancarditis, polyarthritis, chorea, erythema marginatum, subcutaneous nodules.
- Minor criteria: Arthralgia, fever, raised PR interval.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Treatment (Acute Phase):**
    *   IF Patient is NOT allergic to penicillin:
        *   THEN Administer Tab. penicillin V 500 mg BD for 10 days
        *   OR Administer Inj. benzathine penicillin G 1.2 million units as a single dose
    *   ELSE IF Patient IS allergic to penicillin:
        *   THEN Administer Tab. erythromycin 250 mg QID for 10 days

2.  **Secondary Prophylaxis Duration:**
    *   IF Patient has Rheumatic fever without carditis:
        *   THEN Duration of prophylaxis is For 5 years after the last attack OR until 21 years of age (whichever is longer).
    *   ELSE IF Patient has Rheumatic fever with carditis but no residual valvular disease:
        *   THEN Duration of prophylaxis is For 10 years after the last attack OR until 21 years of age (whichever is longer).
    *   ELSE IF Patient has Rheumatic fever with persistent valvular disease (evident clinically or on echocardiography):
        *   THEN Duration of prophylaxis is For 10 years after the last attack OR until 40 years of age (whichever is longer).
        *   NOTE: Sometimes lifelong prophylaxis may be required.

3.  **Secondary Prophylaxis Medication:**
    *   IF Patient requires secondary prophylaxis:
        *   THEN Administer Inj. benzathine penicillin 1.2 million units every 3 weeks
        *   OR Administer Tab. penicillin V 250 mg BD daily
        *   OR Administer Tab. erythromycin 250 mg twice daily

4.  **Arthritis Treatment:**
    *   IF Patient has arthritis:
        *   THEN Administer Tab. aspirin 2 g QID (adults)
        *   OR Administer Tab. aspirin 80-100 mg/kg/day (children) until symptoms persist
        *   OR Administer Tab. naproxen 10-20 mg/kg/day

5.  **Carditis Treatment:**
    *   IF Patient has carditis:
        *   THEN Administer Tab. prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day for short periods (up to a maximum of 3 weeks).

6.  **Chorea Treatment:**
    *   IF Patient has chorea:
        *   THEN Administer Carbamazepine OR Sodium valproate (preferred over haloperidol)
        *   OR Administer Haloperidol 0.05 mg/kg/day
        *   Treatment duration: Continue up to 1-2 weeks after symptoms subside.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Penicillin V, Benzathine Penicillin G, Erythromycin: Use Erythromycin if patient is allergic to penicillin.
- Haloperidol: Carbamazepine or Sodium valproate are preferred for chorea.
```
