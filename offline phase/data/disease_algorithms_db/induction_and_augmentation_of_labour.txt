DISEASE: Induction And Augmentation Of Labour

ABBREVIATIONS:
- ARM: Artificial rupture of membranes
- HIV: Human Immunodeficiency Virus
- GBS: Group B streptococcus
- IV: Intravenous
- mcg: Micrograms
- mL: Milliliters
- IU: International Units
- L: Liter
- min: Minute
- bpm: Beats per minute
- PGE2: Prostaglandin E2
- mcg: Micrograms
- Ringer's lactate: Lactated Ringer's solution

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Assessment of cervical condition (dilatation, length, consistency, position, descent)
- Monitoring fetal heart rate
- Monitoring uterine contractions (frequency, duration, strength)
- Monitoring maternal vital signs (pulse, blood pressure)

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - IF the goal is induction or augmentation of labour, THEN proceed.

2.  **Artificial Rupture of Membranes (ARM):**
    - IF HIV and/or hepatitis are highly prevalent, THEN consider leaving membranes intact as long as possible.
    - Monitor fetal heart rate.
    - Perform cervical exam (consistency, position, effacement, dilatation).
    - Use sterile gloves and an instrument (amniotic hook or Kocher clamp) to rupture membranes.
    - Note amniotic fluid colour.
    - IF thick meconium is present, THEN suspect fetal distress.
    - Monitor fetal heart rate during and after contraction.
    - IF fetal heart rate is abnormal (<100 or >180 bpm), THEN suspect fetal distress.
    - IF membranes have been ruptured for 18 hours, THEN give prophylactic antibiotics:
        - Penicillin G 2 million units IV, or
        - Inj. ampicillin 2 g IV, every six hours until delivery.
    - IF good labour pattern (3 contractions in 10 min, each >40 sec) is not established one hour after ARM, THEN begin oxytocin infusion.
    - IF labour is induced due to severe maternal disease (e.g., sepsis, eclampsia), THEN begin oxytocin infusion at the same time as ARM.

3.  **Cervical Assessment:**
    - Perform cervical exam and assign a score based on Table 5 criteria.
    - IF cervix score is 6 or more (favourable), THEN induce labour with oxytocin alone.
    - IF cervix score is 5 or less (unfavourable), THEN ripen the cervix using prostaglandins or a Foley catheter before induction.

4.  **Oxytocin Infusion:**
    - Use with caution due to risk of fetal distress (hyperstimulation) and uterine rupture.
    - Administer cautiously in IV fluids (dextrose or normal saline).
    - Gradually increase infusion rate until a good labour pattern is established (3 contractions in 10 min, each >40 sec).
    - Maintain this rate until delivery.
    - Monitor maternal pulse, blood pressure, contractions, and fetal heart rate every 30 minutes.
    - IF fetal heart rate is <100 bpm, THEN stop infusion and manage for fetal distress.
    - Initial infusion rates:
        - IF multigravida, THEN start with 2.5 units oxytocin in 500 mL dextrose at 10 drops/min.
        - IF primigravida, THEN start with 5 units oxytocin in 500 mL dextrose.
    - Increase infusion rate by 10 drops/min every 30 minutes until good contraction pattern is established.
    - IF hyperstimulation occurs (contraction >60 sec or >4 contractions in 10 min), THEN stop infusion and use tocolytics:
        - Terbutaline 250 mcg IV slowly over 5 min, OR
        - Salbutamol 10 mg in 1 L IV fluids (normal saline or Ringer's lactate) at 10 drops/min.
    - IF good contraction pattern not established at 60 drops/min:
        - Increase oxytocin concentration to 5 units in 500 mL dextrose and adjust rate to 30 drops/min (15 mIU/min).
        - IF multigravida or previous caesarean scar and induction has failed, THEN deliver by caesarean section.

5.  **Prostaglandins (Cervical Ripening):**
    - Monitor maternal pulse, blood pressure, contractions, and fetal heart rate.
    - PGE2 forms: 3 mg pessary or 2-3 mg gel. Place high in posterior vaginal fornix. Repeat after 6 hours if needed.
    - Discontinue prostaglandins and begin oxytocin infusion IF:
        - Membranes rupture, OR
        - Cervical ripening achieved, OR
        - Good labour established, OR
        - 12 hours have passed.

6.  **Misoprostol (Cervical Ripening):**
    - Use only in highly selected situations (e.g., severe pre-eclampsia/eclampsia with unfavourable cervix, fetal death in utero).
    - Place 25 mcg in posterior vaginal fornix.
    - Repeat after 6 hours if needed.
    - IF no response after two doses of 25 mcg, THEN increase to 50 mcg every 6 hours.
    - Do not exceed 50 mcg at a time or four doses (200 mcg total).
    - Monitor uterine contractions and fetal heart rate.

7.  **Foley Catheter (Mechanical Dilation):**
    - Avoid in women with cervicitis or vaginitis.
    - Insert high-level disinfected/sterile speculum.
    - Insert high-level disinfected/sterile Foley catheter through cervix, ensuring bulb is beyond internal os.
    - Inflate bulb with 30 mL water.
    - Give traction and strap catheter to thigh.
    - Leave in place until contractions begin or at least 12 hours.
    - Deflate bulb before removal.
    - Proceed with oxytocin infusion to augment induction.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Prostaglandins: Avoid Foley catheter in women with obvious cervicitis or vaginitis.
- Oxytocin: Use with caution in women with previous uterine surgery (increased risk of rupture).
- Misoprostol: Use only in highly selected situations.
