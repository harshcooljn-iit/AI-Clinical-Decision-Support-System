```text
DISEASE: Guillain-Barre Syndrome (GBS)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- GBS: Guillain-Barre Syndrome
- CSF: Cerebrospinal Fluid
- NCV: Nerve Conduction Velocity
- IVIG: Intravenous Immunoglobulin
- Px: Plasma exchange

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Required: Progressive weakness (mild paresis to complete paralysis) AND generalized hypo- or areflexia.
- Supportive:
    - Clinical features: Rapid motor progression (ceases by 4 weeks), relative limb symmetry, frequent cranial nerve involvement (especially VII), autonomic dysfunction, preceding infection.
    - CSF features: Elevated or serially elevated protein, cell count <10 mononuclear cells/mm³.
    - Electrodiagnostic findings: NCV slowing/conduction block, increased distal motor latency, F-wave abnormalities.
- Reducing possibility: Asymmetric weakness, persistent bowel/bladder symptoms, severe bowel/bladder dysfunction at onset, CSF cell count >50 mononuclear cells/mm³, well-demarcated sensory level.
- Exclusion: Other causes of acute neuromuscular weakness (myasthenia gravis, botulism, poliomyelitis, toxic neuropathy), abnormal CSF cytology.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Non-pharmacological treatment
IF GBS is diagnosed THEN
    Monitor Airway, Breathing, and Circulation.
    Consider mechanical ventilator support if needed.
    Manage autonomic dysfunction.
    Initiate physical therapy.
ENDIF

// Pharmacological treatment
IF GBS is diagnosed THEN
    Consider Plasma exchange (Px) OR Intravenous Immunoglobulin (IVIG).
    // Both Px and IVIG are equally effective. Corticosteroids are NOT recommended.

    // Plasma exchange (Px)
    IF Px is chosen THEN
        Administer Px regimen:
        Remove a total of 200 to 250 ml/kg of plasma.
        Perform four to six treatments on alternate days.
        Shorten duration if coagulopathy is present.
        Replacement fluid: Saline combined with 5% albumin.
    ENDIF

    // Intravenous Immunoglobulin (IVIG)
    IF IVIG is chosen THEN
        Administer IVIG regimen:
        Dosage: 0.4 g/kg per day.
        Duration: 5 consecutive days.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Corticosteroids are NOT recommended for GBS treatment.
```
