DISEASE: Bacterial Skin Infections

ABBREVIATIONS:
- OD: Once daily
- QID: Four times daily
- TDS: Three times daily

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Mainly clinical diagnosis.
- Gram stain and culture-sensitivity may be used if required.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF the bacterial skin infection is localized (e.g., Impetigo, Folliculitis, Furuncle, Carbuncle, Ecthyma) THEN
    APPLY topical antibacterial cream twice daily for 7-10 days.
    - Options include: neomycin, silver sulfadiazine, or framycetin.
ELSE IF the bacterial skin infection is widespread or involves deeper tissues (e.g., Erysipelas, Cellulitis) OR if systemic symptoms (fever, malaise) are present THEN
    ADMINISTER systemic antibacterial therapy.
    - Option 1: Tab. azithromycin 500 mg OD for 3-5 days.
    - Option 2: Cap. amoxicillin 250 mg QID for 7 days.
    - Option 3: Cap. amoxicillin 500 mg TDS for 7 days.
    - Option 4: Tab. erythromycin 500 mg QID for 5 days.
ENDIF

NON-PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT:
- Maintain good personal hygiene.
- Ensure proper nutrition.
- Wash affected area with soap and water.
- Drain pus if present.
