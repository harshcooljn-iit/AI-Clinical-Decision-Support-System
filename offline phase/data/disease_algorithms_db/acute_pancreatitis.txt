DISEASE: Acute Pancreatitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- TC: Total Count
- DC: Differential Count
- S.: Serum
- USG: Ultrasonography
- CECT: Contrast-Enhanced Computed Tomography
- Inj.: Injection
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- SC: Subcutaneous
- ICU: Intensive Care Unit
- ARDS: Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
- NPO: Nil Per Os (Nothing by mouth)
- NG: Nasogastric
- WHO: World Health Organization
- APACHE II: Acute Physiology and Chronic Health Evaluation II
- Ranson's score: Ranson's Criteria
- Glasgow score: Glasgow Criteria

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation (epigastric pain, history of risk factors).
- Physical examination (tenderness, guarding, rigidity, fever, tachycardia, tachypnoea, shock).
- Laboratory tests (increased TC with neutrophilia, increased S. amylase >3x normal, increased S. lipase >3x normal, low S. calcium).
- Imaging (USG Abdomen, CECT Abdomen, Chest X-Ray).
- Prognostic scores (Ranson's, Glasgow, APACHE II).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

// Initial Assessment and Management (General)
IF (Patient presents with symptoms suggestive of Acute Pancreatitis) THEN
    // Investigations
    Perform TC with DC, S. amylase, S. lipase, S. calcium, USG Abdomen, CECT Abdomen (if severe/unresolving), Chest X-Ray.
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    Implement NPO.
    Consider NG tube insertion.
    Avoid alcohol.
    // Pharmacological treatment
    Initiate fluid resuscitation.
    Correct electrolyte imbalance.
ENDIF

// Pain Management
IF (Patient has pain) THEN
    Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 75 mg IM 8 hourly.
    IF (Pain persists or worsens) THEN
        Administer Inj. tramadol 50 mg once daily.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Acid Suppression and Prophylaxis
Administer Inj. pantoprazole 40 mg IV 12 hourly.
Administer Inj. octreotide 100 microgram IV/SC 6 hourly to 12 hourly until resolution of symptoms.

// Management based on Severity (Non-Necrotizing vs. Necrotizing)

// Non-Necrotizing Acute Pancreatitis
IF (Diagnosis is Acute Non-Necrotizing Pancreatitis) THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    Continue NPO, NG tube insertion, per urethral catheterization (if needed).
    // Pharmacological treatment
    Continue fluid resuscitation and electrolyte correction.
    Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 75 mg IM 8 hourly.
    Administer Inj. pantoprazole 40 mg IV 12 hourly.
    Administer Inj. octreotide 100 microgram IV/SC 6 hourly to 12 hourly until resolution of symptoms.
    // Surgical treatment
    Usually no surgical intervention required.
    IF (Cause is gallstones) THEN
        Perform Cholecystectomy after acute pancreatitis resolves.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

// Necrotizing Acute Pancreatitis
IF (Diagnosis is Acute Necrotizing Pancreatitis) THEN
    // Non-pharmacological treatment
    ICU monitoring.
    Oxygen by mask.
    Continue NPO, NG tube insertion, per urethral catheterization.
    // Pharmacological treatment
    Continue fluid resuscitation and electrolyte correction.
    Administer Inj. meropenem 1 gm IV 8 hourly.
    Administer Inj. diclofenac sodium 75 mg IM 8 hourly.
    Administer Inj. pantoprazole 40 mg IV 12 hourly.
    Administer Inj. octreotide 100 microgram IV/SC every 6 hourly to 8 hourly.
    // Surgical treatment
    Consider Pancreatic necrosectomy.
    Consider distal pancreatectomy (for disease in body and tail).
    IF (Cause is gallstones) THEN
        Perform Cholecystectomy after acute pancreatitis resolves.
    ENDIF
ENDIF
