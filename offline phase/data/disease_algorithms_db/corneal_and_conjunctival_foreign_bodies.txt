DISEASE: Corneal And Conjunctival Foreign Bodies

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CMC: Carboxy methyl cellulose
- IOP: Intraocular pressure
- AC: Anterior chamber
- BD: Bis in die (twice a day)
- QID: Quarter in die (four times a day)

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Symptoms: Foreign body sensation, watering, redness, history of trauma.
- Signs: Conjunctival or corneal foreign body (with or without rust ring), conjunctival injection, eyelid edema, mild anterior chamber reaction, superficial punctuate keraptopathy.
- Investigations: Determine mechanism of injury, size, weight, velocity, force, shape of object. Document visual acuity. Examine for iris tears, transillumination defects, lens opacities, AC shallowing, asymmetrically low IOP. Evert eyelids and inspect fornices. Dilate eye and examine posterior segment for IOFB. Avoid MRI if metallic foreign body suspected.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

Corneal Foreign Body Treatment:
IF corneal foreign body is present THEN
    Apply topical anesthetic (e.g., proparacaine 0.5% eye drops).
    REMOVE FOREIGN BODY:
        IF foreign body is superficial THEN
            Remove with cotton tip applicator, 26 no needle, or fine forceps at a slit lamp.
        ELSE IF multiple superficial foreign bodies are present THEN
            Remove by irrigation.
        ENDIF
    REMOVE RUST RING:
        IF rust ring is present THEN
            Remove as completely as possible on the first attempt.
            IF removal is difficult or deep/central THEN
                Consider leaving the rust ring to allow migration to the surface.
            ENDIF
            IF ophthalmic drill is needed THEN
                Use ophthalmic drill.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    MEASURE DEFECT:
        Measure the size of the resultant corneal epithelial defect.
    TREAT DEFECT:
        IF epithelial defect is present THEN
            Treat with antibiotics and carboxy methyl cellulose (CMC) eyedrops.
            IF necessary THEN
                Pad the eye.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    PATIENT INSTRUCTION:
        Alert the patient to return ASAP if symptoms worsen.
ENDIF

Conjunctival Foreign Body Treatment:
IF conjunctival foreign body is present THEN
    Apply topical anesthesia.
    REMOVE FOREIGN BODY:
        IF multiple or loose foreign bodies are present THEN
            Remove with saline irrigation.
        ELSE
            Remove with cotton-tipped applicator soaked in topical anesthetic or fine forceps.
        ENDIF
    PRETREATMENT (Deeply Embedded):
        IF foreign body is deeply embedded THEN
            Consider pretreatment with cotton-tipped applicator soaked in phenylephrine 2.5% to reduce bleeding.
        ENDIF
    LEAVE IN PLACE (Small/Inaccessible):
        IF foreign body is small, relatively inaccessible, and buried subconjunctival THEN
            Consider leaving it in the eye unless infectious or proinflammatory.
            IF it surfaces later THEN
                Remove it more easily.
            ENDIF
        ENDIF
    SWEEP FORNICES:
        Sweep conjunctival fornices with glass rod or cotton-tipped applicator soaked with topical anesthetic to catch remaining pieces.
    TREATMENT:
        Apply topical antibiotic (e.g., bacitracin ointment BD; trimethoprim/polymyxin B or moxifloxacin/ciprofloxacin/ofloxacin drops QID).
        Apply artificial tears for irritation.
ENDIF

Follow-up:
IF corneal foreign body was treated THEN
    Follow up as with corneal abrasion.
    IF residual rust ring remains THEN
        Reevaluate in 24 hours.
    ENDIF
ENDIF
IF conjunctival foreign body was treated THEN
    Follow up as needed, or in 1 week if residual foreign bodies were left in the conjunctiva.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- MRI: Avoid if history of possible metallic foreign body.
