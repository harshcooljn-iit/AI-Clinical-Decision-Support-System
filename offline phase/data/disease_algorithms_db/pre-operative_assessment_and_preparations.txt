DISEASE: Pre-Operative Assessment And Preparations

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Hb: Hemoglobin
- TLC: Total Leukocyte Count
- DLC: Differential Leukocyte Count
- CT: Clotting Time
- BT: Bleeding Time
- ECG: Electrocardiogram
- IV: Intravenous

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Preoperative assessment involves evaluating the patient's disease, condition, urgency of surgery, type of surgery, and potential risks/benefits.
- Routine investigations include Hb, TLC, DLC, CT, BT, urine routine examination, chest X-ray (for patients over 30 years), and ECG (for patients over 30 years).
- Special investigations may be needed based on the procedure and patient condition.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Information Gathering:**
    IF the patient is scheduled for surgery THEN
    - Gather and record all relevant patient information concisely.
    - Devise a plan to minimize surgical risks and maximize patient benefits.
    - Consider potential adverse events and plan for their management.
    - Ensure the patient (and relatives) understand the surgical plan, including benefits, risks, and alternatives.

2.  **Preoperative Assessment:**
    IF a surgical patient is identified THEN
    - Evaluate the nature and extent of the disease.
    - Evaluate the patient's overall condition and organ system status.
    - Determine the relative urgency of the surgery.
    - Consider the type of surgery and available alternatives.
    - Assess the relative morbidity and mortality of the disease and the surgical procedure.
    - Consult specialists (physician, cardiologist, anesthetist) if needed.
    - Document current medications and known allergies.

3.  **Informed Consent:**
    IF surgery is planned THEN
    - Obtain informed consent from the patient (and close relatives) after detailed discussion by the surgeon (or assistant).
    - Discussion should cover the nature of the procedure, expected benefits, involved risks, and possible alternatives.
    - Allow ample opportunity for questions and clarification.

4.  **Preoperative Preparation - Investigations:**
    IF the patient is undergoing surgery THEN
    - Perform routine investigations: Hb, TLC, DLC, CT, BT, urine routine examination.
    - IF the patient is over 30 years old THEN
    - Perform chest X-ray and ECG.
    - Perform special investigations as needed based on the procedure and patient condition.

5.  **Preoperative Preparation - Patient Hygiene & Belongings:**
    IF the patient is admitted pre-operatively THEN
    - Remove lipstick, nail polish, and cosmetics that may mask cyanosis or interfere with pulse oximetry.
    - Remove dentures, spectacles, contact lenses, artificial limbs, artificial eyes, hearing aids, jewelry, cash, and mobile phones before moving to the operating theatre.

6.  **Preoperative Preparation - Withholding Feeds:**
    IF the patient is undergoing surgery THEN
    - Withhold feeds before surgery based on the patient's age, type of anesthesia, and nature of the surgery. (Specific guidelines not provided in text).

7.  **Preoperative Preparation - Bathing & Clothing:**
    IF possible, THEN
    - Have the patient take a bath on the day before and the morning of surgery, paying special attention to the operative area.
    - Ensure the patient wears only clean hospital clothing.

8.  **Preoperative Preparation - Hair Removal:**
    IF hair removal is necessary THEN
    - Perform hair removal as close to the time of surgery as possible.
    - IF shaving is required THEN
    - Shave immediately before surgery.
    - Use a clipper or chemical depilator instead of shaving to minimize skin damage and reduce postoperative wound infection risk.
    - Ask about history of allergic reactions to chemical solutions.

9.  **Preoperative Preparation - Skin Scrubbing:**
    IF scrubbing is performed THEN
    - Work away from the operative site during scrubbing.
    - Wash visibly soiled skin with soap and water before using surgical scrub.
    - Administer preanesthetic medication as per policy.

10. **Preoperative Preparation - Skin Antisepsis:**
    IF skin preparation is required THEN
    - Use one of the following surgical scrubs:
        - Povidone-iodine (combined with detergent)
        - Chlorhexidine gluconate (alone or with cetrimide)
        - Alcohol (70% ethyl or isopropyl alcohol)

11. **Preoperative Preparation - Drug Treatment:**
    IF relevant THEN
    - Administer prophylactic antibiotics (see specific antibiotic prophylaxis guidelines).
    - Administer antihypertensives, IV fluids, anticoagulants, Vitamin K, etc., as indicated.

12. **Preoperative Preparation - Non-Drug Treatment:**
    IF relevant THEN
    - Perform rectal washouts or stomach wash.

13. **Handling Medicolegal Cases:**
    IF the case is medicolegal THEN
    - Provide first aid.
    - Stabilize the patient.
    - Guide and help in transferring the patient to an appropriate center.
    - Send information (in duplicate) to the police.
    - Prepare a medicolegal report.
    - Preserve and seal clothes, fluid, and stain samples as indicated.
    - Respond to information requests from the police.
    - Arrange for a dying declaration if indicated.
    - Preserve all X-rays and patient records.
    - Respond to court summons.
    - IF death occurs, handover the body to the police.
    - IF discharge/referral occurs, inform the police.

14. **Care During Transfer (Emergency):**
    IF an emergency transfer is required THEN
    - Identify the degree of emergency.
    - Perform resuscitative measures (manage shock, provide oxygen).
    - Transfer in a well-equipped ambulance to the appropriate referral center.
    - Obtain prior intimation and confirmation of readiness at the referral center.
    - Have a doctor or paramedical staff accompany the patient.
    - Prepare a referral slip containing:
        - Patient's condition upon initial presentation.
        - Diagnosis and resuscitative measures taken.
        - Reasons for referral.
        - Referral destination.
        - Precautions advised during transportation.
        - Other relevant information (staff, equipment, communication).

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Povidone-iodine: May cause patient skin sensitivity occasionally.
- Chlorhexidine gluconate: Should be avoided in preparation of the eye. Not effective in presence of organic material (soap, sand, oils, blood, body fluids).
- Alcohol (70% ethyl or isopropyl alcohol): Should not be used on mucus membranes or open wounds (causes desiccation). All traces must be dry before applying drapes.
