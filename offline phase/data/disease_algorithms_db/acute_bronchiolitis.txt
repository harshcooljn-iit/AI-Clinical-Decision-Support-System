DISEASE: Acute Bronchiolitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Syr.: Syrup
- URTI: Upper Respiratory Tract Infection
- SpO2: Peripheral Oxygen Saturation
- O2: Oxygen
- NaCl: Sodium Chloride
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- hr: Hour
- mL: Milliliter
- tab: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical diagnosis based on symptoms (running nose, cough, wheeze, rapid respiration, chest indrawing, difficulty feeding, hypoxaemia, cyanosis, respiratory failure).
- History of viral URTI in family members.
- Physical exam findings (respiratory rate, chest indrawing, feeding difficulty, cyanosis).
- SpO2 monitoring.
- Chest X-ray may show hyperinflation and atelectasis (not primary diagnostic tool).
- Lab tests (leucocyte count) are usually normal or show lymphocytosis.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has mild disease (ambulatory treatment at home):
    THEN:
        - No antibiotics.
        - Administer Syr. paracetamol 10-15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours for fever.
        - Monitor for improvement.
        IF patient shows overall improvement with no evidence of chest indrawing, cyanosis, or difficulty in feeding:
            THEN: Continue treatment as above.
        ELSE IF patient shows partial improvement:
            THEN: Reassess after 2 days or earlier if patient deteriorates.
        ELSE IF patient shows no improvement or deterioration:
            THEN: Manage as severe disease (hospitalize).

IF patient has severe disease (needs hospitalization):
    THEN:
        - Administer oxygen (humidified O2) by oxygen hood or nasal catheter.
        - Administer intravenous fluids if the child is unable to feed.
        - Administer Adrenaline inhalation 0.3 mg/kg by nebulizer (dissolved in 3 ml saline) every 4-6 hours as required.
        - IF Adrenaline is not available:
            THEN: Try salbutamol inhalation 0.15 mg/kg (dissolved in 3 ml saline) and continue if response is observed.
        - Administer Syr. paracetamol 10-15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours for fever.
        - Do not use sedatives.
        - Monitor respiratory rate, lower chest indrawing, difficulty in feeding, excessive crying, cyanosis, and oxygen saturation every 4-6 hours until significant improvement.
        - IF child does not improve/deteriorates:
            THEN: Look for underlying heart disease (myocarditis/congenital heart disease), get a chest X-ray, and look for complications (massive collapse, infection, pneumothorax) and manage accordingly.

Non-pharmacological treatment (for all severities, especially mild):
    - Normal saline drops in nostrils as needed, especially before feeds.
    - Home remedies (ginger, honey, tulsi) for cough control.
    - Plenty of liquids orally.
    - Nurse in a comfortable environment.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Antibiotics: Not indicated for uncomplicated acute bronchiolitis.
- Steroids (inhaled systemic): Not recommended for treatment.
- Sedatives: Should not be used.
- Ribavirine inhalation: No role in uncomplicated patients.
