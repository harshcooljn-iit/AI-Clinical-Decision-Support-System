DISEASE: Insomnia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- mg: milligram
- Tab: Tablet
- nitrazepam: Nitrazepam
- clonazepam: Clonazepam
- zopiclone: Zopiclone
- zolpidem: Zolpidem
- diazepam: Diazepam

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Difficulty initiating sleep.
- Frequent awakenings from sleep.
- Early morning insomnia.
- Non-restorative sleep.
- Patient distress due to decreased sleep (especially in the elderly).
- Eliciting stressful situations or symptoms of causative illness.
- Duration of symptoms varies.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient presents with symptoms of insomnia (difficulty initiating sleep, frequent awakenings, early morning insomnia, non-restorative sleep) AND patient is distressed by decreased sleep THEN proceed to treatment.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment (Sleep Hygiene):**
    IF Patient has insomnia THEN recommend sleep hygiene measures:
    - Maintain a regular sleep-wake schedule.
    - Avoid daytime naps.
    - Limit time in bed to usual duration.
    - Avoid large meals near bedtime; eat regularly.
    - Avoid stimulants (caffeine, nicotine, alcohol) especially in the evening.
    - Engage in mild to moderate exercise in the morning.
    - Avoid evening stimulation (TV); substitute with reading or relaxation.
    - Practice relaxation techniques (meditation, progressive muscle relaxation).
    - Ensure comfortable sleeping conditions.
    - Treat any underlying cause of insomnia.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment (If sleep is inadequate after non-pharmacological measures):**
    IF Sleep is inadequate after implementing sleep hygiene THEN consider pharmacological treatment for a short period (2-6 weeks).

4.  **Medication Options:**
    IF Pharmacological treatment is indicated THEN choose one of the following options:
    - Option A: Tab. nitrazepam 5-10 mg, ½ to 1 hr before bedtime.
    - Option B: Tab. clonazepam 1-2 mg, ½ to 1 hr before bedtime.
    - Option C: Tab. zopiclone 7.5-15 mg, ½ to 1 hr before bedtime.
    - Option D: Tab. zolpidem 5-10 mg, ½ to 1 hr before bedtime.

5.  **Patient Education and Follow-up:**
    IF Pharmacological treatment is initiated THEN:
    - Educate patient on sleep hygiene instructions.
    - Caution patient regarding the risk of dependence with diazepam and nitrazepam.
    - Plan for tapering off benzodiazepines (if used) in 4-6 weeks.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Nitrazepam and diazepam: Use with caution due to risk of dependence.
- Benzodiazepines (including diazepam, nitrazepam, clonazepam): Patients with a history of substance abuse or dependence should be monitored closely or alternative treatments considered.
