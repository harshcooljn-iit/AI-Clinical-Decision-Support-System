DISEASE: Animal Bites - Dog Bite (Rabies Prophylaxis)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- HRIG: Human rabies immunoglobulin
- HDCV: Human diploid cell vaccine
- RVA: Rabies vaccine adsorbed
- PCECV: Purified chick embryo culture vaccine
- IM: Intramuscular
- ACIP: Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices
- MMWR: Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation (prodromal, excitation, convulsions).
- Exposure history (bite, mucous membrane, open wound).
- Animal evaluation (healthy/available for observation, rabid/suspected, unknown).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Animal type is Dog, Cat, or Ferret AND animal is healthy AND available for 10-day observation:
    IF Animal develops clinical signs of rabies during the 10-day observation period:
        THEN Start rabies post-exposure prophylaxis (vaccination).
    ELSE
        THEN Do not start vaccination.
    ENDIF
ELSE IF Animal is rabid or suspected rabid:
    THEN Immediately start rabies post-exposure prophylaxis (vaccination).
ELSE IF Animal is unknown (escaped):
    THEN Consult public health officials regarding rabies post-exposure prophylaxis.
ENDIF

# Treatment Steps (if prophylaxis is indicated)
# 1. Wound Care
# 2. Passive Immunization (HRIG)
# 3. Active Immunization (Vaccine)

# Immediate Measures (within 3 hours)
IF Bite/injury occurred:
    THEN Scrub wound and edges with soap and water.
    IF Wound is a puncture:
        THEN Swab deeply in the wound and around its edges.
    ENDIF
    THEN Apply virucidal agent (1% or 2% benzalkonium chloride OR povidone-iodine) and rinse.
    THEN Administer Tetanus prophylaxis as indicated by patient's immunization status.
    THEN Do not suture bite wounds immediately.
    IF Suturing is required:
        THEN Hold for 24-48 hours.
        THEN Apply minimum number of stitches.
        THEN Administer antirabies immunoglobulin locally under the cover of stitches.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

# Post-exposure Prophylaxis based on Immunization Status

# Non-immunized persons
IF Patient is non-immunized:
    THEN Administer HRIG:
        - Infiltrate full dose around wound(s).
        - Administer remaining volume IM at an anatomic site distant from vaccine administration.
    THEN Administer Vaccine (HDCV):
        - Inject IM (deltoid area).
        - Schedule: One dose on days 0, 3, 7, 14, and 28.
    # Alternative Vaccines (RVA, PCECV)
    THEN Administer Vaccine (RVA or PCECV):
        - Administer IM for prophylaxis.
        - Check antibody titers at 2 to 4 weeks.
    THEN Withhold Corticosteroids, antimalarials, and other immunosuppressives if possible.
    THEN If history of hypersensitivity:
        - Cautiously give immunoprophylaxis in a controlled setting.
        - Have antihistamines and epinephrine available.
ENDIF

# Previously immunized persons
IF Patient is previously immunized:
    THEN Do not administer HRIG.
    THEN Administer Vaccine (HDCV or PCECV):
        - 1.0 ml, IM (deltoid area).
        - Schedule: One dose on days 0 and 3.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- HRIG: Not administered to previously immunized persons.
- Corticosteroids, antimalarials, immunosuppressives: Should be withheld during treatment if possible (interfere with active immunity).
- Vaccine (HDCV, RVA, PCECV): Not administered in the same syringe as HRIG (may partially suppress antibody production).
