DISEASE: Myocardial Infarction

ABBREVIATIONS:
- MI: Myocardial Infarction
- Tab: Tablet
- mg: Milligram
- IV: Intravenous
- SC: Subcutaneous
- rTPA: Recombinant tissue plasminogen activator
- Inj: Injection
- mmHg: Millimeters of mercury
- ECG: Electrocardiogram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Chest pain, breathlessness, perspiration, syncope
- Tachycardia or bradycardia
- Hypotension or hypertension
- Sudden cardiac arrest
- ECG changes showing ischemia or infarction
- Raised cardiac markers

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient presents with Myocardial Infarction (MI):
    THEN:
        - Hospitalize and provide complete immobilization.
        - Administer Oxygen at 4-6 liters/min.
        - Administer Oral uncoated chewable Aspirin 325 mg.
        - Administer Tab. Isosorbide dinitrate 5 mg sublingually, up to 3 times.
        - Administer Tab. Atorvastatin 80 mg.
        - Administer Tab. Clopidogrel 300 mg.
        - Administer Inj. Morphine sulphate 10 mg or Inj. Diamorphine 5 mg IV.

        IF thrombolysis is considered:
            THEN:
                IF time of admission is within 30 minutes:
                    THEN:
                        - Consider thrombolysis with intravenous streptokinase or alteplase.

                        IF streptokinase is chosen:
                            THEN:
                                - Administer Inj. streptokinase 1.5 million units in 100 ml of saline as an intravenous infusion over 1 hour.
                                - Monitor for hypotension and manage with fluids and slower infusion rate if needed.

                        IF alteplase (rTPA) is chosen:
                            THEN:
                                - Administer Inj. alteplase regimen over 90 minutes:
                                    - Bolus dose of 15 mg.
                                    - Followed by 0.75 mg/kg of body weight.
                                    - Followed by 0.5 mg/kg body weight over 60 minutes.

                ELSE:
                    - Thrombolysis is not indicated due to time elapsed since admission.

        - Administer Inj. Metoprolol 5 mg IV every 2-5 minutes for a maximum of 3 doses.
        - Change to Tab. Metoprolol 50 mg every 6 hours for 48 hours.
        - Followed by Tab. Metoprolol 100 mg every 12 hours.
        - Administer Inj. Nitroglycerine IV at 0.6 – 1.2 mg/hour.
        - Administer Inj. Heparin 200 units/Kg SC twice daily for 5 days (in addition to oral aspirin).

    - Monitor ECG, blood pressure, and cardiac enzymes.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:

CONTRAINDICATIONS TO THROMBOLYTIC THERAPY:

IF thrombolytic therapy is considered:
    THEN:
        IF any of the following absolute contraindications are present:
            THEN:
                - DO NOT administer thrombolytics.
                - Absolute contraindications: Active internal bleeding, previous sub-arachnoid or intra-cerebral hemorrhage, non-hemorrhagic stroke within the last 1 year, uncontrolled hypertension (systolic > 180 mmHg, diastolic > 110 mmHg).
        ELSE IF any of the following relative contraindications are present:
            THEN:
                - Use caution or DO NOT administer thrombolytics based on clinical judgment.
                - Relative contraindications: Current use of anti-coagulants, recent (< 2 weeks) invasive surgical procedure, recent prolonged (> 10 min) cardiopulmonary resuscitation, known bleeding diathesis, pregnancy, hemorrhage prone ophthalmic conditions, active peptic ulcer, disease, recent history of severe hypertension.

        IF streptokinase is considered:
            THEN:
                IF patient received streptokinase within the preceding 5 days to 2 years:
                    THEN:
                        - DO NOT administer streptokinase due to risk of allergic reactions.
