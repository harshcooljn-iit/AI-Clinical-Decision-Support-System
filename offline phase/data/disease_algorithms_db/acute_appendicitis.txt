```text
DISEASE: Acute Appendicitis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Tab: Tablet
- ciprofloxacin: Ciprofloxacin
- mg: Milligram
- BD: Twice daily
- metronidazole: Metronidazole
- TDS: Three times daily
- gm: Gram
- Inj: Injection
- IM: Intramuscular
- ranitidine: Ranitidine
- Cap: Capsule
- omeprazole: Omeprazole
- CECT: Contrast-Enhanced Computed Tomography
- USG: Ultrasonography
- RIF: Right Iliac Fossa
- McBurney's sign: Tenderness at McBurney's point

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation (pain migration, anorexia, nausea, vomiting, fever, McBurney's sign, rebound tenderness).
- Blood counts (neutrophilia).
- Imaging (USG Abdomen, CECT Abdomen).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has early catarrhal appendicitis with minimally inflamed appendix THEN
    IF Patient can receive oral medication THEN
        THEN Administer Tab. ciprofloxacin 500mg BD AND Tab. metronidazole 400 mg TDS
        OR Administer Inj. cefotaxim 1 gm BD (second generation cephalosporin)
    ENDIF
    Administer Inj. diclofenac 75 mg IM
    Administer Tab. ranitidine 150 mg BD
    OR Administer Cap. omeprazole 40 mg BD
ELSE
    Perform Appendicectomy (open or laparoscopic approach)
ENDIF
```
