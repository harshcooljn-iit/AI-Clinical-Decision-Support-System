DISEASE: Diabetes Mellitus

ABBREVIATIONS:
- DM: Diabetes Mellitus
- IDDM: Insulin Dependent Diabetes Mellitus
- DKA: Diabetic Ketoacidosis
- GTT: Glucose Tolerance Test
- OGTT: Oral Glucose Tolerance Test
- HbA1c: Glycosylated Haemoglobin
- HDL: High-Density Lipoprotein
- LDL: Low-Density Lipoprotein
- VLDL: Very Low-Density Lipoprotein

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Diagnosis is made by demonstration of hyperglycaemia (random plasma glucose > 200 mg/dl).
- Diagnosis can also be made using Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) cut-off levels (see Table 10).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    IF Patient presents with symptoms of DM (polyuria, polydipsia, unexplained weight loss, glycosuria, ketonuria) AND random plasma glucose > 200 mg/dl THEN Diagnosis of DM confirmed.
    ELSE IF Fasting plasma glucose > 126 mg/dl THEN Diagnosis of DM confirmed.
    ELSE IF 2-hour plasma glucose during OGTT > 200 mg/dl THEN Diagnosis of DM confirmed.
    ELSE IF Fasting glucose is 110-125 mg/dl AND 2-hour plasma glucose during OGTT is < 200 mg/dl but >= 140 mg/dl THEN Diagnosis of Impaired Glucose Tolerance.

2.  **Initial Therapy (Hospital Setting):**
    IF Patient is newly diagnosed with DM (or recovering from DKA) THEN
        - Initiate treatment with fast-acting (regular) insulin.
        - Initial dose: 0.5-1.0 unit/kg/day.
        - Administer Inj. Regular insulin 0.1-0.25 units/kg subcutaneous injections every 6-8 hours before meals.
        - Monitor blood glucose levels frequently (e.g., before meals).
        - Adjust total daily insulin dose based on blood glucose monitoring over 1-2 days to determine the patient's requirement.
        - Once stable on 6-hourly injections, switch to a "2 daily injections" schedule.

3.  **"2 Daily Injections" Schedule:**
    IF Patient is switched to "2 daily injections" schedule THEN
        - Use a combination of intermediate-acting (e.g., lente) insulin and fast-acting (regular) insulin in a ratio of 2-3:1.
        - Calculate total daily dose (TDD).
        - Inject 2/3 of TDD before breakfast (combination of intermediate and fast-acting insulin).
        - Inject 1/3 of TDD before dinner (combination of intermediate and fast-acting insulin).
        - Example: If TDD is 30 units, inject 20 units (14 units lente + 6 units regular) before breakfast and 10 units (6 units lente + 4 units regular) before dinner.
        - Monitor blood glucose levels before each meal and adjust insulin dose accordingly.
        - Target blood glucose levels: Fasting 80 mg/dl, After meals 140 mg/dl (acceptable range 80-240 mg/dl).
        - Target early morning (3 AM) blood glucose level: > 70 mg/dl.

4.  **Insulin Dose Modification:**
    IF Blood glucose levels are outside the target range THEN
        - Adjust insulin dose by 10-15% (generally not more than 6 units).
    IF Patient is newly diagnosed and stabilized THEN
        - Expect a gradual decline in insulin requirement (up to 0.5 units/kg/day) over weeks to months.
    IF Patient is being discharged from the hospital THEN
        - Decrease total daily insulin dose by 10% due to increased activity at home.

5.  **Monitoring and Control Assessment:**
    - Monitor blood glucose before each meal and at bedtime initially (first few weeks).
    - Reduce frequency to twice a week after stabilization.
    - Monitor blood glucose at 3-4 AM periodically to detect early morning hypoglycemia.
    - Monitor urine for sugar initially 3-4 times daily before meals.
    - Monitor urine for ketones once daily initially.
    - Measure HbA1c every 3 months.
        - HbA1c 6-9%: Very good control.
        - HbA1c 9-12%: Fair control.
        - HbA1c > 12%: Poor control.
    - Measure serum lipids (cholesterol, HDL, LDL, VLDL, triglycerides) and urine for protein once every year.
        - Target serum cholesterol: < 200 mg/dl.
        - Target LDL: < 130 mg/dl.
        - Target triglycerides: < 140 mg/dl.
    - Perform thyroid function tests once every year.

6.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Diet:
        - Maintain a regular eating pattern synchronized with diet and insulin dosing.
        - Calorie mixture: 55% carbohydrates, 30% fat, 15% proteins.
        - Avoid refined sugars. Use sugar-free carbonated drinks.
        - Reduce animal fats and replace with vegetable fats.
        - Calorie intake split: 20% breakfast, 20% lunch, 30% dinner, 10% mid-morning snack, 10% mid-afternoon snack, 10% evening snack.
    - Physical Activity:
        - Advise usual exercises (walking, jogging, swimming, tennis).
        - For unusual exercise on a scheduled day, reduce insulin dose by 10-15%.

7.  **Patient Education:**
    - Teach self-care:
        - Technique of measuring insulin in the syringe (always draw regular insulin first).
        - Technique of subcutaneous injections and importance of rotating injection sites (arms, thighs, buttocks, abdomen).
        - Adherence to diet and regular exercise.
        - Recognizing and managing hypoglycemia symptoms at home.
