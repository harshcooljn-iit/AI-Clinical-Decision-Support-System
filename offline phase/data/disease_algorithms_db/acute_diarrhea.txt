3. DIAGNOSTICS:
    - Clinical features: Frequent stools, vomiting, fever, dehydration (some or severe).
    - Investigations (based on indications):
        - Stool microscopy
        - Blood urea, S. electrolytes (SE), arterial blood gas (ABG)
        - Infection screening by TLC, DLC, band cell count, ESR, CRP
        - Blood culture
        - Chest X-ray, CSF, others (as required)
        - Stool culture (no role)

4. TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

    IF Dehydration is present:
        THEN Maintain hydration.
            IF Some dehydration:
                THEN Use Low osmolarity ORS (75 ml/kg in 4 hours under observation).
                    IF Dehydration corrected after 4 hours OR child was not dehydrated initially:
                        THEN Send home with instructions for ORS (2:1 dilution as accepted, 10 ml/kg per large stool).
                        THEN Advise to report back if vomiting persists or no urine for >8 hours.
                    ELSE IF Dehydration not corrected after 4 hours:
                        THEN Repeat same amount of ORS in next 4 hours.
                            IF Dehydration corrected:
                                THEN Send home.
                            ELSE IF Dehydration does not improve in 8 hours OR worsens:
                                THEN Abandon ORT and give IV fluids.
            ELSE IF Severe dehydration OR shock OR contraindications to ORT OR failure of ORT:
                THEN Give IV fluid therapy.
                    IF Shock:
                        THEN Push 20 ml/kg of Ringer's lactate or normal saline over 15 minutes.
                        THEN Repeat up to twice more if shock persists.
                        THEN Use central venous pressure monitoring if shock persists after 60 ml/kg.
                    ELSE IF Some dehydration:
                        THEN Give 75 ml/kg of N/2 saline over 8 hours.
                    ELSE IF Severe dehydration:
                        THEN Give 100 ml/kg of N/2 saline over 8 hours (half in first 2-3 hours).
                    IF Child passes urine after initial fluid bolus:
                        THEN Continue N/2 saline as indicated (75 ml/kg for some, 100 ml/kg for severe).
                    IF Child does not pass urine after hydration:
                        THEN Consider parenchymal renal failure.
                        THEN Give fluid challenge (20 ml/kg normal saline).
                        THEN Give frusemide injection (1-2 mg/kg).
                        IF Still no urine:
                            THEN Consider parenchymal renal failure and manage accordingly.

    // Non-pharmacological treatment:
    THEN Maintain nutrition: continue breastfeeding, continue normal light diet (e.g., khichri, dalia, banana, mashed dal).
    THEN Do not dilute or stop milk.
    THEN Give extra food during recovery.
    THEN Use HAF (rice, kanji, buttermilk, dal soup, coconut water, weak tea) in place of or along with ORS.
    THEN Avoid soft drinks, sweetened fruit drinks, and strong tea.

    // Pharmacological treatment:
    IF Specific therapy is indicated (Frank blood and mucus in stool OR >10 pus cells/HPF):
        THEN Give Syr. ciprofloxacin 15-20 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses for 5 days.

    IF Giardiasis suspected (trophozoites seen on stool microscopy):
        THEN Give Syr. metronidazole 15 mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses for 5 days
        OR Give Syr. tinidazole 50 mg/kg/day single dose (max. 2 g).

    IF Cholera suspected (severe watery diarrhoea):
        THEN Use fluid therapy as mainstay.
        THEN Use antibiotics to prevent spread:
            IF Give Syr. doxycycline 5 mg/kg (max 200 mg) in single dose.
            OR Give Syr. furazolidone 5 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses for 3 days.
            OR Give Syr. cotrimoxazole (TMP) 8 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses for 5 days.
            OR Give Syr. erythromycin 30 mg/kg/day for 3 days.

    // Principles of ORT:
    THEN Give ORS in small sips.
    THEN Vomiting is not a contraindication unless persistent.
    THEN Contraindicated in altered sensorium or paralytic ileus.
    THEN Stop ORS as soon as diarrhoea stops.

    // Antiemetics (use cautiously):
    IF Vomiting persists despite ORS in sips:
        THEN Consider other causes (meningitis, URI, dyselectrolytaemia).
        THEN Give IV fluids if vomiting persists.
        THEN Occasionally, give 1 or 2 doses of metoclopramide (0.5 mg/kg) or domperidone (0.5 mg/kg) before giving IV fluids.

    // Contraindicated drugs:
    THEN Avoid antimotility agents (e.g., diphenoxylate, atropine) and antisecretory agents (e.g., loperamide, salicylates).

    // Monitoring:
    THEN Record vitals (pulse, BP, CFT, respiratory rate, temperature) hourly/6 hourly.
    THEN Monitor for improvement/worsening of dehydration signs.
    THEN Record urine output, stool frequency, and consistency.

    // Admission criteria:
    IF PEM grade III OR age <3 months OR associated severe systemic infections (septicaemia, meningitis, pneumonia):
        THEN Admit.

    // Persistent diarrhoea (>7 days):
    IF Diarrhoea persists for >7 days:
        THEN Investigate for lactose intolerance, incipient infections (UTI, rare gut organisms).

6. CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    - ORT: Altered sensorium, paralytic ileus.
    - Specific drugs: Listed within the specific therapy sections (e.g., Shigella strains resistant to ampicillin/cotrimoxazole).
    - General contraindications: Antimotility agents (e.g., diphenoxylate, atropine) and antisecretory agents (e.g., loperamide, salicylates).
