DISEASE: Snake Bite

ABBREVIATIONS:
- WHO: World Health Organization
- ASV: Anti-snake venom
- BP: Blood Pressure
- PR: Pulse Rate
- RR: Respiratory Rate
- Hb: Hemoglobin
- PT: Prothrombin Time
- APTT: Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time
- FDP: Fibrin Degradation Products
- D-Dimer: D-Dimer
- ELISA: Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay
- Inj.: Injection
- mg: Milligram
- ml: Milliliter
- tab: Tablet
- hr: Hour
- tab: Tablet
- R: Reassure
- I: Immobilize
- G-H: Get to Hospital
- T: Tell the doctor

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical signs and symptoms (e.g., swelling, pain, bleeding, paralysis, ptosis).
- Identification of snake (if possible).
- Blood tests (clotting time, Hb, platelets, PT, APTT, FDP, D-Dimer).
- Urine examination (proteinuria, RBC, hemoglobinuria, myoglobinuria).
- Serum tests (creatinine, urea, potassium).
- Vital signs (O2 saturation, BP, RR, postural BP).
- ECG, X-Ray, CT, ultrasound (limited benefit).
- ELISA for snake species confirmation.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient is bitten by a snake THEN
    # First Aid
    IF patient is conscious THEN
        REASSURE patient and control anxiety (R)
        IMMOBILIZE bitten part by splinting the limb (I)
    ENDIF

    # Emergency Management
    GET patient to the nearest hospital (G-H)
    TELL the doctor about symptoms (T)

    # Things NOT to do
    DO NOT apply tourniquet
    DO NOT wash the bite site with soap or other solutions
    DO NOT make cuts or incisions near the bite
    DO NOT apply extreme cold or herbal/folk remedies
    DO NOT suck venom
    DO NOT give the victim drinks, alcohol, or drugs

    # Initial Assessment and Stabilization
    ENSURE airway, breathing, and shock management
    RELIEVE pain with non-sedating analgesic (e.g., oral paracetamol)

    # Determine Envenomation Status
    IF there are definite signs of envenomation (coagulopathy or neurotoxicity) THEN
        # Administer Anti-snake venom (ASV)
        ADMINISTER polyvalent ASV (effective against Indian cobra, Russell's viper, saw-scaled viper, Indian krait)

        # ASV Dosage
        IF patient is an adult OR child THEN
            INITIAL DOSE: 8 to 10 vials (each vial is 10 ml reconstituted ASV)
            ADMINISTER ASV over 1 hour at a constant speed
            MONITOR patient closely for 2 hours
        ENDIF

        # ASV Reactions
        IF anaphylaxis is evident THEN
            DISCONTINUE ASV
            ADMINISTER Inj. hydrocortisone 100 mg + antihistamine OR Inj. adrenaline subcutaneously
        ENDIF

        # Repeat Dosing
        IF coagulation is not restored THEN
            REPEAT DOSE: 5-10 vials (half to one full dose) every 6 hours
            UNTIL coagulation is restored
            UNLESS a species is identified against which polyvalent ASV is ineffective
        ENDIF

        # Massive Envenomation
        IF snake is King Cobra or Australian elapid (injects massive venom) THEN
            MASSIVE DOSE: 50+ vials may be required
        ENDIF

    ELSE
        # No signs of envenomation
        OBSERVE patient closely for signs/symptoms of envenomation
    ENDIF

    # Follow-up
    IF patient is discharged within 24 hours THEN
        ADVISE patient to return if symptoms/signs worsen (bleeding, pain, swelling, difficulty breathing, altered sensorium)
        EXPLAIN about signs/symptoms of serum sickness (may manifest after 5-10 days)
    ENDIF

ENDIF
