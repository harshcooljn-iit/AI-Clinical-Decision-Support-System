```text
DISEASE: Scabies

ABBREVIATIONS:
- mcg/kg: micrograms per kilogram
- Tab.: Tablet
- GBHC: Gamma benzene hexachloride
- SOS: As directed (Stat Omnis Signum)

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination
- Inspection of mites from scabetic burrows

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient is an infant, neonate, child, pregnant, or lactating mother:
    THEN Apply Permethrin 5% generously after bath, at bedtime, covering the entire body surface below the neck (except face).

ELSE IF Patient is not an infant, neonate, child, pregnant, or lactating mother:
    THEN Administer Tab. ivermectin 200 mcg/kg as a single stat dose.
    THEN Repeat Tab. ivermectin 200 mcg/kg after 2 weeks.

    // Alternative topical treatments (not mutually exclusive, but usually one is chosen)
    // Option 1: GBHC lotion
    THEN Apply GBHC lotion 1% single overnight application below neck on entire body surface after a thorough scrub bath.
    THEN Wash off GBHC lotion the next morning.

    // Option 2: Permethrin 5% (can be used in adults too)
    THEN Apply Permethrin 5% generously after bath, at bedtime, covering the entire surface of the body below neck (except face).

    // Symptomatic treatment
    THEN Administer Tab. cetirizine 10 mg daily (antihistamine for itching).

NON-PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT:
- Maintain personal hygiene.
- Disinfect bedding and clothing.
- Treat all family members simultaneously.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Ivermectin: Not specified in the text, but generally contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity.
- GBHC: Not specified in the text, but generally contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity.
- Permethrin: Not specified in the text, but generally contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity.
```
