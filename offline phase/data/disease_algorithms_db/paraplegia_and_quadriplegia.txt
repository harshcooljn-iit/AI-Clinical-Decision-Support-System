DISEASE: Paraplegia And Quadriplegia

ABBREVIATIONS:
- Inj.: Injection
- IV: Intravenous
- QID: Four times a day
- mg: Milligram
- d: Day
- TDS: Three times a day
- SC: Subcutaneous
- U: Unit
- INR: International Normalized Ratio
- Tab.: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical assessment of motor weakness (lower limbs for paraplegia, all four limbs for quadriplegia).
- Neurological examination to determine the level and nature of the lesion (cerebrum, spinal cord, motor roots, peripheral nerves, myoneural junction, or muscles).
- Imaging studies (e.g., MRI, CT) to identify the cause and location of the lesion (compressive or non-compressive spinal cord lesions).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF etiology is compressive myelopathy THEN
    urgent surgical intervention is required.
ELSE
    pharmacological treatment based on specific indications.

IF indication for corticosteroids exists (neoplastic spinal cord compression, traumatic spinal cord injuries, acute transverse myelitis, multiple sclerosis, neuromyelitis optica, sarcoid myelitis, ADEM) THEN
    Administer Inj. methylprednisolone 1 g IV for 3-5 days.
    Continuation of steroids depends on the underlying cause.
ENDIF

IF fever is present THEN
    Consider causes: quadriplegic fever (loss of thermoregulation) or infection (urinary tract, lung, skin, bone).
    Treat underlying cause.
ENDIF

IF bladder dysfunction is present THEN
    Assess type of dysfunction:
    IF detrusor spasticity is present THEN
        TREATMENT: Tab. oxybutynin 2.5–5 mg QID OR Tab. imipramine 25–200 mg/d.
    ELSE IF urinary dyssynergia (failure of sphincter relaxation) is present THEN
        TREATMENT: Tab. terazosin hydrochloride 1–2 mg TDS or QID, intermittent catheterization, OR condom catheter (men) OR permanent indwelling catheter.
    ELSE IF bladder areflexia (acute spinal shock or conus lesions) is present THEN
        TREATMENT: Catheterization.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

IF bowel dysfunction is present THEN
    Implement bowel regimens and disimpaction protocols.
    Ensure at least biweekly evacuation.
ENDIF

IF patient is within the first 2 weeks of injury/paralysis THEN
    RECOMMENDATION: Use calf-compression devices.
    RECOMMENDATION: Anticoagulation with Inj. heparin 5000 U SC every 12 h OR Tab. warfarin (target INR 2–3).
ELSE IF persistent paralysis exists THEN
    RECOMMENDATION: Continue anticoagulation for 3 months.
ENDIF

IF risk of decubitus ulcers is present THEN
    PROPHYLAXIS: Frequent position changes.
    PROPHYLAXIS: Use special mattresses (water or air beds).
    PROPHYLAXIS: Cushion pressure points (sacral prominence, heels).
    IF ulcer develops THEN
        TREATMENT: Careful cleansing.
        TREATMENT: Surgical or enzyme debridement of necrotic tissue.
        TREATMENT: Appropriate dressing and drainage.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

IF spasticity is present THEN
    RECOMMENDATION: Stretching exercises.
    TREATMENT: Tab. baclofen 15–240 mg/d in divided doses.
    TREATMENT: Tab. diazepam 2-4 mg at bedtime (for leg spasms interrupting sleep).
    TREATMENT: Tab. Tizanidine 2–8 mg TDS.
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for the listed drugs, but standard contraindications apply based on drug properties and patient comorbidities).
