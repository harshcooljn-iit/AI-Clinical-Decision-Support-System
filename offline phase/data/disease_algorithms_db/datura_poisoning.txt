DISEASE: Datura Poisoning

ABBREVIATIONS:
- IV: Intravenous
- IM: Intramuscular
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram
- Inj.: Injection

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation includes: Gastric irritation, dry mouth/throat, burning stomach pain, difficulty swallowing/talking, giddiness, ataxia, incoordination, flushed face, dry hot skin, hyperthermia, diplopia, dilated pupils (loss of accommodation), conjunctival reddening, drowsiness, erythematous rash, full bounding pulse (later weak/irregular), muttering delirium, hallucinations, stupor, convulsions, coma, respiratory failure.
- History of ingestion of Datura stramonium (seeds or fruits).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment & Decontamination:**
    IF time since exposure is within 1 hour AND patient is asymptomatic OR large ingestion suspected:
        THEN consider early gastric emptying (stomach wash).
        IF gastric emptying is performed:
            THEN use 1:10,000 potassium permanganate solution OR 5% tannic acid solution.
    ELSE:
        THEN proceed to supportive care and pharmacological treatment as needed.

2.  **Temperature Management:**
    IF patient is severely hyperthermic (high core body temperature):
        THEN prioritize aggressive temperature reduction using ice water or evaporative cooling (mist and fans).
        ELSE:
            THEN monitor temperature and use supportive measures.
    NOTE: Antipyretics and simple cooling blankets are ineffective for severe hyperthermia in this context.

3.  **Agitation/Sedation:**
    IF patient is agitated:
        THEN administer benzodiazepines.
        IF using midazolam:
            THEN administer Inj. midazolam 0.01-0.1 mg/kg IV.
        IF using lorazepam:
            THEN administer Inj. lorazepam 0.05-0.1 mg/kg IV.

4.  **Severe Poisoning/Anticholinergic Effects:**
    IF patient has severe poisoning symptoms (e.g., severe delirium, hallucinations, significant anticholinergic signs):
        THEN consider Inj. physostigmine.
        THEN administer Inj. physostigmine 1.2 mg IM or IV.
        IF symptoms persist after half an hour:
            THEN repeat Inj. physostigmine 1.2 mg IM or IV.
        MONITOR FOR SIDE EFFECTS: Bradycardia, heart block, excessive secretions.

5.  **Convulsions:**
    IF patient develops convulsions:
        THEN administer Inj. diazepam 10 mg IV slowly diluted.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- No specific contraindications listed for the treatments mentioned, but caution is advised with physostigmine (monitor for bradycardia, heart block, secretions).
- Antipyretics and simple cooling blankets are ineffective for severe hyperthermia.
