DIAGNOSTICS:
- Physiological, behavioral, cognitive symptoms indicating repeated continued use of opioid drugs despite related problems.
- Opioid intoxication symptoms: Euphoria, dysphoria, apathy, psychomotor agitation/retardation, impaired judgment, pupillary constriction, drowsiness, slurred speech, impaired attention/memory.
- Opioid withdrawal symptoms: Dysphoric mood, lacrimation, nausea/vomiting, muscle aches, diarrhea, yawning, fever, insomnia, pupillary dilatation, piloerection, sweating.
- Impaired social or occupational functioning.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

# Non-pharmacological Treatment
IF patient has Opioid Use Disorder THEN
    - Provide individual counseling.
    - Provide family support.
    - Encourage joining self-help groups.
    - Consider prolonged inpatient stay with rehabilitative inputs (beneficial for highly relapsing disorder).
ENDIF

# Pharmacological Treatment (Acute Withdrawal)

# Option 1: Paracetamol and Dextropropoxyphen
IF patient requires pharmacological treatment for withdrawal symptoms THEN
    - Administer Tab. paracetamol 500 mg and dextropropoxyphen 32.5 mg.
    - Start dosage at BD (Twice daily).
    - Gradually increase to maximum 2 tablets TDS (Three times daily).
ENDIF

# Option 2: Buprenorphine and Naloxone
IF patient requires pharmacological treatment for withdrawal symptoms THEN
    - Administer Tab. buprenorphine 2 mg and naloxone 0.5 mg.
    - Start dosage at 1 tablet BD (Twice daily).
    - Increase dosage to maximum of TDS (Three times daily) or QID (Four times daily) depending on severity of withdrawal symptoms.
ENDIF

# Option 3: Clonidine
IF patient requires symptomatic management of withdrawal symptoms THEN
    - Administer Tab. clonidine 50 mcg BD (Twice daily).
    - Monitor BP (Blood pressure) carefully.
ENDIF

# Option 4: NSAIDs
IF patient has muscular pains associated with withdrawal THEN
    - Administer NSAIDs (Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs).
ENDIF

# Long-Term Treatment (Managed by Psychiatrist)

# Naltrexone for Craving Reduction and Abstinence Maintenance
IF patient has been opioid-free for at least 7-10 days AND requires long-term treatment THEN
    - Assess baseline hepatic functions.
    - Administer Tab. naltrexone 50 mg/day orally.
    - Monitor hepatic functions once a month while on naltrexone treatment.
    - Continue drug for a period of 6 months (or longer if appropriate).
    - IF significant liver disease develops (e.g., several-fold increase in serum transaminases) THEN
        - Withdraw naltrexone.
    ENDIF
ENDIF

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Naltrexone:
    IF patient has significant liver disease (e.g., several-fold increase in serum transaminases) THEN
        - DO NOT administer naltrexone.
    ENDIF
