DISEASE: Chronic Suppurative Otitis Media (CSOM)

ABBREVIATIONS:
- CSOM: Chronic Suppurative Otitis Media
- URTI: Upper Respiratory Tract Infection
- HCl: Hydrochloric Acid
- w/v: weight/volume
- Cap: Capsule
- mg: Milligram
- kg: Kilogram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Otoscopy: Mucopurulent/mucoid/dry discharge, central perforation.
- Hearing test: Conductive deafness (tuning fork test).
- Examination: Middle ear mucosa (normal/edematous), audiogram, microscope.
- Culture: Pus culture and sensitivity.
- Imaging: X-ray mastoids, HRCT temporal bone.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF Patient has CSOM:
    THEN Perform Aural toilet (dry mopping or careful suction).

    IF Pharmacological treatment is indicated:
        THEN Administer topical antibiotics:
            IF No marked inflammation:
                THEN Use ciprofloxacin HCl 0.3% w/v ear drops 2-3 drops 3-4 times daily.
            ELSE IF Marked inflammation is present:
                THEN Use prednisolone 0.5% + chloramphenicol 5% + lignocaine 2% + acetic acid 2% ear drops 3-4 drops 3-4 times daily.

        IF Profuse mucopurulent discharge AND associated URTI:
            THEN Administer systemic antibiotics:
                IF Patient is an adult:
                    THEN Use Cap. amoxicillin 750-1500 mg in 3 divided doses.
                ELSE IF Patient is a child:
                    THEN Use amoxicillin 20-40 mg/kg in 3 divided doses.
                ELSE
                    THEN Antibiotic choice depends on culture and sensitivity report.

    IF Surgical treatment is indicated (ear dry, infection nidus treated):
        THEN Consider myringoplasty after assessing hearing status.

PATIENT EDUCATION:
    - Advise patient to avoid water or dust entering the ear.
    - Advise patient to seek immediate treatment for URTI.
    - Advise patient NOT to instill oil if there is discharge or known tympanic membrane perforation.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Oil instillation IF there is discharge OR known tympanic membrane perforation.
