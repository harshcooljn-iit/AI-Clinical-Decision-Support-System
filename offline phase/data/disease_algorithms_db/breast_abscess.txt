```text
DISEASE: Breast Abscess

ABBREVIATIONS:
- PO: By mouth
- gm: gram
- mg: milligram
- d: day
- tab: tablet
- cap: capsule
- qd: once daily
- tid: three times daily
- IM: Intramuscular
- IV: Intravenous
- NPO: Nothing by mouth
- PRN: As needed

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical examination (palpation, inspection).
- Imaging (ultrasound, mammography) may be used, especially for deep abscesses.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF abscess is superficial THEN
    - Apply constricting bandage.
    - Stop breast-feeding from the affected side.
    - Expression of milk with a breast pump to avoid engorgement.
    - Pharmacological treatment:
        - IF patient can take oral medication THEN
            - Administer Tab. amoxicillin (PO): 1-2 gm/d divided in 2-3 doses for 5 days OR
            - Administer Cap. ampicillin (PO): 2-4 gm/d divided in 2-3 doses for 5 days
        - Administer Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg thrice daily.
    - Surgical treatment:
        - Perform radial incision.
        - Gentle exploration with finger or Kelly forceps.
        - Abundant lavage with chlorhexidine-cetrimide solution.
        - Insertion of a corrugated drain.
ELSE IF abscess is deep THEN
    - Apply constricting bandage.
    - Stop breast-feeding from the affected side.
    - Expression of milk with a breast pump to avoid engorgement.
    - Pharmacological treatment:
        - IF patient can take oral medication THEN
            - Administer Tab. amoxicillin (PO): 1-2 gm/d divided in 2-3 doses for 5 days OR
            - Administer Cap. ampicillin (PO): 2-4 gm/d divided in 2-3 doses for 5 days
        - Administer Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg thrice daily.
    - Surgical treatment:
        - IF abscess is near nipple THEN
            - Perform peri-alveolar incision.
        - IF abscess is beneath the breast THEN
            - Perform incision beneath the breast.
        - Gentle exploration with finger or Kelly forceps.
        - Abundant lavage with chlorhexidine-cetrimide solution.
        - Insertion of a corrugated drain.
ENDIF
```
