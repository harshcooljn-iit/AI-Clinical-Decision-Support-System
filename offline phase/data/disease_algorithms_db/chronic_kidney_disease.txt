DISEASE: Chronic Kidney Disease

ABBREVIATIONS:
- BP: Blood Pressure
- CHF: Congestive Heart Failure
- Cr: Creatinine
- GFR: Glomerular Filtration Rate
- IHD: Ischemic Heart Disease
- ADPKD: Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease
- EPO: Erythropoietin
- mg: Milligram
- mcg: Microgram
- kcal: Kilocalorie
- SC: Subcutaneous
- mmol: Millimole
- ml: Milliliter
- tab: Tablet
- inj: Injection
- wt: Weight
- kg: Kilogram
- dl: Deciliter
- m2: Square meter
- yr: Year
- mmHg: Millimeters of mercury

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Kidney damage (structural or functional abnormality) for > 3 months.
- OR Decrease in GFR to < 6.0 ml/min for > 3 months.
- GFR calculation: (140 - age) * (Wt. in kg) * (0.85 if female) / (72 * Cr in mg/dl)
- Staging based on GFR levels (Table 1).

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    - Measure BP.
    - Urine analysis for proteinuria, hematuria, and sediments.
    - Estimate serum creatinine (screen at least yearly).
    - Assess risk factors (obesity, smoking, diabetes, hypertension, family history).

2.  **Diagnosis Confirmation:**
    - IF Kidney damage is present for > 3 months (structural or functional abnormality) OR GFR < 6.0 ml/min for > 3 months, THEN diagnosis of Chronic Kidney Disease is established.

3.  **Staging:**
    - IF GFR > 90 ml/1.73m2/min, THEN Stage I.
    - IF GFR is 60 – 89 ml/1.73m2/min, THEN Stage II.
    - IF GFR is 30 – 59 ml/1.73m2/min, THEN Stage III.
    - IF GFR is 15 – 29 ml/1.73m2/min, THEN Stage IV.
    - IF GFR < 15 ml/1.73m2/min, THEN Stage V (End-stage renal disease).

4.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    - Decrease protein intake to 0.6 g/kg/day.
    - Moderate sodium restriction to 60 mmol/day.
    - Phosphate restriction to 1000 mg/day.
    - IF obese, aim for BMI < 25.
    - Smoking cessation.

5.  **Pharmacological Treatment (Step-by-Step):**

    **Step – 1: Identify Etiology Clues:**
    - Check for history of hypertension or diabetes mellitus.
    - Check for symptoms related to lower urinary tract or recent infections.
    - Check for features of connective tissue disease.
    - Check past medical records for proteinuria.
    - Check for pregnancy-related problems or past renal evaluation.
    - Check family history of hereditary kidney disease (e.g., ADPKD, Alport syndrome).

    **Step – 2: Identify Reversible Factors:**
    - Check for obstructive nephropathy, urinary tract infections, extrarenal infections.
    - Check for nephrotoxic drugs, hypovolemia.
    - Check for congestive cardiac failure, pericardial tamponade.
    - Check for hypokalemia or hyperuricemia.

    **Step – 3: Blood Pressure Management:**
    - IF proteinuria > 1 g/day, THEN BP target is < 125/75 mmHg.
    - ELSE IF proteinuria < 1 g/day, THEN BP target is < 135/85 mmHg.
    - Choose one of the following antihypertensives:
        - Tab. frusemide 40-160 mg/day.
        - OR Tab. amlodipine 5-20 mg/day.
        - IF diabetic nephropathy, THEN Tab. enalapril 1.25-5 mg/day.

    **Step – 4: Manage Comorbidities:**
    - IF hyperlipidemia, THEN Tab. atorvastatin 10-20 mg/day.
    - IF anemia, THEN Inj. EPO 80-120 unit/kg/week SC.

    **Step – 5: Prevent and Treat Complications:**
    - Malnutrition:
        - Dietary protein intake of 0.6 g/kg body weight/day.
        - Dietary calorie intake of 30-35 kcal/kg/day.
    - Cardiovascular disease:
        - At least yearly screening.
    - Renal osteodystrophy:
        - Target calcium: 8.8 to 9.7 mg/dl.
        - Target phosphorous: 3.5 to 5.5 mg/dl.
        - Target Calcium-Phosphorous product: < 55 mg2/dl.
        - Treatment: Phosphate restricted diet, Tab. calcium carbonate 1 g/day, Tab. vitamin D3 0.25-2 mcg/day.

    **Step – 6: Prepare for Renal Replacement Therapy:**
    - Early referral to Nephrologists.
    - Vaccination: Hepatitis B vaccine 40 micrograms, 3 doses (0, 1st, 2nd month), followed by booster at 6th month.
    - Early creation of arterio-venous fistula IF GFR < 25 ml/min OR serum creatinine > 4.0 mg/dl.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- (None specified in the text for the listed drugs, but general contraindications apply based on drug specifics and patient comorbidities).
