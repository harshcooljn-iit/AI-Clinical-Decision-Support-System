```text
DISEASE: Atrial Fibrillation

ABBREVIATIONS:
- AF: Atrial Fibrillation
- BD: Twice daily
- INR: International Normalized Ratio
- OD: Once daily
- Tab: Tablet

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Clinical presentation (palpitation, breathlessness, giddiness, tachycardia, irregularly irregular pulse).
- ECG confirmation of atrial fibrillation.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF patient has Atrial Fibrillation (AF) THEN

    // Step 1: Address Precipitating Factors
    IF precipitating factors (e.g., thyrotoxicosis) are present THEN
        Treat the precipitating factors.
    ENDIF

    // Step 2: Assess Hemodynamic Stability
    IF patient is haemodynamically unstable THEN
        Perform electrical cardioversion using 200 joules.
    ELSE
        // Step 3: Rate Control (if haemodynamically stable)
        Administer Tab. Metoprolol 25-100 mg BD.

        // Step 4: Anticoagulation
        Administer Tab. Warfarin 5 mg OD.
        Monitor INR and adjust dose as needed.
    ENDIF

ENDIF

SURGICAL TREATMENT:
- Ablation around pulmonary veins is an option.
```
