DISEASE: Optic Neuritis

ABBREVIATIONS:
- MRI: Magnetic Resonance Imaging
- Tab: Tablet
- Inj: Injection
- gm: gram
- mg: milligram
- kg: kilogram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Sudden, progressive, profound visual loss.
- Decreased dark adaptation.
- Impaired color vision.
- Visual obscuration in bright light or with exertion.
- Possible mild dull eye ache, aggravated by ocular movements.
- Abnormal papillary responses (sluggish or afferent pupil defect).
- Fundus examination: Hyperaemia, blurred disc margins, disc oedema, obliterated cup, congested/tortuous veins, possible splinter haemorrhages/exudates.
- Slit-lamp: Possible inflammatory cells in vitreous.
- Visual field defect: Often relative central or centrocaecal scotoma.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

IF underlying cause is identifiable and treatable THEN
    Treat the underlying cause.
ELSE IF underlying cause is idiopathic, hereditary, or associated with demyelinating disorders THEN
    No specific effective treatment.
ENDIF

IF considering corticosteroid therapy THEN
    IF patient has acute optic neuritis THEN
        IF oral prednisolone therapy alone is considered THEN
            Oral prednisolone therapy alone is contraindicated because it does not improve visual outcome and increases the risk of new attacks.
        ENDIF

        IF intravenous methylprednisolone is considered THEN
            IF brain MRI scan is normal THEN
                IF visual loss affects both eyes simultaneously or subsequently within hours/days THEN
                    Administer Inj. methylprednisolone 1 gm/day (or 15 mg/kg/day) intravenously in 2-4 divided doses for 3 days.
                ENDIF

                IF only good eye is affected THEN
                    Administer Inj. methylprednisolone 1 gm/day (or 15 mg/kg/day) intravenously in 2-4 divided doses for 3 days.
                ENDIF

                IF slow progressive visual loss continues THEN
                    Administer Inj. methylprednisolone 1 gm/day (or 15 mg/kg/day) intravenously in 2-4 divided doses for 3 days.
                ENDIF
            ENDIF
        ENDIF

        IF intravenous methylprednisolone is administered THEN
            Follow with Tab. prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day orally for 11 days.
            Taper the Tab. prednisolone dose over the next week.
        ENDIF
    ENDIF
ENDIF

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Explain the potential for recurrence and permanent vision loss.
- Advise avoiding factors that provoke transient visual obscurations (exertion, heat, stress, anger).
