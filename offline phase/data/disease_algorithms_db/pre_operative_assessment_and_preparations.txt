1. DISEASE: Pre Operative Assessment And Preparations
2. ABBREVIATIONS:
    - Hb: Hemoglobin
    - TLC: Total Leukocyte Count
    - DLC: Differential Leukocyte Count
    - CT: Clotting Time
    - BT: Bleeding Time
    - ECG: Electrocardiogram
    - IV: Intravenous
3. DIAGNOSTICS:
    - Preoperative assessment involves evaluating the patient's disease, condition, urgency of surgery, type of surgery, and potential risks/benefits.
    - Routine investigations include Hb, TLC, DLC, CT, BT, urine routine examination, chest X-ray, and ECG (for patients over 30 years).
    - Special investigations may be needed based on the procedure and patient condition.
4. TREATMENT ALGORITHM:
    - IF the patient is undergoing surgery:
        - THEN gather and record all relevant information.
        - THEN devise a plan to minimize risk and maximize benefit.
        - THEN consider possible adverse events and how to deal with them.
        - THEN ensure the patient and relevant parties understand the surgical plan (informed consent).
    - IF preoperative assessment is needed:
        - THEN consider the disease and its extent.
        - THEN consider the patient's condition and organ systems.
        - THEN consider the urgency of the surgery.
        - THEN consider the type of surgery and alternatives.
        - THEN consider the morbidity and mortality of the disease and procedure.
        - THEN discuss medications and allergies with the patient.
    - IF informed consent is required:
        - THEN discuss the procedure, benefits, risks, and alternatives with the patient and relatives.
        - THEN allow time for questions and clarification.
    - IF routine preoperative investigations are needed:
        - THEN perform Hb, TLC, DLC, CT, BT, urine routine examination.
        - IF the patient is over 30 years old:
            - THEN perform chest X-ray and ECG.
    - IF special investigations are needed:
        - THEN perform investigations based on the procedure and patient condition.
    - IF preparing the patient for surgery:
        - THEN remove cosmetics (lipstick, nail polish) that may interfere with monitoring.
        - THEN remove non-essential items (dentures, spectacles, jewelry, phones).
        - IF withholding feeds:
            - THEN withhold feeds based on patient age, anesthesia type, and surgery type.
        - THEN bathe the patient on the day prior and morning of surgery, paying attention to the operative area.
        - THEN remove hair as close to surgery time as possible, preferably with clippers or chemical depilator, not shaving.
        - IF using chemical solutions for skin prep:
            - THEN ask about history of allergic reactions.
        - WHILE scrubbing:
            - THEN work away from the operative site.
            - THEN wash visibly soiled skin with soap and water before surgical scrub.
        - THEN administer preanesthetic medication as per policy.
    - IF choosing a surgical scrub:
        - IF using Povidone-iodine:
            - THEN use it combined with detergent.
            - THEN note it is effective against gram-positive bacteria.
            - THEN note it has residual activity and works in organic substances.
            - THEN note it can be absorbed through skin and used on mucus membranes.
            - THEN note potential for skin sensitivity.
        - IF using Chlorhexidine gluconate:
            - THEN note it is broad-spectrum and better bactericidal than povidone-iodine.
            - THEN note it has residual activity.
            - THEN note it is ineffective in the presence of organic material (soap, oils, blood).
            - THEN note it should not be used for eye preparation.
            - THEN note it can be used alone or with cetrimide.
        - IF using Alcohol (70% ethyl or isopropyl):
            - THEN note it is effective against gram-negative/positive bacteria, mycobacteria, fungi, viruses (not spores).
            - THEN ensure all traces are dry before draping.
            - THEN note it should not be used on mucus membranes or open wounds.
    - IF drug treatment is needed:
        - THEN administer prophylactic antibiotics (see specific section).
        - THEN administer antihypertensives, IV fluids, anticoagulants, Vitamin K, etc., as relevant.
    - IF non-drug treatment is needed:
        - THEN perform rectal washouts, stomach wash, etc., as relevant.
    - IF handling medicolegal cases:
        - THEN provide first aid.
        - THEN stabilize the patient.
        - THEN guide and help with transfer to an appropriate center.
        - THEN send information (duplicate) to the police.
        - THEN prepare a medicolegal report.
        - THEN preserve and seal clothes, fluid, and stain samples.
        - THEN respond to police inquiries.
        - THEN arrange for a dying declaration if indicated.
        - THEN preserve X-rays and patient records.
        - THEN respond to court summons.
        - IF death occurs:
            - THEN handover the body to the police.
        - IF discharge/referral occurs:
            - THEN inform the police.
    - IF transferring the patient:
        - THEN identify the degree of emergency.
        - THEN perform resuscitative measures if needed (shock management, oxygen).
        - THEN transfer in a well-equipped ambulance.
        - THEN intimation and confirmation the readiness at the referral centre.
        - THEN ensure a doctor or paramedical staff accompanies the patient.
        - THEN provide a referral slip with relevant information (condition, diagnosis, measures taken, reasons for referral, precautions, etc.).
5. CONTRAINDICATIONS:
    - Chlorhexidine gluconate should be avoided for eye preparation.
    - Alcohol should not be used on mucus membranes or open wounds.
    - Povidone-iodine may cause skin sensitivity in some patients.
