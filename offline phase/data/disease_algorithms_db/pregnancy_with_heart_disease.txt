DISEASE: Pregnancy With Heart Disease

ABBREVIATIONS:
- NYHA: New York Heart Association
- IM: Intramuscular
- PTT: Partial Thromboplastin Time
- IV: Intravenous
- Cap: Capsule
- Inj: Injection
- Kg: Kilogram
- G: Gram
- ml: Milliliter
- mg: Milligram

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Echocardiography is diagnostic.
- Clinical assessment based on NYHA class.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  MANAGEMENT SETTING:
    IF patient has heart disease and is pregnant THEN
    manage at a tertiary level center with a multidisciplinary approach.

2.  NYHA CLASSIFICATION:
    IF patient has heart disease and is pregnant THEN
    assess physical activity limitation to classify NYHA class I to IV.

3.  HOSPITALIZATION:
    IF NYHA class is III or IV THEN
    hospitalize throughout pregnancy.
    ELSE IF NYHA class is I or II THEN
    manage as an outpatient with frequent antenatal visits and admission at 38 weeks.

4.  LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS:
    IF patient has heart disease and is pregnant THEN
    recommend rest for 10 hours each night and 1 to 2 hours after each meal.
    Permit light housework and walking without climbing stairs.
    Prohibit heavy work.
    Advise to avoid high salt intake.
    Screen and treat excessive weight gain, abnormal fluid retention, anaemia, pregnancy induced hypertension, infections.

5.  PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT (Rheumatic Heart Disease):
    IF patient has rheumatic heart disease and is pregnant THEN
    administer Inj. benzathine penicillin 1.2 mega units IM every 3 weeks.
    Treat any infections with appropriate antibiotics.

6.  PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT (Mechanical Prosthetic Valves):
    IF patient has mechanical prosthetic valves and is pregnant THEN
    administer Inj. heparin throughout pregnancy to maintain PTT at 1.5 to 2.5 times the normal control.

7.  ORAL ANTICOAGULANTS:
    IF patient requires oral anticoagulants during pregnancy THEN
    CAUTION: Oral anticoagulants are not safe during pregnancy due to risk of congenital anomalies.
    IF pregnancy is in the first trimester THEN
    do not administer oral anticoagulants.
    ELSE IF pregnancy is after the first trimester THEN
    administer oral anticoagulants and continue until 4 weeks before delivery.
    Oral anticoagulants are safe during lactation.

8.  LABOUR MANAGEMENT - CAESAREAN SECTION:
    IF Caesarean section is required THEN
    perform for obstetrical indications only.

9.  LABOUR MANAGEMENT - PAIN RELIEF:
    IF pain relief is needed during labour THEN
    IF patient does NOT have intra cardiac shunts, aortic stenosis, pulmonary hypertension, or hypertrophic cardiomyopathy THEN
    give continuous epidural analgesia (best option).
    ELSE
    administer Inj. morphine for pain relief.

10. LABOUR MANAGEMENT - FLUIDS/MEDICATIONS:
    IF patient is in labour THEN
    restrict fluids to 75 ml/min.
    Avoid bolus oxytocin and methyl ergometrine.

11. LABOUR MANAGEMENT - INFECTIVE ENDOCARDITIS PROPHYLAXIS:
    IF patient has cardiac lesions and is undergoing any operative procedure or in labour THEN
    administer antimicrobial prophylaxis for infective endocarditis.
    Administer Inj. ampicillin 2 g and Inj. gentamicin 1.5 mg/kg (maximum 120 mg) IV or IM 30 min before procedure.
    Follow with Cap. ampicillin 1 g IM or IV; or Cap. amoxycillin 1 g orally 6 hours after initial dose.

12. LABOUR MANAGEMENT - PENICILLIN ALLERGY:
    IF patient is allergic to penicillin THEN
    administer Inj. vancomycin 1 g IV (over 1-2 hours) plus Inj. gentamicin 1.5 g/kg (maximum 120 mg).
    Complete infusion within 30 min before procedure.

13. PATIENT EDUCATION:
    IF patient has heart disease and is pregnant or planning pregnancy THEN
    discuss maternal risk based on type and severity of cardiac lesion.
    Consider corrective surgery preferably before pregnancy.
    Inform about 2-5% risk of congenital heart disease in foetus if mother has congenital heart disease.
    Provide contraceptive advice: sterilization after 2 weeks, progestogen only method, or barrier method.
    Counsel husband for male sterilization (preferably nonscalpel vasectomy).
    In severe cases, consider medical termination of pregnancy if pregnancy is <12 weeks.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Epidural analgesia is contraindicated in women with intra cardiac shunts, aortic stenosis, pulmonary hypertension, and hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
- Oral anticoagulants are contraindicated during the first trimester of pregnancy due to risk of congenital anomalies.
