ABBREVIATIONS:

- Staph: Staphylococcus

- *Povidone iodine*: Povidone iodine
- *Carbolic acid*: Carbolic acid
- *Trichloracetic acid*: Trichloracetic acid

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Conjunctival swab and ulcer scrapings tested with Gram stain, KOH preparation, and culture.
- Routine investigations like complete blood count, blood sugar, urine examination.
- Septic foci should be found.

TREATMENT ALGORITHM:

1.  **Initial Assessment:**
    *   Patient presents with history of eye trauma, pain, redness, photophobia, discharge, and/or dimness of vision.
    *   Examination shows conjunctival and circumcorneal vessel engorgement, localized epithelial disintegration, saucer-shaped ulcer projecting above corneal surface, surrounding grey discoloration (cloudiness), and possible leucocytic infiltration.
    *   Severe cases may have pronounced anterior chamber reaction, often with hypopyon.

2.  **Non-Pharmacological Treatment:**
    *   Maintain proper ocular hygiene by cleaning discharge twice a day.
    *   Remove contributory factors (e.g., trichiasis, foreign body).
    *   Cover the eye with a pad unless discharge is copious; if copious, shield with dark goggles.
    *   Prevent and treat complications like secondary glaucoma.

3.  **Pharmacological Treatment (Antibiotics):**
    *   Start with empirical broad-spectrum antibiotics.
    *   Adjust antibiotics based on sensitivity report.

4.  **Antibiotic Selection and Dosage (Based on Table 1):**

    *   **IF** the organism is Gram-negative (*Gm -ve*) and *Staphylococcus* species:
        *   **THEN** use Gentamicin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 3-5 mg/kg/day I/V or I/M in divided doses.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 20 mg.
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 0.3% drops and ointment 8 hourly.

    *   **IF** the organism is the same as Gentamicin indication:
        *   **THEN** use Tobramycin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer Same as gentamycin.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 5-20 mg.
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 0.3% drops 8 hourly.

    *   **IF** the organism is Gram-positive cocci (*Gm +ve cocci*) and Gram-negative rods (*Gm -ve rods*):
        *   **THEN** use Cefazolin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 25-50 mg/kg/day IV or IM in divided doses 8 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 100 mg.
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 5% concentration as topical drops 8 hourly.

    *   **IF** the organism is Gram-negative (*Gm -ve*), specifically *Pseudomonas*:
        *   **THEN** use Ceftazidime.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 1-2 gm IV 8-12 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 100 mg.
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no topical dose listed).

    *   **IF** the organism is broad spectrum including most aerobic Gram-negative (*Gm-ve*), *Staphylococcus aureus* (*S. aureus*), and *Staphylococcus epidermidis* (*S. epidermidis*):
        *   **THEN** use Ciprofloxacin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 500 mg orally 12 hourly OR 200 mg IV 12 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no subconjunctival dose listed).
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 0.3% drops and ointment 8 hourly.

    *   **IF** the organism requires broad spectrum coverage:
        *   **THEN** use Ofloxacin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 200-800 mg/day orally OR 200 mg I/V 12 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no subconjunctival dose listed).
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 0.3% drops 8 hourly.

    *   **IF** the organism is Gram-positive (*Gm +ve*) and Gram-negative (*Gm -ve*), including *Pseudomonas*, but NOT effective against anaerobes:
        *   **THEN** use Norfloxacin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 400 mg orally 12 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no subconjunctival dose listed).
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 0.3% drops 8 hourly.

        *   **THEN** use Lomefloxacin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 400 mg orally or IV 12 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no subconjunctival dose listed).
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer Initially 5 drops in 20 min or 1 drop every hour for 6/10 hours. Then 1 drop 2-3 times a day.

    *   **IF** the organism is broad spectrum, Gram-positive (*Gm +ve*) and Gram-negative (*Gm -ve*), including anaerobes:
        *   **THEN** use Sparfloxacin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 400 mg orally on day 1, followed by 200 mg once a day.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no subconjunctival dose listed).
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 0.3% drops 8 hourly.

            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:

            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:

            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:

        *   **THEN** use Cephalexin.
            *   **IF** systemic administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer 500 mg orally 6 hourly.
            *   **IF** subconjunctival administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no subconjunctival dose listed).
            *   **IF** topical administration is needed:
                *   **THEN** administer (no topical dose listed).

5.  **Additional Treatments:**
    *   **Iridocyclitis Control:** Use atropine 1% eye drops or ointment to relieve ciliary spasm and prevent synechia formation.
    *   **Necrotic Material Removal:** If ulcer progresses despite pharmacological treatment, consider scraping or cauterization (100% carbolic acid, 10% trichloracetic acid, 10% or 5% povidone iodine).
    *   **Non-Resolving Ulcers:** Consider surgical methods (vasculoplasty, conjunctival hooding and patch graft, tectonic penetrating keratoplasty, tarsorraphy).

6.  **Patient Education:**
    *   Use dark goggles.
    *   Avoid close contact with others for two weeks.
    *   Restrict work/school if significant exposure to others.
    *   Practice frequent handwashing.
